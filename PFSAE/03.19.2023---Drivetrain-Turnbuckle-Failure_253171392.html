Purdue Formula SAE : 03.19.2023 - Drivetrain Turnbuckle Failure

Created by Panchal, Aarnav Nilesh, last modified on Mar 20, 2023

Images:

Description of Failure:

When lining up for launch upleft turnbuckle was seen to have bent rod ends on both sides, upon closer inspection it was found that the upright had also curved into the direction of the differential to move with the new upleft turnbuckle geometry. The turnbuckle itself seems to be unharmed, however, the rod ends are unusable. It was also seen that the upright lower tabs were also bent accordingly to the new differential position. The upleft tabs seem to have a slight angle again due to differential position.

Upon disconnecting the turnbuckles and removing the chain (differential package only resting on bottom tabs and connected to wheels via halfshafts) the car was rolled back to the trailer to be packed up. The differential package then swung fully backwards, pulling the boots on the outboard sides off and exposing the left rear CV. The left rear CV was shredded, the CV insert was also pulled out. This is a joint issue with suspension and is being explored.
