Purdue Formula SAE : Sight Tube

Created by Unknown User (jpeerbol), last modified on Jul 22, 2020

In Previous seasons -6 AN size fittings have been welded at the top and bottom of the tank to make a sight tube so that oil levels in the dry sump tank can be easily checked.

Earl's auto-mate hose end fittings have been used with clear tubing and hose clamps to create the tube, this works but the tubing material is difficult to find that is the proper diameter to fit the barbs and can make a poor seal causing oil leaks.

For the 2020 season 52705K37 tubing will be used to make the sight tube with standard Earl's swivel-seal fittings will be tried. TheÂ 52705K37 tubing should be a similar ID and OD to Earl's Pro Lite hose and should make a tight seal, if it does not the -6AN bungs on the oil tank will be capped and a simple dipstick will be used to check the oil level.
