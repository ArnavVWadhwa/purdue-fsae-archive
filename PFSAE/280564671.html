Purdue Formula SAE : Harness: Heatshrink Sizing

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Dec 06, 2023

Background:

Heatshrink sizing is a less critical part of design than something like splice points but if not done properly it will pose a major problem during manufacturing and sealing of the harness.


Basics:

Heatshrink is sized based on the diameter of each bundle of wires.


Step 1: Add Up Wire Gauges

Before a bundle diameter can be calculated the number of each gauge of wire in said bundle must be added together. To do this right click on the bundle and select show connections within bundle.


From here each connection can be entered and the sum of each type of wire can be taken.


Step 2: Calculate Bundle Diameter

Use the rbracing calculator found here: https://www.rbracing-rsr.com/22759_diameter_shielded.html. Simply enter the sum of wire gauges found in the previous step and observe the output.


Step 3: Choose Heatshrink Size

Choose the largest size heatshrink that has an unshrunk diameter less than harness diameter calculated in the last step. This will make it easier to slide the heatshrink over the bundle and ensure that it is not overstrained and inflexble.


Step 4: Add Heatshrink in RapidHarness

All Raychem DR25 heatshrink sizes have all been created in RapidHarness. All that remains should be to assign different sizes to different bundles. To do so right click again on the bundle but this time choose show bundle properties.


Then hit add covering and assign the sized heatshrink.

Considerations:

  • Order extra of the larger sizes of heatshrink to allow for covering of joints, it is difficult to account for their added size beforehand
