    • could try to see what ktm 450 sx-f teams run, because likely similar system. (both wet sumps)
    • no output shaft for oil pump if we had to go external!
  • 1 vs 4 cyl considerations
    • intake tuning is debatably easier because less interference in the plenum, also the plenum has to be larger and side intake becomes very easy. Optimizing flow becomes much more important because the restrictor becomes a bigger issue as the air volume request is larger per instant even though it is less per average. This means fuel selection becomes more important because fuel choice influences restrictor size which is now more of a limiting factor than before. Moving to stronger fuels may be a good way to draw some more power out of what will become a power limited car, but will absolutely require better tuning knowledge/ ability because then you approach knock significantly sooner and if you are going to limit your air further you need to be 100% confident that the extra power from the fuel compressibility more than makes up for it.
    • Exhaust design becomes much easier, but you lose the benefits from short range scavenging.
    • Muffler design becomes more focused on wave attenuation- but that becomes more simple.
    • Think about cost savings over time from less/ cheaper parts. PF20 PT ~$7.3k
  • Engine scan:
    • ~300 if we just pay for external
    • should try Bolton Works for the quality scan. rumor is it's free if you provide and engine they haven't done.
