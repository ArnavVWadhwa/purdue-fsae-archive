  • Roll Centers (intersection of the line drawn by IC of wheel and contact patch) 
    • As the chassis rolls RCH moves downwards and sideways
    • Why are they placed where they are?
      • Front RCH should be lower than the Rear as this inclination of the roll axis is tilted forward. This causes the front roll couple to be greater than the rear causing natural understeer and excess traction capacity at the rear for acceleration. Should be set to be parallel to the mass centroid axis with compensation in inclination for the mass difference between the front and rear. - K.K
    • How can we control them?
      • Roll centers are controlled by the angles of the control arms. Use this to determine an instant center, and then draw from IC to contact patch. where the lines intersect for each side of the suspension is where the roll center is. -ZM
      • Controlled by:
        • inboard points
        • outboard points
        • tire choice (changes contact patch location, specifically the width of the tire)

      • How can we determine this ratio? (ties in with "Why are they placed where they are?"?)
