Purdue Formula SAE : Stellantis Wind Tunnel

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Mar 13, 2023

CFD Considerations

  • +0.5in ride height with pad extensions
    • Possibly form fitted to tire contact patch
  • No moving ground and rotating tire
  • 25 mm of boundary layer thickness on the ground at test section

Test Plan

  • 4 runs per hour for 8 hours (32 runs)
  • 30s of data record

Sweeps without having to change vehicle configuration are faster (several minutes in between data records to reach steady flow)

CFD changes

  • Domain only extends to -4 m in the x direction to get 25 mm of boundary layer
    • Although precisely 25 mm is hard to get since the cells tend to get pretty big at the edge of the boundary layer
  • 20 prism layers on the ground to make sure ground wall y+ is good
    • Volume change from prism layer cell to outer mesh is big, but don't really want to refine the mesh even more
  • 25 mm of prism layer thickness
  • 5% turbulent viscosity ratio to ensure boundary layer on the ground is close to laminar (as suggest by Dr. Shih)
  • No moving ground and rotating wheels
  • No exhaust
  • No fan
  • Yaw is done through sideslip to make sure boundary layer thickness is correct
    • 2D scenes are weird
