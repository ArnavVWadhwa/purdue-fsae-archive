Purdue Formula SAE : UT-13-00

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Aug 28, 2023

Simulation: CZ-052

Baseline: ML-057

Aero Intent:

  • Increase FWD floor outwash 
  • Pressurizing OBD diff to reduce squirt inwash
  • Limit vorticity formation and reduces losses in the main channel
  • Reduce FW back pressure
  • Increase front tire wake outwash through pressurization
  • Reduce SPOD losses and aero impacts of SPOD
  • Increase forward floor loading through outwash and venting

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Everything

Fillet on the on the edge of the center section to limit vorticity shedding due to outwash in FWD floor and RWD floor.

Moved throat RWD to depressurize inlet and reduce back pressure for FW.

RWD skirt is inclined at 60 deg to reduce shedding edge aggressiveness. Fillet to reduce shedding edge aggressiveness.

Skirt to diff fillet to reduce losses at junction.

RWD skirt are progressively outwashing to pressurize OBD diff and limit inwash of losses. The outwash also produces vorticity to load up diff and reduce back pressure.

Outwashing fence to, well, outwash front tire wake.

First FWD strake is close to fence and more loaded than the fence to limit fence vorticity and loss entrainment.

Second FWD strake is there to support first strake to reduce losses.

Curl at the strake OBD end, called HHD curl, reduces back pressure and load up FWD floor.

Removed ST skirt footplate to unload skirt and reduce vorticity shed and losses in the channel.


Everything

No more SPOD inlet at the front since powertrain doesn't want it anyways. This way, we have a full wing surface to increase downwash for RW.

Fence is much larger than before to increase front tire wake outwash.

SPOD inlet is located on top surface so that rad fan can act as BL remover to improve flow attachment.

Everything

SPOD outlet located near engine to dump losses in a place insensitive to aero.
