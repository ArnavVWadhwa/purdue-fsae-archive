Purdue Formula SAE : FW-13-00

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 26, 2023

Simulation: CZ_001

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

Tip loading the front wing and backing off the center

Backing off the center to reduce loss going into the undertray. Also improve outwash on the suction side. Expected to improve front tire wake control with increased outwash. Should enable widened undertray inlet.

Leading edge for endplate

Added leading edge for front wing endplate to reduce loss and to increase effectiveness of diveplane compared to PF23.

Curved footplate

Curved endplate to house a vortex better and improve local load as well as reduce inwash by inducing a stagnation point on the ground.

Endplate mass flow injector

Added duct connecting outside of endplate the inside behind flaps to inject mass flow into the vortex to reduce vortex bursting. Additional mass flow also increases outwash by the endplate due to increase energy in the region.

Diveplane placement

Diveplane placed right in front of the mass flow injector to increase mass flow through the duct by inducing a low pressure region in front of the inlet.

Connected main element flap and nosecone.

Improve local load and downstream load by better controlling main element flap pressure recovery.

Flow conditioner

Added flow conditioner to improve rear wing load. Placed relatively low to increase pressure near the chassis behind the front wing. Should improve front tire wake control and allows for widened undertray inlet. Pressurization will also increase local load on the undertray inlet by inducing additional downwash.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

What's stagnation point again?

Nosecone connection working as intended.

Vortex channel has vortex bursting.

Mass injector releasing high pressure flow early could help unload the tips and reduce bursting. 

Losses under the nose is pretty good. Again, nosecone working as intended.

Front wing to body panel junction losses is pretty big. Should consider smoothing it out at the cost of manufacturing complexity.

Vortex from front wing strake bursts quickly and the wake is inwashed. This is the severely limiting undertray performance.

Lateral expansion with strakes closer to the centerline could help reduce this inwash, but the vortex formed by the strake would not be good as it is inwashing tire squirt, so that will need to be managed. The lateral expansion will help load up the center and unload the tips, improve tip vortex management.

Stagnation point not great, not terrible. Increasing angle of attack will help rear wing out.
