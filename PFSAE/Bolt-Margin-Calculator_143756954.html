Currently calculates bolt tension, bolt shear, nut tension, flange shear through, flange shear out, bearing yield, and separation failures. Tension failures are taken from worst case from maximum preload without joint separation and joint separation (so all load goes through bolt) failures. Be sure to consider whether you should be dividing load for any double shear connections. Joint stiffness calculations for preload come from Shigley's Frustrum Approach. Shearout comes from Airframe Stress Analysis and Sizing area calculations come from NASA Handbook for Bolted Joint Design. Safety factor calculations come from NASA-STD-5020. Look up tables are used to get the right geometry based on the selection of a hardware.

Recommended torques for NAS 66XX and NAS 62XX bolts with MS21042 nuts:

  • use 160KSI DRY Column MAX value
  • for a 3/8 bolt for example, this would be 405 in-lbf.
  • Using max because we aren't taking running torque into account. Running torque is the torque that isn't coming from preloading the bolt. This is due to the locking feature and any friction between the bolt shank and the hole if torqueing the bolt not the nut. (This is why it's always recommended to turn the nut if possible).

Note: #10 size not listed. Use 50 in-lbf.
