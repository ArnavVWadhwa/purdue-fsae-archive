Process:

We ran a mesh independence study on our 2020-2021 CFD environment to ensure that the we are within an envelope of volume mesh count where our solution is independent of the mesh size. As of now for the study we only varied our volume mesh base size which changes the cell count for the volume mesh and didn't vary anything with our surface control. Alongside varying the volume mesh count we also compared two different mesh geometries; polyhedral and trimmed.  


Decision: 

Theoretically the poly mesher should be better for our use case and it would yield a lower volume mesh count for the same base size compared to trimmed mesher alongside us having better control over the surface mesh. However, the Poly sims are not mesh independent in the slightest based off the mesh sizes we chose and it would take a lot more effort that could be well spent towards actual Aero design to fix it. So, we are sticking with the a trimmed mesher and the same base size.  We have all the data to show that the volume mesh is mesh independent at least. The only thing is that the 0.2 Cl loss is more surface induced than the Volume mesh so it will take time to actually figure out where we are sensitive in terms of surface controls which I'll be doing the next couple of weeks
