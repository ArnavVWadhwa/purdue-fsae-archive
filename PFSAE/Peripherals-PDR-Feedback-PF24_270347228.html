Purdue Formula SAE : Peripherals PDR Feedback PF24

Created by Dien Quang Nguyen, last modified on Sep 18, 2023


Zach's Feedback:

  • If the seat/headrest isn't broken, don't fix it, but don't make that an excuse to not understand the reasoning behind all the design decisions and assumptions behind the current design / analyze the design with updated load cases and ensure structures standards is met
  • Don't forget about sealing/cover for shoulder harness slots on firewall 
  • PF22 drivers complained about nape of neck being too close to shoulder harness bar, make sure you take that into account when placing seat mounts/headrest position
  • For now, the seat can be modelled as a single panel of sandwich panel for CLT calculation. Should def try to break it down and analyze sections of the seat separately though
  • Make sure to add the right hardware and components into CAD for headrest
  • Look into threaded inserts on McMaster for lower seat mounts


Aarnav's Feedback:

  • For headrest bolt calculation, bearing 1.5 and 2.5 can be extrapolated 
  • Loading on firewall bolts/bolted connections might need to also consider deflections from seat due to driver inertial load


Matt's Feedback:

  • PF23 seat felt really good
  • Should keep the shoulder area the same, torso side support as well, those felt fine and fit well last year
  • Butt support could be either the seat for seat inserts, depending on whether you want to use seat inserts or not
  • Space out shoulder strap so they won't dig into my neck when i drive
  • Only issue with seat inserts is steering wheel clearance got really bad when we tried to use them
