Purdue Formula SAE : 0. Relevant Rules

Created by Unknown User (hammerly) on Jun 21, 2022

Reviewing the 2022 FSAE rules, here are the rules that account for a pneumatic shifting system.

Section T.6.1 Compressed Gas Cylinders and Lines covers pneumatic systems.

RegulationRuleComment / Interpretation
T.6.1.1Working Gas - The working gas must be non flammableCompressed air is non-flammable
T.6.1.2Cylinder Certification - The gas cylinder/tank must be commercially manufactured, designed and built for the pressure being used, certified by an accredited testing laboratory in the country of its origin, and labeled or stamped appropriatelyThis means we must use legit purchased parts
T.6.1.3Pressure Regulation - The pressure regulator must be mounted directly onto the gas cylinder/tankThis is the case with the tank that Alex recommended, as well as most HPA (high-pressure air) tanks
T.6.1.4Protection - The gas cylinder/tank and lines must be protected from rollover, collision from any direction, or damage resulting from the failure of rotating equipmentKeep the entire system contained within the Rollover Envelope
T.6.1.5The driver must be protected from failure of the cylinder/tank and regulatorKeep pressurized system behind firewall (and engine)
T.6.1.6

Cylinder Location - The gas cylinder/tank and pressure regulator must be:

a. Located outside of the cockpit

b. One or both of:

  • Inside the Primary Structure Envelope
  • Protected by structure meeting F.5.15 Component Protection

Keep pressurized system behind MRH

T.6.1.7Cylinder Mounting - The gas cylinder/tank must be securely mounted on a path to the ChassisYou cannot mount the cylinder to the engine, it must have its own mounting to the frame
T.6.1.8Cylinder Axis - The axis of the gas cylinder/tank must not point at the driverPoint the axis along the Y direction
T.6.1.9Insulation - The gas cylinder/tank must be insulated from any heat sourcesDon't position the tank right by the exhaust / engine
T.6.1.10Lines and Fittings - The gas lines and fittings must be appropriate for the maximum possible operating pressure of the systemSize lines & fittings properly

There are no rules that explicitly mention pneumatic shifters / mechanical redundancy.

The only rule that mentions blipping the throttle for downshifts is IC.4.1.3 which states that an ETC system may be used to blip the throttle during downshifts. There is no mention of a shifting system controlling the spark / fuel.
