Purdue Formula SAE : Brake Calipers, Purchasing

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified on May 02, 2022

Front Calipers (Wilwood GP200) are a dime a dozen. Summit basically always them in stock, and in a pinch, our team probably has half a dozen of them laying around in various states of broken.


Rear Calipers (AP Racing CP4226) are a bit harder to get. There is only 1 dealer of AP racing parts in the US and that is Essex Parts in NC.

Supply for these calipers is usually tight, although it isn't impossible to find. Basically if you know you're running these on the car, give them a call and place the order ASAP.

Also please double check payment. They will try to use a card on file from previous orders, and chances are that's the card of a former team lead that has since graduated. (how to do involuntary donations, aka credit card fraud)

https://www.essexparts.com/


An invoice from essex from past years for reference.


Notes from 02 May 2022:

Currently difficult to get the composite metallic pads (150-14407K) for the GP200. Wilwood basically isn't producing them. I talked to a Dave from Wilwood engineering on the phone and he suggest Purple compound as an alternative. I mentioned that we run chromoly rotors and we've seen temps up to 900-950F and he said that shouldn't be a significant issue.

His contact is 805 388 1188 Ext 311.
