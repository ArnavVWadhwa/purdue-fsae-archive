Terminal Block Philosophy and why they are on the dyno

Terminal blocks are often used in industrial applications, you might use them for manufacturing controls or something like a test bench. They are great for this application because they are very neat (see din rail) and allow lots of flexibility in terms of connecting and disconnecting connections.

Terminal Blocks are on this generation of the dyno because they allow flexibility for wiring. Every pin from the Motec modules goes to a terminal block, so if you need to change a sensor, add a component, or just change the location of a connector you don't have to re-pin an entire wiring harness. The goal for terminal blocks and din rail on the dyno was to also make wiring cleaner and more neat. We have had issues in the past with messy wiring harness and terminal blocks were selected to combat this issue.


The terminal blocks selected are from phoenix contact and are a spring type so the connection cannot come loose after vibrating on the dyno for hours.

These are dyno only screw drivers, they are the correct size for these terminal blocks.

do not remove from dyno! They are 2.5mm wide

How to use the terminal blocks

  1. Stick your 2.5mm screw driver or smaller into the rectangular hole aiming towards the center of the terminal block and push outwards, this will open up the spring.
  2. Push your wire with ferrule into the terminal. (you can use these without ferrules in a pinch, but ferrules make everything better.)
  3. Remove the screwdriver
  4. Give the wire a light tug and see if it'll come out. If it's loose, try again.
  5. Your wire is now retained in the terminal block

How to Crimp Wire Ferrules

Wire ferrules are crazy simple and make terminal connections way better.

  1. Strip you wire long
  2. Slide ferrule over stripped wire, if you have a correctly sized ferrule it will stop where the insulation starts. It is easiest if you have a little wire sticking out the front
  3. Stick ferrule in crimper and crimp
  4. Trim wires off the front
  5. You're done


How to jumper terminal blocks

If you want to connect multiple terminal blocks together you can use jumpers.

  1. Cut your jumper to how many positions you want to connect together.
  2. Push jumper into center connections of the terminal blocks
    1. I would recommend alternating positions to avoid confusion of what terminal blocks are jumpered together.
