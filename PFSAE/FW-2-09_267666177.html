Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-09

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 18, 2023

Simulation ML-051

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Altered inner diveplane shape

Trying to increase vorticity here resulting in a weaker endplate vortex, which should aid in outwashing the turning vane vortex in Y15.

Alternatively, this could result in more inwash since the vortex may just inwash a ton. Want to see if there is anything to gain here.

Altered outer diveplane shape, and reintroduced the endplate cutout along with an outer strake cutout.

Diveplane shape should keep pressure high on upper surface resulting in a weaker endplate vortex. Weakening the endplate vortex should allow the turning vane vortex to move it further outboard/upward.

Endplate cutout works great in Y15 by allowing higher energy flow lower down to enter into the UT. The problem comes up when in Y0 a vortex that controls tire squirt does not form off the outboard side of the outer most strake. This vortex is key to UT performance, thus as a result a strake cutout has been made to ensure a vortex forms here, and hopefully a strong one.

CFD Analysis

Inner diveplane vortex looks good, outwash seems to be improved. 

Outer diveplane vortex is lower strength, seemingly no performance gain/change in flow field.

Previous:

This iteration shows a more powerful vortex in the 2nd channel, that ultimately bursts due to the increase in inwash due to the endplate cut. 

Seems to me like the cutout just wont work in Y0 due to the increase in inwash at the current amount of cutout. Potentially a smaller cutout would be better, but it really seems like the performance loss is just not worth it. That 2nd channel vortex needs to survive the whole way to manage tire squirt.
