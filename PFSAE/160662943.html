Purdue Formula SAE : 10/24/20 Testing Old Pump with New Setup

Created by Unknown User (dnocon), last modified by Williams, Carson Kenneth on Oct 28, 2020

Old Pump did not help with load control, even with new pressure regulator / gate valve setup. 


RIP Gisele 

Spun her rod bearing (cylinder 1).

We took her apart and the oil seems like it had fuel in it. Oil was very very light. (The viscosity was very low.)

  • Low Viscosity could have stemmed from:
    • Overheating Oil could break it down 
    • Fuel in oil from leaking injector (popped off fuel rail and was spraying fuel)
    • Lambda was not connected and we were not utilizing closed loop
      • open loop is rich asf so running it like that over time could lead to fuel build up in the oil


After Gisele died we tested pump mass flows (time to fill 14 gallon tank) data shown below


Pump no restriction 12 seconds to fill 14 gallons


Restriction low pressure through dyno 7 min for 14 gallons 


Restriction high pressure through dyno 10 minutes for 14 gallons 

-next time engine runs run it at very very low pressure


Restriction high pressure before dyno 10 minutes for 14 gallons
