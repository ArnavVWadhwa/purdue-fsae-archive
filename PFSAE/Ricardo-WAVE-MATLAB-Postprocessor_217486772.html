Purdue Formula SAE : Ricardo WAVE MATLAB Postprocessor

Created by Unknown User (dnocon), last modified on Jun 20, 2022

The Ricardo Postprocessor is pretty bad at creating usable plots. I developed a matlab script to read an excel / csv document and automatically generate graphs based on wave data.


As of June 2022 the postprocessor generates:

  1. Manifold Referenced VE vs. RPM vs. Manifold Pressure (kPa) interpolated (set var graphDensity = 20)
    1. used to correlate WAVE model to real life
  2. Ambient Referenced VE vs. RPM vs. Manifold Pressure (kPa) interpolated (set var graphDensity = 20)
    1. used just to have
  3. Torque ft-lbs vs. RPM vs. Manifold Pressure (kPa) interpolated (set var graphDensity = 20)
    1. used to correlate WAVE model to real life
  4. Torque ft-lbs vs. RPM vs. Manifold Pressure (kPa) interpolated but showing raw values from wave. (set var graphDensity = 20)
    1. used to ensure that data points from WAVE are being interpolated properly
  5. Throttle Pos vs. Manifold Pressure (kPa) (set var graphDensity = 100)
    1. Used to correlate with i2 data as another way to validate TB sizeÂ 
  6. Throttle Pos vs. Torque ft-lbs (set var graphDensity = 100)
    1. Used to show how throttle pos affects the torque delivered (tb sizing confirmation from a drivers perspective)
