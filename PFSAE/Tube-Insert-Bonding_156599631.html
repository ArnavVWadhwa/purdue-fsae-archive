Purdue Formula SAE : Tube Insert Bonding

Created by Unknown User (agarw162), last modified on Aug 29, 2020

This page will explain the steps to properly bond inserts to carbon tubes

Supplies Needed

Bond Line Controller 

  • Rock West Composites Bond Line Controller - 0.0070" : https://www.rockwestcomposites.com/1031-25

Epoxy Supplies

  • Epoxy Type - Loctite E-120HP Hysol: https://www.rockwestcomposites.com/1027-50
  • Epoxy Mixing/Dispensing Gun 
  • Small Brushes
  • Cup 
  • Gloves for Application

3M 220 Grit Sandpaper

Sand Blaster


Step 1 - Insert Machining

Before starting anything with bonding, the main parameter to control is the outer diameter of the insert shaft. Because of the diameter of the bond line controller, the outer diameter of the insert shaft should always be machined to 0.014" under the inner diameter of the carbon tube. Make sure that every single insert being bonded is within 0.014" of the ID of the C.F tube with a +- 0.0005" tolerance. 


Step 2 - Aluminum & C.F Tube Prep

First the surface area of the aluminum inserts that will be bonded must be sanded and degreased. To sand the alumnimum inserts it's easiest to use a sandblaster. The inside of the carbon fiber tube that will be bonded must also be sanded and degreased. The easiest way is to roll up a strip of 3M sandpaper and twist it within the tube. Brake cleaner or a similar type of degreaser can be used to degrease these components. By prepping the components through sanding, it gives it a rough bonding surface hence more bond area. After both surfaces have dried, you are ready for adhesion. 

Note: We should probably buy an etching kit and implement an etching process to increase bond strength


Step 3 - Insert to Tube Adhesion

The preparation of bonding surfaces is one of the key aspects to an insert to tube bond however it is also important to centralize the insert in the carbon fiber tube so that the bond gap is uniform throughout the circumference of the insert.  The method we use is a tiny amount of microbeads known as bond line controller that can be mixed in with the epoxy and give you the specific bond gap you are looking for. Mix a small amount of epoxy in a cup alongside mixing a tiny amount of the beads into the epoxy. When it’s time to bond the inserts to the tubes, take the epoxy and spread it evenly on the inside of the tube. Then take epoxy and spread it evenly on the outside of the aluminum insert surface. Slowly insert the aluminum insert into the tube and spin it slowly so that the epoxy spreads evenly along the surface of the bond. Once the insert is all the way in the tube, wipe off any excess epoxy and allow the tube to cure overnight for at least 24 hours.
