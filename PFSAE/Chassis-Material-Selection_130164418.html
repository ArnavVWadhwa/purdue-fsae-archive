4130 steel cut by VR3

  • Structural tab tube wall tear out failure mode (see structural template and Air frame Stress Analysis and Sizing for calculations) mitigated from improved material properties over 1018 or other cheaper steel.
  • Have access to well documented lug calculations.
  • Material property boost from 1018 to 4130 not necessary for tube failures (stress is real low) or strength of the chassis.
  • Stiffness is NOT impacted when comparing 4130 to other steels (E ~ 29 * 10^6 psi for all steels)

example props from mmpds


Filler Rod

ER70S-2 (selected, used in 2019 and 2018 cars at least)

  • most forgiving
  • when combined with 4130 < 0.125" you don't need preheat, interpass, or postheating processes (we can't do this stuff)
  • welds well over oily/dirty/rusty surfaces (should still prep surfaces real well)
  • quick freezing weld pool
  • ideal choice for tubing and out of position welds
  • Material Properties (AWS A5.18) (min)
    • Yield: 58 ksi
    • Ultimate: 70 ksi
    • Elongation: 28%
    • YOU ARE GIVING UP STRENGTH FOR THE ABOVE BENEFITS

ER80S-2 (not tested)

  • less forgiving than ER70S-2
  • Material Properties (AWS A5.28) (min)
    • Yield: 68 ksi
    • Ultimate: 80 ksi

ER120S (not tested)

  • Material Properties (AWS A5.28) (min)
    • Yield: 105 ksi
    • Ultimate: 120 ksi

Heat Treat

Method: we have typically done a 'stress relief'. this done NOT get to a temperature where changes to grain structure in the steel happens.

Reasoning: stress relief reduces residual stresses in the steel and also tempers martensite at well locations to reduce the chance of cracking in heat effected zones
