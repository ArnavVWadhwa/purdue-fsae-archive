Purdue Formula SAE : NARCCO 17.4in Rack Disassembly

Created by Dien Quang Nguyen, last modified by Singhal, Ria on Oct 31, 2023

Feedback from PF23 season was mainly that there was a ton of play in the steering system. We found out that the NARCCo rack that was used was crunchy when we received it, and it was "decrunchified" before putting on the car. To determine the source of the crunchiness and play, we decided to take apart the PF23 rack.

We also had no idea what the design architecture of the rack looked like on the inside, so reverse engineering the rack was a good learning experience before starting the design of our rack. 

The goal of this page is to document the disassembly, components and lessons learned from the NARCCO 17.4 in steering rack that the team has been using for a long time.

Observations:

  • lots of gunk (not just grease)
    • hella chips
  • back of rack is super chewed up 
  • plastic inserts keep the rack in place
  • housing is all one piece??
  • pinion has 13 teeth
  • retaining ring holds bearing in
  • pinion attachment to column point looks rough. 


Lessons learned for design:

  • emphasis on anti-rotation mechanism to avoid formation of chips
  • no aluminum-on-aluminum translational motion
  • hard stops should be plastic
  • plastic inserts???
