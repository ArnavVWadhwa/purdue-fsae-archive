Halfshaft Angle Analysis

The CAD file used to complete this analysis is PF23_POW_7999_kinematics

  • Parameters:
    • engagement → Turnbuckle thread engagement
    • wheel_offset → Change in wheel height
    • a_left_halfshaft_angle → Left halfshaft angle measurement
    • a_right_halfshaft_angle → Right halfshaft angle measurement
  • The inboard/outboard points of the halfshaft were wave linked from the layout assembly and build off of so that the changing turnbuckle length would factor into halfshaft position

These halfshaft angles are determined for a 37 tooth sprocket with the diff axis positioned such that the starting turnbuckle length yields 62 chain links with ~0 slack. The max turnbuckle length is for compensating chain stretch.

Turnbuckle lengths are used from the PF23 Diff Uprights page.

  • Max Turnbuckle is a rod end thread engagement of 0.25 inches
  • The minimum turnbuckle length is not considered since it would reduce halfshaft angle, though reduce clearances with chassis
    • There is a small amount of adjustment here, so clearances are not changed to a meaningful degree

Wheel Heights are relative to where the car would sit in equilibrium on the ground

  • -1" is the wheel lowering by 1 inch of suspension travel
  • +1" is the wheel raising by 1 inch of suspension travel
  • These are the limits of suspension travel during vehicle operation
