Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 Frame & Tab Jigs + Considerations/ Potential Improvements

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified by Dien Quang Nguyen on Aug 10, 2023

Overall Jig

Each stage was jigged and welded fully before proceeding to the next stage. Reference sets were used to know which jigs 


Considerations/ Potential improvements MUST READ

  • Add in a cutout into the front bulkhead plate for torch clearance when welding front tubes later on
  • Reuse previous jigs when possible (it might be more work to try ad get an old jig to work, but I think we're at the point were the number of new jigs should be less than 10 each year)
  • Be meticulous when placing & constraining jigs. Avoid overlapping holes as much as possible / never do them. 
  • Consider where a tower is relative to where the weld will be. Towers can get in the way when welding and orienting them away from areas being welded is a must.
  • Weld tabs before stage 4 tubes for access / ease of welding on components inside the frame. 
  • Is our current setup overkill? The amount of jigs we use / welding done in jig compared to Baja is crazy Is there spots we could weld out of jig without an effect on tube positioning that would improve weld quality and ease of welding?
  • Find a good way to clean welds and stick with it
  • Make sure holes projected from jig are the correct size (just projecting the circle is a 0.201 hole but you likely need a 0.25 clearance hole)
