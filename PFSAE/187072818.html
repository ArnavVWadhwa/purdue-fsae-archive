Purdue Formula SAE : 7/21/2021 Brake System Design Judge Presentation Notes

Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory on Jul 22, 2021

Key Take Away:

  • The design judge kept emphasizing the importance of utilizing our vehicles friction/traction circle as Formula cars are traction limited in corners and during extreme braking
    • We want to maximize this circle to get the most from the vehicle
    • During Braking make 4 traction circle diagrams two for front and two for the rear; shows weight transfer during braking and our traction available at each tire during this instant
      • Can make the assumption of a bicycle model for braking as we typically look at axle torques during braking and size front and rear components respectively
  • General Suspension: Need to be looking at ALL four tires to maximize grip and ensure responsive feel to drivers
  • Braking: 
    • Criteria:
    • Must overcome available grip
    • Controllable ratio of pedal force to deceleration rate *lbf/g
    • Manageable typical braking force as on brakes 30% of time (120lbf for pro racing, FSAE between 80-120lbf)
    • Lock front first because locking rear first is unstable and causes spins
    • Look at brake factors and use them for component selection (Basically the brake bias from the whole system; bias from caliper, master cylinder, disc radi) 
      • Look at recording for the exact equations (Simple Math)
    • Use data to determine coeff of friction for pads using pressure data and deceleration data
