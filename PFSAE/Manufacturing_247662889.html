Titanium Exhaust: 

(Difficult and long process but worth it) 


Manufacturing and prep work

Donuts/ Spacers

Round stock machined on a CNC machine. Must use mcmaster. John will be your new best friend. 

Feeds and Speeds: 


Flanges

Water Jett only, the flashpoint of titanium can cause the metal to catch fire and start melting in the laser cutter also laser cutter can only do up to 1/4" thickness. 



Pipes and Bends:

Ticon has a list of everything you will need made out of titanium. Recommended pipes bends are 1.25",1D or 1.2D CLR. Recommend that the secondary is made out of 2" pipe. Most mufflers have 2" Slip fit. Ticon only sells 90 and 45 degrees so any other degrees will have to be cut. The horizontal bandsaw is the best way to cut the titanium pipe. The best way to cut angles out of a 90 degree is to print out 1 to 1 drawings and mark on the pipe where to cut. Below is a photo of a fixture made to hold the pipe at the correct angle for cutting. The fixture is tightened  with C clamps. The blue vertical bandsaw in ME also has to be used to cut the straight pipe because there are more teeth per inch than the horizontal bandsaw. (Broke the horizontal bandsaw blade at one point). Make a fixture to hold down the straight pipe because the pipe likes to rotate and travel on the bandsaw. Trust me taking the time to make a fixture will save u time. To get the straight bars down to the desired length the lathe can be used to turn down the pipe. Use a scroll chuck to avoid deforming the pipe. Turning speed for straight pipe is 350-400. 


Alignments:

We used hose clamps with cut outs to layout all the exhaust pipe on the jig. The cutouts provide holes to tac all the pipes into place. We used the collector to align all the pipe into place. This process was the most effective method to fitting up the exhaust and would highly recommend for the future. 

Collector:

The main motivation for a custom muffler was the scarcity of titanium collectors on the market. The muffler is comprised of a plate and and conic reducers. This muffler design allows for the pipes to be packaged tightly. The pipes are welded on the face on the inside of the collector. 


Welding and Jig: 

Overview:

Requires a lot of argon. Welds need to be purged with argon. 

Filler Rod is grade one titanium 1mm or 1.2mm diameter (should have a lot of extra from 2023)

Jig design should make the welders life easier. The easier it is for the welder to reach every point on the exhaust the better the welds and the ease of manufacturing. Christian made a confluence page over jig design using the exhaust jig we developed.
