Purdue Formula SAE : FW-1-03

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Jul 13, 2023

Simulation ML-022

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Added dive plane gurney exit.

Since the floor gurney has no clean flow, I am injecting clean flow to the lower surface of the vertical gurney.

The idea is that it produces a vortex that, while it does inwash initially, outwashes upon vortex separation. This vortex may also merge with the dive plane vortex ultimately powering it up further.

Slight sweep alterations to smooth out the reflex issue.

Smoothing out the reflex should stress the flow less resulting in cleaner flow exiting the final element.

This should result in better RW performance.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

Reducing the reflex didn't seem to fix the issue. Potentially need to control bursting using the endplate.

(1st image is this iteration, 2nd image is FW-1-00)

This iteration has a cleaner outboard side, while the windward side has more inwash at the top. This is likely due to the sweep for the additional inwash at the top. 

To improve on this design, making the gurney triple element and adding more distance that the vortex is developing may be beneficial for controlling the dirty flow.

(1st image is this iteration, 2nd image is FW-1-00)

RW stall near exit, no clue why this is the case, maybe it's related to the sweep.
