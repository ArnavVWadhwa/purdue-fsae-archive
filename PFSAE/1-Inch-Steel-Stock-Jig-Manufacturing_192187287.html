Purdue Formula SAE : 1 Inch Steel Stock Jig Manufacturing

Before manufacturing Jigs, it is recommended to read the confluence pages below. The first page gives a quick rundown on how to use the Manual Mill. The second will go into detail on the different feed rates and mill RPM's to use for different materials. However, the sections below will cover the methods chassis used in PF22 jig manufacturing.


  • Manual Mill Overview


  • Feeds and Speeds


The sections below were used to take a 12 ft piece of 1" by 1" stock from a supplier to create a jig for welding the chassis. Depending on the jig some of the steps might not need to be done, For Example, the jig tower that is fastened to the table did not need to have an inner lug thickness machined. It is also important to follow the sections from top to bottom. For example, if we were to machine the inner lug thickness before tapping the bottom holes, it would not be possible to place the stock onto the vice.


Cutting Stock Down To Size and Facing


-Material Prep for Milling

  1. Mark stock to the desired size (Leave at least 0.12'' extra for the Mill, I would recommend leaving more)
  2. Use a drop saw to cut the stock, support the stock also if it is long (Do not use a dropping speed faster than 2 and make sure the fluid is running)
  3. Clean stock off, it is important to keep stock clean throughout this process as spare material can get in between the mill vice and the stock can become not straight
  4. Deburr edges with a file


-Mill Setup

  1. For Milling the PF22 steel, we used a 1/2'' 4 flute end mill, make sure the end mill cutting length is long enough for the face of the stock (1'')
  2. Place endmill into the Mill (Manual Mill Overview)
  3. Clean Mill jig/Vice using rags and air compressor to ensure no material is on the vice
  4. Place proper parallels into the vice
  5. Place the cut stock into the vice with the long side horizontal, make sure to leave material hanging off the side to be able to face
  6. Tighten the stock, once tightened use a mallet to fix the parallels


-Facing to size

  1. Run the Mill on forward at a speed of ~450 (what PF22 used)
  2. Touch the side of the mill to the face of the stock and zero the X direction
  3. Bring the Mill towards you off the material
  4. When cutting stock we used 0.015'' conventional cuts and for making a nice finishing one 0.01'' and one 0.005'' climb cut
  5. Cut face with at least two conventional cuts (We used a feed rate of 4)
  6. On the last conventional cut leave the mill on the far side of the stock and finish the surface with the climb cuts
  7. Repeat steps in Mill setup and steps 1 - 5 (facing to size) for the other side of the stock
  8. Before finishing the face use a caliper to measure the stock
  9. Keep cutting conventionally until the stock is 0.015 '' bigger than the desired length, then use the surface finishing methods to create a nice face


**Hint, when you measure the stock with a caliper you can input that measurement in the same location where you zeroed the coordinate. Just press once and put in the length. This will help you not have to repeatedly measure the stock.

Milling Inner Lug Thickness

**Before starting this step it is recommended to read the "Cutting down to size and facing" section above. A lot of the same processes will be used to mill this inner lug thickness.Â 

-Cutting Extra Material Off

  1. Take the piece of stock and mark with a sharpie the inner lug thickness, leaving extra material for facing and creating a good finish
  2. Use the band saw to cut some of the inner lug thickness off, the stock is small so please use a guide and wood
  3. Repeat steps in Mill setup and steps 1 - 5 (facing the stock), (cutting down to size and facing section)
