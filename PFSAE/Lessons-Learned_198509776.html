Purdue Formula SAE : Lessons Learned

Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory, last modified on Nov 27, 2021
  1. The most prominent issue that was encountered while manufacturing was the c-channel 
    1. It is extremely difficult to make this part accurately and without issues
    2. 4 1/4"-1/8" Ball nose bits broke taking less than 10 thou a pass
    3. We decided to try something else and used a 3/4"-1/8" Bull Nose bit
      1. Worked relatively well however when taking off material from only one side of the channel, the bit grabbed the material and bent it in the vice as seen below
    4. FOR THE FUTURE: I would look into and consider other methods of adjusting the pedal box for the reasons above. Another issue that may be encountered is during welding the pedal box body warped under the heat and may struggle to slide in the channels along with lining up with the holes. If you must go with a channel design make sure to design around channel you can buy and make sure that it wont fail under the 450lbf into the brake pedal through FEA.
  2. Warping of the Pedal Box Body during Welding
    2. Above is the Pedal Box Body in the jig for welding. The jig is made of 1/4" Aluminum with roll pins to hold the tabs in place. During welding the aluminum plate along with the bolted pedal box warped small but noticeable amounts. Because of this some holes don't line up and I need to widen the holes in the channel to accommodate.
    3. There weren't many issues with holding tabs in place, but it is important to weld from the inside tabs to the outer ones to allow the most room for the welder. 
    4. When using the jig and welding the tabs install the required hardware for the tab being welded to keep it in nominal positioning
