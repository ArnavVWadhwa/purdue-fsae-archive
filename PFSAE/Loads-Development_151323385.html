Purdue Formula SAE : Loads Development

Created by Unknown User (reyno105) on Jun 27, 2020

For most major loadings on the car, a static loading can be worked out from the inertial loading. This may be through Adams Car for suspension loads or simply acceleration times mass for components that are mounted to the chassis. A dynamic load then needs to be added to the static loading. This is because the car is constantly getting more and less grip as it moves over bumps. These causes random additional acceleration on top of the steady loading. This can be seen in the below data. At any acceleration, there is a range of strains being reported. Ideally, the center of these would match your static prediction, and then you can use the scatter to develop a "dynamic factor."
