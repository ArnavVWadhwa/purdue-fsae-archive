Purdue Formula SAE : Throttle Stop FEA Documentation

Created by Aaltonen, Emil Johan, last modified on Jan 11, 2023

To analyze the pedal stop, we imported the model from NX Spaceclaim. In Spaceclaim we added a contact patch where the washers will be that transmit force from the throttle pedal. The pedal stop is steel, and it is split into 4 parts, the two sides, top, and central support.

We used 5 contacts. One for both sides of the central support, and one for the area where each part touched the top of the pedal stop. All bonded contacts because the parts are welded to each other.


The sides and central support are welded to the pedal box body at the bottom. This is modeled by a fixed support at all relevant faces.


We applied a load at the contact patch we created in spaceclaim. This load is based on a free body diagram shown in the throttle insert documentation page.


For the mesh we set the mesh size to 0.05" for the entire assembly. The part is quite intricate and small, so a fine mesh is required.

It was important to both minimize deflection and stress, because any deflection will lead to the pedal being pressed further than we calculated the max distance to be in the "Throttle Cable Travel" section.
