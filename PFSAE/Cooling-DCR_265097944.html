Purdue Formula SAE : Cooling DCR

Created by Feehan, Hugh Timothy, last modified on Jul 25, 2023


Project Scope

  • Overall Goal: Provide enough cooling capacity so the coolant temperature stays below the safe maximum. This should be done without needing to reduce the power the engine is producing. 

System Interfaces:

    • Powertrain
      • The cooling system connects to and cools the engine
      • An oil cooler may also be plumbed into the system
      • There may be packaging constraints from exhaust and fuel
    • Chassis
      • Radiators and other cooling system components will be mounted to the chassis through tabs and other supports
    • Aero
      • The aero team will be providing us an air mass-flow number from CFD to be used in the cooling sim so we can size the radiators
      • They will also give input on the preferred size and exact location of the radiators
      • The aero team will also design the ducts/sidepods
    • SIQ
      • SIQ will be involved with selecting and implementing coolant temperature sensors
      • Also involved with the selection and implementation of the electric water pump


Design Requirements

  • Rules
  • Vehicle Performance
    • Want to keep the coolant temp under a safe value (determined based on a safety margin to the water boiling point at system pressure)
    • Do not want to rely on fans 
      • Keep electrical consumption down
      • Still keep car cool if fans fail for some reason
    • Want to keep system as light as possible
    • Work with aero team to minimize drag
    • Consider packaging for assembly and maintenance
  • Unknown Factors
    • CFD Air Mass Flow
    • Aero preferred cooling configuration
    • E85 + engine mods affect on heat produced

Design Overview

General Concept:

The overall plan is to have two radiators that cool the coolant from the engine. An electric water pump will be used like last year to circulate the coolant. We will have a swirl pot to remove air from the system, a catch can to collect water that overflows from the system as the coolant heats up, and a radiator pressure cap to allow over-pressurized vapor to leave the system in a controlled way. 

Components:

    • Two radiators (likely, pending aero analysis)
      • One mounted on each side of the car, likely in parallel
      • Custom size from PWR/C&R
    • Swirl Pot
      • Custom, likely same from last year
    • Catch Can
      • Custom
      • Carbon wrapped beer cans have worked well
        • Lightweight, strong, passes rules
    • Electric Water Pump
      • Needed because we remove stock water pump for the dry-sump system
      • Chosen based on water mass flow, electric consumption, pump size and weight
      • Davies-Craig model from last year probably will work again
    • Coolant Temperature Sensor(s)
      • Mounted at inlet and outlet of engine
    • Pressure Cap
      • 2 bar/30 psi used last year, will attempt to use 40 psi this year
      • Higher pressure raises boiling point of water, allowing us to run at higher temperature, requiring less cooling
    • Aluminum tubing (for straight cooling line sections)
    • Silicone hoses (for bends in cooling line sections)


Analysis

        Cooling Sim:

    • Simulates car going around a track, and calculates resulting temperatures
    • Seems to correlate fairly well with real life running, however a safety factor of at least 10°F should be kept

        Aerodynamics:

    • Whatever analysis they do
    • Based on preliminary concepts from them (1 vs. 2 rads, air mass flow at a speed), give them a few sizing options to pick what works best

       Testing:

  • Physical testing at Grandprix or other tracks
  • take same approach as sim for readings and safety factor
