CDR Overview

Subjects of the CDR Presentation should include, but are not limited to:

  1. Project Scope
  2. Design Requirements
  3. Design Overview and CAD
  4. Analysis
  5. Manufacturing Overview
  6. Cost 
  7. Schedule


The information provided below is for the most part a written version of the video. I've included some extra examples as well if needed. 


Project Scope

Make sure to answer these questions:

  • What exactly are presenting on? Down to the exact bolt, exact lug, etc. 
  • What problem are you trying to solve with your subsystem? 

Design Requirements 

Present your final and specific requirements

What makes a good requirement? Follow this page → Making Great Design Requirements

Design Overview and CAD

Overview

The goal of the overview is to provide a summary of your design and a collection of all of the decisions that you have made. Be rather specific here – the overview should be detailed enough so that if another student from another team sat down with these overview pages, they should be able to almost replicate your design (barring EXACT dimensions of parts). Make sure to include LOTS of CAD pictures, of many different angles. Summary should also include your material / component selection. Your analysis section will essentially be your justification for why you chose your components / material / geometry, etc. 

Feel free to include as many slides as you need. There is no need to cram every bit of information below onto one slide.

Example

I whipped up this quick actuation example. It includes picture of the components, and the parts that go along with each component. It doesn't hurt to be more specific than this as well – include the exact part number that you are using or more specific dimensions if you feel it is critical to the design.

Analysis

Overview

The analysis section is the meat and potatoes of the presentation. Here, you present quantified reasoning for the component / geometry / attributes that you have chosen. 

In general:

  • Include pictures of the exact excel calculator that you used 
  • Include your margin to your specified design criteria in a margin summary table

Margin Summary Tables

At the end of the day, we care about the margins from your analysis. This tells everyone if you have met your design criteria, and how closely you met your design criteria. The margin summary table, should at least have 1 slide for each component, for each load case. It is important to document everything and show that you have considered load cases for ALL load cases. 

Complex Analysis 

Make sure to define all of your inputs to your complex analysis, and why you chose those specific inputs, even if you feel they are obvious. Make sure you meticulously present your outputs as well, and why these outputs make sense. 

Manufacturing Overview 

Make sure to:

  • Include what tooling is needed to make your part
  • List all ops that your part requires (an op like a manufacturing "action")
    • Traffic Cone / Bushing Example
      • Center drill hole
      • Drill thru hole
      • Turn OD of part 
      • Part off
  • Include approximate stock dimentions and what material is needed including temper and material specification

Cost

Cost should be accurate to within +- 10%. Make sure to include items that you know are sponsored, as your BOM should just be for money but can also be treated as a material acquisition list. 

Schedule

Make a to do list from here to IDR, through MRR. MRR is when we have manufacturing schedule introduced.
