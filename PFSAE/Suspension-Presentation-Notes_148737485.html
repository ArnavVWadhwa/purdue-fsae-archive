  • Suspension – Ben
    • DJ: LapSim – Judge looking for an R^2 correlation
    • DJ: You have a tool and need to know if it is chainsaw or scalpel.  A chainsaw is great for systems engineering to make high-level decisions.  You have a trade-off between insight and accuracy.  You went for insight and that’s good at this stage.  You can add on accuracy later.
    • DJ: Not having a transient simulation can effect results of weight distribution impacts
    • DJ: What happens to aero effects when car spins and its going backwards?
    • TN Take-aways:  DJ wants highlights.  It doesn’t take a lot of explaining.  They know whether you know.  Start high-level, but present in a way the DJ knows there is detail and calculations behind the information by stating conclusions/take-aways in terms of impact on vehicle function driving at relevant competition events to demonstrate understanding.  Speak the lingo and focus on important factors/decisions to indicate you know what is important and why it’s important.
    • DJ: Roll camber gradient (0.32 rear, 0.5 front) is very aggressive.  Suggestion: Run the rear at 0.5 and see what the driver thinks.
    • DJ Feedback
      • I like you think about driver confidence and don’t go for the peak, but look for a plateau
      • I like that you included transient considerations in the back end
      • LapSim continuous improvement – look for areas that are least correlated and look for how to improve accuracy.  Find bad assumptions and add degree of freedom if the complication is worth it.
      • Use simple tools first, and then increasingly complex tools.  Simple tools let you find solution domain, then use complex tools to shrink domain further.  Finally use development time to finalize design.
      • You’ve done a great job
