Purdue Formula SAE : Pin Burnout | Troubleshooting

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Aug 26, 2023

It is important to consistently check high current lines for signs of damage. Here you can see that the end of the wire looked burned and the rubber seal was starting to lift up. This indicated that something had gone wrong.


To resolve issues like this you must remove the connector to get a better idea of what is going on.


In this instance, the end of the wire had burned off and taken the pin with it.


Here is a list of steps to follow when dealing with potential pin burnouts:

  1. Disconnect the affected connector.
  2. Remove the connector and associated components.
  3. Determine whether or not the connector being used is suited for the task.
    1. If so:
      1. Cut down the affected conductor past all signs of damage (if supplies allow, all conductors being the same length is ideal).
      2. Strip the affected conductor and ensure that there are no signs of damage.
      3. Re-crimp the conductor(s) ensuring that a quality crimp is formed
      4. Re-pin the conductors, ideally using a new connector
    2. If not:
      1. Follow the same steps as above but using the properly sized connector
  4. Check the opposite connector for similar damage.
  5. Re-connect the connectors and ensure that the connection is as strain-relieved as possible
  6. Figure out why the problem happened. It was likely caused by improper connector sizing or poor crimping. It is important that we, as a team, do not make the same mistake twice.
