Purdue Formula SAE : FW-13-03

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 29, 2023

Simulation: CZ-008

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Increased chord and added a strake

Increase the chord to place the vortex further outboard, which will increase the outwash and downwash seem by the wake.

Added a strake to produce a counter rotating vortex directly inboard of the main vortex produced by the wing itself. The counter rotating vortex pair should downwash themselves. The existence of the body panel will induce an upwash component, but that should be vastly overpowered. 

The strake itself is going to thin out the boundary layer at the suppressor to body panel junction but thicken it further up, so sidepods will probably take a slight hit. 

The additional pressurization on the inboard section of the wing will move stagnation point up, which is desired. The depressurization of the outboard section will lead to more loss but more importantly a stronger vortex. Actually the junction loss between the strake and the wing is something to be concerned about due to the increased adverse pressure gradient as well as the vortex produced by itself inducing local upwash at the junction.

Description

Undertray downforce gains mostly comes from the additional downwash induced by a longer span powering up a vortex in tire wake. Since this is tire wake we are talking about, I don't trust CFD on this.

Predicted junction losses are here.

The counter rotating vortex formed, but it's very small. Outer vortex did not get noticeably downwashed. The changes in tire wake at the undertray inlet is there, but small. So I think this idea works but much less effective then I thought it would be.
