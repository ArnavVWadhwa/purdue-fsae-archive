Purdue Formula SAE : Bearing Life Calculator

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified by Unknown User (dewey2) on Nov 29, 2020

NTN has good guides that you can google, but this is a small calculator that is adequate for analyzing suspension + drivetrain deep groove ball bearings. It assumes that the bearing is unloaded half the time and at the loads you enter the other half the time. This is conservative in a suspension application where time spent cornering leaves one side of vehicle unloaded so long as loads given are design limit loads (assume 70 mph cornering during all cornering).

An alternative method is to use the supplier calculators such as the one provided by SKF:Â www.skfbearingselect.com

In the tool, bearings, loads, contamination, and fitment can be inputted and it will provide outputs on standard bearing life as well as the SKF bearing life which accounts for more factors than what a standard calculation out of Shigleys could give. The SKF rating life tends to provide a more realistic approximation due to the additional factors being considered and their internal testing.

To expand on the bearing life calculation methods, a weighted average of multiple load cases can be used to obtain a more accurate rating life. For drivetrain, the vehicle will not be operating at peak load for the entire time, so the rating life at the peak load is an underestimate of the actual bearing life. The weighted average works by utilizing a cycle fraction which is defined by the number of rotations for a specific load case divided by the total number of bearing rotations. If you assume that the differential is rotating at the same RPM for all of the scenarios, then it would equate to the amount of time spent in each loading scenario.

For example, it may end up with 25% cycle fraction at 100% loading, 50% cycle fraction at 75% loading, and 25% cycle fraction at 50% loading.
