Purdue Formula SAE : Brake Plumbing Notes

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified on Mar 01, 2020
  • Speedflex (not Autoflex) lines used for brakes, powertrain may be different
  • AN-3 for pressurized portion of circuit
  • AN-4 from reservoir to MC
  • Buy spare -3 olives (probably close to half the total number of fittings)

  • Making brake line segments
    • Thread all necessary fittings, heat shrink, etc onto line
    • Tightly wrap line with electrical tape where cutting
    • Cut using angle grinder with cut-off wheel (as square as possible)
    • Use small screwdriver to flare out steel braided sheath
    • Push olive between layers by pushing line into a flat surface
    • Thread fitting together, using 0.031" feeler gauge to indicate where to stop, should feel significant torque in wrench
    • Do not overtighten
    • Plug and pressure test in water bucket
  • Other notes
    • Use teflon tape on NPT fittings that go in GP200
    • For AP Racing CP 4226, fitting may thread in too far and interfere with piston, use crush washer stack to limit thread engagement
