Jigging Upright Tabs

Basically doing what we did for PF20 again (see picture)

  • Jigs are made to hold the right side of each upright on the lower joint
  • Washers are used as spacers between the jig and upright to shift the rear sprocket left/right to align with the engine sprocket
    • In theory, you should only need 0.25 inches of spacing to be in alignment because that's how far rightward-shifted the jigs are, but will ultimately vary due to mfg tolerances
  • And then one tab and the nut are fastened on the other side of the upright
  • The turnbuckle tabs are hand placed
    • Angle of the tabs should be matched with CAD
      • I think the center of the tab is aligned with the axis of the turnbuckle but I'd check in NX, it may be 10 degrees off axis
    • And you obviously want the rod ends & turnbuckles straight with respect to the uprights (i.e. no spherical bearing misalignment angle)


  • An entire drivetrain assembly is required to accomplish this
    • Uprights
    • Diff
    • Sprocket
    • Sprocket spacer
    • Sprocket snap ring
    • Stability bar
    • Turnbuckles + rod ends set to the appropriate starting length (4 inches center-center on rod ends)
    • CV Housings not required
  • And the engine with a sprocket must be in the chassis


  • To verify sprocket alignment it might be useful to have a straight bar you can set against the 2 sprockets
    • Or maybe throw a chain on it and you could tell how well they're matching up
