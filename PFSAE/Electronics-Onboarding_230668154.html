Purdue Formula SAE : Electronics Onboarding

Created by Skrzyszowski, Mateusz, last modified by Paradise, Hunter on Jul 01, 2023

Welcome to the Electronics Subteam!


Subteam Responsibilities

  • Wiring harness
  • Electrical components
  • Software relating to vehicle electronics and sensor operation (Sensor calibrations, M1 Build firmware, all I/O selections within M1 tune, etc.)
  • Dash layout and design
  • Data acquisition


Project Goal

     The goal of this project is to integrate the main concepts of FSAE electrical system design into one cohesive series of tasks. You can expect to learn about wiring harness design and construction, MoTeC firmware design, circuit design, and electronics theory. Even though you may not be working explicitly on software or electrical design to start, it is still vital that you know the fundamentals of each so you can improve your designs by knowing how different systems interact.


Project Outline

     In this project, you will first control an LED (the brakelight) using the MoTeC M130 ECU with multiple lighting patterns selectable in M1 Tune. Then, you will have to choose a brakelight that is rules compliant to use in your design. Next, you will have to design the wiring system that connects to the LED using RapidHarness (Harness CAD Software) and create an appropriate BOM and cutlist. You will then design a simple shutdown circuit in LTspice and implement it in your harness. Lastly, you will plan the manufacture of your harness design with a BOM and cutlist. Once everybody has finished their onboarding we will choose the best design to construct and test as a goup.


Project Resources

     Before beginning this project it is reccomended that you read through the majority of SIQ confluence pages. This will not only give you knowledge that will aid in your completion of this project but also familiarize you with the layout of the subteam confluence which will be very helpful in the future.


Make sure you check in with your lead as you finish each step.
