Purdue Formula SAE : Step 4: Wiring Harness Manufacturing

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Aug 28, 2023

In this step, you will make all of the necessary preparations to manufacture the harness you designed in the previous steps.


Reading

  • Wiring harness construction: https://www.rbracing-rsr.com/wiring_ecu.html
    • This is incredibly important when thinking about how you would properly manufacture your harness.


Materials Research

  • Your objective here is to find the materials necessary for the manufacture of this harness. Components that you will need to find include connectors for connection to the "main vehicle harness" (The idea is to be able to quickly connect and disconnect the LED without needing to take apart the entire vehicle wiring harness, similar to sensor connections), pins that fit with those connectors, heat shrink that would go around the wires to protect them from the environment (think about shrunk vs unshrunk diameter of the heat shrink), and anything else you may have added. The primary source for everything vehicle electronics/MoTeC related is milspecwiring.com, but there are more retailers listed on Manufacturing Resources | ELE.
  • After choosing the materials you would like to use to manufacture your harness (hopefully based on the rbracing document), be sure to update your connections in RapidHarness.

         


BOM and Cutlist

  1. Utilize RapidHarness' built-in materials list to create a BOM for your harness design in addition to components that may not necessarily show up in the program. (Don't forget the ECU and PDM)
  2. Once you have a BOM created use the cut list tool to create a list of the wire cuts that will be necessary to manufacture your harness. (This may not seem important now but having a list of what you need to cut and where is both helpful for manufacturing and necessary for cost report which is worth many competition points)
