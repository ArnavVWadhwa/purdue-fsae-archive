Week 1

  • Final Drive Ratio Notes (Updated by Aarnav)
    • The ratio of Sprocket in Drivetrain to Sprocket connected to the transmission.
      • Calculated by (Teeth of Higher Gear/Teeth of Lower Gear) = FDR. Ex: 36:12 or 3.00 FDR.
    • Final Drive Ratio is directly proportional to wheel torque and inversely proportional to theoretical top speed.
      • Top speed is not determined solely by FDR, FDR determines a theoretical maximum from the capabilities of the Transmission and sustainable Engine RPMs
      • Wheel Torque Gains Calculation: (Proposed FDR/Current FDR) = Percentage of Wheel Torque Gain or Loss
    • After Talking with Adam and Ben the best choice for our sprockets are as follows:
      • Front: 12
      • Rear: 34
      • Final Drive Ratio: 2.83
      • Torque Figures from Akron E85 Curve with 10% Knockdown for Reference:
          • Going to be expanding spreadsheet for reference numbers
  • Initial Sprocket Design (Adarsh)
    • Front sprocket (12 teeth) created within subassembly 
    • 3/8 end mill is limiting factor, all fillets must be at least 0.39" for ease of tooling. Was originally trying slimmer cutouts, but drill bit size prevented sharp corners
    • Have yet to conduct FEA analysis, but aiming for most weight reduction
    • Working on chain model to auto-update as sprocket/diff location moves, 3 sections completed
  • Drivetrain Skeleton wireframe partially completed (CAD Work will start now) (Aarnav)
  • Drivetrain Mounting Research (Aarnav)
    • Looking at different places to mount the uprights by looking at prior art
      • No better alternatives
    • Will be going with uprights
    • Placement Stuff
      • Need to find out where exactly to put our driveline to maximize chain wrap and eliminate clipping with chassis
        • Want to make sure Halfshafts don't bend too much and minimize stress on them
  • Need to find all calculations for materials stress and other stuff in relation to CVs and Halfshafts, also want to make a spreadsheet to have all constants and requirements for Drivetrain.
    • I am learning a lot about contact stress, but I need to read about moments.
  • Quickjacks! - Need to wait on the finalized rear chassis but we need to research other examples before CAD

Week 6

Sprocket Design

Final Design: 

Features: 6 cutout pattern, acceptable SF and deformation at all load cases, 0.5 lbs (less than PF21), relatively easy to machine cutouts. Al 7055 (T77511), lightweight and durable. Properties from MMPDS-12.

5 cutout pattern has marginally less weight, but drastically lower SF → tradeoff not worth it

Finer mesh will be created using spheres of influence for more detail at important joints. Current mesh at 0.05 in.

Week 8

Final FINAL Sprocket Design:

Features: 6 cutout pattern, acceptable SF and deformation at all load cases, 0.497 lbs (less than PF21), relatively easy to machine cutouts. Al 7055 (T77511), lightweight and inexpensive(ish). Properties from MMPDS-12.

5 cutout pattern has marginally less weight, but drastically lower SF → tradeoff not worth it


Chain Guard:

0.11" thick steel, 2.25" wide

The chain guard will mount to the top of the upper chassis tube and will rest on the undertray (not enough clearance to mount to lower chassis tube).

A separate front sprocket chain guard also needs to be designed, with a < 3mm gap between the two guards.


Week 9

Hall effect sensor:

Based off gap requirements, it is probably best to mount the sensor off the vertical rear chassis tubes. The sensor will detect the drivetrain bolts as they rotate (circled in red).

Chain guard: cutouts for bolts have been added, size can be adjusted based on hardware selection

Will be going into the shop tomorrow to find ideal mounting point for front sprocket chain guard.
