Purdue Formula SAE : Reserving Space in the BIDC High Bay

Created by Unknown User (green338), last modified on Nov 01, 2021

Starting in Fall 2021, all after hours use of the BIDC high bay must be reserved through the BIDC project management system. It is absolutely critical that we follow these regulations to keep our space in the high bay, which is a huge asset to our team.

To reserve any resources in the BIDC (machines, shop space) you need to be added to the current Purdue FSAE project that we have running, which should be owned by the aero lead or chief engineer. The current project is named "Formula SAE Aero 2022." You can check if you have access to this project by logging into this website: https://www.purdue.edu/bidc/os1/accounts/login/?next=/bidc/os1/project/. You should be able to see our project under "Projects." You cannot be added to the project until you have completed the BIDC member agreement quiz (found here: https://www.openpassport.org/Account/Login?ReturnUrl=%2f).

To reserve high bay space, click on the "Resources" tab at the top of the page, and navigate to the "BIDC137 High-Bay" Resource. This will show all reservations for the 8 slots in the high bay. When you find an open time that you want to reserve, go back to the "Resources" tab and select 1 of the 8 slots available. This will allow you to make a reservation.

The time you enter should match our planned work hours to make sure that all of our reservations match up. Each member will be responsible for making their own reservations. The system will not let you reserve a slot without the required safety quizzes being completed as well. These can be found on this website: https://www.openpassport.org/Account/Login?ReturnUrl=%2f.Â 

Once the reservation has been entered, a TA will have to approve it. You'll get an automated email once it goes through.
