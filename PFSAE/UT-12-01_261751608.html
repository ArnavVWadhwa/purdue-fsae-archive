Purdue Formula SAE : UT-12-01

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 26, 2023

Simulation: CZ_003

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Sidepod leading edge and inner side tunnel leading edge realignment

Improved flow alignment to reduce losses.

On the vertical edge, it's for the sole purpose of drag reduction since the cleaned up flow won't be doing anything downstream (for now).

On the horizontal edge, it will increase downforce by improving flow attachment on the top surface, increasing downwash on the sidepods going into the rear wing.

Outer side tunnel raise and diffuser angle reduction.

Increased minimum ride height of outer side tunnel to 2.2 in from 1.8 in. Raised leading edge to 2.35 from 2 in. Outlet ride height isn't changed.

The vertical gap between the outer endplate and the tunnel itself will allow a vortex to form, reducing inwash, improve flow in inner side tunnel.

This will have the downside of increasing vortex strength in the vortex channel due to increased inwashing pressure gradient. 

The reduction in diffuser angle reduces bursting of vortex in inner side tunnel itself. However, this might be overshadowed by the increased vortex strength in the inner side tunnel itself.

Outer side tunnel edge blend

Added edge blend to outer side tunnel to reduce loses induced by the formation of the vortex inside it.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

Sidepod flow alignment is still shit

Vortex strength in the inner side tunnel is reduced and flow attaches better. Working as intended.

Vortex in the outer side tunnel is getting inwashed and creating separation. Either CFD is making things up in this sim or CFD was making things up in last sim. We will see.

So this is not working as intended. I thought vortex in the outer side tunnel is going to get powered up, but it did not work out that way. The vortex got discretized. The first vortex did take advantage of the increased ride height of outer side tunnel, but it got inwashed quickly. Due to vortex discretization, the second vortex got weaker.

The overall decrease in suction reduced vortex strength in the vortex channel, which is a good thing in terms of getting the vortex to survive all the way along the side tunnel.
