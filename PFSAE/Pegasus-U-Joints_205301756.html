Purdue Formula SAE : Pegasus U-Joints

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified on Jan 31, 2022

When aligning the steering system, the u-joints MUST BE IN LINE. in previous years we've used the apex covered u-joints from pegasus (https://www.pegasusautoracing.com/productselection.asp?Product=1490).Â 



Below is the apex covered u joint from pegasus without the cover. The arrows point to holes 1 and 2, which according to the mil spec above are within 1 degree in parallel to the center of each yoke and is visually proven below. The other holes in the image were made by us and shouldn't be considered when looking at the image.

The two holes should be used to align the two u-joints in the steering column. Below is an image of what the correct alignment looks like. Basically, we want the two inside holes to be in line with each other or 180 degrees from each other (PF20 was 180 degrees & PF22 was in line or 0 degrees). Even if other u-joints are used, alignment is still just as important.


Another note: check to see how to protect the u-joints from heat when welding. We have some goop (https://www.pegasusautoracing.com/productselection.asp?Product=3389) that we used on PF22 so see if you can find it or order new. Below is a photo of the goop applied for welding on a test tube (we probably still have some 'ruined' u-joints you can practice with). We ended up reapplying the goop almost every pass (one pass being 1/3 of the way around the joint).
