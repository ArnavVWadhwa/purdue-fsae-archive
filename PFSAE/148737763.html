NASA technical standards

All NASA wide standards that are public.

5000 series documents are great structures + loads references. 6000 is great corrosion reference. 8000 series documents are more meta, but cover things that are important for all of engineering (safety culture, uncertainty quantification, etc)

Highlights:

  • NASA-STD-5001: source for safety factors (borrowed by team atm)
  • NASA-STD-5020: bolted joints (basis of team calculator)
  • NASA-STD-5017: mechanisms. Good reference to safety factors on forces from different sources. IE large safety factor for friction due to uncertainty, lower for backdriving motor. Also interesting discussion of lubrication types and tolerancing issues.
https://standards.nasa.gov/nasa-technical-standards
NASA center standards

All NASA center research standards. JSC and MSFC have lots of good stuff.

Highlights:

  • JSC-65829: source for safety factors. partial overlap with nasa-std-5001, but more detail / differentiation between types of hardware
https://standards.nasa.gov/center-specific-standards
MMPDS-17

Introductory material + appendix material has a lot of info on structures and fatigue + how statistics play in and is very succint. Highlights include:

  • Definitions of all the allowable types
  • Failure types
  • Discussion of various stress strain curves shapes in metals + how they are estimated from allowables
  • Various mechanics of materials topics
  • Explanation of most graphs available:
    • Stress-strain
    • SN (fatigue)
    • dA/dN vs K (fracture)
    • Elevated temp
    • Elevated temp exposure
  • A bunch of info rivets and bolts in the back (properties for a bunch of aerospace ones)
