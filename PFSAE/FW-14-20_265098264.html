Justification

Added tire depressurizer

This is going against all the common sense I have accumulated in my 3 years of doing aero, but I am willing to give it a go. Basically I am just desperate to depressurize the downwind side front tire, which severely limits downwind side front wing performance. The issue of course is you are moving the pressurization forward, while that might seem to be counter productive, my thinking is that this limits pressurization to the very outboard, whereas tire pressurization not only affects outboard, but far inboard as well in high yaw conditions. I am basically sacrificing outboard performance for more inboard performance. It will also power up the vortex that forms across this strake, which should lead to even more wake being thrown at the front tire.

The reason I am going with a gurney rather than just straight out super duper outwash is for two reason:

  1. gurney generates cleaner wake. Although not capture by steady state CFD, the wake generated by gurney is stable as opposed to unsteady.
  2. there is a lot of local inwash in this region, curved outwash simply isn't as effective as gurney in this case. That's because gurney generates its own favorable pressure gradient, rather than rely on flow attachment like curves do
