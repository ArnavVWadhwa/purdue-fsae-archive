Purdue Formula SAE : RW-2-02

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Jul 01, 2023

Simulation ML-013

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Reduced AoA of the main element, altered 2nd and 3rd elements to primarily produce pressure recovery.

Reducing the AoA of the main element increases the amount of pressure being utilized towards DF instead of drag. 

Chord increase for the main element since this component is the main DF generator.

Fully leaning into the pressure recovery route to power up the main element as much as possible. 2nd element diffuses substantially, which leads to the 3rd element (Lieback airfoil) that defuses even more. 

Note: Expecting some flow sep in the center section due to the headrest. Also, due to the reduction of chord for the 2nd and 3rd elements there may be increased risks for flow separation along with the 3rd element being very touchy to flow sep due to the large amounts of diffusing. 

Eliminated vertical endplate gurney and increased camber of dive plane.

As discussed in the previous iteration, the dive plane can have an increased camber to produce higher DF. Additionally, the vertical endplate gurney is producing a lot of drag, so I am removing that portion for drag improvements.
