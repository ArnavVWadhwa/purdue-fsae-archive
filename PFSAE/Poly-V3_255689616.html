Purdue Formula SAE : Poly V3

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Mar 27, 2023

Major Changes:

  • Added 5 geometry operations to fix ground boundary layer issue
    • Ground refinement intersect: takes intersect between freestream and car refinement block
    • Ground refinement subtract: subtracts the freestream out of car refinement block
    • Ground refinement imprint: creates a the surface where intersect and subtract intersect
      • Non-conformal imprinting with intersect pre processing
    • Ground refinement offset: offsetting ground refinement down 50 mm so that the surface of ground is covered regardless of ride height
    • RFN_Ground: surface extrude that extrudes out the imprinted surface out to the thickness of the prism layer
    • Now, we can have a nice and refined prism layer under the car, and a prism layer with more reasonable volume change in freestream
  • Surface control ground number of prism layer to 1 and total thickness to 50 mm
  • Added volumetric control ground with 5 prism layer and total thickness of 12 mm (surface control ground setting Poly V2)
  • Surface control wake now sets prism layer total thickness to 20 mm
  • Added tire wake refinement: refinement at 10% base size
  • Reduced car refinement size
    • Reduced lateral size so that tire wake refinement can be handled by tire wake refinement
    • Cutout for front wing low gradient areas
    • Cutout near the cockpit for low gradient areas
  • Increased the size of wake refinement to capture more of the wake
  • Increased wake refinement size because it's probably unnecessarily refined
  • Increased domain height and length behind the car
  • Removed surface size constraints for freestream surfaces other than the ground to save mesh count by a little bit
  • Removed custom prism layer for bodywork to increase wall y+ out of the buffer layer
  • Increased number of prism layers and decreased prism layer thickness of the tires to get them closer
  • Decreased prism layer thickness and decreased number of prism layers on front wing because prism layers were oversized
  • Decreased prism layer thickness and decreased number of prism layers on rear wing because prism layers were oversized (not as much as front wing)
