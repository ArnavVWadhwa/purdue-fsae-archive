Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-14

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Sep 01, 2023

Simulation ML-060

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

3rd and 4th strakes moved downstream by .5 and 1.1 inches respectively.

Also rounded LE of each strake.

One of the key successes in the previous FW design was the wake management, with this strake orientation it should mimic a similar idea.

The vortex on the inner strake must not only survive until, at minimum, the end of the FW, but also upwash along with the surface of the FW. This allows the 2nd innermost vortex to develop below the innermost vortex, resulting in massive flow redirection upward and outboard, where the two vortices work together.

If this can be solved, we will see the return of the 1.4 Cl UT in Y15, which will result in 4.1 Cl overall. With the current UT iteration UT-1-03, the inlet channel should also have much better flow characteristics. This may solve the side tunnel issue too.
