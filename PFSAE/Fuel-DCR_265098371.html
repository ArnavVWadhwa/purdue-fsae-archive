Purdue Formula SAE : Fuel DCR

Created by Estes, Mitchell Taylor, last modified on Jul 19, 2023

Project Scope:

  • Provide method of storing and pumping fuel to power the engine, while minimizing volume and weight.  Some adjustments may need to be made to accommodate the transition from gasoline to E85.

Stakeholders:

    • Chassis
      • 30-40% increase in size of the fuel tank requires additional space from Chassis
    • Electrical
      • Increased space may require other components of electrical to be moved (i.e. the battery)
    • Aero
      • May need to work around any adjustments to battery location or other components to allow for tank

Design Requirements:

  • Rules
  • Vehicle Performance
    • Design a tank that can hold the required amount of fuel to complete an endurance run
      • Tank must also be as small as possible to fit in confined space behind driver's seat
      • Tank must be as light as possible to compensate for added fuel
    • Design fuel rail to accommodate ethanol's corrosive property 
      • Aluminum must be anodized to withstand ethanol and maintain pressure
      • After experiment, no effect seen on aluminum foil when submerged in E85 for 3+ days
  • Unknown requirements 
    • Exact amount of fuel required
    • Space availability

Design Overview:

Rough Concept:

    • The fuel subsystem will consist primarily of the parts listed below.  Namely, the Fuel tank, with a hydramat of proper proportions to help with fuel pumping, the pump, and rail.  The fuel pump will be attached to the tank (interior/exterior), and will act to pump the fuel from the tank to the fuel rail.  This rail will hold the fuel injectors.  Components may need to be adjusted to be compatible with E85.

Components:

    • Fuel Tank
      • Approximately 30% increase in volume
      • Ethanol resistant liner to protect against corrosion
    • Hydramat
    • Pump
      • internal vs external?
    • Pressure regulator
    • Fuel filters
    • Lines/Hose
      • Must be ethanol compatible
    • Fuel Rail
      • Collecting point for fuel before being injected
      • Must be made from anodized aluminum to resist corrosion
    • Injectors
