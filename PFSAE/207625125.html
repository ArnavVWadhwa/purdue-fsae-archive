Purdue Formula SAE : 2/13/2022 - Friction Measurements (PF22)

Created by Unknown User (xia107) on Feb 15, 2022

Performing this study as its relatively low effort, also a DJ asked about this extensively in 2021 virtual design finals. Would be nice to be able to say we at least considered this factor and have experimentation to prove that we can neglect frictional parasitics in the system.


Principle:

  • With the springs removed from the suspension, suspension friction can be determined by measuring the load to actuate upwards and downwards and calculating the hysterysis loop
  • This should be measured with and without shocks

Process used:

  • Remove springs, Put chassis on blocks. Ensure chassis is mostly level in roll and pitch
  • Place a scale under corner assembly, find a way to slowly (quasi-statically) move the suspension up and down, I used a emergency scissor jack from a car.
  • Zero scale with jack
  • Lift corner assembly very slowly, allow scale to settle and record value
  • Lower corner assembly very slowly, allow scale to settle and record value
  • Do this with and without shock connected for comprehensive data.


Results (summarized);

  • Dampers have quite a bit of friction (duh)
  • Only looking at actuation, friction accounts for anywhere from 0.3 to 2.5 lb (at the wheel end). This variation is not unexpected.
  • Corner weight (unsprung weight) can also be determined with this method, Just account for what you dont have on the car (brakes, wheels, tires, etc)
