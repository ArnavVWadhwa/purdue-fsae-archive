Purdue Formula SAE : Carson's Custom Dyno Design

Created by Williams, Carson Kenneth on Dec 08, 2023

This page outlines how I went about designing the custom water brake for our team. I'm not going to cover the basics of CAD or how press/shrink fits work–This is just general overview of design of my water brake and how I put it together.

If you don't know how water brakes work → https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_brake

We used to run a "vane" style water brake, we ran very low amounts of water at very hot temperatures through it leading to cavitation and concluded it was not safe to run anymore with concerns it might grenade itself and hurt someone,

This was a Land and Sea 9" dyno from probably early 2001 or 2002. The owner of this company passed away and the company has since dissolved which means you can't but them anymore or have them repaired.

So where does this leave us? Overbuild the crap out of it, since diameter has a fifth order function to it, make it larger diametrically, deeper to hold more volume, and more Toroidal to make it even more inefficient.

The CAD is relatively straightforward, but one thing I will point out is that the outer housings are 11 pockets and the rotor has 10. This is because reading through various forums on homemade dynos you need to have a prime number for either the stator or rotor and the opposite needs to have one more or one less than that. I'm assuming this has something to do with crazy vibration physics. Either way this dyno was made as large as possible with the random stock we had laying around.
