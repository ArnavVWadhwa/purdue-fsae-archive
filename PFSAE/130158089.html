2019 Grissom FW Failure Force Calc

Explanation: In post season testing, we saw our right side front wing mounting fail due to a double cone hit on the front right endplate. This section will contain the calculations for the force applied to the rib that caused it to fail so it can be used as an additional design constraint for front wing mounting in the future.

From onboard video (kept atÂ Z:\2020\1_General\4_Testing\Video\9-14-19 Grissom Tire Test\Autocross.mov)

Cone hit occurs at approx 5:39

Cone is accelerated to vehicle speed in ~4 frames

(4 frames)(1/60 seconds/frame) = 0.067s

Drive speed at time of impact = 25.4mph = 11.35m/s

Mass of double cone (approx) = 2*7lb = 14lb = 6.34kg


Assuming worst case scenario of cone accelerated to vehicle speed

dP = m*dv = 72.0kg*m/s

dt = 0.067s

Solve for cone force by finding dp/dt

F = dp/dt = 1080N = 242lbf

For single cone, F = ~120lbflb

Drag force (40m/s): 274N = 61.6lbf


Sum all forces (assuming drag is distributed 50/50 between mounts and all cone force goes through one mount)

FW max force rearwards per mount: 150lbf
