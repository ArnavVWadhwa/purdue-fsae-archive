Take less off at a time and check threads more often.

When thread cutting: taking off 0.010" per pass is a LOT, doing that will likely result in sloppy fits. When you know you are getting close, take 0.001" pass or just do a spring pass between test fits. Also thread files can do a lot of it.

Hub to bearing stuckNot so much a mistake, more a learning: Hub will tend to get stuck in the bearing after heavy testing, despite starting as a slip fit. Use PB blaster on the inboard and outboard interface, wait a while, and tap out with a hammer. Do not be afraid to hit hard SO LONG AS you ensure you do not damage the thread of the hub nut. Do this by threading the hub nut on to be flush and hammering on that.



Front hall sensor hitting trigger wheelHall sensor mounts were not stiff enough. Cable flexibility ( lack thereof) allowed sensor head to be pushed into trigger wheel during testing.


Remake mount to be stiffer (aluminum to steel). solution to be verified
