Purdue Formula SAE : Integration with Other Subsystems

Created by Unknown User (mkotur), last modified by Unknown User (napolia) on Apr 29, 2020

For most of the other subsystems, the change to monocoque is not projected to cause much disruption in terms of design considerations. The biggest changes will be on chassis. The team will likely split into two halves, one that focuses on the rear steel tube frame and one that focuses on the carbon fiber frame from the main roll hoop forward. Information pertaining to these changes will be in other documentation.

Aerodynamics:

Nose height is the main consideration to have when designing the monocoque. As the nose of the chassis raises, aero performance initially increases, reaches a maximum, then decreases. This can be seen in the graphs below, Figures 1 and 2, where we want to minimize both Cd*A and Cl*A. From this data, it was determined that the nose height should be three inches from the lowest part of the monocoque as seen in Figure 3.

Figure 1. Cd*A vs Bulkhead Height

Figure 2. Cl*A vs Bulkhead Height

Figure 3. Side view of monocoque showing nose lift measured from lowest part of monocoque

 

The other aspects of the shape of the monocoque will not be significant enough to affect the aerodynamic performance of the car.

Electronics:

Integration of the dashboard is the main issue that electronics will face; the proposed solution will require more design consideration than has been necessary for steel tube chasses. After talking the current Electronics Team Lead (Michael Tam), it was determined that it would be inconvenient to layup the dashboard directly in the monocoque; instead, mounts should be laid up in the monocoque that the dashboard can connect to.

On the other hand, module placement would be made easier because there would be more space in the cockpit than previously.

Powertrains:

Powertrain is projected to only benefit from a change to monocoque because the rear frame will become removable and the engine will be easier to access and work on during all points of manufacturing and testing.

 

 

Other subsystems are not expected to see any major differences that would require further consideration on their part.
