All Tig welding is not that hard. A steady hand and good hand eye coordination helps. I would estimate that you need about 10 hours of torch time before you'll be ready to weld on a chassis. I have noticed the greatest increase in my welding ability during the chassis welding 'fun' week. It just takes time.

The goal of this page is to provide you with resources to get up to speed on welding as quickly as possible (just incase you needed to start welding the chassis yesterday).

Getting started:

Only worry about practicing on steel first, typically 4130 is what we have around. You will use the same tungsten for steel, aluminum, and stainless. Get the hang of welding regular steel first. Stainless steel is very similar you just need to purge the backside of what you are welding, if it's an exhaust tube cap the ends and let it fill up with argon for a minute or two. If you need to weld aluminum, it's not impossible, just make sure to practice before you start welding something critical. Try to make your practice pieces as representative of what you will be trying to do. The most difficult aluminum you can weld is a small, thin tab to something thick. Radiator tabs are the most difficult thing I have welded, they are an absolute pain and you really don't want to mess up a radiator, make sure to get lots of practice before you start welding those kinds of tabs.

Because welding can be difficult by itself, I like to think about removing as many variables as possible when learning (or always). When you're learning basic torch control and pedal movement the cleanliness of your work and tungsten isn't that important, but once you start welding stuff together get in the habit of prepping you work properly and having clean tungsten (you'll get plenty of practice and find your favorite tip angle). When starting out get a stool, clean your work surface, and make yourself as comfortable as possible, really focus on torch control and pedal control. When you're welding the chassis in jigs you will not get this luxury, so once you start to get more confident try welding standing up, it'll be really hard for the first few minutes.Â
