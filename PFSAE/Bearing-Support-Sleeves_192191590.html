Purdue Formula SAE : Bearing Support Sleeves

Created by Unknown User (mgal), last modified on May 02, 2022


the sleeve holders (aka what these bearing support sleeves go into) will warp when welding, enough clearance on the sleeve is needed. we ended up going back and sanding the outside of the sleeve after welding until the sleeve fit. in the future, we could tape the inside of the bearing support sleeves, or assume the powder coat will add 2 thou and account for this in the manufacturing (although doing this could mess up dry fitting before welding or if the sleeves are being used to place welded components).


  • It is important to make the proper bearing choice within the design phase of your subassembly. There are many types of bearings out there but in our steering assembly, we used needle roller bearings, originally we went with open needle roller bearings without realizing that open bearings start to bind when exposed to the outdoors (Rust). We had to redo a whole steering column to change to a sealed bearing design since the car drove in the rain and seizing become noticeable.
  • Make sure to properly lubricate the bearings since lubrication is important for fatigue of bearing and it is hard to lubricate the bearings when the column is assembled
