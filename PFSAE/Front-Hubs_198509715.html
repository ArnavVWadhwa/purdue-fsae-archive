Purdue Formula SAE : Front Hubs

High Level

  1. Stock Prep
  2. CNC Lathe for inboard side
  3. CNC Lathe for outboard side
  4. CNC Mill Outboard side
  5. Manual Mill Through Hole


1. Stock Prep

Use the manual lathe (both 3 jaw and 4 jaw setup) to turn down stock into a cylinder of approximately the dimensions of the hub

Also drill 1 (only 1) side just enough to get a boring bar in for the CNC operations. This will be the bore on the outboard side.

Diameter should be roughly 0.100" larger than final, and height should be 0.005" to 0.010" larger. This allows you to do a very light facing pass on the CNC lathe if needed/desired.

2. CNC Lathe for Inboard Side Features

This includes turning the features for hub nut threads, bearing face, and the back of the flange that mounts the wheels.

John should be able to guide you in hitting that bearing fit correctly.

Use a previous year's hub nut to test fit the threads after each pass.

3. CNC Lathe for Outboard sides

Flip this guy around in the lathe and fixture using shims to prevent the jaws from marring up the bearing surface.

You'll have to indicate the face and OD to ensure concentricity and parallelism to the last operation you just did.

Run the OD turning and Boring Operation.

We intentionally left this side solid (as opposed to drilling the through hole) so we could fixture without worrying about crushing it.

4. CNC Mill for Outboard Side: Wheel Mount Pattern

Use the 3 jaw to fixture the hub vertically in the mill, with the outboard side up, again using shims to prevent the jaws from marring up the bearing surface.

You'll have to indicate the face and OD to ensure concentricity and parallelism to the last operation you just did. John can demonstrate, but use a hammer to adjust until within desired tolerance and runout.

Run CNC (easier said than done, but see pages on CAM and CNC Setup) Unknown User (xia107) make those pages.

We intentionally left this side solid (as opposed to drilling for the CV pattern) so we could fixture without worrying about crushing it.

5. Manual Mill for Inboard Side: Drilling and Boring Hole

I made a simple fixture plate with four bolt holes to hold onto the hub form the wheel mount. The fixture also needed a large through-hole to make clearance for the hub centering ring. Use this in the vise to hold the hubs with the inboard side up in the mill.

Use an indicator to check for paralellism (between hub axis and machine Z-axis. Although there isn't much you can do if it is slightly out in some directions. A lot of this depends on how well you squared up your fixture block and how well you setup the vise.

Drill Up to the largest size you can, leaving ~0.050-0.100 to final dimension. You'll want to use a boring bar to finish this, as that'll leave a much better finish, and it's unlikely the design will be exactly a drill size.

Toe clamping the fixture block to the table may be better than using the vise due to vertical space restrictions.
