Launch Control Tests

Set a distance from start to finish where vehicle can get up to 30kph, mark with cones

Test will be done with vehicle in 2nd gear

Metric of success will be run with quickest time from 0-30kph, timer starts when rear wheels start spinning (≠0), timer stops when front wheels reach 30kph

Procedures:

  1. Start with a few manual launches (LC disabled, "Default" or "Setting Zero")
    1. Take Tire
  2. Launch once with each LC setting, 1 through 9
    1. Take Tire temp after 3rd, 6th, and 9th run
  3. Collect data after first 9 runs named "LC_
  4. Shift launch area over to start on clean pavement
  5. Launch again with each LC setting, 9 through 1
    1. Take Tire temp after
  6. Record driver qualitative feedback about each launch


Procedures Attempt 2:

Find wide strip where we can launch over and over, and move over later

  1. Start with a couple manual launches (LC disabled, "Default" or "Setting Zero")
    1. Take Tire temps, download data "LC_Dry_R25b_Manual"
  2. Launch twice with LC setting 1
    1. Take Tire temps, download data "LC_Dry_R25b_Setting1"
  3. Repeat Step 2 with LC setting 2 through 9, allowing tires to cool in between


  1. Shift launch area over to start on clean pavement
  2. Launch again with each LC setting, 9 through 1
    1. Take Tire temp after
  3. Record driver qualitative feedback about each launch


Conclusion

The fastest run appeared to have the engine hold roughly 9000 RPM while the wheels slipped until 60 kph. The better way is to look at test data from i2 26 Sept 2020, test 3C, 3D.

Setting 5 seemed like it may not have been working properly, The test data reflects that the engine would hang out 1500 rpm higher than the values in the limit table.


Traction Control Tests

This test will be performed by driver coasting at 25 kph and smashing the throttle. All in 2nd gear

  1. Warm up tires if not already
  2.  Run through settings 1 through 9
  3. Collect data after first 9 runs
  4. Run through settings 9 through 1
