Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-08-UPDATE1 Y15

Created by Matthew James Lamear on Sep 08, 2023

Simulation ML-063

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

Straightened the innermost two strakes considerably.

Also moved innermost strake LE .25 inch downstream.

Reducing the curvature on the inner strakes should help prevent the flow separation in Y15 conditions and aid in reducing the overall BL interactions that occur between the strake and the element.

The CFD picture shows the Y15 flow separation that should be eliminated by straightening the strakes.

Regarding the .25-inch move, in the image in the next row, it is possible that giving the strake some of its own flow prior to getting channeled will aid in reducing this flow sep.

Increased width between innermost strake and 2nd innermost strake.

This should prevent the separation occurring in Y0 on the fan side.

This separation may actually get worse due to the strake straightening, but hopefully, with a curved strake LE, this problem doesn't get terrible.

Giving more space between the BL of the strake and the flow turning with the main element should aid in attachment, but this is the biggest unknown for me.

Cutout a separation point at the same point as 2nd innermost strake.

This should help when transitioning between elements. This aims to prevent the separation seen in the second image.
