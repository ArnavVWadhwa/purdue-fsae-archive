The design event is typically begins with an introduction from the Chief engineer quickly going over high-level vehicle goals and team decisions, and should introduce the teamleads. It is best to have someone with a presentation and lead for each judge, which usually heads up a specific judging section based on the Judging Rubric that can be found at FSAEonline.com in the series resources page. 

In recent history there have been specific judges for 

  • Vehicle kinematics (Suspension)
  • Frame
  • Brakes, ergonomics
  • Aerodynamics
  • Powertrain (drivetrain gets tacked on with powertrain usually)
  • Electronics and Systems integration
    • We have had a specific engine calibration judge before, this responsibility should be Powertrain's, but might ask electronics about engine calibration questions as well, specifically in terms of sensors and stuff.

Sometimes Frame and Aerodynamics get grouped together by the same judge.

Previous in person design events there has been a specific judge for brakes and ergo, but for the Virtual Competition event Frame and Brakes were the same judge.

Overall Feedback notes

  • Aero DJ feedback
    • Targeting design finals will make DJs dig deep.  The conversation we had is the one we need to have to get you to Design Finals.
    • There was a lot of carry over from 2019 report.  I expect to see a totally new report for 2021.  If staying the same, need to prove it is already the best and can’t be improved.
    • Good integration between lapsim and overall team goals, but need even better integration between sub-systems.
  • Chassis/Brakes DJ feedback
    • Great presentation and I did a lot of listening
    • Good demonstration of the engineering concepts
    • Good data to support brakes and chassis design
    • “Good legs on their 3-legged stool”
    • Good considerations into ergonomics even though I wasn’t planning on focusing on that
  • Powertrain DJ Feedback
    • 3-legged stool – tribal knowledge (good), testing (good), models (weak)
    • Need a dyno to be in a “different weight class”. We expect you to have Helmholtz, 1D models, and different tests on the dyno.
    • For gearing, you looked at best driver.  You should also look at worst driver and see when they are at WOT.  So you have to figure out to make car fast for both drivers
    • Overall approach is absolutely excellent.  Build on what you done, test a lot more, and you’ll on your way to being at the top of design.
  • Suspension
    • I saw a lot of great systems engineering.  Used simple tools first then complex tools
    • I liked how they gave driver the fattest sweet spot to help driver control.
    • Interesting insight on perturbations such as mid-corner bumps
    • Correlated loads and correlated models, but bits of the “chainsaw” could be better
    • Design for six sigma experiments would help better understand sensitivities
  • Systems Integration / Electronics Integration
    • Presentation was too focused on details – to get to finals I need to see you’ve done good wiring and good connectors.  That should be a quick “check the box”.  The next level is to determine layers – algorithms, thought process
    • You weren’t ready to answer the detail questions well enough to be a finals team.  I need to see more polish on the readiness
    • I liked the button on the dash to set a data marker
    • Need more discussion on what you did unique
    • Overall, a lot of potential, but room for improvement
    • Our job is to keep drilling down until you run out knowledge
    • We are either seeing if you meet finals or if we need to be coaching you
  • Overall
    • You are at a high-level at each sub-system.  Now you’re held to a higher standard going forward.
    • At Design Finals will be 60 judges asking you the hardest questions they can think of.
    • Think about the questions you were able to answer to prepare for Design finals.
