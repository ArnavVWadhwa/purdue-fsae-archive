Chassis

PF20 Chassis Prelim.pptm

  • Chassis Optimization and Updates: 
    • Do more analysis regarding the changes in the rear of the chassis due to the magnitude of the loads. 
    • A few locations where suspension components are located offset from a node on the chassis will be called out by design judges and should have a reason for not being at the node. Rules compliance, packaging constraints etc. 
  • Ergonomics: 
    • Generally good feedback on improvements over previous years. 
    • Updates to seat model and shift from a seat insert to a carbon seat with padding was discussed and agreed upon as a good path forward. 
  • Manufacturing: 
    • New jigging method was discussed and should be okay for the majority of the tubes on the chassis. More care should be taken when jigging the MRH, FRH, and the BH as these determine the locations of every other tube. 
    • Jigs should be left on the chassis throughout the welding process and should be left on after the completion to avoid deformation due to residual stress prior to normalization. 
  • Analysis: 
    • Rocker Mount: issues with potential fatigue of this joint were brought up. One potential solution would include a gusset to the surrounding tubes though this would place these tubes in bending which is undesirable. 
    • Rear Tube: the rear diff tube was another cause for concern. Care should be taken during the FEA process to ensure this component will not fail or place significant load on the engine. 
    • Full vehicle model: a new concept for determining the stiffness target of the chassis was discussed and was a area of disagreement. This method in addition to methods used in past years (springs in series method) should be used.

Comments: questions and suggestions from Troy Conlin (Electronics TL 2019)

  • Electronic Clutch
    • Motor is sized much greater than the shifter motor; there might be power concerns if both the clutch and the shifter are starting up at the same time (motors have a current spike when starting up) similar to how the starter motor makes everything else go wonky when it cranks.
    • Current draw when the eclutch is held open will be substantial- should run a test to see how long the clutch can be held open at idle. Alternatively, we can try to map out voltage VS battery capacity and try to estimate it
  • Gear Shifter
    • Might be a tad on the complex side- have to make sure that the Teensy can reliably read the encoder data and transmit the data properly
    • running motor through ECU vs Motor Controller- Mike Reising (Electronics TL, Chief) suggested to use an external motor controller in the past, so Troy would prefer that one be used. Personally I think it depends on motor size- for smaller motors like the Neverest 60 we were originally using, running it through the ECU would be alright since the ECU will be able to handle it. Something else that needs to be considered is the difficulty of integrating an external motor controller with the M130.

Aero

Just going to go slide by slide and put my notes/what I was saying for each one. Raunaq should probably comment on the CFD slides since I don't understand this area as well as he does.

Lessons Learned/Goals - Both of these lead to us focusing more on the non-CFD analysis and manufacturing of the package. Reducing the delta from CFD to real life downforce by making the package more accurate to CAD is the biggest goal this year as well as improving our rules clearance

Aero Justification - Summary of lap sim and GP A/B testing results. Lap sim shows more downforce scores more points, GP testing verified the speed increase in real life. Roughly 2x point increase from aero 2019 → 2020

Requirements (Minimum Values) - What I should have called the min values slides. Biggest change is how we determine our aero package goals. In previous years the target for Cl, Cd, weight based on prior aero package, mass flow became a consideration in 18 and 19. This year moving to requirements for Cd, Front %, mass flow. Weight has a max but we are easily passing (should quantify it though). Cl and weight are the two parameters where there is no requirement, just a number that we need to min/max. We have goals that we would like for these but they are first on the chopping block if we are not hitting our Cd, F/R, RMF numbers

Current values - Read the table. We see a 10% increase in downforce from 2019, 30% reduction in weight, most other parameters are similar.

CFD - Raunaq should fill this in eventually. Summary is increase in cell count from 3mn to 35mn. Changed from k-e to S-A turb model. Raunaq and I have issues with our interpretation of real world driving data. Raunaq's take (correct me if I am wrong) is that from a CFD perspective, the shock pot data is not usable due to many uncontrolled factors (wind, noise, temp, etc) and difference in build of aero package compared to CAD. I see these as sources of error between CFD and the package as a whole and something that should be considered when designing parts. You will never be able to control all of these at comp or when testing.

Component Design - Going to go into detail for each part below

FW - Similar to 2019. Low endplate ground clearance to seal lower surface. Large inner flaps to increase downforce and shift balance to front. Just a quick timeline and summary of FW development for future generations: 

2017 - genetic optimization of flap profiles, 3 elements new main element

2018 - flap refinements

2019 - move back to 2017 profiles, split inner channel into 2 sections for more downforce. front limited by mass flow.

2020 - new everything

SW - Component with most changes for 2020. Tunnels rather than wing profiles for making it easier to manufacture and match to CAD. Narrowed UT for yaw performance and weight savings (remove tow bar and reduce plies). Also reduces amount of UT that needs to be supported.

RW - Similar to 2019 since we're still front limited. Improved mounting analysis. Shell mesh, 2 tie rods → 1.5lb weight saving.

Bodywork - No more chassis wrap, switching to molded panels for weight and manufacturability reasons

Composite Analysis - I need to make a confluence page for this. Brief summary is as follows. Program takes inputs (ply mat props, ply order, ply orientation, core props (opt)) and calculates stiffness and compliance matrices. These are then applied to any specific load case (sandwich panel, wing spar/skin) from which stress, strain, deflection, failure force can be determined. "Validated" with one data point on one spar and a couple sandwich panels. Error is about 15-20% at the moment. Biggest guess to this error is material properties, since we are either using expired material or a layup where material props can vary (infusion/wet layup).

Manufacturing improvements - Moving to use the carbon analysis model for layup schedule. Hollow core wings with spars on large elements (FW main element, RW main and flaps). One key thing not in the presentation is that all wings have constant chord/profile/alpha to allow for emergency foam core if hollow core doesn't work. Doing as few wet layups as possible in 2020. Infusion for endplates, wing skins, UT, tunnels, prepreg for nosecone, sidepods, firewall, floorpan, headrest, wheel, fan shouds, oil belt cover.
