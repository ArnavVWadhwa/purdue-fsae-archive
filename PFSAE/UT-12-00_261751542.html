Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Kick lines

Not really visible on the picture, but two kicklines at the rear spaced further apart than PF23's to further shift balance rear.
Side tunnel arrangements

Side tunnel is split into 2 parts, a diffuser generating downforce using pressure recovery, and a piece with low diffuser angle so that inwashed vortex has a surface to act on and prevents overstressing the vortex.

The inner side tunnel diffuser has a flap at the end to reduce adverse pressure gradient.

The outer side tunnel diffuser and the floor edge has a big gurney at the end to depressurize rear tires.

Floor edge flattens out before vortex in the vortex channel breaks down. This does increase adverse pressure gradient on the vortex itself. The goal would be to keep the vortex alive the entire way without flattening out.

The lack of inner endplate on the inner side tunnel flap

The lack of flap allows a vortex to form on the inside, downwashing flow up to rear wing.

Floor leading edge

Rounded leading edge on the inside of sidepods to keep floor attached and maximize radiator mass flow.

Sidepods

Initial outwash with the intention not only to maximize mass flow, but also to physically reduce the size of front tire wake.

A smooth top surface profile designed to downwash flow for rear wing performance at the cost of local load.

Description

Need leading edge

Need more outwash in the inner side tunnel

Outer side tunnel isn't doing anything to stop inwash into inner side tunnel. Really should have seen this coming.

Sidepod doing a great job at reducing tire wake size and inwashing high CpT flow.

And oops, need to get rid of the exhaust.

Outer side tunnel has too much expansion, making this vortex burst.

UT strakes need to start earlier to control the formation of this vortex. The worry then would be increased bursting of strake vortex due to the longer distance the vortices have to travel.
