Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-10

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 19, 2023

Simulation ML-052

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

Outer diveplane altered.

Added endplate kickpoint farther rearward.

Should reduce inwash in a region of particularly high inwash by generating a low pressure region above the inwashing section.

Including a kick point seems to be important for Y15 performance, so testing to see how far back the kick can be to improve the performance.

CFD Analysis


I am realizing that it may have something to do with not allowing wake to go around the outside of the tire which is why UT performance improved with the cutout. So I think having an inwashing endplate may do the trick to take advantage of this effect. 

This vortex gets inwashed, which means that potentially a vortex channel may be used. The vortex channel issue is the main vortex overpowers itself, but what if you feed it another vortex to survive the length of the tunnel.

Not a fan of this vortex geometry, going to revert back. It works somewhat well in Y0, but This isn't great in higher yaw since it doesn't outwash the turning vane vortex much.
