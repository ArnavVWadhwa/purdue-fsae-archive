Purdue Formula SAE : FW-1-08 Y15

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Jul 20, 2023

Simulation ML-027

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

Reduced transition gap to 1.5 in instead of 2 in.

Altered inner sweep to improve separation issues. 

Swept the down washing flap to match the sweep.

Reducing the transition gap gives more space for the vortices to develop and not burst. 

The inner sweep improves the stall characteristics since the sweep is considerably reduced near the nosecone. 

The down washing flap being aligned with the sweep should aid in flow sep from the previous iteration.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

Side tunnel flow is substantially higher energy, potentially too high: could be a CFD error. 

This is seemingly an improvement in side tunnel performance rather than the main UT.

(2nd image is FW-1-05, 1st image is this iteration)

Wake management is slightly worse for the swept version compared to 1-5 on the windward side.

Due to the swept down washing flap there is a substantial vortex forming. It downwashes resulting in improved flow management to the RW and stops the detached flow from the FW from increasing in altitude. As a result, side pod ingests more dirty air resulting in lower mass flow rate compared to previous two iterations.

The outer vortex in the 2nd image does not burst at all, while this iteration still bursts. The endplate does some heavy lifting here. 

With that said the wake management is surprisingly improved for this iteration when looking at the incoming flow for the windward side tunnel. 

(2nd image is FW-1-05, 1st image is this iteration)
