Created by MacNab, Zachary Robert on Mar 04, 2023

"Introduction to Modeling Structural Adhesives" was used as in initial baseline for modelling the joints and getting a rough idea of what parameters we need to obtain from testing. 

Modelling Methods

Linear Elastic

3M says these are "typically used for initial evaluations". They only require Hook's law and only require two material properties. This model is limited to small strains and to epoxies that exhibit linear behavior at small strains. 

Hyperelastic

For flexible structural adhesives, (typically polyurethanes) 

Strain energy density function, not sure what this means

Differences between linear elastic and hyperelastic

"Uniaxial, biaxial, and planar tension", describing stress states that material models can be derived from physical testing

Mooney-Rivlin model: 

Experimentally determined coefficients and "first and second deviatoric strain invariants... elastic volume ratio"

Viscoelastic

All in all, structural adhesives are viscoelastic materials, just like tires. 

Temperature and strain dependent

Higher stiffness at faster strain rates and lower temperatures

"Viscoelastic material models are of interest to simulate creep, stress relaxation, and impact events."

possibly large simulation "time penalty" (it takes long to run)

Small strains

can be modeled as linear viscoelastic materials

"Strain history" 

relaxation modulus, which is approximated with a prony series
