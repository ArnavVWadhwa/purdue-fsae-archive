What is chassis subteam?

The chassis subteam designs and builds the frame of the formula car and the cockpit, including all the controls of the car. The design work primarily has to do with producing a car that is structurally sound and easy to operate whikle being as light as possible.

What are the the subsystems of the chassis?

Frame

  • Designs, manufactures, and tests the frame of the car, which is made of round steel tubes welded together.
  • Images of the frame in CAD, FEA, and in production are shown above.
  • Lead: Dien Quang Nguyen 

Pedalbox

  • Designs, manufactures, and tests and manufactures the pedals, their mounting, and the associated components like the brake lines and throttle cable
  • Lead: Aaltonen, Emil Johan 

Steering

  • Designs, manufactures, and tests the steering wheel and steering column, along with their mounting onto the frame of the car.
  • Lead: Singhal, Ria 

Shifter/Pneumatics

  • Designs, manufactures, and tests the shifting mechanism of the car from the driver interface to the drivetrain.
  • Lead: Rumsey, Helen Nicole 

What is the goal of this project?

The goal is to test a particular design of the bulkhead to determine whether it deflects less than an inch in the event of a head on collision to ensure driver safety. Since we are using a standard honeycomb and a diagonal bulkhead support, extra testing is not required to validate the design according to the rules but we often consider different designs, so knowing how to do this analysis is critical. 

The bulkhead is the front part of the frame that goes infront of the driver's feet.

The project will help you develop CAD modeling skills and teach you the basics of the ANSYS workbench structural analysis software. These skills are critical to chassis team members, as the design process revolves around NX and ANSYS workbench.

ANSYS (FEA(Finite Element Analysis(Physics simulation software))): 

ANSYS is the FEA software used by the team for all components. Research licenses are available on ME lab computers which offer unlimited nodes. Student licenses are also available with limited node counts if you wish to get more familiar with the software. FEA is used by the team for components or load cases which are beyond the scope of reasonable hand calculations. Examples of components for which FEA is very useful include full chassis, isolated sections of the chassis where high loads are expected, suspension uprights, etc. FEA should not be used for simple studies such as suspension lug strength, two force member calculations, bolt calculations, etc (See below for resources for these calculations).
