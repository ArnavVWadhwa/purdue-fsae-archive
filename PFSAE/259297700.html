All the fundamentals, very little racecar stuff (especially no F1). The point is to get you to understand aerodynamics, not racecar aerodynamics.

There is no equation you can solve by hand to cheat through aerodynamics, even CFD has enough errors to the point where you can't use it effectively and reliably without understanding aerodynamics principles.

It's all about what you know and how well you know it. You can't design anything without understanding how it works.Â 

Skipped through a lot of math and avoided mentioning a lot of math heavy methods like the Blasius solution, because math is not the point. You can read them up in books provided in extra resources.

Read the pages in order so you know what I am talking about. I know it's fun to jump straight into airfoil performance characteristics, but you will get lost.
