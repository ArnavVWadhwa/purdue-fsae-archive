Things to Find

    • Master Cylinder Bore Size
      • Bigger Bore means less travel and more force required to lock up wheels
      • Smaller bore means more travel and less force to lock up wheels
      • Based on required brake line pressure and desired force at pedal?
      • Brake Line Pressure = Force at Master Cylinder/Master Cylinder Bore Area
      • Tune to Win recommends the same or very similar front and rear bore sizes
        • Keeps piston travel distance similar
    • Pedal Ratio
      • Ratio of force at master cylinder to force at pedal
      • In a standard master cylinder setup (horizontal), can be calculated using pivot distance ratio
        • In variable angle master cylinder setup (like most FSAE setups) trig is required and pedal ratio changes with displacement 
      • Ideal ratio seems to be between 3:1 to 5:1
    • Master Cylinder Orientation
      • Most teams seem to use angled setup right behind brake pedal
        • Pros
          • Very compact package
        • Cons
          • Pedal ratio is non linear
          • Harder to bleed brakes
          • Moment is applied to master cylinder
      • Horizontal Master Cylinder
        • Larger package
        • Pedal ratio is linear


Things to Look into

    • Electronic Brake Bias Adjustment
      • Current Setup can't be mounted to steering wheel since it is a physical connection (Tilton Remote Brake Bias Adjuster)
        • Drivers say it is too hard to adjust brakes during race
        • Electronics would allow controls to be moved to steering wheel
      • Pros
        • Drivers can adjust brake bias and keep both hands on the steering wheel
        • Sensors could allow current brake bias setting to be displayed
        • Price is around the same or less than Tilton MSRP
      • Cons
        • More complicated than current setup
          • More points of failure
          • More testing required to validate functionality
        • Motors may be heavy
          • Stepper motors appear to be between .5-5 lbs depending on torque output
          • Need to figure out how much torque is required

Things to research: 

    • Single Pedal Box assembly vs. 2 pedals attached to the same arm
      • Effects in (# = Importance) : 
        • Mass (1) 
        • Rigidity (1)
        • Ease of Use / Compatablity in Chassis Design (2)
          • Last posted link has some guidance into the differences on each design.
      • Single Body creates less  faliure points improving rigidity. Saves mass due to less rails and connectors involved. More research has gone into single body (PF23 and external sources). (7/13/23)

      • Ask for pros/cons of PF23 pedal box design, what drivers want changed/improved and what elements they want to carry over to PF24
        • Look for ways on how to reduce mass while keeping/improving rigidity. 
        • U of Western Macedonia has input on how to impove mass effects
