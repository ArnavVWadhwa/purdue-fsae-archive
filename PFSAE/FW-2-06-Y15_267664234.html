Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-06 Y15

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 13, 2023

Simulation ML-048

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Altered strake orientation.

Increased width of first channel to improve vortex generation in this region.

Currently, there is no vortex forming here, but with more space, it's possible that a vortex could form. With an additional vortex, inwash should be reduced, and df from UT should improve. 

The 2nd strake was lengthened back to its original length because the inwash was simply too high, instead my strategy is to lengthen the 3rd strake even more to alleviate some of the vortex bursting.

The 4th strake should aid in vortex bursting on the 3rd strake; however, if this does fail it will likely fail on the 3rd strake bursting.

Making the 4th strake any longer will burst the vortex due to the slot gap, so there's not much to play with here.

Turning vanes are closer together now. (1.5 inches closer)

This won't affect Y15 much, but in Y0 this is intended to merge the two vortices into one.

CFD Analysis

Wake is still roughly the same as the previous iteration, thus, UT performance didn't change much; however, FW performance improved due to the vortices acting on their respective surfaces longer.

The first channel still fails to generate a vortex even with the implemented measures. Potentially need to start looking into changing the pressure distribution to decrease the pressure outboard. 

The vortex shed off the vortex generators puts a suboptimal vortex on the lower surface of the rear wing that downwashes and outwashes, which inhibits RW downforce production due to the downwash on the flow. 

To improve this aspect, the vortex may be able to be powered up more, or the vortex may be able to be outwashed more by other factors such as the downwashing flap. 

Something to begin considering in future iterations.
