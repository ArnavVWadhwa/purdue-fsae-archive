Purdue Formula SAE : Cooling Charge Gain

Created by Unknown User (dnocon), last modified by Williams, Carson Kenneth on Apr 01, 2021

Testing 3/28/2021

First Run Testing With Cooling Charge Gain

  1. Don't worry about the lambda spikes, that's pretty normal without any good wall wetting tuning.
  2. Looking at the Closed loop Lambda average its about 7-11% at most points. 
  3. To adjust for this and bring it down cooling charge needs to go down as well.


Second Run Testing with Cooling Charge Gain (Carson went up and made it worse)

  1. Ben quickly saw lambda was acting funny and pulling the data you can see the average is between 11-15% at most points.
  2. Closed loop rails at 15% so it wont go any higher.
  3. Don't be dumb and make the cooling charge numbers go up like Carson did.


Third Run Testing with Cooling Charge Gain (Better not perfect... yet)

  1. Its still pretty far off from 0% (about 6-10%)
  2. Can be adjusted for the temperatures we were running at to be even lower cooling charge values.
  3. Currently sitting at these values below

    4. For 7C it could be closer to 8.0 as we were running at about 4.4C on 3/28/2021. The higher temperatures will require hotter temperatures outside to full tune out the table
