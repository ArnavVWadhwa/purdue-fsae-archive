Purdue Formula SAE : New Suspension Tabs (PF22)

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified by Unknown User (ehoerl) on Mar 13, 2021

Why?

Traffic cones suck.

Our analytical pin bending margins suck.

Doing fea on a lug sucks.

The lug calculator gives negative margins for highly loaded suspension lugs because the #10 bolt is strong enough on its own. In reality, the traffic cones help take some of the bending. nOn-LIneAr fEa COnTaCt MoDelS have been used to write positive margins, but those models are always kinda sketchy, they don't include preload, and we shouldn't be doing FEA on lugs.


Design Parameters

Loads 1900 lbf or 800 lbf as bounding loads: Z:\2021\3_Suspension\01_Design\06_Loads\Copy of CA_Loads_from_GG_v_2022_1cyl_v6.xlsx 

  • Front LCA is vertical with no spacer
  • Front UCA and Rear UCA and LCA are horizontal with an .063 washer (NAS620 for tight tolerances and small diam)
  • E/D 1.5 (with 0.25" bolt) (allows for #10 to be drilled up to 0.25 if they yield)
    • 0.75" outer diameter on all tabs

  • For tabs that are at a significant angle to the chassis tube make the inner side of the tab 90deg to the tube axis

Comparison

Pros of vertical no bushing:

  • no bushing
  • smaller bolt length
  • positive analytical bending margin
  • load stays truly in plane
  • fully weld without removing from jigs? (don't need to flip the car)


Cons of vertical no bushing:

  • If yield occurs, idk what you do. Maybe drill up and add sleeves??
  • Harder to make via grinder (curved face rather than flat - although some of the horizontal ones still had curves)
  • Rear tierod tabs? (small gap so may need to be horizontal)
  • welding acute angles (rear control arm forward connections 
  • suspension motion possibly? loosens bolt (need extra retention method)
