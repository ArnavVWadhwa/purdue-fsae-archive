Purdue Formula SAE : References (Structures)

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified by Unknown User (agarw162) on Apr 23, 2022
  • Shigley’s Mechanical Engineering Design
    • Very good for many realms of mechanical design
    • Has the frustum method for calculating bolted joint stiffness, which is a very good empirical route.
  • NASA-STD-5020
    • The NASA go to for calculating margins on bolted joints
    • Use this for all margins calcs except for possibly separation. Very conservative for separation. 
  • NASA Technical Memorandum 10694:
    • The older NASA go to calculating margins on bolted joints. 
    • May be better to use this for separation margins as there is less conservatism relative to 5020 which is likely over conservative on gapping.
  • Roark's Formulas for Stress and Strain:
    • Stress concentration of various geometry’s
    • Lots of useful analytical/empirical stress and deflection equations
  • Airframe Stress Analysis and Initial Sizing:
    • Lug calculations and lots of other things
  • The 1.5 & 1.4 Ultimate Factors of Safety for Aircraft & Spacecraft - History, Definition and Applications
    • Link: NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS)
    • background on safety factor selection. shows typical selection of a 1.1 or 1.0 on yield with a 1.4 on ultimate
  • Practical Finite Element Analysis
    • Gives good insight on what is actually happening when running FEA and teaches meshing basics
  • Your very own ME 323 Notes:
    • Krousgrill knows how it be
  • FSAE online documents on threaded fasteners:
    • https://www.fsaeonline.com/cdsweb/gen/DocumentResources.aspx
    • Then design event → reference documents → threaded fasteners
    • This is fsae's own reccomendations for threaded fasteners, but looks similar to what we landed on (nasa requiriments)
