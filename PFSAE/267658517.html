Helen's Feedback

  • does less bent tubes really make manufacturing easier? last year we wanted more bent tubes so there was less joints that needed welding and potentially less errors
  • i'm curious what you're plan is to get down to 62 lbs - i don't think there's many places you can take out weight and still have a rules compliant chassis
  • little thing - i don't think we need to call it mock v5 since it's a silly name out of context of the test. can just call it ideal set-up or something like that
  • i think there needs to be a "design philosophy" for tradeoff between weight and torsional rigidity - like how are you going to decide what the balance between the two are?
    • in my mind this is one of the only places you have room to do frame design since there's so many rules for everything else
  • maybe silly thought - do we need to mount the engine using all six points? can you beef up the bottom mounts and get rid of the top floating tubes?
  • alternate approach to welding? could be hard to get the table into the room. can we do sections of it at once?
  • btw ALRO contact took almost two weeks to respond so don't do that last minute 
  • we haven't bought filler rod in a couple of years
  • one thing i wish i did was trying to understand a front crash case better because that's why we added the side tubes back in - but we were adding them back to pass internal checks not rules so was our front crash load case correct? (does the whole frame see 20G or not because isn't the impact attenuator supposed to absorb it?)
