Purdue Formula SAE : 2024 Goals

Created by Unknown User (dnocon), last modified by Rumsey, Helen Nicole on Oct 20, 2023

2024 Team Vision Statement:

To represent the pinnacle of project-based undergraduate engineering at Purdue University through the SAE Collegiate Design Series by developing the abilities of team members to their maximum potential in engineering and team building.

2024 Season Mission Statement:

To produce a consistently performing vehicle and foster a cohesive team; to drive our team into the Top 10 in every event, Design Finals, and win overall at the IC FSAE competition through justified engineering decisions and set the team up for continued success.

Level 4 Goals: 

  1. Set team up for future success, including:
    1. education
      1. specifically through the entire engineering design cycle
    2. documentation
    3. culture
    4. financially
    5. successful recruitment
  2. Continuously challenge ourselves technically and maintain ethical decision making.
  3. Win overall at competition including achieving top 10 in every dynamic event and winning design event.

Aerodynamics

  1. Achieve vehicle performance targets listed below
    1. Meet mass flow target as determined by powertrain to complete endurance without fan use. Currently 0.125kg/s mass flow per radiator (6.5"x10") for maximum 90C coolant temp at 99F ambient temp.
    2. Achieve balance target as determined by suspension, currently 55%. Provide sufficient adjustment to account for simulation errors and driver preference, and to tune according to vehicle dynamics goals (likely +/-10%).
    3. Achieve ClA > -4.00, L/D> 2.5, weight < 30lb. Chose these values to maintain 2020 weight and stiffness while improving aero performance. Should result in a xx point gain overall for aero package.
  2. Document and validate through physical tests custom tools used
    1. CFD macros, slurm commands, sim environment justification (cell size, turbulence model, mesh refinement justification)
    2. Full vehicle WT test + on track testing to validate CFD environment
    3. Composite calculators (FEA, hand calcs, manufacturing calcs)
    4. Destructive testing of 2020 wings to validate stiffness and points of failure calculations
  3. Improve overall package finish
    1. Leading edge seam flange leftovers
    2. Trailing edge straightness
    3. Maintain positional accuracy to 1/16" for all components in all dimensions
    4. Keep all wings aligned to within 1 degree of designed angle (keep wings parallel to each other/perpendicular to endplates)
