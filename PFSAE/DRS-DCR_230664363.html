Purdue Formula SAE : DRS DCR

Created by Fava, Nicola Bernardo, last modified on Aug 02, 2022

Project Scope

  • Design a mechanism that can alter rear wing flap AoA in order to reduce drag coefficient and increase vehicle acceleration
  • Potential Worth ~22 points via lap sim, 10 for DRS 12 for shifter, most points in Endurance L1 goals: Aero CdA <2.00
  • Rapid, reliable automated actuation ~ 90ms
    • Driver Comfortability
    • Maximize effectiveness
  • Minimize impact on rear wing performance (mounting location, pivot points)
  • Don't compromise the structural rigidity of the rear wing

Stakeholders

    • Powertrain
      • Share air tank/pneumatic equipment
    • Chassis
      • Driver comfortability with fail safe button
      • Mounting (location and structure) for air tank and necessary pneumatic equipment (hoses, solenoid 
    • SIQ
      • Fail safe button on steering wheel
      • Automation using vehicle characteristics (steering angle angle,  RPM, throttle input, brake input, wheel speed (slip ratio))
      • Limit switch? (Don't need to have a hard stop in the endplate)
    • Suspension

Actuation Methods Outlook

Electronic

-Pros

  • Potential for automation
  • Rapid activation ~<.1s
  • Relatively light ~<1lb

-Cons

  • Cost ~>$500
  • Reliability
  • Additional mounting

Mechanical

-Pros

  • Simple system, reliable
  • Low cost ~<$100
  • Light weight ~<1lb

-Cons

  • Slow activation
  • Driver dependent
  • More responsibility on the driver

Pneumatic

-Pros

  • Potential for automation
  • Rapid activation ~<.1s
  • Robust, reliable
  • Easily integrated with pneumatic shifting
  • Mid Cost ~<$300 (automation)
  • Piston + hosing alone is light weight ~< 1.5lbs

-Cons

  • Additional mounting
  • Higher degree of complexity

Design Requirements

  • Store extremely highly compressed air to maximize number of actuations possible
  • Operate at as low of a supply pressure as required to meet actuation times
  • Size cylinders and valves to be as small as possible while providing enough force/flow to achieve actuation speed goals at worst case loads
  • Limit exhausted air as much as possible
  • Not affect downforce generation
  • Not affect rigidity of the rear wing
