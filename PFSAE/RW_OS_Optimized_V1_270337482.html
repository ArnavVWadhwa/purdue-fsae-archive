Purdue Formula SAE : RW_OS_Optimized_V1

Created by Shi, Maoyuan, last modified on Sep 02, 2023

Simulation Yaw 15

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

Eliminate the flow separation in the rear wing close to the endplate in Yaw 15 flow conditions.

Changes:

  • The AoA of the second element increased by 1 degree.
  • The AoA of the third element decreased by 3 degrees.
  • The AoA of the fourth element increased by 2 degrees.
  • The chord of the third element decreased by 0.05 inches.

Most of the flow separation happens at the third wing element of the rear wing. By lowering the angle of attack of the third element, the air is not being pulled up as much which should help with reducing the flow separation. The angle of attack of the other wing elements and the chord length were changed to fill the trailing edge of the fourth element to the end of the endplate.

If separation still happens the angle of attack of the third element could be further reduced or a vortex generated by the front wing should be looked into.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

The flow separation problem got even worst. And instead of flow separating on the third element, it separates now on the second element. This is understandable because the angle of attach of the second element increased by 1 degree, which increases the chance of flow separation happening. This change combined with a decrease in the angle of attack of the third element means there is a lower suction pressure behind the second element pulling the air upwards. 

Other methods of eliminating the stall in Yaw 15 condition should be explored. 

Moving forward:

  • Front wing wake management.
  • Endplate cut out.
