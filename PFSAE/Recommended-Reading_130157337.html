Purdue Formula SAE : Recommended Reading

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified on Apr 03, 2021

Vehicle Dynamics / Kinematics

  • Tune to Win: the starting place. No math, but good intuition for a large number of vehicle performance topics.
  • Race Car Vehicle Dynamics (RCVD): this is the primary resource for vehicle dynamics, but is thick and still does a poor job of covering certain topics such as Roll Center targets, Ackermann targets, and more.
  • OptimumG seminar slides: as much as it pains to me to admit that anything from OptimumG is any good, these go into considerable depth on a variety of subjects. However, I think the tires section is best and the others fail to provide enough detail to be helpful without a live presenter.
  • https://virtualracingschool.com/academy/iracing-career-guide/setups/to-setup-or-not-to-setup/
    • This is a tuning guide made for iRacing setups, but is equally applicable in real world setup. The other good resource for this is ch 12 in Race Car Vehicle Dynamics. The difference is that this resource is set up to walk you through tuning, while the RCVD one is more for troubleshooting tuning.
  • Explanation of how a mountain-bike style damper (like what we run) works and how the low and high-speed settings adjust the damper curve: https://www.bing.com/videos/search?q=high+speed+damper+adjustment&view=detail&mid=57051B9094237031A11F57051B9094237031A11F&FORM=VIRE
  • Good overview of damper tuning in terms of ratios and transient maneuvers: A-Guide-To-Your-Dampers-Chapter-from-FSAE-Book-by-Jim-Kasprzak.pdf (kaztechnologies.com)


Brakes

  • https://dspace.mit.edu/bitstream/handle/1721.1/119947/1080312700-MIT.pdf?sequence=1&isAllowed=y
    • This MIT paper has some useful information specifically relating to friction coefficient vs temperature for pads and discs with Wilwood GP200 calipers being used in the front since 2018. 
    • It also has testing of gp200 brake caliper to failure.
