Purdue Formula SAE : 2021+ fan model

Created by Unknown User (rkumaran) on Aug 31, 2021

Working on redoing the fan model. This should be a feature in fsaeSTAR_V5.0 onwards. 


The current interface based approach to fan modeling is relatively simple, but very inflexible. Right now we apply a fan boundary condition on some arbitrary surface on the radiator outlet surface. I've got a little cylinder extruding out from the back of the radiator, and the face of that cylinder is the fan outlet. The fan boundary on this outlet applies a pressure gain over the interface, as defined by the fan performance curve. This does a reasonable job of modeling a fan slapped on the radiator outlet, but can't handle an offset between the radiator and the fan. 


The new fan model uses a new fluid volume for the fan, and gives each fan its own region. The region is defined with a fluid momentum source to model the fan using a fan performance curve. This should give a lot more flexibility with fan location, and decouples the fan physics from the radiator physics (two different regions).
