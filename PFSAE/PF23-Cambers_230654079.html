Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 Cambers

Created by MacNab, Zachary Robert, last modified on Jul 21, 2022

Static camber is something that can be adjusted, and I'm not terribly worried about it. As long as we have a good window of adjustibility, I think we're fine. 

Camber curves, specifically incorporating compliance, is the tricky and in my opinion more important part. 

General Design

Firstly, it is rather obvious that your front camber gain under heave should be LESS than your rear camber gain under heave. This is because you also get camber gain through the steering and your KPI that you don't get in the rear. 

Secondly, you have to break up the ideal camber under accel/braking and cornering. Accel/braking ideal camber is 0 - largest contact patch and makes you more power limited. Cornering is about -1 to -1.5 according to Ben, so getting the best of both worlds would possibly be achievable with camber curves.
