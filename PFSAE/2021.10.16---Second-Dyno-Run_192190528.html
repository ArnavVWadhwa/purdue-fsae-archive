Purdue Formula SAE : 2021.10.16 - Second Dyno Run

Created by Unknown User (dnocon), last modified on Oct 16, 2021

Changes Made Since Last time

  • Adjusted Pillow block mounting with jig stock
  • Added flywheel to help with rpm target PI controller
  • Harness changes
  • Clutch handle


Chronological Timeline of Events:

  1. Initially struggled with starting the engine
    1. Adjusted crank / cam hysteresis, debounce, threshold values
    2. Engine will not start without at least 1-2% throttle. Throttle control needs to be more precise.
    3. adjusted wide pitch and narrow pitch threshold values
    4. Important note: Sparkplug wire causes enough interference that when the cam position sensor wire was next to it, we got poor cam pos signal.
  2. Attempted VE table
    1. the new dyno hardware setup worked great. 
      1. the flywheel worked very nicely, not much vibration at higher rpms
      2. pillow block mounting was also improved
      3. clutch worked
    2. attempted VE from 3500-6000 rpm
    3. VE numbers are all around 26-28 percent and don't scale with throttle position or rpm
      1. very weird, we decided to not continue for the rest of the day until that was figured out
      2. upon further discussion the likely culprit was the mounting of the fuel injector
        1. the fuel injector mount did not allow the spray to atomize because it was placed so far back in the runner
        2. because the fuel did not atomize properly it could not combust properly. The fuel was able to atomize in the cylinder and burn on its way out of the exhaust. 
          1. this is why we did not smell any fuel from the exhaust, but the cylinder had soot, and the oil had fuel in it
        3. this also explains why the header did not glow, even when we ran very retarded ignition timing
    4. tub getting extremely hot, need to find appropriate solution to cool down tank further.
