Introduction

A dynamometer or dyno is a device that can measure both rotational speed and torque of an engine at the same time. 

There are many types of dynos, but the type we use is a waterbrake. 

In it's most simplest form, a waterbrake dyno converts rotational power into heat energy. Water will enter into an absorber, the absorber will convert the rotational energy from the engine, via friction and turbulence, to heat energy. The heat energy is transferred into the water.
