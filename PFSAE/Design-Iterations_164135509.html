  • So there was a 30% increase in downforce in yaw, not entirely sure why. Subsequent iterations show similar performance increase in yaw, but not as much downforce as this design.

  • Downforce in straightline is lower, but who cares because we don't need downforce in straghtline.

A picture of the design CAD (top) and the airfoil I designed for it (bottom).

3D pressure scenes (15 yaw) for the top (top) and bottom (bottom) of the car. The design is very dependent on the leeward element (top of both pictures). The lower pressure region underneath the leeward element (top of second picture, dark blue) contributes significantly more to downforce than the higher pressure region above the windward element (bottom of first picture, yellow-orange). This could cause a problem if the very large airfoil stalls. You'd lose a lot of SW downforce rather suddenly and the car could get out of control.
