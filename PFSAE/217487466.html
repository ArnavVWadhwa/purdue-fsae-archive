What do spal part numbers mean?

  • https://thermofans.com.au/blogs/news/what-do-spal-model-numbers-mean
  • You will likely want a puller fan (A at the end of the part number)

         


For PF22:

Square fans selected to fit directly on the back of the narrow radiators: VA69A-A100-87A 12V aka 2A square fans (115mm diameter fan)

Triangle circular fans because 2A square fans were out of stock: VA31-A101-46A 12V aka triangle fans (130mm diameter fans)

These are the comp spec fans for PF22

Replacement summit fans selected based on availability: VA32-A101-62A 12V aka IX-30103018


Alternatives:

VA69A-A101-87A 12V

  • Expensive, high current draw, and does not move that much more air, we could not run two of these on the car
  • Double the current draw but not double the mass flow, is an OEM part for a "Gas Gas" trials bike, and could be found here:
    • https://www.tryalsshop.com/Fan-Cooling-SPAL-Gas-Gas-p389557546
    • https://www.trialstoreusa.com/fan-for-gas-gas/

         

       

  • VA37-A100-46A (130mm diameter fans) larger than squares, slightly more mass flow, slightly more current
  • VA36-A100-46A (130mm diameter fans) larger than squares, slightly more mass flow, slightly more current


Comparing Fan Data:

  • I don't know the static pressure of our radiators with or without shrouds, but it's probably between 25-150 Pa, I've been using 100 Pa to compare fans.
  • https://www.verus-engineering.com/blog/informative-8/radiator-fans-in-depth-explanation-and-information-32
