Purdue Formula SAE : UT-12-10

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Jun 13, 2023

Simulation: CZ-023

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Added caketin

Caketin is an unsprung device used to control tire squirt. Due to its unsprung nature, it can be mounted close to the tires, which is useful due to the limited amount of space available in this region. I don't really want to reduce the width of UT since that's load lost.

The biggest advantage of unsprung nature of the part is that it can ride super close to the ground. In case where the car is unloaded (no tire deformation), it has 0.4 in of ground clearance, 1.1-1.4 in lower than the rest of the undertray. When the car is under load at high speed, the part should be very close to scraping but not quite. CFD runs 0.35 in of tire deformation, so it should also be 0.05 in above the ground. The low ground clearance is essential in controlling tire squirt by effectively isolating it.

This design is simply an outwash airfoil. However, a full skirt can be fantastic, something to run for future iteration.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

The caketin is doing exactly what it's supposed to do. Isolated tire squirt throughout its chord. Needs to get longer since wake is immediately inwashed behind the it.

Much better vortex health in the diffuser along the tips. They still breakdown, but that can be solved with larger caketin.

The much powerful vortex core breaks down faster once the caketin ends and inwash increases again, leads to a slight reduction in load in this region.

Side tunnel vortex depowered due to inwash, not by too much, but enough to make a slight difference.
