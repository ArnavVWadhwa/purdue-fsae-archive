Purdue Formula SAE : PF25 Start Point Thoughts

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Jan 28, 2024

FW

FWEP LWR Edge Vortex

Remove FP/switch to sympathetic shedding edge to clean up endplate vortex.

Strake Top Edge Vortex

Which direction is strake top edge vortex shedding? Need better vorticity slices with directionality

Strake Vortex for Squirt Suppression

FW OBS vorticity is at a relatively good y position for squirt suppression. Just needs to be lower in z. How can we achieve this?

  1. Camber crank to power the vortex up to increase imaging on tyre SW.
  2. Shed the vortex lower to directly reduce z height and increase SW imaging.
  3. Increase strake length to shed the vortex later, hence reducing the distance for it to upwashed. Also increases SW imaging.
  4. Depower/moving squirt suppressor IB to reduce upwash locally
  5. Reduce FW OB upwash

Keep in mind that powering up this vortex also increases loss. This is the main tradeoff for squirt suppression. Is your step in cleaning up squirt more beneficial than simply depowering the vortex system that does the cleaning. 

Chassis Corner Cleanup

Add multielement winglets to chassis corner to fix separation and generate local load which should propagate to FWMP. Downstream aerodynamic benefits will be limited but should take a step in cooling, since all the losses get ingested in the SPOD.

IB Reverse Vorticity

This reverse vorticity can be interesting if correctly placed, but it currently does more harm than good. It separates flow conditioner and pushes chassis corner wake OB. But it looks like the vortex does help with cooling somewhat. Move the vortex OB should improve FLE downwash and wheel wake outwash (assuming chassis separation is fixed). In terms of z height, lowering the vortex will improve FLE downwash and LWR wake outwash at the expanse of mid wake/UPR wake inwash.

Flow conditioner needs to be realigned regardless.

IB DP Separation

Fix

Squirt Suppressor IB Separation

Separation might be due to poor CpT onset. But need VelZ slices to ensure that it is not the problem. This separation then blows up by squirt suppressor vortex.

Squirt Suppressor Vortex

The squirt suppressor vortex first gets fired into TROD (or so I think). Fire it elsewhere

The vortex then smashes into SPOD OB LE, inducing SPOD separation and killing the vortex itself. Pull SPOD LE back to fix that.

This vortex can do a lot of good by increase floor edge circulation.

A broader question: can be shed the vorticity another way? The squirt suppressor adds pressurisation to chassis, which offloads FW. You'd imagine generate the vortex through outwash then downwash will be more successful in that respect. But then can we recover the downwash? Is the downwash is the dominant factor in this case? Maybe removing the pressurisation will dominate.

FWEP UPR Edge Vorticity

Is this vortex good? You could argue not since we took a gain by moving it OB. But IB DP was also a gain, which powered up this vortex. But what did that do to overall floor edge inwash? Is floor edge inwash good? By depowering this vortex, you reduce inwash, which gains you CpT, but reduces local load. What's the balance here? We tried hard in the past to reduce the inwash, but is there evidence that inwash is actually bad?

I am also fairly sure this vortex creates way more inwash in CFD than it does in the tunnel (from PF23 results) and just general observation on track will the car drives over dusts.

FC Shedding Edge

FC has a very thick shedding edge. Reduce shedding edge thickness to clean and power up the vortex.

The shedding edge is very sympathetic. We can power up the shedding edge and increase IB downwash. But is the downwash dominant here or clocking of UPR wake?

UT

FLE Pressurisation

Clean up losses and put a bluff wall at the FLE to increase pressurisation and wake repulsion. The loss here comes mainly from squirt suppressor separation, should be easy to fix.

In general, wake control using pressurisation is very robust and can be very powerful. Using a vertical wall also doesn't produce lift like pressurisation using floor channel does. Maybe what you can do is add the pressurisation through a wall and and depressurise the channel for iso wake control but Cz gain.

I don't imagine adding wake control will be very reward in CFD is 0 XW cases, but it will likely help in other conditions as well the real world, as LWR and mid wake shape is likely horribly wrong.

FLE Vorticity

The vorticity that runs along the OB floor is shed by this guy, which is reflexed. Remove the reflex and add up to load up the vortex as well as local load.

This vorticity is also shed right at the LE, you might get some CpT benefit by softening off the curve. But you would shift the vorticity OB. Is that a good thing? 

FWD Floor Outwash

This is more of an open question. Add FWD floor outwash is good for FWD floor loading and FWD chassis loading and some would propagate to FW. But since we have so more inwash already, should we just focus on cleaning up floor edge shedding and keep piling on dP across the shedding edge?

Another argument that we used to use for adding outwash is rear tyre squirt control, which it would, but the question is do we really want that much outwash at the rear? Sure we would increase CpT but we would loss vorticity. But then you could argue we can add in vorticity in other ways, like using strakes and stuff.

Anyways this is more of an open questions right now. We don't have the evidence to back up the argument in either direction.

Maybe a study we can do is to just add a ton of outwash at the front using strake that go into the ground, you can even put slip BC on the strake to remove any boundary layer losses associated with it. Actually this would be very good to do.

Raised OB Tunnel

Raised OB tunnel for vortex induced load in exchange for GE load. This will need a big project to study

Main Tunnel GE Load vs. Vortex Load

Could be interesting with another vortex vs. GE study. I imagine some combination of the two will be the best though. GE has the benefit of wake repulsion and robustness. Vortex induced load has the benefit of not pressurising bottom the front chassis as much. I think the best will like be the floor profile of GE with some sort of shedding in between suction kicks.

Discretise Floor Edge Shedding

Vortex under the cone is way overloaded, need to discretise the vortex to improve shedding and maintain health.

Adding top vanes is just one way to do it. You could also add bottom vanes. I would imagine that would be more effective at breaking up shedding, but they also add back pressure which reduces shedding. But then you could argue they create local load through curvature which propagates IBD. My intuition says back pressure addition might dominate, but I'm not sure.

Floor Edge Shedding Health

Add sympathy to the shedding edge. Don't have to do it for all the shedding edges. Maybe start with the OB most one.

Diff Shedding

There is so much CpT here...use it

RW

Downwashing wing

Shed a negative sense vortex to clock high CpT flow IB and increase downwash. Important to not shedding anything IB, as that vortex will be in positive sense.

Tip Outwash

Shed UPR EP vortex low to improve outwash
