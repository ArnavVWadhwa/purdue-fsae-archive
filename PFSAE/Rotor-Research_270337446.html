Purdue Formula SAE : Rotor Research

Created by Komensen, Aiken Leander, last modified on Aug 29, 2023

Rotor Shape

Cross Drills:


 

  • GM Study​
    • Seems to have noticeable cooling benefits, mainly at higher temperatures.​
    • Note that the benefits of drills can depend on air flow around the rotor.​
    • Notes that in theory, there could be too many holes that cause a drastic temperature gradient across the rotor.​
    • More drills decrease apparent friction at lower temperatures.​​
    • Pad Wear Rate seems to increase at higher temperatures compared to solid rotors.​
    • Notable Dust build up.​
    • Cross drilling will reduce fatigue life, cracks will form quicker, up to twice as fast.​
  • Pros
    • Potential thermal capacity improvement
      • The improvement is dependent on how much air is passing through the rotor.
      • This means increases in speed or rerouting air to the rotors (such as the sports car in the study did by rerouting air from the intake).
      • If no air passes through the tire/rotor, there is little to no improvement in the thermal capabilities of the rotor.
    • Weight-saving (Biggest benefit)
    • Allows gas to escape
  • Cons
    • Drills allow for fracture points
      • Heat builds up around the drills as air flows through it and that can cause cracks to form around the rotor.
    • Notable dust build up, though not sure if this is the case for the SAE car

Slots

  • Pros
    • Better removal of brake dust
      • This has substantial evidence backing it unlike many other properties of the rotor geometry mentioned before.
    • Weight saving
  • Cons
    • Less weight saving in comparison to drills.
    • unproven thermal benefits (As far as I can find. Best bet is to test once the dyno is ready).
    • Harder to manufacture.
    • increases pad wear significantly compared to other designs.
  • Notes on alternative slot designs
    • Several companies have created alternative slot designs for their brake rotors that are seemingly able to capture the proposed benefits of cross-drilled and slotted rotors. 
    • An example of this is the j-hook rotors from ap racing. 
      • J-Hook Rotors | AP Racing Radi-CAL Vented Discs by STILLEN
    • The main disadvantage of alternative rotor designs is the difficulty of manufacturing. The shapes are often more complex and might not be worth the time investment to do so.
    • Additionally, since these are from the company themselves, we still need to check the validity of the claims ourselves,
    • They also lack the weight saving offered by drills but could be a good alternative solution should we be able to manufacture them.

Vented

(Vented Rotors on an LMP1 car)

  • Pros
    • By far the most proven and best way to increase thermal capacity if possible
    • Provides weight saving similar to drills.
  • Cons
    • Not sure if we have enough space to fit vented drills, especially if we are including drills.
    • Might mess with the strength of the rotor.
