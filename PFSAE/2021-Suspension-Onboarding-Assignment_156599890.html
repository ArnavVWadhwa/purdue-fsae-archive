Purdue Formula SAE : 2021 Suspension Onboarding Assignment

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified on Oct 27, 2021

Anti-Roll Bar Design Assignment:

Pre-Requisites:

  1. Complete Bike Pedal Project
  2. Read Chapter 17 of Race Car Vehicle Dynamics, Milliken and Milliken, Chapter 17, Page 607-661
    • This section seems to do a good job of giving basic understanding of the components of a suspension, terminology, etc. Focus on content regarding SLA suspension.
    • https://drive.google.com/file/d/1beXnd3ZTY7-RCddXpm_gndgWSs6IfhwV/view?usp=sharing
  3. Read through this powerpoint on how ARBs work in the context of our car - Basics of ARB
  4. Watch youtube videos to supplement your knowledge on vehicle cornering behavior and ARBs (in tools below)

Project Objective:

  • To design a front Anti-Roll Bar for the PF20 vehicle.
    • This task is meant to be a carrier for learning other major tools used by our subteam: NX, Excel, ANSYS
    • Understand the major phases of designing a component of FSAE Suspension: Design Constraints, Component Modelling, Validation


Steps

  1. Use the ARB stiffness calculator under tools to determine the basic geometry properties of your design
    1. For the most part, the general aspects of the design are determined for you: It will be a tube in torsion that reacts through an arm on each end. The point of this is to select parameters to hit a design target (50lb/in as per the powerpoint)
    2. Reference Material Selection Guide to determine what properties you want
    3. Keep in mind you cannot select whatever diameter and wall thickness you want, there are only specific discrete options available, check out this supplier https://www.aedmotorsport.com/
    4. Example of tube sizes available
    6. Your goal is design an ARB with 5 stiffness settings (10, 20, 30, 40, 50 lb/in - Wheel rate equivalent)
  2. Size and Design Droplinks
    1. Typical Suspension links will use a tube (steel or composite) with an insert on each end. The insert fits into the tube (either welded or bonded) and has inserts that accept a rod end. The point of a rod end is to convey forces (ideally in the direction of the axis of the tube) but it allows for free rotation.
  3. After setting all the parameters of your ARB design, its time to model the components in CAD. Hopefully at this point you have finished your team-wide CAD training and practice. In case you need to reference that again, it is linked below in tools
    1. Take this opportunity to use and follow all of the team CAD practices and habits including
      1. Using a layout file for initial modelling, in the downloaded .zip, look for PF20_Sus_FrontARB_Layout.prt
      2. Saving (using Wavelink) all components to their separate .prt files with appropriate name convention
      3. You will have to model the ARB, ARB arm, Mounting for ARB to chassis, and Droplinks
      4. The necessary hardware for the assembly should be in the .zip file with the environment file
      5. Assemble those saved components into a subsystem .prt file, use proper assembly constraints
      6. If you're confused about these CAD details, be sure to reference Evan's NX tutorials again, he explains how this team uses the software.
    2. The necessary environment file for your assignment can be downloaded here. You should be able to take this package and work on the CAD independently on your computer
      1. https://drive.google.com/file/d/1sAKla6AttHyUf4kGbaZgTATsJfXran3c/view?usp=sharing

     4. Another detail to think about is how you will manufacture the components you design.


Tools to use:

  • Material Selection Guide - This article will help you make decisions for material usage when designing components- Material Selection
    • Strength (Yield Strength)
    • Stiffness (Young's modulus)
    • Environment Temperature
    • Ability to Manufacture
    • Chemical Reaction (Corrosion, response to surrounding components)
  • ARB Stiffness Calculator - Anti-Roll-Bar (ARB) Combined Stiffness Calculator
    • This spreadsheet will complete the stiffness calculations based on geometry information that you enter.
    • It will look intimidating at first, but there are only 6 or 7 parameters needed from you before it will start giving you useful information. The reason there are so many columns is so you can change a single parameter in increments and see. visually on the plot, a trend.
  • CAD Resources - CAD
  • Supplementary Youtube videos that help explain concepts:
    • Cornering Behavior
    • Function of ARB
