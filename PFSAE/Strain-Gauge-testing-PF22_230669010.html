Purdue Formula SAE : Strain Gauge testing PF22

Created by Fava, Nicola Bernardo, last modified on Aug 24, 2022

Purpose

Place Strain Gauge strategically along the undertray to measure the fractional change in length to validate 2% deflection target defined in FEA.


Strain is the amount of deformation of a body due to an applied force. More specifically, strain (ε) is a device whose electrical resistance varies in proportion to the amount of strain in the device. This change in resistance is measured using specialized conditioning electronics, and then converted to a strain value using a manufacturer-provided gage factor.

Procedure

  1. Place a strain gauge in the following locations on the underside of the undertray (according to layup zones) to obtain deflection readouts from Motec:
  2.  
    1. Near a chassis mount
    2. Center of the undertray
    3. Diffuser
    4. Sidewing
  3. Run five straight line tests at 40 mph and monitor/ record strain gauge results
