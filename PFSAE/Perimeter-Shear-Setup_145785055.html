Purdue Formula SAE : Perimeter Shear Setup

Created by Unknown User (mkotur), last modified on Apr 27, 2020

The Perimeter Shear test is a test required by the SAE rules to prove that a composite panel is equivalent to the steel tubes it replaces in a space frame. The main purpose of the Perimeter Shear test is to input the peak failure forces to obtain the shear strength of the panel. This document explains the procedures to follow for a successful Perimeter Shear test.

To begin with, the composite panel must be at minimum a 100 x 100 mm square shaped panel. It is possible to test a 500 x 500 mm panel if desired, however, that would be a waste of carbon as the same panel could be cut up into smaller panels for multiple tests. Therefore, it is recommended to make panels that are slightly over the minimum dimensions (i.e. 101 x 102 mm, for example). It is impossible to have a perfect 100 mm square panel therefore make sure that the panels are slightly larger because if they are smaller, then the judges looking at SES may choose to invalidate your test results. The panel should have bare flat edges without any excess carbon hanging over the edges, as shown in Figure 1 below.

Figure 1. A tested Perimeter Shear panel

Once your panel is ready, the next step would be to set up a time to use the MTS machine in the Civil building (HAMP G150). As of 2020, the person in charge of the lab is Robert Hershberger (rhershb@purdue.edu). Make sure you check if the same person oversees the Civil lab, if the person has changed, then update the previously mentioned name and email address. Make sure you mention the club you are working for, and the machine you want to use.

After having set up a time, you must now install the perimeter shear punch as shown in Figure 2 and place the composite panel on top of the metal support. The punch has a diameter of 1 inch and the panel is centered on the metal plate such that the hole in the metal plate aligns with the punch. The metal plate has a 32 mm diameter hole, specified by rules.


Figure 2. Perimeter Shear Setup

Once the panel is setup, set the feed rate of the MTS to a reasonable number such that the punch does not quickly crush the panel. For past tests, this number was between 2 mm/min and 3 mm/min. Once the punch goes through the bottom laminate of the panel, you will see the force vs displacement curve drop. Once that happens you may stop the test. Make sure you take a picture of the setup with a measuring tape to be used as an indicator for a scale.
