Suspension

Frank's Notes:

This is the 2nd year we've talked to Gene Lukianov for a design judge. He seems to be a very laid back person with a focus on fundamentals rather than fancy simulations. He'd much rather see that students have an understanding of fundamentals via use of FBDs and hand calculations. He didn't care much for the lap sim point sensitivities. For example, I think it would go a long way with scoring points with him if we walked through the process of using TTC data to draw and FBD of the tire contact patch, then used those moment calcs to develop tierod loads and then therefore develop steering loads. He also highly values  considerations for the ergonomic aspect of suspension such as steering and braking input forces.

Aside from the "Gene Specific" comments, it also seems like the appendix should be more thorough. For the most part, the judges believed that we did all the calculations with our fundamental structures hand calcs, but having those margins and actual spreadsheets printed out would help a lot with some of the discussions.


  • How did you incorporate driver confidence in the vehicle?
    • Talked about the tires and driver input
  • Talked about aero and integration with suspension 
  • Talked about chassis and integration with suspension
  • Elaborated on all suspension kinematics 
  • Talked about drivability and tuning, how those two effected each other
  • Wanted to understand the physics of brakes
    • Don't need "fancy simulation tools", excel will do fine
  • "Hook" oversteer w/ bump steer out in rear and bump steer in on front

Feedback from Chris (judge):

  • a really optimized frame weighs around 60 lbs so there's room for improvement (acknowledged that might have a negative impact/ not make sense with our LLTD approach)
  • suggested we don't need stress relief for the frame
  • better measurement check of frame (SUS points)
  • choose a filler rod stronger than 4130
  • want stronger correlation between ANSYS & torsion test (constrain test different to not over constrain / misrepresent rigidity)
  • need better understanding of degrees of freedom


Helen's thoughts:

  • the judge was split up between frame and ergonomics so only about 20-25 minutes was spent explaining the frame, which i think was sufficient time for the content i wanted to talk about
  • conversation was mostly was focused on our approach for a frame/ torsional rigidity and making sure we understood the impact of chassis rigidity 
  • needed a better understanding of degrees of freedom in ANSYS and how that impacts our torsion test/ leads to a stiffer TR output from the test since the chassis is over constrained 
  • didn't really talk about analysis of different load cases or lugs in the first round (if there was one judge focused just on the frame we would have had time to cover that stuff but tbh i wasn't as confident in it so i didn't want to bring it up)
  • spent a little bit of time talking about manufacturing so i think it's important to know about the welding and jigging and why we made those decisions but it wasn't the focus of the conversation
