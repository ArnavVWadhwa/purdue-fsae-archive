Purdue Formula SAE : 3. Drivetrain DCR

Created by Gurnani, Sidh Dharam, last modified by Horton, Tyler Jason on Jul 12, 2023

Project Scope

  • Goals:
    • Efficiently and reliably transfer power from the engine to drive wheels
    • Optimize system to maintain or reduce mass of overall subteam from PF23, while also ensuring safe operation
      • Investigate differences between using a spool or limited slip differential and see if it is worth switching to a spool
    • Determine the appropriate final drive ratio for best performance in dynamic events
  • System Boundaries:
    • Suspension: Physical Connection (Hubs get loaded a lot) , VD aspect
    • Powertrain: Tuning aspect
    • Chassis: Physical Connection (Tubes get loaded a lot)


Design Requirements

  • Rules
  • Known:
    • Differential carrier will have a way to adjust chain tension
    • Must be able to support the forces coming from the engine
    • Traditionally, system is designed to withstand 1.65 G maximum longitudinal acceleration and overall vehicle weight of 650 lbs
  • Unknown:
    • Final tire size
    • Max expected longitudinal acceleration with the 2024 suspension
    • First gear torque from engine (to understand what the sprocket has to be rated at)
