Purdue Formula SAE : Pedal Box Decision Making - PF20

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified by Unknown User (reyno105) on Mar 12, 2021

Influences:

  • Ergonomics:
    • Driver cockup data
    • Foot resting angle
    • Lateral restraint of foot (foot tray)
    • Motion ratios
      • Ability to modulate brake pressure
      • Enough mechanical advantage to lock up brakes
      • Throttle and cable pull motion ratio
    • Adjustability
      • Overarching concerns:
        • Weight
        • Manufacturability
          • Tolerances and warpage introduced during welding
          • How to be able to weld it together, then have the PB assembly slide fore-aft nicely
        • Strength
  • Surrounding Environment
    • Lower chassis tubes
      • Angle of tube in XY plane
        • Change in pedal height through PB travel
        • Effect on ergo
        • Driven by bulkhead height
          • Bulkhead height gives way to aero gains and losses
      • Angle of tubes in XY plane
        • Non parallel makes extremely hard to make tabs
  • Manufacture ability
    • Take advantage of the waterjet
    • Materials
      • All steel means welding
      • Aluminum means use of hardware or complex machining operations
    • Jigging
      • Previous years: Use of water jet steel plate and pressed in roll pins to constraint components in XY plane for welding
  • Validation
    • Two load cases (with different safety factor expectations) for brake pedal:
      • Rules dictated driver force input into pedal (~450 lbf)
      • Actual driver max force input (~170 lbf), taken from JP Brake test at MI Comp, 2019
      • Look at data for more specific values
    • Load case of 100 lbf of force input from driver
      • Mainly testing end-stop tab in load case
  • Other Details
    • Brake over-travel switch
      • Make sure it actually works
        • Make separate assembly file in cad with MC at max compression position
      • Product selection: Use E-stop switch, rated for wet locations (brake fluid)
        • Will work when its supposed to, won't work when its not supposed to
    • Throttle travel stop (on both sides)
      • General Design: Bolt captured by nuts on a tab
  • E-Stop Botton for Brake Overtravel
    • Use reliable Estop Button, check out IP (ingress protection) rating
    • Octopart part number 51-253.026
