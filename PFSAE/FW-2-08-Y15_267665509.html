Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-08 Y15

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 17, 2023

Simulation ML-050

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Removed endplate cutout
After reevaluating, I dont think the cutout is what made the vortex go into the first channel. This was likely the result of additional inwash generate by the new foot plate design. Thus, I have kept the foot plate design while removing the cutout. 
Reduced curvature near inner portion and increased curvature more outboard

Should result in more outwash more outboard, resulting in less wake the UT sees.
Added diveplane to end of inner section of endpalte.

This diveplane should improve the counter rotating vortex approach by increasing the endplate vortex strength while also generating an additional downashing-outwashing vortex.

CFD Analysis

Wake is practically identical to FW-2-07, which got 7 more points from UT performance.

Previous:

Lower down, the previous iteration has much better wake management. This is because the cutout allows higher energy flow to enter lower down. 

Instead of using a cut, potentially curving the endplate inwashing could provide additional room for this flow field to return without losing performance above this location.

By inhibiting the endplate vortex, the turning vane vortex gets outwashed more. 

This results in superior RW performance since the vortex gets sliced by the endplate instead of staying alive below the RW.

In fact, with better diveplane geometry, generating a counter-rotating vortex here could outwash the turning vane vortex even more.
