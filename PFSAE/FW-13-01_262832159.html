Purdue Formula SAE : FW-13-01

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 28, 2023

Simulation: CZ-006

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Suppressor airfoil

Part of the job of the squirt suppressor is to increase pressurization far under the front wing near the nosecone to reduce losses and suppress separation of front wing to bodywork junction. And the way pressurization is created, as stupid as it is, is to have stagnation on the suction side. Therefore, the pressure side leading edge is designed so that flow can stay attached even at high negative angle of attack.

To reduce lift generated by the horizontal part of the suppressor, suction side is flattened out with minimal camber.

Since the job of the suppressor is to downwash and outwash, aka changing flow direction, the trailing edge is designed to maximize flow turning, hence the high camber located so far aft. Of course the downside would be flow separation. But since I will already be running at a negative angle of attack, the suction side would not mind too much.

Main element center section angle of attack adjustment

Increased negative angle of attack on the center section to optimize stagnation point. Reduced by 4 degrees. Trailing edge is still flat to maintain a good slot gap with the nosecone.

Added tire squirt suppressor

The horizontal part that's attached to the body panel is intentionally angled so that the stagnation point is on the top surface to produce some amount of pressurization to help flow attachment in yaw. I used downwashing airfoil even though I want pressurization for the exact reason of downwash, so that undertray leading edge gains more load.

The vertical part is there to produce additional outwash to load up front wing center section more with laterally expansion, and to close down wing tip wake generated by vortex bursting for healthier flow to undertray.

The issue with a device like this is a very lossy vortex will be produced near the ground in a sensitive area. So unless that vortex can be significantly outwash, there will be net performance loss.

Partial gurney

Added gurney to near the front wing-body panel junction to reduce junction losses and improve yaw sensitivity. I don't want the gurney to go the full way to minimize losses.

CFD Results Analysis:

Conclusion: I'm small brain

CFD Picture

Description

Junction losses did reduce, but by a difference mechanism. Well, gurney flap is gurney flap, but I'm talking about squirt suppressor.

Instead of produce some amount of pressurization on top, there is almost no pressurization, despite the already pressure side separation inducing angle of attack. The suction produced increased inwash, hence boundary layer on the body panel itself was reduced.

So this is a complete disaster...The increased inwash due to suction produced by the suppressor inwashed high wake ever so slightly (although I don't really care about high wake too much). Low wake was also inwashed due to the vortex shed by the suppressor. Additional wake was also created by the upwash in the vortex interacting with boundary layer on the ground. I think they call it pick up? For the suppressor to work, it has to be moved back.

So the result is undertray got nothing except for even more wake.
