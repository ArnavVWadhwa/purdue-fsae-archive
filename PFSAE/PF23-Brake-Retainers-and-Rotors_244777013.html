Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 Brake Retainers and Rotors

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified on Nov 21, 2022

CAD Preparation

The easiest way to stay organized is to make a new NX assembly and bring in the 4 parts (FR and RR) Retainers and rotors and place them so their bases are all planar. Plan out which bolt holes (presumable 10-32 bolts, X4 each) to be used for each part. If parts are LH/RH asymmetric, place them such that they are symmetric about the X plane so CAM and CNC is easier. For PF23, the only asymmetric part was front retainers, so I places its 4 bolt holes at the 12, 3, 6, and 9 o'clock positions. Take care to align them relative to the CSYS like how it would be in the machine.


Also create new .prt files for your manufacturing parts. The purpose of this is to -

  1. Have the parts waved linked in so that it is the correct orientation for machining and at the CSYS origin
  2. For rotors, add thickness to represent stock thickness, pre-ground.

Fixturing

Make a block with the bolt holes projected down for your fixture block. Also include a 1/2-13 threaded hole in the center to hold down the stock plate and to keep the center cutout in place after the CNC contours out the inside.

As part of the fixture, you'll also need a "puck" or "cap" in the center. This will have a shoulder to locate the stock in the XY plane, and keep it bolted down. I also chose to counter bore so the 1/2 in socket head is less proud, but this is optional. Just don't crash an endmill into it.

Dimension a just has to be less than the thinnest stock you are working with so it actually clamps and doesn't bottom out.

The shoulder height should also be substantial enough to touch off with the Haimer indicater. This will ensure that throughout all the setups, the XY origin is the same. This way the holes drilled into the part stock will line up with the tapped holes in the fixture block.Â 


Stock Prep

Laser cut or waterjet discs with a 1in hole in the center for the puck. The OD should be 0.150-0.2in larger than the overall part dimensions. Just make sure to use a belt sander to knock off the edge of using laser cutter. That part gets hardened from the laser heat and destroys endmills.



CAM

The CAM is pretty straightforward. Just drill the holes first so you can bolt the 10-32s in during a tool change. Also make sure your cam has the tools clearing the bolt heads.
