Purdue Formula SAE : 2. Stress and Strain

Created by Unknown User (napolia), last modified on Apr 28, 2020

Stress

A force (F for axial, V for shear) per unit area acting on a plane. 

Normal stress (sigma): force (F) acts perpendicular to the plane. Normal stress is a stress that occurs when a member is loaded by an axial force (normal to the plane/face of cut). A normal stress will occur when a member is experiencing tension or compression. [1][8]


Shear stress (tau): force (V) acts parallel to the plane. Shear stress results when a load is applied parallel to an area. While axial normal stress is constant over the cut face, shear stress is not. At the edges of the surface, shear stress is zero. It is at a maximum in the center. We are most concerned with the maximum value and will therefore only list equations for that. [1][3][8]


Strain

A deformation per unit length of dimension in comparison to original length.

Normal strain (epsilon): stretches or compresses material. It is related to normal stress with the Young’s Modulus (E), which is a material property. 

Shear strain (gamma): changes the angle between lines within the material. It is related to shear stress by the Shear Modulus (G), which is also a material property. [1][3]
