Purdue Formula SAE : Harness: CAD

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Dec 08, 2023

Background:

General CAD of the harness serves three purposes: space claim, wire lengths, and bundle geometry. The most important of these is certainly bundle geometry, that is where the harness will split into different branches and how each connection will come off of the harness. This will later allow for splice points to be determined and will help with bundling wires in RapidHarness. Wire lengths are important for wire sizing and for knowing how big areas of the harness will be. If lengths are not taken from CAD a method such as strings must be used to determine the lengths of different areas of the car and subsequent bundles. That would be undesirable and lead to many areas being longer or shorter than desired. Space claim is less important for the harness body but serves a major role in the creation of tabs and ensuring that larger components such as the rectifier, ECU, PDM, etc. will fit where desired. While general harness CAD can certainly be overdone, time spent here will save time later on in the form of not having space conflicts, proper bundle lengths, and well thought out branching and splice points.


Basics:

Step 1: Create Points

There must be points on the ends of each wire or bundle designating the ends of the connections. This will be done in a sketch in the modeling application.


Step 2: Switch to the Electrical Application

Switch to the electrical application. This is where all routing will be done. Unfortunately, things can only be organized into feature groups in the modeling application.

Step 3: Create Ports

First go to more.


Then qualify.


Then select multi and click the little paper icon at the bottom of the tab.


Expand the tab and name the port accordingly.


Specify the origin point that you created earlier and a vector that the wires should come out of. Using a normal face for this is recommended.


Step 4: Create Spline Path

Select spline path. 


Select the end points.


Expand tab and specify bundle diameter. This does not have to be accurate but should be somewhat representative of the real size.


Adjust the spline as needed by adding more points.


Routing Considerations:

  • Ensure that wires do not splay out right at the branch point, they should follow the bundle for a little bit past it, this is to allow for proper sealing of the joint and to account for its slight inflexibility later.
