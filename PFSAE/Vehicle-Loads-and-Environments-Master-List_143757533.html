PF22 learnings - we found it only took ~30 lbs to actuate the shift which was well within the limit of the cable we purchased (70 lbs compression + 140 lbs tension) but had issues with the cable breaking. basically, just because it only took 30lbs to actuate didn't mean that our drivers weren't applying more force than that. we added hard stops so that the stops would take the excess force instead of the cable, which increased the lifetime of the cable, but still resulted in the cable breaking.

General Vehicle Lifetime: 500 milesMid-speed 30-50 mph

A general guideline for one lifetime of a PF vehicle. Envelope set based on the fact that PF22 had a few extra weeks of testing compared to the typical 1 year cycle, and upon going to the first competition the vehicle had 430 miles of driving. Biggest concern for this is rotating components (suspension gang) although should be considered for chassis as well as a general durability guideline for the team.
