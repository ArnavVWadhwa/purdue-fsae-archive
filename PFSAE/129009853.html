Purdue Formula SAE : 9/14/2019 Grissom - Hoosier R25b vs. LCO Track Testing

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified on Apr 02, 2021

Summary:

  • Skidpad times get more consistent, but stay centered about the same mean value. More throttle variation but stabler RPM with LC0.
  • There is a clear benefit in acceleration of 0.1 to 0.2 seconds. This is worth ~10 - 15 points.
  • Autocross times remain approximately the same, but far less drama in LC0 runs. Driver feeling was that R25b was maxed out while there was likely additional performance remaining in the LC0.
  • Endurance times were significantly better with the LC0 than R25b; however, driver fatigue may have played a part in this result. R25b temperatures were far below ideal while LC0 temperatures were at ideal. 
  • Tire wear fairly equivalent
