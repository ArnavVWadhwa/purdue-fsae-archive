Purdue Formula SAE : UT_1_2_JL

Created by Jonathan Robert Lee, last modified on Jan 27, 2024

Currently, the design is not great. This is probably because this is my first undertray attempt, and I barely know what I'm doing. I will work this week on getting the undertray up to at least Cl = -1 so that I have a decent undertray to experiment with.


I will fix this weird side tunnel distribution; it is probably separating.


Other highlights to fix include having a shorter intake section and moving the throat and, therefore, the pressure center of the undertray up further. Strakes also probably need to be shorter.


I do have this nice little slice of black, though. But it's hard to tell if it is under the main undertray or sidepod. Maybe I can see it in the 3D scenes better, and I will update this later; I just can't run the program from my apartment. I hope and think it is under the undertray, but the region is small widthwise, so I still need to figure that out.


Later Future:

May try to recreate and examine this graduate student's undertray (Link). It relies on venturi tunnels and x-axis vortices running throughout the undertray, similar to the Aston Martin Valkyrie. Geometry is on the left; diffuser CFD is on the right.

He claimed a ClA = -1.79 after three iterations, and we probably could add a lot more little elements to the design to push it further.

He even used NX and was nice enough to include some detailed schematics.Â However, I'll need to adapt this design considerably around the rear suspension.


Other ideas:

Having all of the side tunnel air come from under the car and outwash all of the front tire wake.


Strapping even more airfoils above the side tunnel or onto the side pods. Or, could the side tunnel even become a series of airfoils like the rear wing?

Blown diffusers?
