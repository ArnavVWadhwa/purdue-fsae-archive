Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Reduce endplate separation


To reduce the endplate separation exhibited in the first image, we can utilize a half airfoil to retain flow attachment, similar to what is done on the FW. The main goal of this is to reduce drag. This reduces the amount of dirty air being merged into the dive plane section vortex.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

The curvature on the endplate did work to reduce the flow separation on the outer portion; however, the low energy flow above the main element returned. This may be due to the extremely low pressure at the leading edge of the endplate airfoil. Reducing the amount of curvature should fix this issue. (this is why we see a slight reduction in RW performance)

Overall we reduced drag substantially by utilizing this method, reaching the highest L/D yet.
