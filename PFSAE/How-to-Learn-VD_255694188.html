Purdue Formula SAE : How to Learn VD

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Apr 13, 2023

In addition to looking through the other tabs in Vehicle Dynamics Knowledge, as well as all of confluence, laid out here is my recommended process for learning VD. Be prepared for a LOT of reading.

Step 1 - Tune to Win

This is especially a must if you have very little previous experience with cars and vehicle dynamics.

MAKE SURE YOU ARE TAKING GOOD NOTES. IT MAY TAKE 10x THE TIME BUT YOU WON'T FORGET EVERYTHING YOU READ

My notes for example:

Step 2 - RCVD

This book is dense so take your time. Try to understand the mathematics behind the things you learned in Tune to Win even if it takes you an hour to figure out a single graph.

Much of the material covered in Tune to Win is covered in RCVD just at a higher level. That is why we read Tune to Win first. Be sure to fill in any gaps in your notes with material from RCVD.

Step 3 - Simulations

Now it is time to take the basic knowledge you have gained from Tune to Win and RCVD and try to implement it through the use of MATLAB or Excel.
