Purdue Formula SAE : Ride Height Studies

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Oct 22, 2021

Ride Height

  • Reduction in ride height = tire squash + suspension travel
  • Tire squash
    • Hoosier data for tire spring rates: https://www.hoosiertire.com/contingency_rates/fsae/
    • Downforce:
      • Assuming 40 mph
      • Assuming 3.45 Cl*A
      • Downforce = 152 lb
    • Car + person: 380 + 160 = 540 lb
    • Tire squash = 0.33 in
    • So the 0.35 already built into fsaeSTAR is good
  • Suspension travel
    • 2021 Michigan endurance data
    • The pike between -0.5 and -0.25 is PROBABLY straight line
    • The other peak is PROBABALY cornering
    • The 2021 endurance track is a slow one, so I can get behind this argument
    • So we are looking at -0.2 in for cornering
  • Conclusion: at 40 mph, we are looking at 0.55 in decrease in RH. At 70 mph, we are probably looking at around 0.9 in decrease in RH.
