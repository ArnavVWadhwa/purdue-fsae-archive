Purdue Formula SAE : Throttle Pedal FEA Documentation

Created by Aaltonen, Emil Johan, last modified on Jan 11, 2023

To analyze the throttle pedal, we imported the model from NX into Spaceclaim. In Spaceclaim we added a contact patch for the drivers foot, and the bolt attached to the pedal stop. The pedal is aluminium.Â 


The load case we are considering has the pedal being pressed all the way. This means that the pedal stop can be modeled as a fixed support, as the pedal is being pressed into it. The pedal also pivots around the throttle pedal insert. This is modeled as a remote displacement in the hole that the insert goes through. Finally, the pedal is also constrained to only rotate about the insert by the springs, which is modeled as another remote displacement preventing the pedal moving side to side.


We applied a 150lbf load at the contact patch we made for the drivers foot, and a 20lbf load pulling the pedal back at the throttle cable holder.


The mesh was set to .05" for the whole part.

The main goal was to find what mass could be removed. This is generally done by cutting out slots and making the whole pedal shorter. Note that the image shown is not the final design, we managed to cut quite a bit more mass. Especially making the pedal shorter is beneficial for the pedal itself, the throttle stop, and the throttle pedal insert.
