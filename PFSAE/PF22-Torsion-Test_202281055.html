Purdue Formula SAE : PF22 Torsion Test

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified on Feb 03, 2022

The team has a couple working dial indicators in Rm6 and more can be checked out from the ME machine shop (aka talk to John) for 24 hours (prefers for it to be overnight). 

Mass of bar is 122.5 lbs IRL (ANSYS weight is 75.6 ) 

→ fixing the dimensions of the bar results in new bar mass of 125.33


The bar we use for torsion test is 119 inches long, with the pivot at 45 inches from one end. To try to ensure we started our measurements at zero loading - we added a weight of 30 lbs to the shorter end of the bar (blue bucket) to offset the extra length on the other end of the bar. We tacked the pivot block to the jig table to prevent it from moving.


To account for the additional weight added to the bar, we added a 30 lb force in ANSYS. The ANSYS simulation used is TorsionTest_Simulation_dp8_h located at Z:\2022\4_Chassis\3_Simulation\1_LLTD\LLTD & TT. The results from the test are the excel sheet named PF22_Torsion_Test at Z:\2022\4_Chassis\3_Simulation\1_LLTD. Refer to the excel sheet for the calculation on how much weight to apply and results.


Below are pictures from the torsion test for reference. Pieces of the torsion test stand used might still be in 6. Refer to LINKED PAGE for info on designing the test stands (Klaveano, Alexander Ryan can you link/make a page about the test stand design & manufacturing).


White bucket was used for adding weight to twist the chassis and measure displacement.

Blue bucket of scrap hardware that was used to counter act the extra weight of the bar.

We tacked the pivot to the table to prevent is from moving.

Note the blue bucket on the left and the white bucket on the right.

Locations of the dial indicators - we had four on tabs in the chassis that were near nodes and one on the bar.

Closer look at some of the mounts that were used - back was fixed to the table while front was fixed to the bar.
