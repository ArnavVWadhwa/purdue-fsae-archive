Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-04

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 11, 2023

Simulation ML-046

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

Added additional inner strake.

Altered 3rd inner strake to not contain a swept strake. 

Back to operation make this inner vortex not burst. 

Adding another strake should continue to alleviate some of the vortex bursting by continually moving from channel to channel. 

The 2nd element to 3rd element slot gap produces some substantial wake that bursts the vortex, so I'm pushing the strakes to before the slot gap.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

No longer bursting inboard; however, the 2nd inner strake now bursts due to the excessive inwash from the gurney near the end of the sweep. 

I think the goal is to just make the bursted section as outboard as possible, ideally eliminate the bursted section, but if it needs to exist at least it's farther outboard. 

Pretty considerable separation on the downwind side, need to increase slot gap here.

This iteration shows that the outwashing section is ingesting some of the dirty flow, whereas the bottom is not. The bottom also hardly breaks down at all. This is ultimately the goal. Once the strake orientation is found for the bottom case, we should be in the 4.00s in Y15 while maintaining 4.9 in Y0.
