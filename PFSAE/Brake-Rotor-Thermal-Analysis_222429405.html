Purdue Formula SAE : Brake Rotor Thermal Analysis

Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory, last modified on Jun 15, 2022

Home: Model lives in Purdue-FSAE Github, ask Kurisko, Keith Gregory  or Unknown User (agarw162) for access


Model:

  • The only source of heat input into the system is heat flux generated from pressing the pads against the rotor surface
    • Calculated with basic energy considerations and assumptions
      • Vehicle of mass M (kg)
      • Initial Velocity of Vi (m/s) before brakes applied
      • constant deceleration of a (m/s^2) until final velocity
      • Final velocity of Vf (m/s)
      • Del t (s) time under braking
      • ASSUME: All Kinetic energy of vehicle is converted to heat
        • Rotational and Translational
      • Front Rotors take ~65% of braking capacity due to longitudinal load transfer (Approximate value)
      • Added Effects that will reduce kinetic energy that the brakes must dissipate are:
        • Aerodynamic Drag
  • The main source of heat output or loss is through convection
    • Brake disc and vehicle travel with such high speed and rotational velocity, most of the heat transfer is through convection and the remaining is radiation
      • ASSUME: 90% convection


Control Volume:

1-rotor:

in: energy from deceleration

out: energy removed from system via radiation and convection

Assumptions:

Radiation: 

Convection: rotor spinning in place, calculate Reynold's number from this → get nusselt number → get heat convection coefficient *Note: Included in cycle model but not in validation model

Free Convection (used in validation model as vehicle was stopped)

During braking, drag from aero takes energy away from rotors
