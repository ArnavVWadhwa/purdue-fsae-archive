OMAX Path Creation

  • Move DXF file so that the bottom left of the file is aligned with the bottom left of the waterjet plate
    • The best way is to select all and use the move tool
      (hotkeys: S+W, to select all within a window, and then M to move from its initial location to its new location. Selected lines appear in yellow.)
    • After the stock is correctly placed, make sure to deselect all lines (use D+W to deselect within the window). Different colored lines correspond to different types of cuts.
      The cut qualities and their colors are found in the quality tab on the bottom, or by using hotkey Q.


  • Select all paths to be cut and assign them a quality. Most often will use quality 3.
  • Add lead in and lead out from holes. To do this, select or use hotkey V. Apply leads to inside of holes, making sure that they appear on the bottom of the hole, with the "V" facing up. Repeat for all holes.
  • Create tabs to prevent pieces from falling through the gaps on the table and to prevent movement of the pieces. To do this, right-click on Lead Vo and select create tab (or use shortcut "Ctrl+T"). 
    • Under tab setup, use gap and leg length of 0.05 and select none for tip quality.
       
    • Select a convenient location for tabs. Flat sides are best. For larger pieces, use more than one tab, on opposing ends of the piece to make sure it does not shift or fall through.


  • Now start the cut path from the bottom left of the table and pick the most logical path that would take the least amount of time to cut. To do this select line (hotkey L). Quality should be set to traverse automatically (green line or quality "Traverse"). Lines should go from terminal to terminal on the leads and the tabs.
  • Preferrably, set up the path to cut all holes inside a part first before cutting the outer circumference. 
  • After each piece that is pathed, check that each cut is on the correct side of the piece (inside for things that will fall through and outside of the pieces that are kept).


    • To do this, select on the right side, and select the starting point at the bottom left of the piece.
    • The thick red line represents the diameter of the water. The pink line represents the lines of the piece. The outer edges of the pieces should be red so that the waterjet does not cut into the piece, while inner holes and pieces that fall through should be red on the inner edge so that the outer radii of the holes are correct.


      • If the path is on the wrong side, switch the order that the path goes into the terminals. This will switch which side of the piece the water will cut.

Guidelines for choosing tab orientation

At the most basic level, there are 4 options for tab placement on a part: top, bottom, left and right. 

  • 4 different tab placement (image is for demonstration purposes, realistically only 1-2 tabs should be needed for a small part, if at all):

The orientation of tab placement is important because it affects how the piece is cut (outer cut or inner cut). Depending on the situation, certain orientations might be preferred over others. Below is a short guide to the pathing of each tab placement to ensure an outer cut. 

  • Tab below a piece: Lead in from the left, lead out from the right
  • Tab on the left of a piece: Lead in from the top, lead out from the bottom
  • Tab on top of a piece: Lead in from the right, lead out from the left
  • Tab on the right of a piece: Lead in from the bottom, lead out from the top

When the opposite pathing direction is used in any of the above scenarios, the result would be an inner cut. The general rule to ensure an outer cut for any orientation is to lead in from the tab on the "left-side" and lead out of the tab on the "right-side", when viewed from the perspective where tabs are pointing towards us. 

When having more than 1 tab on a piece, make sure to connect all loose tabs except for the one that leads in and out of the piece.

Auto-path 

In both ProtoMAX LAYOUT and OMAX, there is an option called "Auto-path", which does all the hard work for us.

When clicked, all the pathing will be automatically added, including leads and tabs.

However, there are limitations to this tool, such as excessive tabs placement for large pieces, lack of extended tabs (which could cause bending of piece during removal from sheet), errors in hole leads (holes are cut on the outer side), etc. Therefore, always make sure to check all pathing even after using auto-path and fix any errors, or simply do everything manually. 

To better optimize auto-path options, open the configuration tab for auto-path by right-click on the icon and select "AutoPath (Advanced & Configure)"
