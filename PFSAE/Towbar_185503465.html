Purdue Formula SAE : Towbar

Created by Unknown User (amattu), last modified by Panchal, Aarnav Nilesh on Dec 05, 2021

Goals:



Towbar Details: CAD Designation PF22_POW_6400_1_UprightTowbar

  • Lugged Joints on the uprights
    • NAS Hardware in single shear to make machining uprights easier (less operations)
  • Asymmetric Towbar Braces
    • Braces fit onto uprights in such a way that they are angled the same, only difference will be length of brace members to account for differences in Upright sizes
  • Cross Brace between Lug Connections and Towbar Braces
    • Thrown into assembly as a failsafe to account for adverse load cases due to human error (someone drops the car onto the towbar sideways)

Manufacturing:

  • Lug Connections
    • Added into Upright and Upleft assembly alongside the rest of the CNC Operations, basically added an outer profile done with a 5/8 end mill, to get the fillet in between the upright and the lug we used a 1/4 ball end mill
  • Towbar Braces
    • Waterjet out of .125 in 6061 T6 Al
  • Cross Brace
    • Waterjet out of .063 in 6061 T6 Al
  • Assembly:
    • Cross Brace will fit into the Towbar braces with waterjet cutouts and be welded together from the outside face of the Towbar Braces
    • Once finished the Towbar Braces will be welded onto the Towbar Tube
    • Once cooled an angle grinder or similar tool will be used to check the lower 180 degree rules in VE.2.1.2.f of the FSAE Rulebook
  • Post-Production Processes
    • Will be sent to be anodized orange as per FSAE Rulebook rule V.E.2.1.2.c
