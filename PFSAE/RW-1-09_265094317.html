Purdue Formula SAE : RW-1-09

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Jun 27, 2023

Simulation ML-010

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Fourth element changed to a pressure recovery airfoil.

The final two airfoils are primarily producing drag with the pressure generated, thus using an airfoil that's main purpose is pressure recovery will reduce drag and improve DF generation of the main two elements.
Diveplane introduced.

Generate some additional downforce while also powering up the endplate tip vortex. Powering up the endplate tip vortex results in additional upwash which is useful for that section of the RW.
Increased chord for 2nd element, decreased chord of 3rd element.

Chord increased for 2nd element for higher surface area where DF can be generated.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

The considerable flow sep at the endplate would benefit from Chenkai's endplate design. This is another reason why the drag increased. The issue is there is simply no endplate there.

Diveplane does not work as well as it used to since we have a smooth transition at the leading edge of the endplate. The vortex is barely generated and separates too early. Would likely do better in yaw 15. Substantial drag gain from the diveplane, probably not a good idea unless we need it for yaw 15.
