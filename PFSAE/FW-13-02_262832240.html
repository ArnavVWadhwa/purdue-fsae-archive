Purdue Formula SAE : FW-13-02

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 29, 2023

Simulation: CZ-007

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

New squirt suppressor

Okay bare with me...I know this is getting stupid. The idea of this additional winglet is to produce a vortex that's locally outwashing low wake. Sure mid and high wake will get inwashed, but they are mid and high wake. Additionally, the downwash created here should load up undertray leading edge, although I don't know if the lift gain is can be canceled out by downforce gain in undertray.

Fully expecting the loss to be ingested into the undertray (especially since the vortex is upwashing inboard, so ground boundary layer is not going to be great) but I just want to see if this works. If it does, plan is to make another counter rotating vortex up top with an inwashing vertical vane, so that the counter rotating pair will outwash each other. But yeah, in short, not expecting great performance right now, just want to do it as a proof of concept.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

As expected, low wake got outwashed more and mid to high wake got inwashed. The additional outwash loaded up inner side tunnel more, leading to improved UT performance.

The additional downwash also improved sidepod top surface attachment, leading to improved rear wing performance.

Improved energy delivery to the rear wing.

The vortex generated by the suppressor smashes into the pushrod and dies, which is not a problem since I don't know what else I would do with it. Suspension will definitely change so this feature probably won't exist on the new car.

UT leading edge got a reduction in pressurization, leading to improved local load.
