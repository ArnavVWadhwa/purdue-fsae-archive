Frame

Engine Mounting

Tool access and overall serviceability for cooling + water pump + exhaust manifold area is shit

Mounting engine to tabs is shit

Engine location + frame dimensions + system packaging

Tabs get bent and warped over time, also hard to line them up 

More time taken for assembly and disassembly of affected systems (cooling, exhaust manifold, water pump)

Consider shifting engine location backwards to clear space for affected systems


Frame Jig Towers Did not use hole tool (used project curve instead, and...)

Time and effort spent replacing towbar (angle grind, rewelding, etc.)

Delay and loss of testing time

Conduct more thorough reviews of CAD and analysis?

Toe tray

Used rivets for toe tray assembly

Oversight in design, underestimated driver forces and miscalculated rivet connection strengths

Unquantifiable effects to driver control and response throughout roughly 1-month testing periodUsed nuts and bolts instead of rivets

Pneumatics

Air compressor

Air compressor hose was punctured without having spares → inability to pressurize tank to full (required) psiSuspected that repeated positioning of the hose at tight angles placed excessive stress on puncture area, leading to failure

Inability to run full endurance (switching drivers without needing tank recompression) for 1 testing weekend

Expenditures for replacement and spare hoses


Temporary: 

  • taped hose with 950psi rated tape → didn't work (obv)
  • bent steel tube and welded to fittings → worked up to 2500psi with severely limited set-up

Permanent(?):

  • ordered spare lines online → works fine
  • ordered (dubious) back-up hand pump rated to 4500psi 
  • Use large air tanks (like the ones used for welder) instead of air compressor?


Pneumatics 

Pressure lines

Line expanded over duration of testing, leading to eventual blowout

Underestimated magnitude of thermal radiation from engine 

Overlooked possibility of hot oil leaking from nearby oil tank, causing damage to exposed lines

Possibly induced unexpected stress to related systems (engine, transmissions, etc)

Delayed testing schedule and reduced testing time

Possible reason for last-minute "engine rebuild"

Insulated all lines within engine proximity with insulating sleeves.


Pneumatics

Working pressure regulator

Cross-threaded a regulator, while the other spare regulator stopped working halfway through testing season

Negligence during part assembly

Lack of understanding of regulator setup

Blewout 3 valve - tank lines due to regulator not working

Set team back in terms of testing time (happened mid-way through testing weekend)

Note that when pressurizing tank from fully empty state (0 psi), release the regulator set screw and wait until tank is pressurized to desired capacity before adjusting output pressure via regulator set screw


Pneumatics

Air tank

Pneumatics tank had insufficient capacity for full endurance

Shifting frequency and volume was higher than expected

Projections made based on mechanical shifting tendencies were inapplicable for pneumatic shifting

Inability to run full endurance during testing (without refill)

Ordered upsized tank for competition

Need to redefine shift frequency range for future iterations

FloorpanFloorpan crushed at bolted locationsProbably due to excessive torque of bolts → bent washers → crushed floorpan

Potential lack of stability and constraint on driver's seat and related components (fuel tank, battery, ecu, pdm, etc.)

Waterjet new floorpan, bonded to aluminum inserts (which will get bolted down to tabs)


Floorpan No clearance between parts



Seat

Mounting

Bolted connection between seat and floorpan was widening hole in floorpan, as well as firewall

Repeated stress on bolted joints was too much 

Compliance in seat, affecting driver safety and performance

3D printed standoffs that are bonded to the floorpan and follow seat contours to act as mounting location.

Aluminum plates bonded to both sides of firewall to be used for bolted 


Seat

Serviceability

Using threaded bolts to mount the seat


The assembly and disassembly process (serviceability) was severely limited due to nuts being located between the bottom of floorplan and the top of the undertray (tight area to maneuver)

Assembly and disassembly times were too long, and viewed negatively by design judges

Consider using pins for mounting instead of nuts and bolts for quick disassembly (reference steering wheel quick disconnect)


Seat

Seat belt slots

Harness slots in the seat were not well wrapped and remained too sharp, resulting in damage to seat belts (tear)

Placement of harness slots was also possible rule violation (as per rule... in the 2023 rule book)

Did not consider rules when cutting slots

Didn't wrap slots with sufficient masking tape/cover

Might have not passed tech (due to a tear in the seat belt)

Cost of possibly needing new seat belts

Watch out for seat harness rules and sharp edges when cutting slots


Seat harnessDelayed ordering of seat harness Later in the season (which is likely around January), they are usually out of stock Possible disqualification (?)Order seat harness earlier in the season (around Nov-Dec?)
