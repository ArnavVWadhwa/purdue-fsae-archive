Purdue Formula SAE : Full Custom Steering Rack Timeline | PF24

Created by Singhal, Ria, last modified on Dec 21, 2023

steering rack went through hell and back this design season. this page details everything that went down over the course of the entire design cycle.


DCR: 7/25/23

  • custom steering rack was first proposed here

DCR 2: 8/9/23

  • presented initial research and decided custom steering rack was worth pursuing
    • in hindsight, this should've been a bigger deal/discussion

PDR: 9/16/23

  • presented initial cad
  • got feedback to switch spline attachment to pinned adapter

CDR: 10/9/23

  • all fea and design done
  • no rack concerns raised

IDR: 10/14/23

  • got feedback to not have aluminum-on-aluminum contact
  • redesigned rack for MRR with 3D printed locating inserts

MRR: 10/22/23

  • no manufacturing or welding concerns raised

steering design freeze: 10/22/23

  • steering was one of the first subsystems to freeze
  • design froze right after MRR

team-wide design freeze: 10/29/23

  • steering was cleared to build
    • clearly should not have been

baja expresses concern for 3D printing bushings: 11/22/23 (over thanksgiving break)

  • the only locating features for the rack shaft at this point were 5 3D printed inserts
    • this was the major issue
  • other issues were identified:
    • sealing
    • maintenance
    • anti-rotation
    • manufacturability and assembly of entire rack
  • housing required a full redesign

zach, ria, and aarnav come up with new rack design: 11/26/23

  • see this page (Post Design Freeze Design Changes | Steering Rack PF24) for details
  • ground-up redesign plan
  • aarnav and ria become co-owners of rack housing

rack housing full redesign: 11/27/23-12/3/23

  • new hand calcs
  • new bolted connection
  • delrin machined seals
  • new tolerancing for pinion, bearings, wave spring stack
  • nothing is welded anymore

CDR 2: 12/4/23

  • LOTS of concerns raised
    • sealing
      • delrin vs wiper seals
    • linear bearings?
    • anti-rotation
      • is it worth doing
      • is there a good way to implement this
    • effects of manufacturing tolerances on gear interface
      • how do you tolerance a spur gear
    • manufacturability of middle housing
    • aluminum rack deflection from 20 deg pressure angle
      • should the rack be steel?
    • tolerancing of rack locating features
    • manufacturing timeline
    • how to measure if gears are in tolerance
    • how to test interfacing between gears
    • how to perform maintenance
      • lubrication choices
        • red lithium
        • oil
      • does the whole thing need to be detached every time

custom steering rack gets killed (rip): 12/4/23

  • following a detailed discussion with carson and aarnav at panda express right after the second cdr, the decision was made to no longer pursue the custom rack later that night  
  • there was no time to properly fix everything wrong with the rack
    • would be a 100% redesign of everything including gears, contact forces, and all calcs
  • meeting ano deadline would be nearly impossible even if design was done and perfect
  • moment of silence for the custom steering rack

everything u-joint down is redone in CAD with NARRco rack: 12/7/23

  • travel concerns
    • with NARRco rack, had to confirm travel was doable
    • new hard stops will limit the travel
  • mounting was redone
    • tab fea was redone
    • tab got slimmer
    • gussets got bigger
    • towers had to update with new hardware
    • clevises were updated
  • rack cover can stay as it is
  • steering pot will need to be redesigned

NARRco rack purchased: 12/10/23

  • bought two 17.4 in 4.0 rack speed NARRco racks
  • new lock-to-lock is around 118 deg each way
  • tab jig has to be remade


lessons learned:

  • all design reviews were essentially pointless
    • people that needed to be there weren't there
    • people that were there were probably tired from having multiple reviews back to back in the same day
    • the design should've gotten ROASTED very early on
  • realistically, we shouldn't have pursued this
    • lack of background research
      • seals
      • forces with varying manufacturing tolerances with gears
      • tolerances, slips, fits
      • manufacturing feasibility
      • maintenance
      • lubrication
    • lack of modelling
      • new gear modelling software should help 
    • making a steering rack is much harder than anyone thought
  • given that it was our first time doing this, the design should've been 100% replaceable with the NARRco rack even after manufacturing and assembly
  • CAD parameterization and organization is very important


improvements for next year:

  • new systems need more oversight by people who know what's going on
    • experts (faculty, sponsors, alumni)
      • would be hype if we could compile a network of alumni that are experts in specific things to contact with questions throughout design instead of just at IDR
    • senior members
    • baja
  • actual justification for design instead of just winging it
  • RAISE ANY AND ALL CONCERNS EARLY
  • we need education on better CAD practices
    • parameterization
    • user expressions
    • interpart referencing
    • organization (layouts, wave links, top-down modelling)
  • we also need better education on component design fundamentals early in design cycle
    • dfm/dfa
    • tolerancing
    • types of fits


recovery and moving forward:

  • it was good that we did not change rack speed too much so NARRco rack could be substituted in without anything getting too messed up
    • travel can be compensated for
  • talks about manufacturing the rack design when peak manufacturing season dies down
  • do we want to full send custom steering rack properly for next year
    • pros
      • could actually work with sus to customize parameters
        • make rack as small as possible
      • comp design event? idk how this works
    • cons
      • is there a point? 
      • backup plan?
      • can we actually make a rack that's better than the NARRco rack?
