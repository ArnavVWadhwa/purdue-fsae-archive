Appendix Slide Used

  • Interest in pneumatics
    • Paddles, 2-button clutch
    • 4 solenoids
  • Order of button pressing?
    • Research pneumatics
  •  Q: Fixed air volume? Sizing? FS?
    • A: Determined using the lap sim and average shifts using mechanical shifter → Found tank was too small because drivers shift more with pneumatics
  • Q: What if there is a leak in the system?
    • A: Warning systems at 1000-1200 psi and no shifts remaining
      • Q: Mechanical backup?
        • A: Not on the car but one was designed alongside pneumatics
  • Q: Example of integration between subteams?
    • A: WSS gap testing and integration into upright
      • Q: Calibration or validation of sensor?
        • A: The dyno results were much better than advertised → Radial distance failed much closer to advertised than axial
      • Q: What about thermal expansion of the corner assembly/rotor teeth?
        • A: Sensor rating checked against temperature stickers placed on each component
      • Q: Test under braking to ensure sensor function?
        • A: There was no noise in data collected indicating that there were no problems
      • Q: How many test days with WSS?
        • Know total testing time/distance
    • A: Designed chassis with other teams for minimum viable tunability
      • Q: Is rules minimum chassis stiff enough? Which tubes were removed? When cutting how did you backtrack after removing a necessary tube?
        • A: The testing was destructive so once a necessary tube was cut the test was over
    • Prepare many examples of system-system interaction
  • Dash Overview
    • Q: What is inside?
      • A: BB adjuster, dash easy to take off, not many connectors
        • Q: Space issues? Dash cover? → Dash autosport connector messy
  • Harness Overview
    • Q: How was it made?
      • A: Concentric twisting
    • Q: Floating mass? → Connector mounts are a point of failure especially when hanging as well as mass (Did not like connectors)
  • Components
    • Q: Does body cover GPS? Why is it mounted so close to the frame? → Radiation or absorption? Issues?
    • Q: What type of antenna? Split pole? What frequency?
      • Know antenna design + all components
    • Q: What is the box on the front right side panel?
      • A: Timing system → cutout window for timing beacons
    • Q: Sampling rate for WSS and linear pots?
      • A: Not Adjustable/Standard → Know actual sampling rate
        • Q: Concerns with too high a sampling rate?
          • A: Know aliasing
    • Q: What is ethernet for?
      • A: ECU
  • Q: Anything Cool?
    • A: Autoshifting, traction control, launch control
  • Q: Charging system? What battery?
    • A: Lithium-ion
      • Q: Rectifier output?
        • A: Current validation slide
      • Q: Safeties in place for lithium-ion?
      • Q: What style of lithium-ion?
      • Q: Difference between stock and new battery? → Lithium-ion batteries will keep taking current (whereas other batteries discharge electrons by gas...) → Can be used to measure energy absorption through weight of the battery
        • A: Output
  • Q: What does the rectifier do?
    • AC → DC
  • Q: Anything Else?
    • A: Harness design (Component levels 1 - 4 used to decide can bus priority)
      • Q: CAN TPS (Twists per inch)?
        • A: 2
      • Q: How is the harness grounded? Why is it set up like that?
      • Q: What if a shielded wire is grounded on both ends?
        • A: Looses shielding effect → Becomes antenna
  • Noise
    • Q: Why does high power create noise?
      • A: AC outputs create noise because of EMF
        • Q: Do you have sensitive signals next to high power?
          • A: Yes, but they are all shielded
    • Q: How can noise be mitigated?
      • A: Route sensitive signals around high power lines or use more shield
    • Q: Are there any disturbances in the power supply?
      • A: Yes, in i2 the ECU battery voltage can be seen fluctuating at a low frequency
        • Q: Why does it fluctuate? What are the effects?
          • A: It does not matter because the ECU controls voltage and ground
    • Q: Is there any caused by the antenna? → Sometimes high drawers create fluctuations that are hard to deal with, capacitors could be used to filter out low-frequency changes
      • A: EMF
        • Q: How would you size the capacitors?
          • A: They would be sized according to the frequency of fluctuation
    • Q: How does wire shielding prevent noise?
      • A: EMF that is generated drains to the ground of the shield (Faraday cage)
        • Q: What about noise on the ground?
    • Q: Where is the shielded wire?
      • A: They are spliced together and then grounded on the engine
  • Q: For engine mapping what sensor is used to determine engine load?
    • A: MAP
      • Q: What is the settling frequency?
  • Q: What data logging system do you use?
    • A: None
  • Q: What sensors do you use?
    • A: Sensor list... tire temperature sensor desired but too expensive
  • Q: Can I see some data?
    • Q: Explain the longitudinal/lateral variating in the GG diagram
  • Q: What else DAQ? PDM?
  • Q: Why no closed-loop lambda sensor?
    • A: On the dyno, the lambda aim is followed well, there is not enough compensation time on the short tracks for closed loop
    • A: In a closed loop if lambda fails the engine will run overly rich
  • Battery 
    • Q: Battery type?
      • A: Antigravity...
    •  Q: Where is it located?
      • A: Left side of the car, behind the firewall
    •  Q: How is it sized?
      • A: Able to complete a full endurance without charge (no alternator)
    •  Q: How is the battery mounted
      • 2 straps
    • Q: How would you troubleshoot not over-discharging the battery? 
      • A: Charging port for battery near master switch
      • A: The battery has a heat shield and temperature sticker (BMS will fight itself if one side is hotter than the other)
    • Q: What are the largest loads on the vehicle's electrical system?
      • A: Crank, FPump, Fan (Not always on)

    • A: One main pass-through
