Purdue Formula SAE : Poly V6-3 Viscosity Limiter a1=0.35

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Apr 21, 2023

Background

Viscosity limiter what makes SST SST. It's a term applied to eddy viscosity as calculated by the blending of K-e and K-w turbulence model. As the name suggests, it limits the magnitude of eddy viscosity so that separation characteristics can be better predicted. This term is relatively easy and safe to play around with because it is not associated with any PDEs solved by the turbulence model. a1 is the coefficient in the viscosity limiter that determines the activation point and magnitude of activation. The higher the value, the less activation, and the higher the viscosity in activated zones.

Results

Front wing (as shown below) has a slight reduction in separation. The reduction in separation is occurs despite the increase in wall shear stress, and that is due to the assumptions made in the SST model.

Rear wing is where the magic really happened. Both flaps correlate much better with wind tunnel. Lower flap separation is completely eliminated compared apart from regions near the endplate. If anything, endplate induced separation is too small now. Upper flap still doesn't correlate like lower flap does, but there is an improvement where flow separation decreased.Â 

There is an error in this in terms of wind tunnel vs. on track boundary condition. However, for rear wing, this error should be negligible. Another error would be freestream, I mistakenly ran these at 40 mph instead of 60 mph. While 60 mph does tend to provide better flow attachment, the difference is small.
