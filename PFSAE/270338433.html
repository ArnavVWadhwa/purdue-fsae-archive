Purdue Formula SAE : Pressurizing the air tank | Pneumatics

Created by Dien Quang Nguyen, last modified on Aug 28, 2023

POC: Dien Quang Nguyen 

Description: This is a guide to refilling the pneumatic air tank while it is mounted on the car and connected to the entire pneumatics system

Refer to the Air Compressor Operation Manual for details on maintenance.


Step 1: Turn off the pneumatics valve

  • Check the red ON-OFF Valve to ensure it is switched to "Exhaust state". This is indicated by the "EXH". 

When the Valve has been set to Exhaust, you can begin the pressurizing process


Step 2: Setting up the air compressor accessories

The air compressor that the team is using operates using a water-cooled mechanism. This means that a water tub and water pump must be setup to be able to use the air compressor, or it will overheat, and it might blow up.

Here are descriptions and instructions for setting up each component of this system:

Water tub/bucket:

  • Fill a tub/bucket with at least 20 L / 5 gallon of (non-distilled) water → This should be at least half of "the pneumatics tub"
  • This is the water that will be circulated through the air compressor for cooling 
  • It is probably better to use distilled water to prevent cavity buildup within the air compressor, but this is not fully understood and we also did get the air compressor from ebay so idk if its even worth it.

Water pump:

  • Stick this to the walls of the water tub/bucket 
  • Make sure it is fully submerged in the water at all times

Water pipe:

  • Connect one water pipe from the water pump to the air compressor and one pipe from the air compressor to the water tub (you can leave it floating in the water tub, just make sure it remains submerged when the air compressor is running)


Depending on where you are pressurizing the tank, there are a few precautions and requirements. Below I have outlined my preferred setup for pressurizing in the Formula shop (which is being renovated as of August 2023) and at Grissom AFB.

Important:

  • Connect the water pump directly to the power outlet 
  • Use an extension cord to connect the air compressor to the power outlet
  • Operating the air compressor and water pump at the same time draws a significant amount of current, do not connect them both into the same extension cord or the fuse/breaker will be triggered midway through

  • The hose connecting the air compressor to the air tank is rather short, so you might need to place the air compressor on wooden blocks or a simp stool (more on this in the Grissom setup)

Important:

  • As with the shop setup, you need to connect the water pump and air compressor to different extension cords
  • Usually, there should be a green, long extension cord (in the packout) and a yellow outlet extension cord that is connected to the trailer. Use the green cord for the air compressor and the yellow outlet for the water pump.
  • Below is an example of what not to do
  • Place the air compressor on a simp stool (or wooden blocks), positioned at the gap in the undertray, and the water tub/bucket close to the air compressor. The goal is to get the air hose within range of the air tank
  • Again, make sure the water pipe coming out of the air compressor is submerged into the water tub/bucket at all times



Step 3: Connect the air compressor to the air tank

To pressurize the tank, the air compressor must be connected to the tank. This is done through a connection from the air compressor outlet to the tank inlet.

(Air inlet picture of the real tank inserted here)


  • You might (probably) need to loosen the tension bolts on the clamp that is securing the air tank to the chassis
  • It is a pain to access these bolts with the diff and chain in place. Below is the setup that I found to be very effective at accessing this location.

  • FYI, the tension bolt used for mounting the air tank in PF23 is 7/16"


  • Once the bolts on the air tank mounts are loose, you should be able to turn the tank around and access the inlet
  • Connect the air compressor to the air tank using the hose quick disconnect. 
    • Watch this video if you don't know how to do this → Quick Disconnects - Hydraulic Hose Connectors - YouTube
    • Make sure it is securely connected (you should be able to feel the "click")


Step 4: Before sending it

  • Before turning the air compressor on, check that:
    • the voltage shown on the name plate of the air compressor matches with the power supply
    • oil level is at the middle place of the oil glass
    • quick connect fitting is properly placed
    • water is being circulated through the water pipe (the pump should be plugged in by now). You should see water coming out of the pipe that has one side connected to the air compressor and the other submerged in the tank.
      • If, at any point, water stops circulating, turn off the air compressor immediately and double check the power sources/fuse. Only run the air compressor with the cooling system operating  
    • Both the thumb screws on the output (red) and input (green) side are loose


Step 5: Sending it

Turn on the air compressor. 

It will start to vibrate strongly and make loud sounds. This is completely normal.

Once you feel the vibration and hear the sounds, wait for 10 seconds before tightening the output thumb screw. Then another 5 seconds before tightening the input thumbscrew. Now pressure is building in the tank. You should see the dial turning on the pressure gauge.

Now all you have to do is continue monitoring the pressure gauge, water pipe and temperature of water in the tub (ideally with a thermometer, but feeling by touch should be sufficient).

When you feel the water getting "too warm", stop the air compressor, loosen first the output thumb screw, then the input thumbscrew to release the built up pressure and let it rest until the water cools down.

Usually it takes 2-3 "rounds" of compressing to reach 4000psi, and I strongly advise to not attempt compressing the tank to 4000psi in one go, that is 0-1500 psi → 1500-3000 psi → 3000-4000 psi

Repeat the above steps until you reach 4000 psi or the desired pressure. Note that this air compressor is only rated for 4500 psi.


Step 6: Clean up

Once you have finished pressurizing the air tank:

  • disconnect the quick connect from the air tank
  • unplug the air compressor and water pump
  • disconnect the end of the water pipe connected to the water pump
  • drain all water from the water pipe connected to the air compressor
  • put the air compressor in a safe place 
  • drain the water (if in the shop) or move the water bucket/tub elsewhere (if at grissom)
  • position the air tank into its original location and put the tension bolts back on
  • once you have ensured the air tank is tightly and securely mounted with proper clearance to the drivetrain chain, you are good to go
