Goals for PF24

  1. Build a powerful and reliable powertrain package
  2. Knowledge transfer
    1. Want to get everyone more knowledge and hands on experience
    2. Lost a lot of knowledge with everyone who graduated this past year

Goal 1:

The peripherals will likely look similar to the ones from last year, we will just improve on them with the knowledge we gained during the testing and competition season. 

Goal 2:

  • First step is going through Confluence on your own
    • Powertrain pages but others as well
      • Huge amount of info on POW design, and design in general
  • I'm working on a YouTube playlist of good informational videos I have found
    • Most of them have nothing to do with FSAE, but rather the powertrain fundamentals
  • Myself and other senior members will be making lecture style videos that will live on Confluence
  • Have you guys in the shop getting used to working on the car

Plan for PF24:

Intake:

  • PF23's worked well
    • Slight modifications to increase volume for boring out the block
  • Look into adding bell mouth on the intake (to satisfy design judges??)

Exhaust:

  • Worked okay
    • Make sure we understand the design decisions that were made
    • Modify for larger displacement
    • Allow for more room for cooling routing
    • Make sure manufacturing gets done better
    • Look into 4-2-1?
    • Stick with titanium

Fuel:

  • Worked okay for PF23
    • Need to design correctly the first time
      • Be extra careful because we will need more gas for E85
      • Also consider fuel consumption during rain driving (more because more tire spin)
      • Make easier to work on
      • Can be designed to avoid making bends with the aluminum
    • Consider bigger injectors for E85
      • Want to stay in the linear range of the injectors
        • Hit over 60% duty cycle (during chassis dyno day)

Oil:

  • Worked well on PF23
    • Need to run the stock oil cooler for sure
      • Dropped oil temps by about 30C
    • Need to modify the dry sump tank so de-aeration is better
      • Bigger vent line on tank will help (soda bottle)

Cooling:

  • Worked well on PF23
    • Look into reducing mass of system for PF24
      • Smaller fan
      • Smaller radiator
    • Maybe consider -16AN lines for portions of the routing

Drivetrain:

  • Worked well on PF23
    • Make sure welded straight to avoid failing a rodend again
  • Continue with diff tuning
    • Spool manufacturing
      • Some people think it would be nice to try at Pittsburgh

Engine:

  • No turbo
    • Not sure its worth it at the moment
      • Will add weight and complexity
      • Still think there is room to grow while still remaining NA
  • Modify engine internals
    • Bore block
    • Pistons
    • Connecting rods
    • Head studs
    • Head gasket
    • Port/deport head
    • Cams
  • Need to build a spare engine for comp
    • Work on an engine parts inventory
  • Improve engine model (GT)
  • Look into upgrading the internal oil pump
  • Look into switching to iridium spark plugs (cooler)
