Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 Design Process

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified on Dec 04, 2022

Frame PDR and CDR presentations are saved on the drive: Z:\2023\4_Chassis\2_Frame

The CDR doesn't include side tubes, my final VIP presentation details updated analysis and the final design.


General Process

  • define objectives and goals (what new design components would improve last years design?)
  • "research & development" - support design decisions (PF23 Ergo Study & PF20 Frame Sensitivity Testing)
  • work with suspension to edit suspension points (main change was straight FRH to eliminate tube)
  • play connect the dots & follow to rulebook
  • analyze frame for operating load cases & set up torsion test - PF23 Frame Analysis (& Other Frame Peripherals)
  • analysis on PF23 frame design to tie back to PF20 Frame Sensitivity Testing

Considerations for next year

  • remove FBH cross tube - requires running custom IA test but could be worth the weight loss (just need to decide if the time is worth it)
  • fully understand what impact the frame sees during a front impact - the purpose of the IA is to absorb energy so would the frame itself ever see enough of an impact to need the nSIS tubes? or can we loose weight
  • work with all subteams to mass down on tabs - tab thickness across the car could likely be reduced but not everyone fully analyzes their tabs & reduce their mass
  • re-examine bulkhead raise - did we gain the expected benefit in aero performance? or can we lower it and eliminate the tubes needed to support the bent tubes
  • does the top of the MRH need to be the minimum bend radius?Â 
  • different option for cantilevered beam for rocker in the rear?
