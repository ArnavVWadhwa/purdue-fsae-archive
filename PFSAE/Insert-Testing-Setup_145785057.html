Purdue Formula SAE : Insert Testing Setup

Created by Unknown User (mkotur), last modified on Apr 28, 2020

The insert test is not required by SAE rules; however, it is useful to validate the FEA models created. The main purpose of the insert test is to make sure the designed inserts can carry the desired loads that are applied on the composite panel from the tabs, without any failure occurring. This document explains the procedures to follow for a successful Insert test. Note that these are preliminary tests and in the future the testing method may change for inserts.

To begin with, it was decided to make a 3-point bending and Perimeter shear sample with the specified dimensions in their respectful documents and place an insert in the center of each panel. To secure the insert into the panel, surround it with film adhesive before placing it into the core material. Afterwards, put a pin inside the panel to prevent any resin from getting stuck inside the insert hole. The insert size will depend on the material used, the load applied, and (if applicable) the minimum required surface area that the insert has on the monocoque (SAE rules). Figure 1 and 2 represent a perimeter shear and 3-point bending insert setup.

Figure 1. Perimeter Shear Insert Panel

Figure 2. 3-Point Bending Insert Panel

As it is shown on Figures 1 and 2, the placement of the panel and the support fixtures are the same as for the original perimeter shear and 3-point bending samples. The main difference lies with the load applicator. In this case, a custom-made fixture was designed such that it could fit inside the “placement holder” and be held in place with a pin, as shown in the Figures. Then, an internally threaded section would be able to have a bolt screwed inside to connect the fixture to the panel. Finally, many washers were stacked on both ends of the panel such that the panel was tightened. Once the panel is tightened, the load applicator can start moving downwards at a feed rate of approximately 2.5 mm/min. When the force vs displacement curve suddenly drops, the sample has failed. The objective of this test would be to make sure that the panel fails before the insert does at the specified load. Once an accurate model has been developed, it may be unnecessary to test the panel to failure and rather test the panel up to the desired load to avoid using many test samples. Furthermore, the insert load applicator should be remade with a uniform diameter rather than a step as during the perimeter shear test, the load applicator started buckling, as shown in Figure 3 below.

Figure 3. Insert Fixture Buckling

The buckling of the load applicator can be prevented by increasing the smaller diameter and making sure that the screw in fastened all the way through.
