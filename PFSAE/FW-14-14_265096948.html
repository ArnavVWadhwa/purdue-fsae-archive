Justification

Got rid of vortex channel

Okay this is more like an experiment. I just have a hard time believing vortex channel actually help with reducing inwash, with the vortex locally inwash. Vortex channel is supposed to work by pressurization the outboard ground, which make sense, but I can't help but think this overpowered by inwash created by the vortex itself. The pressurization is near unnoticeable. So I just want to dive deeper into the mechanism behind the vortex channel setup.Â 

On another note, I think this will have performance improvements from the reduction in wake feeding the wing.

Running this in yaw 0 because it's quicker.

CFD Results Analysis Yaw 0

CFD Picture

Description

Inwash

Pressure

Left is inwash with vortex channel and right is inwash without vortex channel. From the rough looks of it vortex channel does reduce overall inwash. Though there is more inwash closer to the vortex core, it dies down near the ground, which, if the pressurization theory is true, make sense. Just to make sure, I created a value that linearly relates to inwash mass flow but not quite (because I'm lazy) in star, and the result is the flat plate has 4.4% more inwash.

Another factor here that supports the pressurization theory is that the inwash extends further outboard without the vortex channel. It's next to unnoticeable, which makes sense since the pressurization difference is minimal.

After a lot of thinking, I still am not convinced. I don't think this tiny of pressurization change is 4.4% change inwash. I keep coming back to my pressure gradient idea that is apparently wrong. I don't know what to think really.

The four vortices are hereby named 1, 2, 3, and 4.

Since the flat plate is more lossy early on, vortex 2 and 3 are weaker since they get a lot more wake fed into them compared to VC setup, but 4 was able to stay much stronger and less losses since all the losses hits 4 once 1 breaks down in VC setup. This is not an issue in flat plate setup. This resulted in sizable FW and UT gains.
