Questionable Designs (from the FSAE facebook)

This is a huge list of all the things that two particular design judges posted as bad examples. While the mentality that of X design is always bad in all cases is not legitimate, most of the designs should be avoided. If there is not time to do adequate analysis, then definitely avoid things like rod ends in bending, mounting suspension to to non-nodes, rod ends attached in single shear, etc. But if the analysis time is there and the best design has on of these things, but is properly analyzed, go for it. We run cantilevered ARB in the front and the change in spring rate from its bending has been accounted for. We also mount the diff to the middle of a tube. The tab calculations, beam bending calculations, and FEA breakout model of the section indicate that it is strong enough, so it is then an acceptable design.
