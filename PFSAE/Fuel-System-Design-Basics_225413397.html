Purdue Formula SAE : Fuel System Design Basics

Created by Unknown User (dnocon), last modified on Jun 20, 2022

Use this page for general tips. Keep detailed info to design notebooks.

The information here is very high level, and for high level education purposes only.

Design:

  • OVERSIZE THE TANK
    • you aren't saving too much weight... give it like a 1.4-1.5x factor of safety. PF22 had a 1.4FS on 93 and had a 1.1 FS if the team ever decided to switch to E85
  • Consider welding
    • minimize amount of welding required by altering the geometry of the tank to use extrusion
      • see PF22 fuel tank vs a PF20 style tank
    • Oversize welded joints to overlap by at least 0.125in, this way you do not need to use filler when welding (just melt the overlap) makes welding 5x easier
  • When choosing fuel lines it will likely be between -6an and -4an. You can do the pressure loss math for either, but for a FSAE car it is unlikely to make much of a difference. 
    • -6an ultrapro was used for PF22 fuel and oil systems (there was only 2 different size lines on PF22: -6an and -3an (brakes))
    • standardizing fittings makes your life way easier, you don't need to carry more spare materials if you don't need to. You can also buy extras and the cost wont be that bad.
    • PF22 was standardized to only -6an straights and 90s
  • Baffling
    • weve never done any analysis on this but add some sort of baffle to the tank, especially if the shape is susceptible to slosh
    • can just be a cover of some sort inside the tank, above the hydramat
  • Connecting to the hydramat
    • weve tried to manufacture our own 37degree flared tubes to adapt to -an / 37 degree JIC style fittings but these have always leaked. We have the flaring tool for 37 and 45 degree styler fittings if you want to reattempt.
