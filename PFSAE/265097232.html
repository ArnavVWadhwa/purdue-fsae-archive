Project Scope (Present problem statement and stakeholders):

Problem Statement:

  • Manual pulling of data takes time away from the testing season.

Stakeholders:

  • Powertrain
    • Engine sensors/injectors/coils and engine control systems
    • Fuel/Coolant/Oil temperature sensors
    • Manifold/Fuel/Oil pressure sensors
    • Lambda sensor
  • Suspension
    • Wheel speed sensors
    • Tire pressure sensors
  • Chassis
    • Air tank pressure sensor
    • Steering position sensor
    • Mounting + Space
  • Aero
    • Ride height sensors
    • Pitot sensor
  • Electrical
    • Global position sensor
    • Button for logging
    • Dash for ^


Design Requirements (Present known requirements, ie. Vehicle Performance, rules, and define unknown requirements):

  • Goals
    • Could have switch/button on the dash to pull data
      • And/or indicator on the dash
    • Mounting should be low
  • Requirements
    • Must be securely placed on the car
      • Case should be aroundÂ  5 x 5 x 2 inches, snug fit for the Mini PC while allowing room for the backup power source and ethernet splitter
      • Device shouldn't shake
    • Maximize testing time
      • Should be less than 60 seconds to pull data
      • Less than 2 minutes to access data at the end of the day
    • Minimize weight (minimal effect on performance when removed)
      • Less than 2 lbs
    • Must be inexpensive compared to MoTeC alternative
      • Less than $200
    • Must give an indication that data has been logged
      • Light on button will serve as the indicator
    • Must option of manual data pull
      • Switching back and forth between wireless and manual
      • Takes less than 60 seconds to switch between pulling methods
    • Does not require an external power source
    • Should be completely removable
    • Must be easily accessible


Design Overview (Rough concepts for solution + Trade study with a recommended path forward):

  • Option 1:
    • A button with built-in LED will be installed on the dash
      • The button will be connected to the external GPIO board that is connected to the Mini PC
    • Using Python
      • Read the button input and pull data from the ECU
    • The ECU will be connected to the Mini PC using ethernet
    • Extra hole in the case of the Mini PC for the other end of the ethernet splitter, to keep the option of a direct ECU to laptop data pull available
    • Mini PC will have a backup power source - 1 12V battery or 8x AA batteries
      • The primary power source is the car as the PC only draws 6W and needs 12V to power
  • Option 2:
    • Make macros for normal laptop pulling
  • Option 3:
    • Wireless router on car

    • If 3D print ~ $2
