Purdue Formula SAE : RW-2-00

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Jun 28, 2023

Simulation ML-011

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
For this new series of RW, I am testing the three-element version of Chenkai's design. 

The main element is for downforce, then it transitions to airfoils that excel at pressure recovery, where the 2nd element is altered to be a middle ground between pressure recovery and downforce generation, and the final element is primarily for pressure recovery to reduce the low-pressure region that primarily produces drag due to the AoA.

I'm a bit worried about the endplate not having enough area to prevent flow separation on the lower surface of the final element, something to look into when looking at the results.

2nd element may have too much pressure recovery and should potentially look more like a normal airfoil. If the pressure recovery is too much here this is something to alter.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

Despite reducing drag by 7pts, this RW has significant separation. Going to increase the slot gap, and potentially look into AoA changes too.

Pressure distribution is decent, would like the main element to be generating more DF. Going to try a more downforce producing airfoil for the 2nd element.

This separation at the endplate has been a consistent thing, I'm going to switch to Chenkai's endplate design to attempt to fix this.
