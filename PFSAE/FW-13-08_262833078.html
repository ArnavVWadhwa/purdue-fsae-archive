Purdue Formula SAE : FW-13-08

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 31, 2023

Simulation: CZ-013

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Cutout geometry change

Made the cutout into a slot. Technically, this will shed three vortices instead of two, but that's good. The second vortex is going to be quite weak and probably negligible. The main reason for this change is so that there is still material present to block inwash of outer vortex wake.
Twisted and flapped outwashing winglet

Changed bottom airfoil shape to a symmetric airfoil to reduce downforce loss due to mounting of the winglet.

Reduce bottom chord to further reduce downforce loss.

Added a flap to increase vortex strength from this device to suppress tire wake and reduce rear wing upwash more.

Move the whole setup inboard to place the vortex closer to the chassis of the car so it's more effective in downwashing rear wing flow. Although the additional outwash produced by the flap is probably going to offset the physical distance moved.

The vortex interaction between the bottom and the top co rotating ortex pairs is going to be quite complicated so I am not exactly sure what is going to happen. The guess is they are converge in vertical distance due to the difference in upwash caused by different distance to front wing flaps. The two pairs should then outwash each other. The bottom vortex pair should get closer to the tires, so who knows what's going to happen if it gets too close. 

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

Vortex generated did not get stronger. The flap itself did very little.

More front wing downforce with the twist. 

The lower vortex migrated to next to the top vortex downstream, which made them upwash each other, resulting in increased height at rear wing.

The inner vortex ended with more loss since the cutout itself increased inwash, this inwash is then met with a wall, creating a big chunk of loss and immediate breakup of the vortex.
