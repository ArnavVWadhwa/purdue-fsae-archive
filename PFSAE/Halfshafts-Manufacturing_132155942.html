Purdue Formula SAE : Halfshafts Manufacturing

Created by Unknown User (dewey2), last modified by Unknown User (amattu) on Feb 13, 2023

Before halfshaft manufacturing can begin, the drivetrain (minimum uprights + differential + inboard CV housings) must be mounted on the car along with both rear corners mounted on the car.

The main steps to "make" the halfshafts are:

  • Use halfshaft jig to determine overall halfshaft length
    • Be sure to check wheel travel 2 inches upwards (because suspension is unloaded, it is at lowest position, and the suspension has 2 inches of travel)
    • Adjust jig length such that CV tripods cannot fall out at any position
    • Lock jig with jam nuts to measure center length of jig (see picture)
    • Unlock jig, remove from car, and extend jig back to length measured on the car
    • Measure overall length of jig
    • This length is what the halfshaft should be cut to
  • Cut halfshaft to length
    • Take an even amount off of both sides
    • The carbide chop saw that Baja has can be used to cut them. Alternatively, use a lathe with carbide bit to continually face the end of the halfshafts until spec is reached. I (Adarsh) took off 25 thou per pass
  • Machine snap ring grooves

      • Relevant dimensions for grooving. Note 1/8" lip from the end of the halfshaft and the inner diameter of the groove
      • DO NOT GROOVE PAST THE SPLINED REGION – the halfshaft will likely fail in time due to the introduction of a stress concentration
    • The grooves can be done on the lathe, using the proper width HSS tool or with a dremel attachment on the lathe. Alternatively, they can be done by hand, but is not recommended.
