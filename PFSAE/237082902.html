Purdue Formula SAE : Why is Matt LaMear's UT Less Sensitive to Front Tire Squirt/Wake

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Oct 09, 2022

Treatment of Front Tire Wake (Chenkai's on the left, Matt's on the right):

Chenkai's UT has centralized inlet that starts very early at the center, and attempts to push out tire wake. But if there is tire wake forms too far outboard, the outwash does not have enough strength to push all of the wake away. This results in tire wake being inwashed into the UT through the side tunnels, which leads to higher pressure in the diffuser due to the reduction in vorticity.

Matt's UT, on the other hand, wait for tire wake to mostly dissipate before attempting to use the high energy airflow on the side tunnels. This turns out to be a more robust way of handling unknown tire wake geometries.

Treatment of Upwind Side Side Tunnel

Due the inlet geometry of Chenkai's UT, the wake form on the upwind-side side tunnel is even lower-energy then tire wake, this make the upwind side completely unusable.

Matt's UT treats the upwind-side much more kindly. Even though it eats a lot of tire wake, the tire wake is higher energy. This means the error in one tire's tire wake affects the overall downforce less.

Direct Comparison

A front wing change that pressurized downwind side front tire caused an increase in tire wake volume - the same effect as the one unintentionally (and mistakenly) caused by chassis change.

Under the new flow field with Matt's undertray, we only lost 0.04 Cl overall. UT only lost 0.09, and the decrease in upwash in the side tunnel is responsible for rear wing gaining 0.05.

Whereas Chenkai's UT caused a loss of 0.35 Cl overall. 0.25 from UT, 0.05 from RW, and the rest come from a less ideal chassis.
