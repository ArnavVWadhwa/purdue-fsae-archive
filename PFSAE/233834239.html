Purdue Formula SAE : Radiator Movement (fsaeSTAR V6.2)

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Feb 08, 2023
  • Export both radiators and fans together as a parasolid. Make sure they are 4 distinct bodies.
  • Import the parasolid into STAR. Uncheck simplify assembly structure. Do NOT change the name of anything
  • Open up a 3D scene
  • Open up Assembly 1, body 1, then surfaces. Right click on ColoredFace1 and choose split by patch
  • If the body is a fan, split the surface into "Fan Inlet" and "Fan Outlet" appropriately (capitalization matters). The name of the remaining faces does not matter
  • If the body is a radiator, split the surface into "Inlet" and "Outlet" appropriately (capitalization matters). The name of the remaining faces does not matter
  • Click on File → Macro → Play Macro, and choose RadiatorMove.java under src → random. The macro will take care of everything else for you.
  • Make sure the macro did its correctly by checking
    • The existence of CFD_RADIATOR, CFD_DUAL_RADIATOR, CFD_FAN, and CFD_DUAL_FAN
      • Anything with DUAL in it should be on the right hand side of the car
    • Assembly 1 is gone
    • The y-axis of the radiator coordinate systems is normal to the inlet of the appropriate radiator
