Mechanical Shifter

In case pneumatics are not functioning as intended, the mechanical shifter will serve as a backup to shifting gears.

Rule limitations:







Criteria and Constraints:

Criteria:

    • Up and down shift without driver having to pull in clutch
    • Up shift by pulling, down shift by pushing
    • Design a new handle system that would only require the driver to use the shifter handle
    • Clutch should be actuated only on the downshift of the shifter handle
    • Make a system that is shifts more smoothly, more ergonomic, reliable, and durable than PF20

Constraints:

    • Room in cockpit, Chassis Templates
    • Allow for adjustability for driver preference and to find the best force required setting
    • Driver Ergonomics

    • Auto shift engages clutch only on downshift and there must be an option for driver to manual hold in clutch to either shift or activate launch
      • On auto downshift clutch must be engaged before full angular displacement of shifter at WR450 end

    • MUST MUST Interface with Current tabs and setup with Welded Frame and hardware

Idea Generation:



Technical Stuff:

To actuate downshift, cable location must be at height where

Motion Ratio: I set the ratio by setting clutch linear displacement to .6-.8 as this ensures the clutch is disengaged before the shifter is actuated


Testing:

  • Gathered Data:
    • Force to engage Clutch 157N
    • Clutch Linear Actuation: 0.4in for sprocket to start to rotate freely, 1in for clutch to be fully disengaged
    • Shifter Torque: 42 lb-in
  • Shifter torque was measured using a luggage scale which was wrapped around the dirt-bike shifter and pulled until the force required to engage was reached
  • Using Christian's strain gauge device the for to engage the clutch was determined
  • Using the clutch cable I determined the linear actuation
    • First I marked the cable at rest
    • Pulled the cable until the sprocket rotated freely and marked again (dist is .4in)
    • Pulled cable all the way in an marked (dist is 1in)


Design:

Motion Ratios:

  • Developed the ratio by ensuring the Clutch is disengaged first before shifter is engaged
  • I did this by setting the shifter linear displacement of  0.541" to a clutch target of 0.6" displacement
    • In theory the clutch will be able to freely rotate when it reaches 0.4" and then shift after, ensuring the clutch is disengaged when shifted
  • Motion ratios can be adjusted as the shifter cable will be designed to attach at different points along the shifter if need be
