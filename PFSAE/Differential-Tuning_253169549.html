Tunable variables:

  • Preload
  • Ramp Angles
  • Number of active clutch interfaces

Preload

Preload determines the minimum torque difference required across the differential to "open" it up.

Too low preload will result in a quicker transition to an open diff, resulting in inside tire wheelspin.

Too high preload will keep the axle locked longer.

PowerLow PreloadHigh Preload
Low SpeedMore understeerMore oversteer
High SpeedBetter turning/More oversteerMore understeer


CoastLow PreloadHigh Preload
High SpeedMore oversteerMore understeer

Varying shim thickness can have an effect on internal preload via Belleville washers.

Try reducing preload/shim thickness until inside tire wheelspin is noticeable.

Thicker shim = higher preload.


Ramp Angles

40/50 is stock, shallower is quicker lock, steeper is slower lock



Active clutch interfaces

Less active clutch interfaces softens the locking in the diff, leading to a larger potential difference in inside/outside wheel speeds. This can potentially improve forward traction at the cost of more understeer. However, when heavy throttle is applied, the diff will lock and move towards oversteer.
