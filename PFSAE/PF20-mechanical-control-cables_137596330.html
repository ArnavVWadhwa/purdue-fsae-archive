Purdue Formula SAE : PF20 mechanical control cables

Created by Unknown User (jpeerbol), last modified by Rumsey, Helen Nicole on Feb 17, 2022

This page includes all the information you need to make your own control cables, specifically for PF20 the clutch and throttle cables.

All the parts to make the cables can be found at Venhill USA.

The diameters of these cables are not guaranteed to be perfect, but they have been tested for at least a season of testing we not found to be undersized, if you want to take the risk you can try smaller cables to save weight or whatever.

Outer cable conduit is less likely to cause you an issue, so as a rule of thumb I like to have one spare length of outer conduit to make a new cable and 2 spare lengths of inner cable in stock at all times.

Cable ends are cheap, have a few spares, they can save you if you need to try and rebuild a cable.

Note: cable lube is not needed for these cables because they are teflon lined conduits.

I know I have preached that having spares are important and they are, but  if your cable is breaking you probably are abusing your cable and need to rethink your design so that is easier on the cable.

Make sure to have at minimum enough cable to make a spare cable, it is guaranteed that your most important cable will break the day before comp and getting cable parts shipped quickly can be difficult. Make you life easier and purchase spare parts.

Clutch Cable Parts

2.0mm cable 1x19 twist: https://www.venhillusa.com/venhill-cable-inner-wire-2mm-1x19-twist.html , go with the galvanized cable, DO NOT get the stainless, it requires a special flux to bond the solder to


Cable Outer Conduit Housing with Teflon Liner - 6mm OD https://www.venhillusa.com/venhill-cable-outer-conduit-housing-with-teflon-liner-6mm.html

For PF20 the clutch cable outer needs to be about 9 feet long, the inner cable will be slightly longer than that so 10 feet will be the estimate for the inner cable.

Engine Side Cable End: Trumpet Nipple - 3.5mm-6.3mm OD x 5.1mm H (2.2mm Wire Bore) https://www.venhillusa.com/venhill-trumpet-nipple-3-5mm-6-3mm-od-5-1mm-height.html

Clutch Handle Side Barrel: Barrel Nipple - 6mm OD x 9.5mm H (2.1mm Wire Bore) https://www.venhillusa.com/venhill-barrel-nipple-6mm-od-9-5mm-height.html

Cable Adjuster 8mm x 1.25 Pitch Threads 25mm OAL https://www.venhillusa.com/venhill-cable-adjuster-8mm-125-25.html

Back up incase you need more cable adjustment https://www.venhillusa.com/venhill-cable-adjuster-8mm-125-42.html

Cable Ferrule https://www.venhillusa.com/venhill-cable-ferrule-for-6mm-conduit-6-35mm-od-nose.html this is perfect for 6mm OD conduit, 2mm cable, and the two cable adjusters spec'd above

Lock nut 8mmx1.25 from McMaster 96276A210, standard nuts have not been an issue nylocks could be experimented with there are issues with the nuts backing off over time from vibration. These thin nuts allow for more adjustment than a typical nut or nylock.

How to make cables

Find THE black box in the project cabinet that has all the materials (bird cage, cables, solder, ect.). Plug in the solder to start getting it heated up.

Measure the correct length cable and housing for your application (note they won't be the same length) - probably nice the record the length that you did so if you need to make another one quickly it won't be too big of a hassle to get the lengths again

Add the correct barrels and ferrules and adjusters. (make sure you do this before you go and bend and solder the wires) (also make sure you're choosing the correct size barrel - the two ends may not match)

**insert image here of example of shifter**

Use the bird cage tool to get the wire looking nice (video on this page about how to use it https://www.venhillusa.com/cable-inner-wire-bird-caging-tool-vt13.html) - might take some practice to get an appropriate size bird cage (you still want it to fit into the barrel nipple **gag**) 

Dip the barrel nipple **gag** into the flux, which should be in THE black box (go just past the top of the barrel nipple **gag**) and then dip it into the solder for 10-15 seconds (kind of go until the flux stops burning off).

The end is going to be pretty hot so just let it hand there for a little bit and don't burn yourself. Try not to move it around too much while the solder is still hardening (accidentally flipped it over once and the bird cage slipped out of the barrel nipple **gag**)

Do the same thing on the other end of the wire.
