Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 Dyno Intake Design

Created by Unknown User (kinsellj) on Jul 31, 2022

The design of this will follow preliminary CAD work done back in 2021 for varying the plenum volume and runner length of the PF20 Intake. CAD for plenum spacers, lower intake runner subs and the plenum bottom section has already been done, but needs to have CAD validated, before manufacturing takes place. All CAD files are located for now under PF23 → CAD → POW → 2000_Intake → Dyno_CAD

Runners:

Runner CAD is already complete with models printed back in the spring of 2021 (one of which I still have). The purpose of these is to turn the intake "vertical" (see picture). The CAD model for this is being kept the same for now, but may change depending on what injectors we use for testing, or the angle we have to clock the engine to get it to fit on the WR450F dyno. Everything on the intake side will be 3d printed, with the runner stubs being made from PLA on a hobby printer. The lower section of the plenum is larger and cannot fit on a standard home 3d printer, thus XOMETRY will be used to print this on the cheap (< 200 bucks). It will be made from SLS nylon, which will help since it has mega overhangs. 

The design includes a shelf for tubing to rest. The OD and ID needs to be set based on availability and wall thickness of runner tubing. Right now we have a diameter of 1.5" as per the PF20 intake. The thinnest wall thickness tubing that can be found on McMaster is 1/4", thus the cad will be updated to accommodate this. Final shelf OD is 1.78 to accommodate the +/- 0.02" thickness tolerance.

McMaster Part File Link: Multipurpose 6061 Aluminum Round Tube, 1/8" Wall Thickness, 1-3/4" OD | McMaster-Carr

Lower Plenum Section:

Shelf design for this is the same as the runner. The lower plenum geometry is the same as PF20. Only other major change is a added port at the bottom of the plenum for an optional tap to connect to the fuel pressure regulator (design same as PF22).

Plenum Spacers:

Pretty self explanatory. We will make these with Xometry if we see the need to. Only issue is bolt clearance on the smallest spacer (far left). We can have the nuts on the insider part of it, or order longer 10-32 bolts to go clean through both spacer flanges. Just something to keep in mind.
