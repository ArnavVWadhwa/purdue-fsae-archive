Purdue Formula SAE : SW/UT Parameter Sweeps

Created by Bermack, Christopher Richard, last modified on Jan 11, 2021

SW Flap Gurney

  • this might be worth looking into, I'd be interested to see what happens with gurney flaps on a hybrid wing/tunnel
  • extrudes on upper flap: sweep 0.25 inch increments from 0 to1 inches
  • look at downforce (Cl) numbers and lift efficiency (Cl/Cd)

SW Slot Gap

  • don't do this; I've decided on a hybrid design, so slot gaps are irrelevant

SW Endplate Gurney

  • probably a new member SW design project
  • extrudes on SW endplates: sweep 0.5 inch increments from 0 to 2.5 inches
  • keep gurney + flap span constant; increase in gurney length corresponds with decrease in flap span
  • look at downforce (Cl) numbers and lift efficiency (Cl/Cd)

SW Endplate Devices

  • likely another new member SW design project
  • research canards, louvres, vortex generators, other endplate devices
  • feel free to go kinda crazy with this and see if we can get some performance improvements
  • look to other FSAE teams' designs to see if you can find something cool
  • look at downforce (Cl) numbers and lift efficiency (Cl/Cd)
  • goal to increase downforce while maintaining SW lift efficiency
  • also look for any significant changes in RW performance

UT Strake / UT Length

  • don't do this yet; still nailing down UT design, so strakes might be irrelevant
  • varying number of strakes under UT, varying length of UT profile

FW / UT Intake Distance

  • collaborate with Chase and the FW gang to do this
  • varying distance from rear of FW to front of UT profile
  • don't want UT to be too close since pressure fields from FW might interfere

Sidepod Inlet/Outlet

  • I will do this; there is room to optimize mass flow for radiators, seeing if we can run 9x6
  • varying inlet and outlet for sidepod, looking primarily at mass flow and SW drag numbers
