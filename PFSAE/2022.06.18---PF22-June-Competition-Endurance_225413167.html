Looking at the variance graph the most time JP made on Dom was at the end of the first passing zone,Â  JP's braking event is later and shorter, you can see a large difference in vehicle speed. It looks like dom understeered at the start of the turn? JP did get into 4th gear, but he did have similar lap times without getting into 4th gear. Where JP felt like he could make up the most time on the course was the entrance of passing zone 3 into that braking section. JP was flat through that entrance.


JP's notes:

Passing Zone 3 felt like it made the biggest difference and was the scariest section, when I could hold it flat through the entrance I felt like I was able to use what the car had to offer. Every time I held that flat I thought for sure I was going to smack the inside cone, confidence in the aero stability and strength was necessary to fully attack that section.

Comparing my best and second best, 0.5 sec difference

The slaloms felt nearly perfect, focusing on nailing the entry speed and making a perfect sin wave with the steering and keeping the tires attached felt like the best way to use the slaloms. At the end of passing zone 3 braking slightly earlier, but not as strong and coming off later felt like the best way to brake. It gave me just enough more time to gauge my speed. Mastering the corning speed is more important than hard braking (the braking is still hard, but the mental processing is on the speed, not just focused on braking hard.)
