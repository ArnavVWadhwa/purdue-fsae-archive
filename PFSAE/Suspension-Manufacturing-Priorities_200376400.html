Purdue Formula SAE : Suspension Manufacturing Priorities

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified by MacNab, Zachary Robert on Jun 01, 2022

This page contains some of the justification for the priorities and order of components manufactured for PF22 during Fall 2021. Basically Frank's two cents on why he did things in the order he did.

See also PF22 Parts Burndown (Suspension)

General rules of thumb and considerations

  • Powdercoat and anodize deadline
  • Machine shop and John and more busy near the end of semester, work on stuff that he needs to be more involved with earlier (think CNC lathe)
  • What can be saved for other members that don't require John's attention? Traffic cones were likely last on my list as someone else could pretty easily take them over.
  • Rocker and Control arm components had to be done relatively early for welding and powdercoat. These parts are good to have ready to machine - ie stock and drawing ready. Every once in a while, something comes up and you cant run CNC (John is out sick, has a meeting, etc) so just have that ready so you can still take advantage of the time.

Chronological Order of Big Ticket Machining Items (Names are clickable hyperlinks to the manufacturing guide)

  1. Camber Pieces - Just getting back from summer, hadn't touched a mill or lathe for several months. Camber Pieces was a fairly simple part to start off with, involved a bit of everything: flycutting, drilling, milling, some weird part setups, and a touch of CNC
  2. Wheel Centers (PF22) - Relatively simple CNC work, required CNC lathe so wanted to get done early.
  3. Rear Hubs - Once again, required CNC Lathe so wanted to get done sooner. More complex that front so did this first.
  4. Front Hubs - Naturally follows Rear Hubs
  5. Rear Upright - All CNC work, not trivial, but relatively easy to do when the ME shop gets a bit busier. More complex than front so attacked this first.
  6. Front Upright - Naturally follows Rear Upright.
  7. Hub Nuts - Simple Manual Lathe work that could be done without much input from John. This is also pretty much the last of the components that need anodizing.
  8. Brake Discs - Want to get this done with some time to spare for assembly as it needs to be sent out for surface grinding.
  9. Brake Carrier
