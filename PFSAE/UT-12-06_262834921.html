Description of the Change

Justification

Reverted outwashing winglet change and sidepod inlet change

It didn't work
Vortex channel transition

Keeping the vortex alive for the entire length is hard, so not doing it. Instead, downwashing curved gurney is used to leverage the inwash and create a little bit more additional loading.

Yes, this does mean a little bit more adverse pressure gradient for the vortex, but the tradeoff here I think is worth it.

Side wings everywhere

Vortex in the outer side tunnels also don't survive. So instead of getting them to survive, just use wings instead. There should be more than enough inwash in this region to feed all of the flaps.

The worry is the junction on the top flap where angle of attack transition. Depends on the precise amount of local inwash there, there may or may not be separation across it. The the vortex structure will change accordingly. If there is no separation, vortex should be downwashing for rear wing, so that's good. If it separates, there is no vortex structure.

The main concern with side wings, as it always has been, is the amount of upwash it creates might be too much for rear wing.
