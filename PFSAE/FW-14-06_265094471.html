Purdue Formula SAE : FW-14-06

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Jun 26, 2023

Simulation: CZ-035

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

No more outwashing winglet

I don't see how I can make the outwashing winglets yaw insensitive due to the 15 deg angle that it sees, so I'm removing them. There will probably be a small hit to UT and RW, but it's not gigantic.
Indented inner strake

This should reduce flow separation in yaw. When the inner strake leading edge was in front of outer strake leading edge, it sees high inwash angle. But if it's behind, flow already got turn a little by the outer strake, so inner strake separation should reduce. This does mean outer strake now will see a little bit more inwash, but I think the trade off is worth is since the inner strake is still supporting the outer strake downstream.
Unconnected flaps and nosecone

This is a way to solve downwind side chassis separation. This basically allows high energy flow to be injected into the region that would separate. This mean a smaller span for the flaps, so downforce will reduce from that, at least in yaw 0, but the section is dominated by junction losses anyways, this won't be a big loss.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

Issue fixed, also lost too much downforce. The entire front wing lost suction for some reason. Although I don't think this accounts for everything, but it is contributing to it.

Inner strake still separating. But the wake does propagate inwash less now, which is contributing to undertray performance.

Rear wing is separating here for no reason at all. Either this sim is wrong or last sim is wrong. This separation contributed to the whole wing losing suction.

Looking at the wake profile and the downwash profile, there should be higher suction in the upwind side if there is no separation.
