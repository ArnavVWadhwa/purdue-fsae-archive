Purdue Formula SAE : FW-14-03

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Jun 18, 2023

Simulation: CZ-027

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Reduce outer ride height to 2 in and adjusted fourth element chord and angle of attack to maintain maximum loading in front of the front tires.

Need more tip loading for outwash and downforce in general.

This change also made the middle dip unnecessary, further reducing inwash compared to the last iteration.

Center section reprofile

Kept center airfoil from FW-14-02 with ride height of FW-14-01 to keep so centerloading for downforce. Plus, the losses going into the undertray really isn't enough for it to be a concern.

To keep losses low, FW-14-01 second element and top element chord and angle of attack are used. With changes in main element geometry, this does mean reduced surface area and reduced load. But this is probably worth it.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

Better than FW-14-02 but still worse than FW-14-01, although there really isn't a change in UT performance, though that can just be CFD since the improvement in flow field is small.

Despite the decrease in flap root loading, the losses didn't really reduce.
