Strain gauges:

Strain gauges are EXTREMELY sensitive to wire lengths! By wire, I mean the connection between the strain gauges themselves and the wheatstone bridge. Makes sense if you think about it

~15cm wire: 2V output

~45cm wire: 3.8V output

^^^ These are eyeballed measurements but show wire lengths are important!!! KEEP WIRE LENGTHS CONSISTENT FOR ALL STRAIN GAUGES!!!!!!!! Mount SGAs on secure mounting points to minimize wire play

Lessons:

  • Don't underestimate the cost of electronic components in the BOM, prices can change from year to year
  • Time management is important, make sure to be using time effectively (staying up all night can be good but be careful)
  • PCB - Consider the size of components to make sure they scale with the size of the board so you don't have resistors larger than op amps
  • Strain gauges take a hell of a lot more time than they would seem
  • The ideal PCB stackup for low noise is signal gnd gnd signal
  • Be careful when wavelinking parts in cad - it is very easy to mess up coordinate systems which might require a new part or a lot of time to fix
  • The detail of electronics CAD is not the end of the end of the world
    • A lot of time will be wasted with people making changes if it is continually made nice and up to date
    • All that matters with it is spaceclaim and wire lengths which might change during manufacturing anyways
    • Ie. connectors, routing, etc.
  • The schedule is completely offset from everything else (about a week)
  • When learning M1 build make sure to take it slow and actually learn and not cheat through it
  • Make sure your daq can support your sampling with the inputs it has
    • Real time sampling vs non real time operating system (myrio is real)
      • Off computer clock vs itself
  • Confluence is very nice and f around and find out
  • Excel columns can auto adjust if you double click them
  • Learn Motec software before you design the harness dumbass
