Purdue Formula SAE : Brake Disc Blanchard Grinding

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified on Jan 25, 2022

We typically machine our brake rotors from stock that is anywhere from 0.010" to 0.030" thicker that the final rotor dimensions. The thickness is then brought down by grinding. Having Purdue RMS (Research Machining Services) do them has been for the most part successful in the past. For reference, attached is the general process I followed to be able to get this done.


  1. Fill out the RMS work request (google this) and attach your drawings for the brake rotors with it. The PF22 example package is attached to this page.
  2. After RMS responds to you with a quote, forward that quote to BOSO (boso@purdue.edu). BOSO will sign off on it, and also provide any account numbers needed.
  3. Drop off parts at RMS.
