Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory, last modified by Nowakowski, Justin Thomas on Oct 12, 2023

High Level Goals

  1. Improvements 
    1. Rebound travel (softer corner/roll springs)
    2. Decrease vehicle roll
    3. Less lateral load transfer (Lower CG or longer track *Lower CG preferred)
    4. Less weight
    5. Improve tunability of static toe, camber, ride height
    6. Decrease compliance and friction??
  2. Ways to achieve
    1. Switch to third spring in front actuation to improve pitch control so softer corner springs can be used
      1. Adds weight but improved chassis control
      2. Difficulty packaging
    2. Pullrod front suspension to lower CG especially with added mass of third spring
      1. Carbon bonded rod in tension (perform more testing)
    3. Much stiffer ARBs that must be tested to confirm performance to control roll gradient
    4. Switch to carbon control arms
      1. Less weight potentially
      2. Increased stiffness
      3. Require ultimate and fatigue test samples for compression and tension to validate
    5. 8" rims for improved cornering stiffness
    6. Shims on tirod or pull/push rod for quick adjustments with rod ends for more fine adjustment
