Keith's Feedback:

  • How where to forces determined? How was 1 degree of play requirement determined and is that achievable
    • Kurisko, Keith Gregory , the forces we referenced in our presentation were from the Design Judges Web Page. They provide a benchmark for what a driver can put into the system and not what the suspension will. These forces include the max torque on the steering column by the driver and the radial load for what they might apply during operation or when they are getting out.
    • I have been looking into the play of the system and it seems that 1 degrees may be impossible to hit with our current hardware due to the play in the U joints. The 1 degree of play has always been the target, but I do not know of any studies by our team to see if we have ever met our target, which is one reason I want to test all past available cars to see what we hit on those.
  • What is Radial load x and y come from?
  • I think the reason that we know it works or did it in the past isnt good justification. There should be some decision matrix or tradeoff study done with quantitative information to make these decisions
  • We need someone to dive into steering not just from the mechanical design aspects but understanding the connection between driver and ground through tires (thinking ahead to design presentation
  • Great ideas for steering design and layout. Good to hear there are rough calcs to determine placement of buttons
  • How will steering reduce weight? Is there a percent target?
    • In our system, the only parts that can potentially lose weight are the support structures, bearing carriers and steering rack. We need to determine if the support structures were sufficient for last year's car to adequately reduce movement in the system before we can make any judgement there.
