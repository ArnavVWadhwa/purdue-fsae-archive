Purdue Formula SAE : UT-1-01

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 27, 2023

Simulation ML-057

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Moved strake more inboard and curved it initially

This should aid in housing the vortex better
Outer sweep changed to a more defined kickpoint.

This eliminates the artifacting occurring at the inlet, while also giving the side tunnel a defined kick point to generate more front downforce.

Additionally, this may increase initial inwash which may help power up the vortex in the strake channel in the side tunnel. This may also hurt UT performance if the vortex inwashes its wake earlier.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

NOTE: Was accidentally using the incorrect nosecone, that's where the FW/overall DF improvement came from.

UT performance failed because the curvature was not housing the vortex properly downstream, need to widen the channel. 


Sweep kickpoints definitely succeeded in doing their job here. Very defined suction peaks. I'm going to keep this sweep moving forward for some additional forward DF.

As a result of the nosecone correction, the high-pressure region at the inlet was much reduced, which improved FW performance considerably.
