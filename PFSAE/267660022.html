Purdue Formula SAE : Oxygen | Sensors

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Jul 31, 2023

Notes:

  • EFI systems can adjust themselves to correct for errors between desired and delivered air/fuel ratios through monitoring the oxygen content of exhaust gases
    • More oxygen means running leaner assuming no leaks
  • A leak will show the engine as running leaner as exhaust gases will flow out and oxygen will flow in due to non-constant output cycle
  • Basically, a battery that changes potential based on the level of oxygen present
    • Layer of zirconium oxide changes the sensors output voltage based on the partial pressures of the oxygen molecules
      • More oxygen means less output voltage
  • Closed loop means that actual injection is adjusted based on lambda reading while open loop is used just for data
  • Cold oxygen sensors have inaccurate readings so the ECU ignores them for a small period after start in a closed-loop system
    • Heated Exhaust Gas Oxygen (HEGO) sensors are made for this purpose
      • They have heaters so that they work on engine start as startup is critical for emissions as engines run slightly rich when they are cold
  • Increased CAM duration can cause issues as some unburned intake mixture can flow through the exhaust increasing the oxygen reading
  • Sensitive to exhaust backpressure (rich will read richer and lean will read leaner)
    • Sensors should be installed after turbochargers for this reason
    • The closer the sensor is to the engine the greater the transient accuracy will be due to delay time
