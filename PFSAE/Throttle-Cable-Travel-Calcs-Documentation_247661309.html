Purdue Formula SAE : Throttle Cable Travel Calcs Documentation

Created by Aaltonen, Emil Johan on Jan 04, 2023

The throttle cable travel calcs were done using excel, but the formula was derived from the geometry displayed below.

The rectangle on the left represents the throttle cable holder, and the two angled rectangles represent the throttle pedal itself at different angles. L2-L1 is the throttle cable travel over the angle between L2 and L1.

Our analysis fixed the initial pedal angle, pedal height, and pedal distance based on other design considerations. Then, the distance from the pivot and the pedal travel in degrees (angle of pedal in initial position - angle of pedal in fully pressed position) were each varied, outputting a throttle cable travel calue at each combination.

Ask the intake guys for the desired cable travel, and the drivers how much angular travel they want, and you know the distance you want your connection points to be from the pivot. In our case, we wanted the drivers to have the chance to test out different angular travels so we have several connection points, and thus several possible pedal ratios.

Here is the formula we used, but it is probably easier to go directly to the source: Formula drive → 2023 → Chassis→Throttle Pedal→Throttle Pedal Calcs (Excel File)
