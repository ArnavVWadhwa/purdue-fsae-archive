Purdue Formula SAE : Counter-Rotating Vortex Pair Dynamics

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Aug 15, 2023

Counter Rotating Vortex Pair

Counter rotating vortex pair is the simpler of two types of vortex pairs. You have a counter rotating vortex pair when you have two vortices close to each other spinning in opposite directions. Counter rotating vortex pair is often found on airplanes with wing tip vortices from left and right hand side wings. And that's what we will base our discussion on.

Effect of vortex pair can be analyzed by thinking about shear stresses, since that's the dominate force.

Let's idealize the this vortex pair to start with. Let's say they are ideal vortices whose vortex core can be modeled with solid body rotation, and that the two vortices are of the same strength, core size, tangential velocity, and axial vortex, and they are exactly 2 vortex core radius away. In that case, neither vortex gets subjected to shear stress on the side closest to each other. While normally forces in all directions cancel out, the lack of shear stress on the inside means that z component don't. Since the vortex is downwashing on the inside, it means it's lacking upward shear force, resulting in net shear force in the -z direction, translating the vortex downwash. Since the two vortices sees the same downwash shear force, both are translated downward. That's why airplane wingtip vortices sinks down, and that's why FAA tells pilots wake turbulence can be avoided by taking off before the takeoff point of the plane in front of you.

When the two vortices are of different strength, the interaction changes slightly. The weaker of the two vortices can get upwashed if the strength difference is that significant. However, the analysis method remains the same. Understanding this ideal case is already very useful.
