Purdue Formula SAE : Tire Mounting

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified on Jan 16, 2022

Suspension needs tires on wheels and from what I have heard, the tools that we have to do that kind of suck from experience, I can tell you the tools we have to do it suck, so we have tire shops do that.

Most tire shops do not have the equipment to mount tires for us as our tires and wheels are much smaller than that of passenger cars.

Below is a list of shops that can work with our parts. Be sure to check hours especially for weekends.

  • Mr & Mrs Tire - 1094 Sagamore Pkwy W, West Lafayette, IN

    • #1 choice
    • $10 / tire
    • Usually pretty quick turnaround, same day or next day
    • Seems extra-thorough - they saw that we marked slow leak on one of the wheels and found which bolt holes it was leaking from and marked them!


  • Indian Motorcycle of Lafayette - 1165 S Creasy Ln, Lafayette, IN 47905

    • $50/tire if you did NOT purchase the tires from them, otherwise $30. I was able to finesse the $30 option.


If for some reason a shop isn't available and you have to mount them yourself, below is what we have learned about doing it.

The tool looks like this (ours is grey). There are many youtube how-to use it, find one and watch it as this isn't a comprehensive guide, rather it's just my notes and suggestions.

Prepare for this process to be very frustrating and potentially dangerous. During PF22 assembly, we attempted to mount 4 dries and 4 wets ourselves, and barely completed 3.


  • It helps to bolt the base of the tire changer to something to keep it stable. An old jig plate is a good candidate.
  • Wet tires and brand new tires seem to be more difficult to mount. I have only worked with well worn LC0, which are relatively thin wall and not very stiff. Be prepared for a lot of wrestling with the tire.
  • Apply lubricant on the wheel flange. The grease that drivetrain uses for CV joints works well.
  • Use some tape on that circular platform, as well as shop rags on the wheel centers to prevent wheel center damage.
  • When inflating to seat the bead, be prepared for this to be a tad loud and violent. Keep hand and fingers away from the tire and outer surface of wheel shell.
    • Safety glasses and hearing protection are a must. The tire will hopefully hold air, start to inflate and expand up to the wheel flange very quickly.
