Purdue Formula SAE : 2022-2023 Testing Plans

Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory, last modified on Mar 15, 2023

This document outlines the testing plans that are required or wanted to validate design decisions and build a further understanding of the vehicle. The goal is to know what we will test and how it will be tested/post processed to save time during testing and be better prepared when submitting the design report. We must determine what we need to have on the main design report slides and work backwards to determine what analysis tools are needed and what testing procedures will be.


Suspension/Vehicle Dynamics:

  1. Desired Testing
    1. Dynamic Tests:
      1. Validate prediction vehicle capabilities
        1. Use Skidpad for lateral acceleration and Fz (shock pot data), yaw rate
        2. Acceleration for max longitudinal accel
        3. Braking sim validation, 70mph and 40mph braking (IMU long accel, shock pot Fz, Vehicle speed, wheel speed (SR))
      2. Strain Guage Testing
        1. Validate tire and suspension loads and improve tire model
        2. Get tirod force data to compare with driver feel and aligning moment
      3. Tire Temperatures
        1. Record temperatures after single skidpad and autocross for 16" LCO, compare to 18" LCO
        2. TTC Data analysis of tire temps
      4. Dynamic event suspension setup
        1. Determine the best toe, camber, tire pressures (maybe springs/damper setting/diff setting/ride height/roll centers)
          1. Run through a single event and record tire temperatures and lap times
            1. Make adjustments and identify how tire temp is affected/performance
    2. Static Tests:
      1. Compliance
        1. Toe
        2. Camber
      2. CG Location
      3. Kinematic Validation
      4. Suspension Friction
