Purdue Formula SAE : Spool Final Design Notes

Created by Panchal, Aarnav Nilesh on Apr 28, 2023

Purpose: To provide a locked axle option for Purdue FSAE vehicles

Spool Criteria/Benefits: The spool assembly needs to be swappable with the current differential package while holding against current loading conditions and making the subsystem lighter. With the spool assembly design, a total of 8 lbm is saved in comparison to the current system.


Spool Assembly


Spool Body Design: The spool body was designed as a differential replacement providing a solid locked axle keeping wheel speeds constant across the rear axle unlike a limited slip differential which becomes more open as a function of a set ramp angle and factory preload option. The body features multiple tapped holes and 3 exterior lugs. The tapped holes are used for bolted joints and studs to connect inboard CV housings and the lugs are used to connect the sprocket. The body also has grooves for snag rings to help constrain the spool body axially with the uprights. Lastly, the body features an "X" pattern cutout used to save weight. The X cutout is used as the bolted connections cause the spool body to have the diameter it does, the X pattern can help mitigate the weight added.


Spool CV Housing Design: The CV housings were designed to save weight from the steel equivalent from the differential. Each housing utilizes the CV insert design used in the suspension hubs. The left CV housing has lugs used to bolt the sprocket into the assembly with while the right housing does not. Both use a through hole that a socket head #10 bolt can be inserted through to thread into the spool body.


Spool Sprocket Design: The spool sprocket was made so that the sprocket would bolt into the spool body and CV housing. This would mate the system together and simplify sprocket manufacturing as a wire EDM spline was no longer needed. The sprocket was designed as per the original drivetrain sprocket design page, the only edits made for the spool were the addition of bolted connections.


FEA

Spool Body Analysis: FEA cases were ran using fix conditions to simulate housing and sprocket connections with remote forces used for upright connections. Both cases of a moment and a”beam” model were run. The above is the moment model using loads derived in the Drivetrain loads spreadsheet, while the below is the beam model.


Spool CV Housing FEA: An FEA case was ran using fix conditions to simulate body and sprocket connections with a launch moment load. The bolted joints between the housing and body were treated as fixed cylindrical supports and the CV insert faces being loaded were compression only supports. The moment load is the same load used for the body found in Drivetrain Loads spreadsheet.


Spool Sprocket FEA: An FEA case was ran using fix conditions to simulate bolted connections and a frictionless spool body support with a launch load on the top ¼ of teeth using pre-derived forces and pressure angles from Fall design. The Drivetrain Loads and Structures sheets contain the necessary information.
