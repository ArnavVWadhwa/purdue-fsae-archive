Purdue Formula SAE : PDR Deliverables to Analysis Checklist

Created by MacNab, Zachary Robert, last modified on Aug 29, 2023

This page takes analysis checklist items and relates them to our PDR design review

  • Start with a free body diagram
  • Estimate results with hand calculation
    • Is this sufficient for your application? Do you need further, more complex analysis?
  • Obtain material properties from an approved source (MMPDS)
  • Gather and organize all loads, temperatures, accelerations, and number of cycles.
  • Sanity check material properties. Ensure that they cover the full range of tempersatures
    • If using FEM software, sanity check True Stress/Strain compared to Engineering Stress/Strain
      • They are different. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stress–strain_curve
  • Include relevant thermal, weld, and casting knockdown factors. 
  • ensure blends / fillets are large enough to avoid low cycle factigue and that the mesh adequately captures the stress concnetration
  • Consider environmental corrosion
  • Ensure parts are sliding are resistant to galling/high friction
  • Ensure interfacing parts to not collide under intended range of motion
  • Consider tool access
  • Sanity check you use principal stress for brittle materials, not Von Mises
  • ensure fatigue calculations are corrected for temperature
  • verify human rated factors of safety are used if safety critical
  • Assess margin of safety on yield, ultimate, elongation, stability, fatigue, and joint gapping
  • Double check units at all steps
