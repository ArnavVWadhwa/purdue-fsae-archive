Purdue Formula SAE : Single Cylinder Design Decisions PF22 | ELE

Created by Unknown User (jkrawitz), last modified by Paradise, Hunter on Jul 16, 2023

Requirements: 

  • needs to be easy to switch between both engine harness'
  • doesnt get in the way of intake
  • not in contact with moving or hot parts
  • able to read all sensors on the dyno
  • able to run engine


Wires routed along bottom of table all in one bundle on the right side.

  • They are routed together to make it easier to track and protect them. 
  • Routed along table instead of in air
    • doesn't get in the way of intake which will be changed frequently
    • means people using the dyno can't just toss stuff on the table
  • right instead of left side to avoid running through wet area by dynamometer and due to more sensors on left side of engine and cart.


Came with

  • gear position
  • crank position(not accurate enough at low speeds)
  • Engine/coolant temp
  • Starter
  • Stator


Added:

  • Hall effect for cam shaft for engine synchronization
  • Oil pressure for safety
  • Different rectfier because single cylinder has a single phase AC output instead of 3-phase


Kept PDM and Dash wiring the same for ease when switching between 4 and 1 cylinder engines

If a wire goes to ECU and PDM/Dash/Dyno part that stays on car between engines it was left on the dyno and tape was put on the ECU connector as well as a note was put in the pinout to track it because that results in less pinning/depinning overall and is easier to keep track of.
