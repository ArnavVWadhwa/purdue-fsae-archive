Salisbury/Drexler

To model a Salisbury/Drexler diff, the following parameters affect how lockup is applied:

  • Differential tuning
    • Preload
    • Ramp Angles - factory setting is 40/50
    • Number of clutch plates
  • Vehicle tuning
    • Driving torque on carrier
    • Negative braking torque on carrier

It seems that steeper ramp angles to mimic an open diff is largely used in professional motorsport. Simulations and testing should lean towards less aggressive lockup and reduced caster (3 - 5 degrees) with preference to plant the inner rear tire with appropriate suspension tuning. Note that the steepest available ramp angle on the Drexler is 60 degrees. 

Start with simulations of open diff and then work Drexler into it? 

Quaife

Suspension requirements - Same open diff requirement - stiff springs, light rear sway bar, reduced caster. At least 20% of rear weight must be on the inside rear tire at all times. 

How do we model a Quaife vs 50-60 Drexler if both have same suspension requirements?
