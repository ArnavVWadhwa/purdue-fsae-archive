Direct from Todd

  1. Design report
    1. General advice

                                               i.     Follow Design Judging Score Sheet outline

  1. Suspension
  2. Frame/Body/Aero
  3. Powertrain
  4. Cockpit/Controls/Brakes/Safety
  5. Systems Management/Integration
  6. Manufacturability/Serviceability
  7. Aesthetics/Style
  8. Creativity

                                              ii.     Make content proportional to score sheet scores by header and sub-topics (Design-25%, Build-25%, Validation-25%, Understanding-25%).  This is the “Development V” or “Design Cycle”.  All sections should follow a similar outline/flow.  Start with the most important factors, those that impact achievement of goals the most (Pareto).

                                             iii.     Determine who will be the lead presenter for each category at competition and ensure they are integral in writing the report.

                                             iv.     Read each other’s sections and judge effectiveness in answering the primary and secondary questions posed by the score sheet for that section.  And ask “so what?” or “why?”.  Example: Electronics – “The focus for the 2020 electronics system was improving the shifting system and increasing the available I/O ports to interface with the rest of the car.”  Why?  Tell me how this improves the vehicle performance and thereby increases the number of points achieved at competition.

                                              v.     S.4.3.1 The Design Report should contain:

                                             vi.     a.         A brief description of the vehicle with your team’s design objectives

                                           vii.     b.         Discussion of any important design features and vehicle concepts.

                                          viii.     c.          Analysis and testing techniques that were utilized

                                             ix.     Define an acronym the first time used or in a table.

                                              x.     Incorporate images & graphs in line with text.  Currently at 6 pages in length.  Total allowed = 5 pages + 3 pages of required drawings (3 views of vehicles, each on a separate page)

                                             xi.     Be sure terminology is clear and won’t be confused with something else.  Example drivetrain “uprights”.

  1. Specific suggestions on rough draft

                                               i.     Intro – Needs to have “promotional/marketing tone” and start with team-level goals (Include Function-Event-Points matrix) then explain the approach (team organization, design process / development “V”, choosing/rationale of sub-team goals, system integration). 

                                              ii.     Each main section (category) intro – Start with sub-team goals based on Function-Event-Points matrix (to support team goals).  Lose “promotional” tone, but still make it interesting and engaging to read.  Tell a “story” from beginning to end.  Intro – “here is what we set off to do”, middle – “here is the engineering and associated decisions/tradeoffs we made to create the final product”, end – “here are the end results, how they compare to what we originally aimed to do, and what we learned about our predictions, models, variable sensitivity/importance, ect.).  Make sure it passes the “who cares? Or “why are you telling me this” test.

                                             iii.     Suspension – instead of “minimal time was spent on kinematics, the lapsim model was used to run sensitivity analysis and determine that kinematic improvements were not the largest factor in improving track time.  Instead, the lapsim revealed that structural changes posed greater opportunity and therefore became the focus for this year’s design.

                                             iv.     Highlight creativity/innovation small and large.  How did you accomplish all requirements (weight, stiffness, functionality) in a clever way?

                                              v.     How do systems integrate?  What are trade-offs/impacts of one system to meet the requirements of another and why?

                                             vi.     Aero – if front biased was ideal, why was it designed to 50/50?
