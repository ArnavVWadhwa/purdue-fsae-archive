Purdue Formula SAE : Unidirectional Flow Device (UFD)

Created by Unknown User (mart1731), last modified on Sep 09, 2021

Introduction. 

This device is intended for increasing the scavenging effect of the exhaust gasses. It utilizes the concept of creating a space where the  backpressure from the exhaust gas is significantly reduced and a space in the exhaust can create a larger vacuum for the increasing the desired scavenging effect. 

Expanding the exhaust pipe incorrectly can lead to a pocket at the UFD, too large of a space in the UFD  will result in a pocket at around atmospheric pressure and slow overall exhaust flow. Too small a pocket and the desired backpressure effect is negligible. The degrees of the throttling walls from the matter significantly, too large of an angle from the horizontal would send a negative pressure wave back to the engine.


Benefits

Enough space on the exhaust runner. 

Significantly improves scavenging. 

Placement on the exhaust runner would be close to supports.  


Complications

Well sizing.

Throttling angle and sizing.


Specification terms:

Bulb: rigid spherical blown-up space. 

Conduit: piping that extends into the bulb.  


Specifications from the patent

20-degree angle 

The pocket cross-sectional area may be in the range of from 120 to 300% of the cross-sectional area of said first extension member. An ideal range is approximately 150-200%.

The diameter of conduits 220 and 232 is shown to be approximately 75% of the diameter of the bulbous area.

The length of the top segment is equal to the 50 to 110% diameter of the conduit.
