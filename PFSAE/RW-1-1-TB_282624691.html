Purdue Formula SAE : RW-1-1-TB

Created by Tanuj Aditya Basu on Dec 13, 2023

Simulation TB-001

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Added a more conventional 4 element design to Eeshwar's unconventional Onboarding

When looking at Eeshwar's onboarding design, I found that it had a really high lift coefficient for a design that looked like it was prone to stall with its main element. Noticed that it was positioned way lower and closer to the engine intake than any other design.

Wanted to see if I could optimize this design with a four-element rear wing. Put a main element I had used for my onboarding and then used more conventional airfoils for the second, third, and fourth elements.

Also had a main element sweep to a more curved airfoil to increase energy flow and not have it separate in the outer region.

Also has an endplate with a vertical gurney and a dive-plane-like device to add downforce.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

Section 0: Interesting look at pressure in the center section, the proximity to the engine intake could be helping. Second and third element are okay but can be improved, fourth is lacking. 


Section 1: Fairly standard, but could be improved further.


Main problem with this rear wing design is the drag. Way too much drag and I believe it is from the vertical gurneys on the sides. Going forward I want to optimize the airfoil designs for each element and also to reduce the size of the vertical gurneys to reduce drag.
