Purdue Formula SAE : Part 3 NX Drawing

Created by Crooks, Troy A, last modified by Arafat, Adam on Sep 27, 2023

Once you have finished designing and doing the CAD for your part in 3 dimensions, it is important that you also have several 2 dimensional views of your part with pertinent dimensions to refer to during the manufacturing of the part. 

What should it include?: 

Views(not the Drake one):

In a standard drawing, you should generally have the following views of your part: 

  • Top
  • Front
  • Side
  • Isometric (not for dimensions, just to get a 3 dimensional look at the part) 

NB: THIS IS A GENERAL RULE OF THUMB, see dimensions section for further guidance. 

Dimensions

Your drawing should include every unique dimension on your part. Pay careful attention to the word "unique", any redundant or unnecessary dimensions clutter drawings and make them impossible to read.

Example 1: PF23 Insert

Here are some important things to highlight in this drawing: 

  • Notice how there are only 3 of the 4 aforementioned views. Given that we are trying to avoid redundancy in our drawings, consider why there is not a top view. 
  • Notice how there are no dimensions on the isometric view.
  • Consider the following section of the drawing: 

Notice how the length of the head of this insert is not explicitly stated in the drawing because it can be easily calculated using the overall length and length of the shaft (0.65 - 0.5 = 0.15)


Example 2: PF23 Wheel Shell

Notice how only one of the bolt holes is dimensioned. If you have many holes of exactly the same size, only dimension one (unless they are adjacent to very similarly sized holes that they could be confused with, use your judgement). 

In summary: DON'T REPEAT YOURSELF
