Purdue Formula SAE : Pedal Box Welding Jig

Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory on Nov 30, 2021
  1. Â Jig design
    1. Goal:
      1. aligns tabs with body tubes
    2. 1/4" 6061 Al was used as a plate to fixture the pedal box assembly in place for welding
    3. The most important part of the jig is relative hole placement
    4. The holes on the jig are the 4 retaining pins in the body tubes and the pin holes for holding tabs
      2. HOLDING TABS: Using Chassis 1/4" roll pins
        1. There must be a total of 3 pins to hold a tab in place; 2 on one side with a third on the other side as seen above in green
        2. In NX sketch, use tangent constraint to place the holes tangent to the respective sides of the tabs; NOTE: When placing the holes ensure there is a gap between the roll pin and body tubes to ensure welds can be made without interfering with roll pins
    5. LESSON LEARNED:
      1. Refer to lesson learned section for more specifics, but make sure the tubes are fixed to the plate and the plate is thick enough to avoid warping during welding
      2. I would incorporate the jig into the Chassis Jig Plates on the end where it would not be obstructed by the chassis itself
      3. INSERT IMAGE OF JIG PLATE AND LOCATION OF IDEA
  2. Manufacturing Jig
    1. When dimensioning the technical drawing choose one of the pedal box body retaining holes to reference all other holes to
    2. Once in the mill jaws place the first hole and set x and y to zero as this is the referenced hole
  3. Assembly of Jig and prep for welding
    1. Insert all roll pins into the fixture
    2. Bolt Body tubes to fixture
    3. Dry fit all tabs and ensure no major gaps along with inserting all master cylinder, brake pedal, accelerator hardware for aligning tabs
    4. File or grind tabs until desired fit, once reached remove all tabs
    5. Wire brush and use acetone to clean all Body Tubes and Tabs for clean welding surface
    6. Choose the inner most tab and place into fixture with relative tabs and hardware to ensure placement along with a square
    7. Tack weld the tab and remove supportive tabs and hardware to finish the welding around this tab
    8. Rinse and repeat for all other tabs MAKE SURE to work on tabs from the middle most tab to the outer most
