DAQ CRAM (Compact Rapid Acquisition Machine)

WHAT IT DOES:

  • Takes 12V in and outputs +5V, -5V, and +1.5V to power WHAM (Strain Gauge Amp)
  • Also outputs 3.3v to power on-board microcontroller
  • On-board microcontroller to handle computation/comms over CAN

REQUIREMENTS:

  • 12 Analog in with ADC sample rate 500SPS+
  • CAN bus
  • Compact size
  • Future-proof so that we can use it for many many years

SPECS:

  • STM32F407VET6 microcontroller
    • 16 ADC, shitton digital IO
    • 2x CAN bus
    • Battery backed RTC
    • SD Card support
    • shitton peripherals (UART, I2C, SPI, you name it)
    • Giant maker/hobbyist community
    • Extremely popular in the industry so it's actually beneficial to our career
    • Extremely powerful (168MHz, shitton flash/ram)
    • Bare Metal/Arduino/RTOS/Micropython support so extremely flexible
    • $3 Cheaper than ATMEGA2560 which was the original plan
    • basically its limits are wayyyyy beyond our capabilities atm so we're chillin
  • Credit card size
    • 3.375" x 2.125"
    • widely used standard, perfect size
  • Other peripherals:
    • Micro SD card holder for data logging, if needed
    • SPI Flash / FRAM ICs for short-term high speed data logging

STUFF IN CONSIDERATION:

  • on-board variable power supply with output range of 0.5V-10V?
    • can power more peripherals
