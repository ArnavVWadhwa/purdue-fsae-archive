Background: 

PF23 is back on the R6 platform. The goal of the exhaust team is to maximize performance, reliability, and reduce the weight profile. The team will integrate research, WAVE, and dyno testing to validate all performance goals. As of now (July) the target weight of the exhaust has not been finalized. As for reliability, the design will follow high-level manufacturing and assembly processes. Firstly the team will have to finalize target rpm values to decide target exhaust runner length. PF20 has a useful runner length excel calculator, validated for the R6. With this tool, the team can seamlessly start the conversation with intake about runner length and harmonics. The exhaust sub-team will work closely with intake to optimize performance utilizing harmonics. PF22 had a heavy exhaust system, we will look at possibly minimizing the weight of the exhaust system on PF23 by using titanium and doing more muffler research, design, and aftermarket muffler modifications. The exhaust ports are located on the front of the engine, in contrast to the PF22 with the rear port. I would like to utilize a low center of mass design with the final outlet to be on the side of the car. Previous PF22 had issues with sound amplification and heat issues with the aero packaging. Previously the exhaust has low priority on space claim and had to change the design many times to accommodate other subsystems. The goal is to start talking about final designs early with cooling and aero. 


Goals: 

  • Performance:
    • Exhaust runner length tuned to the desired RPM - 10,000
    • Exhaust header tuned to engine firing sequence.
    • Utilize Helmholtz principles for forced inertial supercharging.  
  • Reliability:
    • Ease of assembly 
    • Ease of maintenance. 
    • FEA validation
  • Weight
    • Research into titanium. 
    • Custom/ Aftermarket modified muffler.
