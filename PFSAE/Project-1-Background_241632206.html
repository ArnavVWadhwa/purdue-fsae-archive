Purpose:

The goal for this project is to improve on a very basic rear wing already provided to you. You can improve on it by moving it around (with in rules), changing airfoil, adding flaps, removing flaps, adjusting flap arrangements, or any other aero device you can think of. While you are making the CAD, make reference to the existing CAD so that you can learn the ins and outs of making good quality CAD.

  • Project summary
    • Inputs
      • Rules Requirements: 
        • No more than 250 mm rearward of the rear of the rear tires 
        • Must be no higher than 1200 mm above the ground
        • Inboard of two vertical planes parallel to the center-line of the chassis touching the inside of the rear tires at the height of the hub center-line
      • Simple two-element rear wing
    • Outputs
      • Finalized CAD Model 
      • Performance values from CFD
      • Presentation justifying your design decisions.

STEPS

  • Design target: rear wing Cl*A = 1.35 in a 0 cross-wind environment.
  • Build a rear wing assembly that's located in the correct location. Achieve the correct location by moving your surfaces, not by moving the origin.
  • Do NOT use any assembly constraints. Do not move the parts in assembly. Make sure the surface are in the right place by drawing them in the right place.
  • Show Matt or Joe the assembly before proceeding to CFD. MAKE SURE IT'S IN THE RIGHT PLACE.
  • Again, make sure it's in the right place. Ideally, you should design the rear wing in the context of the rules assembly, or the CAD reference files. 
  • Bug Matt about running these in CFD, and we'll get these sims going. 
  • You have 3 attempts to hit the design target. You MUST run at least 1 iteration per week. But the more frequent you run iterations, the better it is, and the sooner you actually get to work on PF24 design.
  • You need to justify you reasoning behind each iteration. Copy this page to your local word document or whatever software you prefer and fill it out up to geometry before running CFD:
    • Aero Design Notebook Template
  • You will need to fill the page out after receiving CFD. Make sure to notify Matt or Joe so that they can check it.

After you have completed the three iterations, onboarding is considered complete.
