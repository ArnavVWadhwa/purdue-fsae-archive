Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 Frame Tube Justificiations

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified on Nov 25, 2022

Tubes not mentioned on the list below are required by rules and are at the rules minimum sizing. The list below is nonSES rules and an explanation of tubes that are slightly different from previous years. 


Front

The bulkhead was raised 1 inch for front wing performance, resulting in a bent tube in the front. The circled tube in the image below, wouldn't have been required by rules in previous years but due to the bent tube it's required in this design to support the bend.

The black lines indicate where we had tubes in previous years. We removed them in the PF23 frame to reduce weight. In order to remove the lower one of those two tubes, the FRH was made straight since rules require a bend in the FRH to be supported by a tube. This required moving suspension points so that they all fell on a line.

The indicated tube isn't required by rules but make pedal box, steering, and floor closeout mounting a lot easier / lighter.


Middle

The pink tube isn't required by rules, but is need in a front impact more than 5G and helps with torsional rigidity. 

The MRH is slightly wider from previous years to give the driver's some more elbow room.


Rear

The highlighted tube is bent (a change from last year) to reduce the amount of welding.

The orange tubes below are used for engine mounting. The rear most tube is required by rules but the other two tubes are additions.

The rear frame member below is 1 x .095 in which is thicker than the rules required 1 x .049 due to the engine, differential, and dampers all mounting to the same tube.

The rearmost lower frame member tube is the car's quick jack (and analysis was done to ensure it didn't need upsized).
