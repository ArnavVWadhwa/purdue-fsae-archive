Purdue Formula SAE : Poly V6-2 Final Convergence Mode

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Apr 17, 2023

Background

Wiggles in solution due to high relaxation cause convergence to take unnecessarily long time. However, if lower relaxations are used, the iteration to convergence increases. Therefore, a compromise can be reached by using higher relaxation initially, and when the solution near convergence, switch to a lower relaxation to reduce oscillations.

Settings

  • First order upwind initialization (for both segregated solver and SST model)
    • Iterations: 200
    • Velocity relaxation: 0.9
    • Pressure relaxation: 0.3
    • SST relaxation: 0.85
  • Second order upwind
    • Velocity relaxation: 0.7
    • Pressure relaxation: 0.3
    • SST relaxation: 0.7
  • Final convergence mode
    • Start of final convergence mode: 100 convergence score
    • Velocity relaxation: 0.6
    • Pressure relaxation: 0.3
    • SST relaxation: 0.6
