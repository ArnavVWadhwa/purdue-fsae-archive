Purdue Formula SAE : Cooling Rules

Created by Feehan, Hugh Timothy, last modified on Jun 21, 2023
Rule Number/Name Text From Rulebook Notes/Summary 

 V.1.1  Open Wheel 

 

 

The top 180° of the wheels/tires must be unobstructed when viewed from vertically above the wheel.  

 

The wheels/tires must be unobstructed when viewed from the side.  

 

No part of the vehicle may enter a keep out zone defined by two lines extending vertically from positions 75 mm in front of and 75 mm behind, the outer diameter of the front and rear tires in the side view elevation of the vehicle, with tires steered straight ahead. This keep out zone will extend laterally from the outside plane of the wheel/tire to the inboard plane of the wheel/tire. 

Cooling parts cannot be on top of the wheels or to the outside of the wheels. 

 

Cooling parts cannot be within 75mm of the front or rear of any tire. 

V.1.4  Ground Clearance 

Ground clearance must be sufficient to prevent any portion of the vehicle except the tires from touching the ground during dynamic events. Don’t let cooling parts hit the ground. 

F.5.11  External Items 

 

Any item which meets all three of:  

  • Located outside the Component Envelope F.1.15 
  • Located above 350 mm from the ground 
  • Could load the Primary Structure during a rollover 

 

External Items must meet one of the two conditions:  

 

  1. Be attached at a Hoop to Brace node or a fully Triangulated structural node without the ability to create a moment at the node

 

  1. When not attached at a node as described above, then: 
  • Additional Structural bracing meeting F.3.2.1.o must be added to prevent bending loads 
  • Additional calculations must be performed to show the member will not fail in bending or shear, even if unbraced F.5.11.3 

 

External Items should not point at the driver 

Easiest solution with side or rear radiators is to make sure cooling parts are below 350mm from the ground so these rules don’t apply. 

Keeping the cooling system within the Component Envelope will be difficult packaging-wise.

If the radiator is not fully below 350mm, perform analysis to show that the mounting will break rather than load the Primary Structure

 T.1.6  Thermal Protection 

 

 

When seated in the normal driving position, sufficient heat insulation must be provided to ensure that the driver will not contact any metal or other materials which may become heated to a surface temperature above 60°C.  

Insulation may be external to the cockpit or incorporated with the Driver’s Seat or Firewall.  

 

The design must address all three types of heat transfer between the heat source (exhaust pipe, coolant hose/tube, Accumulator Container) and the panel that the driver could contact (seat or floor):  

 

  1. Conduction Isolation by one of the following:
  • No direct contact between the heat source and the panel 
  • A heat resistant, conduction isolation material with a minimum thickness of 8 mm between the heat source and the panel. 

 

  1. Convection Isolation by a minimum air gap of 25 mm between the heat source and the panel 

 

  1. Radiation Isolation by one of the following: 

 

  • A solid metal heat shield with a minimum thickness of 0.4 mm 
  • Reflective foil or tape when combined with conduction insulation.

Minimum 25mm between cooling items and the seat, floor, and anything else the driver can touch. (Importantly, the side pods)

T.1.8 Firewall(s)

A Firewall(s) must separate the driver compartment and any portion of the Driver Harness from:  

  1. All components of the fuel supply, the engine oil, the liquid cooling systems, any lithium batteries 
  2. (EV only)

 

Any Firewall must:  

  1. Be a non permeable surface made from a rigid, Nonflammable Material
  2. Seal completely against the passage of fluids (the Firewall itself, edges, and Floor Closeout) c. Be rigidly mounted 

 

The Firewall must extend sufficiently far upwards and/or rearwards and/or sideways where any point on the drivers body less than 100 mm above the bottom of the helmet of the tallest driver must not be in direct line of sight with any part mentioned in T.1.8.1 above  

 

  1. Firewalls composed of multiple panels must overlap and be sealed at the joints Sealing between firewalls must not be a stressed part of the Firewall
  2. Grommets must be used to seal any pass through for wiring, cables, etc
  3. Any seals or adhesives used with the Firewall must be rated for the application environment
Chassis Subteam is responsible for the firewall, but we should still check that our stuff is properly separated from the driver. 

T.5.4  Coolant Fluid 

Water cooled engines must use only plain water with no additives of any kind. No antifreeze, etc. 

 T.5.5  System Sealing 

 

Any cooling or lubrication system must be sealed to prevent leakage.  

 

The vehicle must be capable of being tilted to a 45° angle without leaking fluid of any type.  

 

Flammable liquid leaks must not be allowed to accumulate.  

 

Two or more holes of minimum diameter 25 mm each must be provided in the lowest part of the structure or belly pan in such a way as to prevent accumulation of liquids and/or vapors.  

 

Absorbent material and open collection devices (regardless of material) are prohibited in compartments containing engine, drivetrain, exhaust and fuel systems below the highest point on the exhaust system. 

Make sure the cooling system doesn’t leak. 

T.5.6  Catch Cans 

 

Separate catch cans must be used to retain fluids from any vents for the engine coolant system and engine lubrication system. Each catch can must have a minimum capacity of 10% of the fluid being contained or 0.9 liter, whichever is greater.  

 

Any vent on other systems containing liquid lubricant or coolant, including a differential, gearbox, or electric motor, must have a catch can with a minimum capacity of 10% of the fluid being contained or 0.5 liter, whichever is greater.  

 

Catch cans must be:  

  1. Capable of containing boiling water without deformation 
  2. Located rearwards of the Firewall below the driver’s shoulder level 
  3. Positively retained, using no tie wraps or tape 

 

Any catch can on the cooling system must vent through a hose with a minimum internal diameter of 3 mm down to the bottom levels of the Chassis 

Catch can will likely need to be 0.9 liter based on previous cooling system capacities. 

 

Catch can must be behind firewall and near the bottom of the chassis. 

 

Hose to catch can must have minimum internal diameter of 3mm.
