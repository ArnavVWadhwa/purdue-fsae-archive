Purdue Formula SAE : Using the Comp Trailer

Created by Horton, Tyler Jason, last modified by Unknown User (agarw162) on Feb 21, 2023

Getting the Trailer Set Up:

  1. Acquire keys and unlock the trailer lot as well as the comp trailer itself
    1. Keychain with black loop in admin drawer of shop 
  2. Get the leveling bars and hitch / hitch adapter out of the trailer
    2. Hitch adapter and pin / cotter pin
  3. Putting the hitch in the truck 
    1. Put the hitch adapter in the truck. This makes the whole smaller so the actual hitch can fit into the truck's hitch properly
    2. Put the actual hitch in the truck. Put the pin through whole on the side, and retain it with the cotter pin. 
  4. Prepare the hitch for receiving the trailer
    1. Make sure all bolts on the hitch are not loose. The ball has fallen off of the hitch before
    2. Install the grey leveling bars onto the hitch. These help stabilize the trailer
    3. The hitch should look like this when you're ready for the next step
  5. Joining the trailer and the truck
    1. Slowly back the truck up until the ball is under the trailer
    2. Make sure the locking mechanism for the trailer is unlocked
    3. Lower the trailer onto the truck until about 50% of the weight is on the truck
    4. Lock the ball onto the truck with the locking mechanism and the lock
  6. Installing the leveling bars
    1. Remove the locking mechanism where the leveling bars sit
    2. Put the stabilizing bars behind the two teeth. Use the lever tool if necessary
    3. Reinstall the locking mechanism. The final product should look like this
    5. Put the leveling bar installing tool back in the trailer
    6. Fully raise the leg and put the crank in low drag mode (fold it down) 
    7. Take the wood from underneath and put it in the bed of the truck. You never know if you have to detach the trailer from the truck at any point
  7. Required Accessories 
    1. Plug in the trailer light cable into the truck. Make sure the cable is situated so that is does not pinch on anything
    2. Attach the safety chains to the rings on the truck. Refer to the picture below on how this should look
    3. Attach the emergency brake cable somewhere on the FRAME of the truck. Once this is pulled, the trailer brakes engage. 
    4. This is where we put it on our truck
    6. Once all is said and done, it should look like this
  8. Trailer Preflight
    1. Check tires for air
    2. Check for any scratches / gashes on the trailer
    3. Make sure all lights function properly
    4. Check payload and make sure that all cargo is secure (generator, tables, floor tiles, spare tire, etc) and cabinet doors are closed
    5. Remove chucks from the wheels
  9. Truck preflight
    1. Rotate the side mirrors on the truck so that they are in “tow mode” (if applicable)
    2. Set the trailer brake controller to the right level
      1. Too low and it will feel like the trailer is pushing the truck forward under braking 
      2. Too high and it will feel like the trailer is dragging the truck backwards under braking

You should be all good to start driving the trailer


General tips for driving with the trailer:

  • "Drive like you are driving on snow"
  • The truck will speed up and slow down slower than normal
  • Use a lot of extra caution when turning
    • Take turns wide and slow
  • Don't be afraid to cut across a yellow line to make a turn or something like that. People should be understanding that you've got a massive trailer behind you, and the roads of West Lafayette aren't really built for trailers.
