Purdue Formula SAE : FW-14-13

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Jul 05, 2023

Simulation: CZ-042

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Complete front wing reprofile

Taking lessons learned from FW-14-12, completely changing outwashing profile. Of course this builds on the assumption that my theory for FW-14-12 wake blowing up was correct.

What this does is that it essentially keeps expansion angle at the outboard low by having large chord. But the aggressive local outwash angle still provides a lot of outwash locally to help separation suppression.

This big planform will ensure a lot of downforce, which is why I also backed off the center section for rear wing performance.

Another thing that might happen with the aggressive local outwash angle is that vortex might be shed off the the trailing edge due to the low relative angle between flow direction and trailing edge direction. These vortices will be locally outwashing on the bottom, which is good. They should migrate outboard if it form along more than one trailing edge, which is also good. If any are formed in between slot gaps, additional local suction will be achieved, which is also good. So this should be good, if they form. If not, increasing outwash angle and increasing slot gap size will help.

This effect is way more powerful than I thought. The downwind suction side lost all suction since the span vector of flaps pretty much matched yaw angle.

Inner vortex much better controlled. And the separation is no more.

Not entirely sure the effect at play here. But I think what's happening is that due to delayed outwash and upwash with increased chord, the wake has more time to propagate inboard and stay down low, cause this wake to blow up....again. Positive is high wake is smaller, which is good for FW, but low wake is larger, which is bad for UT.
