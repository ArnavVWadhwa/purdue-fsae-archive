General Considerations and Decisions

Try to push the caliper as outboard as possible, leaving ~0.150in from caliper to wheel center or wheel center to shell bolts (whichever is closer)

Caliper placement dictates rotor mid plane, which dictates inboard face of the hub flange.

As far as rotor diam, basically trying to run as large a rotor as possible. This means pushing the caliper as close to the wheel shell as we feel comfortable and sizing the rotor to that.



Wheel speed sensor was chosen to be mounted outwards (in R) to mitigate the packaging nightmare of carrier and trigger wheel near the center of the hub. Making the rotor a trigger for the WSS means a slight reduction in pad area which thus far has been deemed acceptable.

The WSS is mounted ahead (negative X) of the upright. This was chosen as if the sensor head were to contact and catch on the upright, it reduces likelihood of the sensor getting dragged and jammed between the upright and the rotor, and instead just gets kicked aside.
