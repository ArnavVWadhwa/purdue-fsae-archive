Purdue Formula SAE : E85 Affects on Aluminum (experimental)

Created by Estes, Mitchell Taylor, last modified on Jul 17, 2023

Test 1

  • Method: make cup shape out of aluminum
    • Make spherical cup out of aluminum foil
    • fill with Fuel
  • Recording method
    • Analyze foil after a few minutes, then an hour, then the next day
  • Result
    • No visual change after 1 hr
    • After 20 hours (next day):
      • All ethanol gone/evaporated/drained
      • Assumption from what was observed:
        • Aluminum foil acquired a hole in the side wall.
        • This hole allowed most fuel to leak into the metal bowl below the aluminum setup.
        • All fuel evaporated, leaving dark oily residue in both the bowl and the aluminum
      • Aluminum also allows water to leak out in drips
    • Improvements:
      • Ensure the foil structure is leak-proof before filling with E85
      • Ensure there are no sharp folds in contact with fuel

Test 2

  • Method
    • Lay paper towel in bowl: easier to identify leak
      • Allows for fuel to have extended time on flat surfaces of aluminum for extended amount of time
    • Aluminum isolated and entirely engulfed in fuel, inside sealed container
      • Allows more direct analysis of aluminum, since no fuel can evaporate. (i.e. color change of fuel? Changes in color/chape/etc of the aluminum)
    • Recording method
      • Photograph each setup every hour till effect is noticed/identified
    • Results
      • Checked after 1 hr
        •  
        • Fuel evaporated to 50%
        • NO noticeable change to sealed container or aluminum.
      • Checked every 2 hr with no change in appearance.
        • Bowl:
          • Refilled bowl every 12 ish hours
          • Poured out old concentrated fuel and refilled with fresh about once a day. (visual color differences: yellowish green haze)
        • Cup:
          • No visible difference
        • ~ 72 hr
          • Bowl still holding fuel (no visual defects to aluminum)
          • Aluminum in cup is still in one piece, with no visible discolorations to the fuel
        • Improvements
          • Scrap the testing.

Conclusion:

  • Not entirely sure what caused the leak in the first experiment
    • Since hole appeared after only 24 hrs, but second experiment showed that aluminum can last 3+ days of exposure
  • E85 doesn’t have any significant effect on aluminum in the short term scope.
    • Since Fuel will never be contained in fuel tank for longer than a few hours to a couple days, this should cause no issues if tank were to be made of aluminum.
