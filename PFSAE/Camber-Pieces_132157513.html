Purdue Formula SAE : Camber Pieces

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified by Unknown User (xia107) on Dec 05, 2021

xia107_PF22_SUS_2003_F_CAMBER_PIECE_dwg1.pdf


  • Approximate machining time: 15 hours for full set of 4 (start to finish including prepping stock)
  • Process:
    • Stock prep:
      • Cut off section of block that can fit all four pieces
      • Use fly cutter to make block have same height and width as camber pieces (recommended to make front and rears share these dimensions)
      • Cut block into 4 pieces for each camber piece
      • Use fly cutter or large end mill to make each block match outer dimensions of camber piece
    • Traffic cone hole:
      • Drill hole for traffic cones in each block
    • Slot
      • (need to be able to touch bottom with ball mill, but not remove material that needs to be part of the radius)
      • End mill slot down to approximately .050" above top of radius with large end mill (9/16). Leave at least .050 on each wall to be finished with ball end mill.
      • End mill smaller slot (3/8") to just above final depth (.020"). Do not go all the way to final depth if it will remove material that needs to be left as part of the radius.
      • End mill even smaller slot (1/4") to final depth. Set Z 0 by touching off of top of walls
      • Set Z 0 by touching off of top of walls. Bring ball mill tip to bottom of slot.
      • Step over slowly with ball mill (.020" approx) until wall thicknesses adequately reduced
        • Ball mill seemed a lot happier when climb milling
    • Angled face:
      • Use tall vice and sine bar to clamp part in at correct angle
      • Use sharpy to put stripes on piece just above vice so that any rotation will be caught
      • Use fly cutter with small passes (.020) to add angled face so that part does not rotate
    • Lug shape blend:
      • Print out drawing of lug face with 1:1 scale (measure to be sure!)
      • Trace shape on to part
      • Use band saw cut to close to correct profile
      • Finish on grinder
    • Lug Shape Blend & Slot (Alternative):
      • Using the CNC Mill for this is more doable 2021 season and forward. The basics of this is:
        • Contour with a flat and ball end mill for the fillet bottoms
        • Surface parallel for the lug shape, flat end mill and ball end mill
    • Bolt holes + counter bores
      • Put part on parallels on the angled face
      • Zero off of the edge of the top face and the side of the vice
      • Spot face with end mill, then center drill then drill
