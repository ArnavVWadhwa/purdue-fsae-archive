Purdue Formula SAE : Standard Team Bolts, Washers, and Nuts

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified by Unknown User (ehoerl) on Apr 01, 2021

The hardware indicated on this page (in reasonable length increments) will be made available to all subteams in the shop and during testing. Any hardware outside of these options is the responsibility of the subteam to keep on hand.

Example video including how to access the CAD library that has this hardware:

Note: a bolt is to be considered structural if it is attaching a component that is having FEA / or other structural analysis performed on it. A bolt is considered non structural if it is being used for retention only and has essentially negligible loading (often the inertial loading of a small component).

Examples:

  • Structural:
    • Suspension control arm attachment
    • Pedal box attachment
    • Aero wing and swan neck mounting
  • Non structural:
    • Floor pan connection (floor pan is held in place by surrounding tubes. The bolts are being used only for keeping it from being lifted upwards and out somehow)
    • Catch cans
    • Electronics switches

For structural analysis of the bolt, use the bolted joint and/or lug calculator. Links:

  • Bolt Margin Calculator
  • Lug Margin Calculator

Bolts


NAS 620X and 660X are interchangeable. Only difference is that NAS660X has slightly longer threads. NAS 660X is available in CAD as it is the larger of the two.

Naming example: NAS6203-20D

  • NAS62 indicates which type of bolt
  • 03 calls out #10 diameter bolt
  • -20 calls out 20/16 inch shank length
  • D adds drilled shank for castellated nut
