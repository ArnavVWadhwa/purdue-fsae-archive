Purdue Formula SAE : Steering PF23 | Design

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified by Dien Quang Nguyen on May 27, 2023

Minimum Viable Product Steering

  • General:
    • Follow all the rules, no rulebreakers allowed
    • Ensure all tolerances align with needs of other sub teams
    • Factor of safety as set by team
  • Mounting:
    • Minimal play, aim for ~.5° (of 7° allowable)
    • Steering wheel cannot go beyond FRH 
    • Assembly should be fully removable (installation/ removal shouldn't require welding or cutting)
    • Steering linkage mounting should be discussed with Sus team lead (possibility of Ackerman steering)
    • Wheel should be connected via quick disconnect
    • Support steering column in 3 locations
  • Ergonomics:
    • Wheel should be placed at optimal distance from driver (~12"-18", find optimal)
    • Reasonable required input force (Roughly 4-11Nm at standstill)
    • Quick disconnect must be activated by seated driver
    • Assembly should handle significant loads from driver / road [(660 N (150 lb.) lateral (radial) force) (100-135 Nm (75-100 ft.lb.) of torque)]
  • Packaging:
    • Ensure placement of rack, column and mounting don't interfere with driver
    • Pneumatic shifting on steering wheel should allow for full use of system and removal of wheel by driver
    • All sensitive parts should be covered for durability
  • Additional Goals:
    • Implement Ackerman steering
    • Fully removable mounting (using hardware)
    • Use Double U-Joint for ease of mounting
    • Adjustable steering?
