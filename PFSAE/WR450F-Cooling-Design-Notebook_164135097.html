Nov 29-Dec 5

  • MatLab Cooling Sim (not updated version) was experimented with in order to get a better understanding of how it worked and what inputs could be changed

  • Standard Sim run with basic 8"x6"x1.26" radiator with 787 fins per meter and 12 tubes at a width of 0.002 meters (look at image at bottom of this page for fin and tube reference)

  • No Radiator Sim run with 0.0001"x6"x1.26" radiator with 787 fins per meter and 12 tubes at a width of 0.002 meters
  • Question: "Why not put 0 for the core height?" Answer:  Matlab does not like zeros, which leads to occasional errors during computations. If a rad core of a height of 0" in put into MatLab, the maximum temperature is 90°C, which is less than it would be if there are no radiators. This does not make sense. Image below.

  • Engine Temperature with a radiator of 0"x6"x1.26" radiator with 787 fins per meter and 12 tubes at a width of 0.002 meters

      • Rads are slanted towards the front wheels to create "pockets" of air. This increases the pressure at the radiators, which makes cooling more efficient.

12/5 (cooling meeting notes)

  • WR450F cooling issues: The R6 engine provided enough electrical power to power virtually an size of radiator fan. The WR450F does not provide enough electrical power (3.8 amps max)to run two large fans, where new fan dimensions will need to be found.
  • MatLab models are OK for now. Preferably needs to validation in the real world.

Powertrain main meeting cooling notes:

  • What to work on: Focus on how thermometers and thermostats affect cooling process allowing for maximum cooling efficiency. Simulation may not take into placement location and type of pump (mechanical or electrical). Need to work on this for next meeting.
    • For maximum cooling efficiency, the best place to install the temperature probe would be at the intake manifold water jacket (right next to the thermostat housing).

Dec 6 - Dec 12

12/10 Cooling Sim Meeting

  • Cooling sim updates and breakdown to find what the best radiator is with given fan types. 
    • New plans are to add a sequential series to the simulation to give more accurate results. Currently assumes the radiators are preforming the same, where one has a fan on at all times and the other one is off unless fan 1 is non-functional.


PF22 Water Pump:

  • PF22 used a EWP80 water pump in order to cool the R6 engine. Due to how the R6 was a larger, 4-cylinder engine, a high coolant flow rate was needed. The WR450F is a smaller 450 CC, which should require a lower coolant flow rate than the R6.
    • Water Pump Calculations: Water_Pump Calculations.xlsx (Confluence cannot view Excel file, please download to view)
  • After simplified calculations, a smaller water pump model could be able to cool the WR450F engine. The Davies Craig has a Electric Boost Pump aimed to cool go-carts and motorbikes, model EBP23.

Fan Options:

  • Due to limited amps of engine, only a view Spal fan model could be found.
    • VA68-A100-83A (167mm)
    • VA75-A101-133A/SH (182mm)
    • VA37-A100-46A* (130mm) Slightly over amp limit
    • VA31-A102-45S (130mm)
    • VA31-A102-46A (130mm)

Dec 22 - Dec 28

Fan Setup:

Based from Dec 21 meeting, the proposed fuel draws around 4 amps, which reduces the cooling fan amp limit. Aim for this week is to discuss with team leads, cooling & fuel members which options would be optimal.

In order to improve the cooling efficiency of the car, the idea of ducting the radiators to one large fan has been proposed. This would allow both radiators to get the same amount of airflow through them, which could improve dual radiator efficiency. Issues with this concept would be difficulty sealing ducting system, difficulty accessing areas that are leaking air after car assembly, and impact of other systems do to ducting spacing and heat rejected (don't want to blow hot air on the already hot engine). CAD modeling with ducting should be able to give a better understanding how feasible this concept would be.

Coolant Catch Can

  • Trying to find best placement and container for coolant catch can
  • Calculations done to find that the cooling system with multiple factors of safety is 7.03 liters
    • Catch Can must be either 10% of total cooling system or 0.9 liters, whichever one is greater
    • 10% of 7.03 is 0.703 liters (< 0.9 liter minimum)
  • Catch Can must be 0.9 liters minimum size
  • Could be aluminum beer can

  • Could be 1 quart (0.946 liter) aluminum canteen


Coolant Thermostat

2019 WR450F does not contain a thermostat, which means it will take longer to warm up.

Solutions

  • Thermo-Bob Thermostat
    • Fairly high price
    • Only has Yamaha 2016-2019 YZ 450F which might not work exactly with the WR450F
      • Might need to request thermostat for WR5450F
    • Could take long time to get due to COVID, lead time is around 20 days estimate.
  • After discussing with team leads, PF22 will not have a thermostat.

Radiator Selection

  • Ran Cooling sim to lock down radiator size after getting CFD numbers from Aero
    • Found that the 9 x 6 is too small to effectively cool the engine
    • Also found that the simulation needs to be redone to be more accurate using more numbers from CFD
    • Decided that the 9 x 7 radiator will be our choice*
    • Once CFD data is complete from aero for new sim we will write that sim and double check that we will be able to effectively cool the engine with the 9 x 7




Radiator Positioning

  •  Radiators do not meet the rule requirements to reach under 350mm, which means they would need to be mount securely on the structure of the vehicle.
  • Options to fix this included
    • Tilt rads forward
    • Make rads smaller to place in better places
    • Make jigs for radiator tabs
    • Move rads backwards and tilted vertical so they do not meet this issues, results in potentially easier methods for areo to make carbon fiber layouts. Would negatively impact the areo flap performance.

Decision has been made that the radiators will be moved backwards to the center of the areo package swoop with a range of fully vertical to whatever angle the areo team finds the most efficient radiator placement.

Revision Descriptions

Revision 0: Reference of what PF20 had. Team will use WR450F instead of the R6 engine, which leads to revision 2 model. Radiator was a C&R racing custom radiator with dimensions 8"x6"x1.26", 787 fins per meter and 12 tubes at a width of 0.002 meters. This revision is the only one that has the R6 as reference, all others are the WR450F engine.

Revision 0.5: Similar setup as Revision 0, but this time with the WR450F and its built in mechanical water pump. Due to this smaller engine, less electrical power is created (3.8 amps limit) which makes it not possible to run 2x 5.2" Spal fans, which each consume 4.8 amps. This leads to Revision 1.

Revision 1: Rev 1 setup would theoretically cool the engine with all parts described, but are most likely not the most optimal options. There would be 2x 7.2" fans 3.3 amp, where one runs continuously with the other one off unless fan 1 is non-operational. There would be an internal water pump built into the WR450F engine what would be used to cool the engine with custom radiators. A risk with this setup is that the high current draw of one these fans is relatively high, as well as consuming battery power if the second fan needs to assist in cooling the engine.

Revision 2: Includes a EWP80 water pump, which is the same water pump used for PF20 engine. This provides a backup water pump in case the mechanical water pump leads to too hot engine temps.

Revision 3: Has a smaller, lighter EBP23 water pump when compared to the EWP80 that should still cool the smaller WR450F engine. This revision provides another backup water pump in case the mechanic pump leads to too hot engine temps. Look at PF22 Water_pump file above for calculation referencing (does not have any data on the WR450F water pump.)

Revision 4: System has smaller, lower amp fans than revision 1 that could both run all the time to assist cooling the engine without tapping into the battery power. Trade of is that selected fans provide less airflow than the larger fan of Rev 1. More research and calculations are in progress with selected fans for comparison purposes.

Revision 5: Cooling system would have ducting running from both radiators to 1 fan. This would allow both radiators to get the same amount of airflow through them, which could improve dual radiator efficiency. For a precaution, an additional fan could be placed in front of another one at a distance and would only be used if the first fan became in-operational.

Issues with this concept would be difficulty sealing ducting system, difficulty accessing areas that are leaking air after car assembly, and impact of other systems do to ducting spacing and where the heat rejected goes. CAD modeling with ducting should be able to give a better understanding how feasible this concept would be.

Revision 6: Has most likely setup for PF22. Two fans on car, with one on and the other as a back up.  Mechanical water pump & engine are already selected, purchased, and acquired. Based off of current areo input, the radiators will most likely be a 9"x7" core at 1.26" deep, but other areo simulation are in effect to compare smaller radiator sizes.

    • Catch Can must be either 10% of total cooling system or 0.9 liters, whichever one is greater
    • 10% of 7.03 is 0.703 liters (< 0.9 liter minimum)
  • Catch Can must be 0.9 liters minimum size
  • Could be aluminum beer can

Radiator Positioning

  •  Radiators do not meet the rule requirements to reach under 350mm, which means they would need to be mount securely on the structure of the vehicle.
  • Options to fix this included
    • Tilt rads forward
    • Make rads smaller to place in better places
    • Make jigs for radiator tabs
    • Move rads backwards and tilted vertical so they do not meet this issues, results in potentially easier methods for areo to make carbon fiber layouts. Would negatively impact the areo flap performance.

Decision has been made that the radiators will be moved backwards to the center of the areo package swoop with a range of fully vertical to whatever angle the areo team finds the most efficient radiator placement.

Revision Descriptions

Revision 0: Reference of what PF20 had. Team will use WR450F instead of the R6 engine, which leads to revision 2 model. Radiator was a C&R racing custom radiator with dimensions 8"x6"x1.26", 787 fins per meter and 12 tubes at a width of 0.002 meters. This revision is the only one that has the R6 as reference, all others are the WR450F engine.

Revision 0.5: Similar setup as Revision 0, but this time with the WR450F and its built in mechanical water pump. Due to this smaller engine, less electrical power is created (3.8 amps limit) which makes it not possible to run 2x 5.2" Spal fans, which each consume 4.8 amps. This leads to Revision 1.

Revision 1: Rev 1 setup would theoretically cool the engine with all parts described, but are most likely not the most optimal options. There would be 2x 7.2" fans 3.3 amp, where one runs continuously with the other one off unless fan 1 is non-operational. There would be an internal water pump built into the WR450F engine what would be used to cool the engine with custom radiators. A risk with this setup is that the high current draw of one these fans is relatively high, as well as consuming battery power if the second fan needs to assist in cooling the engine.

Revision 2: Includes a EWP80 water pump, which is the same water pump used for PF20 engine. This provides a backup water pump in case the mechanical water pump leads to too hot engine temps.

Revision 3: Has a smaller, lighter EBP23 water pump when compared to the EWP80 that should still cool the smaller WR450F engine. This revision provides another backup water pump in case the mechanic pump leads to too hot engine temps. Look at PF22 Water_pump file above for calculation referencing (does not have any data on the WR450F water pump.)

Revision 4: System has smaller, lower amp fans than revision 1 that could both run all the time to assist cooling the engine without tapping into the battery power. Trade of is that selected fans provide less airflow than the larger fan of Rev 1. More research and calculations are in progress with selected fans for comparison purposes.

Revision 5: Cooling system would have ducting running from both radiators to 1 fan. This would allow both radiators to get the same amount of airflow through them, which could improve dual radiator efficiency. For a precaution, an additional fan could be placed in front of another one at a distance and would only be used if the first fan became in-operational.

Issues with this concept would be difficulty sealing ducting system, difficulty accessing areas that are leaking air after car assembly, and impact of other systems do to ducting spacing and where the heat rejected goes. CAD modeling with ducting should be able to give a better understanding how feasible this concept would be.

Revision 6: Has most likely setup for PF22. Two fans on car, with one on and the other as a back up.  Mechanical water pump & engine are already selected, purchased, and acquired. Based off of current areo input, the radiators will most likely be a 9"x7" core at 1.26" deep, but other areo simulation are in effect to compare smaller radiator sizes.
