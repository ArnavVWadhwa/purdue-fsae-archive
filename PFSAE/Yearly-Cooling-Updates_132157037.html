Purdue Formula SAE : Yearly Cooling Updates

Created by Unknown User (kruege18), last modified on Nov 01, 2019

2020

New to 2020 we went to a dual rad, active cooled set up on purpose. Justification for this is that it enabled our required mass flow per side pod to drop significantly, and let the aero team increase front wing down force significantly - giving the car 5% more down force, but also allowing aero distribution to shift forward enough to actually achieve their distribution goals. Cooling weight only increased by 1.5 lbs, which was not enough to offset the gains from the aero package, which meant that this design decision was good. 

These radiators are 8x7x1.26 inch core, single pass radiators from CR Racing run in series. The fans are both 5.2 inch, 342 CFM fans from spal. (VA31-A101-46A). the pump is an EWP80 electric pump, and lines are 1in diameter. 

This cooling system was sized by our cooling sim, which was updated this year to include fan data/ proper radiator properties in CFD. Notes on this are in the Cooling Sim folder. Maximum predicted temperature was 107 degrees F, on a 95F day, with the water pump only operating at .15 kg/s.
