Design Finals:

  • Jason from SpaceX + Paul from Multimatic
    • talked about material selection, heat treatment
    • Paul feedback
      • think of creative loading cases (driver load cases/ mounting of tabs for pedal box, steering, ect)
      • look at 4130 post heat treatment strength 
  • Adam + Neal from SpaceX
    • talked about FEA on the frame, LLTD model, torsion test, load case stuff
    • ? was worried about the correlation between our torsion test model and physical results, thought 17% was pretty high (which the one probe was even higher than that and i don't think they got that)
    • don't think we did a good job explaining why that one probe was so far off (can't really claim it was an outlier when multiple points were out of line)

Design Review Feedback

  • Try to find the relationship between LLTD or Torsional Rigidity
  • Destructive testing with PF20 or PF22 on a chassis dyno could be used to figure out how much LLTD sensitivity or torsional rigidity is needed
    • Unknown User (hammerly) contact at ARC
  • Need better understanding of derivation of load cases and why
  • Aft bell crank cantilevered = bad
  • Funky torsion test results on one probe (better reason why/ retest)
  • Understanding torsion stiffness target and why we care (when do you make the tradeoff between weight and TR or LLTD) ← million dollar question
  • Be creative in rule changes

From talking with Carson Wilke after comp

  • Switch torsion rigidity correlation model from fixed to simply supported - minimize effect of support compliance on results / avoid over constraining model
  • Figure out how to build chassis compliance into lap sim

Powertrain

Preliminary Design:

  • Our judge was from Polaris
    • During prelims he seemed nonchalant
    • Ended up having him again for finals and interaction went better
    • Also talked with him for feedback and even more after "off the record" and he ended up being a very cool dude and gave us advice 
  • Seemed very knowledgeable about engine simulation/WAVE and asked quite a few in depth questions
  • Took time before the presentation to look over the powertrain on our car and get a sense for it
    • Already knew about these:
      • Cam position sensor
      • Knock Sensor
      • Oil pressure/temp sensor
      • Was looking at/touching injector and MAP sensor

Design Finals:

  • Many judges were repeats from either May comp or June prelims
  • Judge from Cummins went in depth with the drivetrain and Carson worked his way through it with him like he did last time with the valve events questions from May design finals
    • Was a little tripped up towards the end when the numbers Frank gave us ended up showing that we are spinning the tires all the way through first and second
      • Wasn't a huge problem, Carson was able to explain his way through it
  • Had the same judge from Roush who asked the same questions
    • Much more prepared this time as this judge's questions were specifically noted from May to improve on
  • Carson worked through the efficiency problem with Skitter (and got his first place vote!)
    • The basic question was: What is the most efficient way to get from one point to another in a straight line?
      • Carson answered: Accelerate hard in the beginning then turn the engine off and coast to the finish (Williams, Carson Kenneth confirm this is correct, this is just what I remember, there was much more including the coffee creamer example)
  • Lots of judges asked about launch control
    • Not a bad thing since Carson understands it and is able to explain everything about it, just interesting that 3-4 separate judges asked about it
  • Was tripped up with the judge asking about octane ratings
    • Asked which fuel has more energy, high or low octane
    • Carson, Dom, and Tyler all agreed that high octane fuel has more energy, which was wrong
      • The correct answer is that higher octane fuel actually has less energy, but because of that, it can be burned more effectively, creating more power.
  • Polaris judge liked the knock sensor, but thought it could be calibrated in a safer way (not throwing a bunch of timing at the engine and letting it knock to find the frequencies)
    • This was "off the record" after design feedback
    • He explained a different way of calibrating it (I can't remember exactly how he said to do it, hopefully Carson does)
  • Polaris judge was also interested in the MAP sensor
    • Surprised that we don't see fluctuations
    • Pulled up M1 tune to see the frequency at which we record MAP (I think it was him that did this)
  • I can't remember where these judges were from but Carson had a good conversation with them about... (can't remember right now)
