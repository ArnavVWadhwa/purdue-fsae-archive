Purdue Formula SAE : Throttle Response

Created by Unknown User (dnocon), last modified by Williams, Carson Kenneth on Apr 10, 2021

The old tune when under heavy throttle from low rpm's creates plateaus, also called "breaking up"

The driver's can also feel this response and describe it as a "gurgling" feeling.

This is due to the tune being so rich its actually causing the spark plugs to foul out and not ignite the fuel.

New tune with unfinished wall wetting tuning climbs steadily, because it is not firing the injectors fully at low rpms and throttle positions for both fuel efficiency and better throttle response.


More wall wetting tuning coming soon.
