Steps for completing a project at the BIDC 

  1. Look through the BIDC "Get Started" page (https://www.purdue.edu/bidc/get-started/)
    1. The "Get Started" page has nearly all the information you need to know, so give it a good look!
  2. Download the Fusion 360 Student license for free (https://www.autodesk.com/campaigns/education/fusion-360)
  3. Complete all the safety tests needed through passport ( https://www.openpassport.org/Account/Login?ReturnUrl=%2fGroup%2fPurdueBIDC )
    1. The system is pretty slow, so be patient.
  4. Create a project proposal using the BIDC project management system ( https://www.purdue.edu/bidc/os1/project/ )
    1. Make sure anyone who will be coming in to work on your project are listed as "members" in your project proposal
    2. Only "members" of a project can be a part of the consultations and/or reservations. 
  5. Join the BIDC Discord team (https://discord.com/invite/TSaw2wY )
    1. The "front desk" voice channel is a great resource for consultations and questions in general.
    2. The "general-chat" is also a good resource.
  6. Join the BIDC Fusion 360 team: Get on discord and join the front desk meeting room, then ask them to be added to the BIDC Fusion team. You should then get an email with a link to join the team.
  7. Create a New Project on the BIDC Fusion team ( make sure it is secret ) 
  8. Create CAM for the part you would like to make and get it approved by Crooks, Troy A , or by a manufacturing TA (you can go to discord and join the waiting room and they will direct you to a TA) 
  9. Create a Reservation on the machine you plan to use, and come prepared to machine your part. 
    1. Manufacturing TAs will help you create your reservations during your consults.
