Purdue Formula SAE : Reason Behind Decrease in Separation with Increase in a1

Created by Zhang, Chenkai on Apr 21, 2023

The SST model blends k-e and k-w together and introduces a viscosity limiter, which serves to reduce overprediction of flow separation.

While the default choice of a1 value of 0.31 seems like a product of empirical correlation, there is actually a deeper meaning to it.

a1 value of 0.31 incorporates Bradshaw's assumption that there is a linear relationship between shear stress and turbulent kinetic energy:

As seen in the text above, applying Bradshaw's relation enforces the equilibrium of turbulent kinetic energy product and dissipation. While this is the case in thin shear flow, it is not the case in boundary layers with high adverse pressure gradient. In fact, in those types of flows, turbulent kinetic energy production is much greater than dissipation. Therefore, to better predict flow separation with high adverse pressure gradient, as seen pretty much everywhere on aerodynamic surfaces of an FSAE car, a1 value must be increased. Going through the equations show that with an increase a1, turbulent kinetic energy production will exceed dissipation, matching the physics of flow.
