Project Scope

this page is meant to define your problem and give a short problem statement, and define your system boundaries. What exactly do the hubs do? What other systems to you interact with? 


Design Requirements

  1. Rules NEED to be considered. What type of fasteners do the hubs need to have? What does this entail when selecting your fasteners? Do our hub nuts need to be hard anodized? What about the hubs? Why or why not? etc.
  2. What are the thermal requirements of the system? this is where to define them. just whipped up this confluence page for temp stickers from pf23. Temp Stickers from PF23 
  3. I think it's reasonable to shoot for no compliance in the system with this. This will help you decide your tolerances and help with tolerance stackups later in the design season. 
    1. also think about servicability with this statement and incorportate it. like we can just pressfit the bearings onto the hub but we obo don't want 
  4. why exactly floating disc brake? did we discuss this with the brakes team? is that what they are going with? if so, how much play do we need to support? its a team effort between hubs and brakes that the floating allowable slop metric is met.
  5. maybe a little more specific with the drive shaft comment, something like "properly constrains halfshaft under all operating conditions".
  6. not only do you want to minimize your weight, but thinking about the fact that the hub is a rotational component, where do you want the majority of the weight to be? 

Design Overview 

  1. I would do the exploration, and present your findings during the DCR. Give yourself a great idea of what bearing we will be using in the design and why
  2. This would tie into number 4 from above, working with brakes to define the actual floating value
  3. Asymmetrical hub, maybe? only if you guys find something that is REALLY worth it. otherwise, keep it symmetrical so each hub manufacturing is more worth it if that makes sense
  4. meeting design criteria and constraints is a given
  5. Reference the video I will do on Safety factors for this, ill explain how they work in greater detail and why they may not necessarily apply here.
