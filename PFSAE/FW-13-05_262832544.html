Purdue Formula SAE : FW-13-05

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 30, 2023

Simulation: CZ-010

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Suppressor optimization

Reduced forward element chord to reduce lift generated.

Reduce both element angle of attack to reduce losses and reduce lift generated.

Increased slot gap size from the first to the second element to delay vortex merging and prevent the first vortex from making lift on the second element.

Increased first element leading edge radius to reduce losses and lift generated.

Overall vortex strength will get lower, but should not be too significant.

Strake optimization

Increased leading edge inwash angle to align better with incoming flow.

Removed outwash angle on the trailing edge. It is still outwashing, just by less. This is done to reduce vortex strength across the strake and reduce wake size and inwash behind it.

Added a little curl to the bottom of the strake. This gives the vortex formed a non-vertical surface to act on to produce some downforce. The curve itself combined with the upward pressure gradient and velocity should produce a suction on its own. The curl also reduces the angle through which flow has to negotiate compared to a straight strake, reduce losses in the vortex.Â 

The other side of the curl also gives a surface for the outer vortex to act on, which will reduce net downforce, but I think it downforce is still going to increase overall.
