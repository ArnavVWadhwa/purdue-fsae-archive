Purdue Formula SAE : Meshing Strategy

Created by Zhang, Chenkai on Mar 18, 2023

Redoing meshing strategy for fsaeSTAR V6.4 after Stellantis wind tunnel and mesh independence study.

Pros:

  • Polyhedral cells can produce the same results as trimmed cells with a much lower cell count.
  • According to previous studies, polyhedral cells tend to produce lower residual
  • Polyhedral cells shows two sides of the main roll hoop wake do not merge, resulting in 2 larger separation bubbles on rear wing. This is consistent with what was observed in wind tunnel.
  • Although the boundary condition change makes this not necessarily representative, polyhedral cells showed better flow attachment in the diffuser and the side tunnel, which is consistent with what was observed in wind tunnel.

Cons:

  • Uses more memory
  • The 2D slices won't look as good since the cells are not organized in the cartesian coordinate system
