ORIGINAL BURNDOWN (More Detailed Steps)

TaskStatus

Rotors:

  • Sizing (X/XX)

Depends on the maximum braking force needed to stop the car and the driver's maximum force able to push on the brake pedal

      • (X/XX) Figure out range of possible rotor sizes given CAD packaging from PF20
      • (X/XX)Figure out all equations needed to accurately determine the rotor size
      • (X/XX)Figure out all unknowns that need to come from other subsystems
      • (X/XX)Apply all variables and solve equations for rotor size
      • (X/XX)Run this result past someone else to confirm validity
  • Material Selection (X/XX)

Need to find a material that is compatible with the chosen brake pads (Composite Metallic for GP200 in front and AP CP4226 pad's in rear that we have) and that has material properties that are consistent and sufficient for the given application

      • (X/XX)Compile list of possible materials that are compatible with the given brake pads
      • (X/XX)From above list, deep dive into how effective these materials are with different types of stresses, thermal effects etc.  Narrow down list to a couple best candidates
      • (X/XX)Check material costs and check with money people that a given material is economically feasible
      • (X/XX)Run thermal and stress simulations on best candidate materials for rotors and make engineering decision based on evidence on which material to choose
      • (X/XX)Run this past someone to confirm validity
  • Drill Pattern (X/XX)

Drill pattern is primarily to pull cool air into the brake rotors and help maintain low temperatures in the rotors under heavy breaking.  The drill pattern does add manufacturing complexity and potential for fractures in the rotor

      • (X/XX)Review research on how drill patterns affect performance, review previous PF brake's reasoning, choose 2 or 3 options to test
      • (X/XX)Check material properties from above and ensure that drilling is feasible structurally with the given material
      • (X/XX)Check with someone who knows manufacturing whether the design is possible to manufacture
  • Stress Analysis (X/XX)

Stress analysis will be essential in ensuring that the brake rotors will not fail due to cracks and other structural issues when in use

      • (X/XX)Test the 2 or 3 designs from the drill pattern selection above and ensure all designs are structurally sound and potentially viable for use
      • (X/XX)Run the conclusions past someone to confirm validity
  • Thermal Analysis (X/XX)

Thermal analysis will be the most important and final determining factor in the feasibility of the rotor design.  Given that the above steps have provided a rotor that is structurally sound and compatible with the given pads, this step will result in the best possible design for the rotor

      • (X/XX)Perform thermal analysis on the 2 or 3 rotor designs after being finalized in the stress analysis
      • (X/XX)Make an engineering decision based on the simulation results which design is the best to use as the final design
      • (X/XX)Confirm with someone for validity
  • CAD (X/XX)

CAD should be self explanatory, model the part designed previously, apply CAD best practices, ensure compatibility with other sub teams and subsystems

      • (X/XX)Preliminary CAD model
      • (X/XX)Check with someone for validity
      • (X/XX)Finalize CAD model
  • Manufacturing (X/XX)

Figure out the best way to manufacture the part to optimize performance, minimize expensive and time to make



Carriers:

  • Material Selection (X/XX)

Material selection will be crucial in ensuring that the carrier is able to be lightweight but also able to withstand any forces applied on it by the rotor or wheel

      • (X/XX)Research what materials carriers are typically made of and why.  Look at previous PF cars and what material was used and why
      • (X/XX)Create a list of 2-3 materials that seem to be best fits based on research
  • Mounting points (X/XX)

Given rotor design above, figure out how many and where the mounting points for the carrier will be

      • (X/XX)Review what the carrier needs to do.  What kind of forces will be present, how a carrier is designed, look at old designs and understand why things were chosen
      • (X/XX)Figure out how the carrier will connect to the wheel and where the mounting points will need to be
  • Bobbin Design (X/XX)

Need to do research to better understand the role of bobbins and how to go about designing them

  • CAD (X/XX)

Create model of carrier and ensure proper compatibility with rotor and whatever it connects to on the wheel

      • (X/XX)Create a preliminary design that lines up with mountain points on rotor
      • (X/XX)Edit design to minimize weight and maximize structural integrity
      • (X/XX)Run stress analysis and confirm/reiterate design
      • (X/XX)Finalize design
      • (X/XX)Confirm with someone for validity
  • Material Stress Analysis (X/XX)

Performing stress analysis will take the 2-3 materials from material selection and give a final choice of what material will be used for the carrier

      • (X/XX)Perform stress analysis on finalized CAD model to determine which material is best to use for the carrier
      • (X/XX)Confirm with someone for validity
  • Manufacturing (X/XX)

Figure out the best way to manufacture the part to optimize performance, minimize expensive and time to make

  • CAD (X/XX)

  • Manufacture/Ensure Manufacturability
