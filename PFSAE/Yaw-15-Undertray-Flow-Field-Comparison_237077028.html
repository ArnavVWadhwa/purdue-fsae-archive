Purdue Formula SAE : Yaw 15 Undertray Flow Field Comparison

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Sep 23, 2022
  • A big design focus this year is to work with front wing and undertray pressure distribution to make to improve yaw sensitivity of the undertray
  • Traditionally we have suffered in undertray yaw 15 performance due to the lack of flow conditioning by front wing
  • We balanced this out with a performance loss in rear wing in straightline to achieve yaw insensitivity
    • Problem is this strategy causes significant aero balance changes in crosswind
  • Below is a comparison of flow field as design progressed

PF22: Most flow misses the undertray. Used wide inlet to force capture the air flow

UT-EF-23 (07/18): A combination of centerloaded front wing and UT inlet strake straightened flow out significantly more than PF22. This UT was used as a baseline quite a period of time.

CZ-201 (09/09): FW has been changed to improve vortex breakdown characteristics, improve flow conditioning upstream of undertray. Undertray has twisted inlet to force inwash. Undertray throat shaped for flow straightening as well as controlling inwashing characteristics of sidewing.

FW-10-26-DP3 (09/22): Front wing endplate underwent modification to further reduce vortex breakdown to help undertray. Front wing diveplanes optimized to help undertray by controlling sidewing inwash.Â
