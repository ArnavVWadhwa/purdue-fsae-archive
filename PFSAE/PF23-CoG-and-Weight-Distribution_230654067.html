Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 CoG and Weight Distribution

Created by MacNab, Zachary Robert, last modified on Jul 21, 2022

From Ben's Article: 

CG Height


Pretty straightforward with the lap time simulation. Lower CG improves skidpad, autocross, and endurance, but worsens acceleration. Non acceleration events outweigh acceleration. So this is a sensitivity that can be traded against weight, downforce, etc.

CG Longitudinal Position


Another fairly straightforward lap sim application, but the answer is not intuitive. The lap sim indicates that the ideal balance or maximum points is ~44% front distribution. However, the change in points from there to a 52% front distribution is less than two. What does change significantly is where the points come from. With a further forward-shifted cg position, more points come from autocross, skidpad, and endurance vs acceleration. FIXME


My input:

CoG Height

We would be using PF20's CoG height for our Adam's sims. 

CoG Longitudinal Position

This is interesting. The lap-sim does not include transient response, so I am weary to trust this statement. While it is very interesting to see that the lap-sim says that 52% is better for autocross, skidpad, and endurance, I would like to see this validated with ADAMS rather than the lapsim. The only problem is that ADAMS doesn't give us points, so I believe we would just be going with a much better lateral acceleration capability. 

Total Weight

I know that we are going to be lighter for PF23, so I think we should use our target mass (~460lbs.) while keeping CoG of PF20. 

Might be worth making a spreadsheet like this one found in Chapter 1 of Race Car Design by Derek Seward
