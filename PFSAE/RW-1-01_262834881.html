Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Reduce upper endplate area

Continuing with the same lower endplate, and altering the top end plate by reducing the total area. The notch is cut out such that two vortices should form.

The idea behind this is that the two vortices are weaker than the single vortex resulting in less drag. Alternatively, when the vortices combine there is potential for additional drag due to mixing. Not entirely sure which direction it will go so I am trying it out.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

We can see a loss in RW downforce due to the additional dirty flow above the main element, indicating that the endplate is too low. Additionally, a vortex hardly forms at the beginning section, resulting in very little merging. Going to scrap this idea for now and go with a more conventional design to move forward with. (2nd image shows the full first vortex)


We can see the streaks of blue indicating a vortex does form, but it's putting unneeded substantial loads on the endplate. Another reason to stick with a more conventional route.Â 

Also, the dive plane section of the lower endplate exhibits a different vortex structure, important to ensure this is consistent moving forward.
