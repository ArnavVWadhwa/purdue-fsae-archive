Purdue Formula SAE : Pneumatics/Shifting Research

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified by Albanese, Katherine on Jul 16, 2023

Pneumatics

  • Custom pneumatic system
    • PF23 was the first year using pneumatics
    • feedback/suggestions on PF23 shifting

      • consider adding a manual clutch in addition to pneumatic clutch to help newer drivers get up to speed

      • consider using a different regulator instead of the palmers pursuit one. That reg was really finicky to work with / unreliable

      • look into harsh environment / high temp tubing. This year's tubing had a glass transition temp of 90 C so when hot oil got on it, we had a blowouts

    • option for values are MAC valves, not too expensive

Electronic

  • Klicktronic electronic shifter
    • two buttons to actuate shifting (up and down - dual acting) - cold alter to be paddle shifters
    • "is very fast and consistent – typically 35 – 50 milliseconds on Japanese gearboxes"
    • 799 euros - $1200
    • doesn't actuate clutch
  • Custom electronic shifter
    • electronic cylinder for up/down shift & either additional cylinder or manual clutch
    • potential for manual and electronic clutch?
    • Yamaha quick shifter kit - https://yamaha-motor.com/p/gytr-yzf-r7-quick-shifter-kit aka commercial actuator for upshifting 

Manual

  • push-pull cable
    • PF20 & PF22 both used push pull cables - PF22 had some issues with breaking cables likely due to exceeding the force rating


Tubing

  • Needing tubes that can resist higher temps (specifically hot oil) some options here but uncertain how high of a temp would need to be withstood (aim for 70C/158F?)
