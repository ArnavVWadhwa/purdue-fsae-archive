Purdue Formula SAE : Flow Vis Mixture Testing

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 07, 2023

1:1.5 TiO2 to Vegetable Oil Mix

  • Did not flow
  • Flows a little under gravity on step surfaces

1:2 TiO2 to Vegetable Oil Mix

  • Did not flow
  • Flows under gravity

1:3 TiO2 to Vegetable Oil Mix

  • Flowed a little bit but not enough to be useful
  • Flows very easily under gravity even on mildly sloped surface
  • Does not drop from the upside down surface
  • Does not dry very quickly (two trips of maybe 5 minutes did not dry it)
  • Dots needs to be moderately sized
    • Too small it doesn't flow
    • Too big it flows too easily under gravity
  • Very easily influenced by inertial forces, even mild turns can cause flow to go sideways
  • Picture 1: top of car, in front of rear wind shield. Inwash observed which makes sense given the suction at generated by the roof
  • Picture 2: rear wind shield. The dot was pretty small, that's probably one reason why it didn't get sheared too much. Another reason might be due to the thick boundary layer generated by the panel gap
  • Picture 3: right hand side of the hood. Outwash generated pressurization in front the the front wind shield. Top dots did not get sheared due to the size of the dots and closeness to the wind shield
  • Picture 4: top of car again
  • Picture 5: whole hood. Outwash profile can kind of be seen, but with larger dots in places and their placements, it should be even better. The dots near the middle that forms a vertical line is weird due to the size of the dots being too big, and they flowed under gravity and inertial forces when turning.
  • Picture 6: flow vis flowing under gravity too much to be of any use

1:3 TiO2 to Mineral Oil

  • Lower viscosity than vegetable oil, but viscosity is still quite high
  • Worked better than vegetable oil

Mystery Meat Stellantis Mix

  • Stellantis' mix consists of an oil that is not being made anymore. The viscosity is close to but still higher than that of water.Â 
  • Flow very well under gravity
  • To apply on vertical surfaces like rear wing flaps, apply a big blob on the leading edge. If the wind is strong enough, it will flow with air flow
  • When approaching a separation line, flow viz puddles

Further Testing

  • Need low viscosity oil, but vegetable oil and mineral oil has too high of viscosity
  • Paraffin, low viscosity motor oil, and kerosene could all work
  • Don't worry about dyes, they make it look pretty, but don't do anything
