Purdue Formula SAE : Aerodynamics DCR

Created by Fava, Nicola Bernardo, last modified by Zhang, Chenkai on Aug 01, 2022

Project Scope

  • Design an aero package that produce enough downforce to offset the drag loss and weight loss
  • Aero package needs to have good drivability, which includes
    • Insensitivity to crosswind
    • No sudden loss of downforce in the operating window of the car

Stakeholders

  • Powertrain
    • Front wing wake reduces peak mass flow potential for cooling
    • Need mass flow and radiator combos from powertrain so radiator sizing and positioning can happen
    • Radiator sizing and position can negatively affect sidewing and undertray performance
      • There is one spot on the side wing where it is insensitive to crosswind
      • The bigger the radiators, the less we can use that position for downforce generation
      • The position in question is also hugely important in rear tire wake and squirt management
        • Both of which are big factors in undertray performance
    • The design of engine intake affects rear wing performance drastically
  • Chassis
    • There are certain chassis designs that aero favors, including
      • High bulkhead: raised bulkhead allows for more center loaded front wing, increasing overall downforce potential and reducing induced drag. High bulkhead also allows more air flow to the undertray
      • High minimum ride height: The higher the minimum ride height, the more design freedom we have, resulting in higher aero performance
    • Chassis needs to accommodate aero mounting
    • Aero rule box in the rear wing area is bounded by the headrest mounting plate
    • Size of the headrest massively affects rear wing performance
  • SIQ
  • Suspension
    • A compromise needs to be reached between suspension performance and aero operating window
      • The smaller the operating window, the more we can optimize the aero
    • Suspension geometry on the rear is the limiting factor in the diffuser expansion we can achieve
    • Front suspension can negatively affect flow downstream to radiators and side wings
    • Aero rule box in the sidewings area is bounded the tires
    • Size of the tires will also determine tire wake and squirt geometry, both of which are integral parts of aero design

Design Requirements

  • Rules
  • Vehicle Performance
    • Produce enough downforce due to losses in drag and added weight
  • Unknown requirements
    • Need mass flow target and radiator combos from powertrainÂ 
    • Suspension geometry
      • Affects diffuser exit height and therefore undertray performance
      • Affects flow downstream to the sidewings area where sidewings and radiators are located
    • Tire size
      • Affects tire wake and squirt geometry, both of which are integral to the design of the aero package
      • Affects the size of the aero rule box
    • Suspension stiffness
      • Determines the operating window of the aero package in terms of ride height, pitch, and roll
    • Need intake geometry from powertrain to properly design rear wing, and by extension, front wing and undertray
      • Rear wing performance determines front wing downforce target
      • Front wing geometry in turn determines to flow structures fed into sidewings and undertray
      • Depending on undertray geometry, undertray performance and rear wing performance could be coupled
    • Need headrest and main roll hoop geometry from chassis to properly design rear wing, and by extension, front wing and undertray

Design Overview

FW

  • Balance out downforce generated by the rear wing
  • Condition the flow for both undertray and rear wing to produce maximum overall downforce
  • If necessary, sacrifice performance to meet cooling target

UT/SW

  • Utilizing the under side of the vehicle as well as the space between the tires to generate downforce
  • This is the most aerodynamically efficient part of the aero package
  • If necessary, sacrifice performance to meeting cooling target

RW

  • Generate as much downforce as possible without creating too much drag

Cooling

  • Based on mass flow target and radiator combos provided by powertrain, we can determine which combo is best for performance
