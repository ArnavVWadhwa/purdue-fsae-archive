Purdue Formula SAE : 0. Steering Relevant Rules

Created by Rumsey, Helen Nicole, last modified by Singhal, Ria on Aug 26, 2022

Relevant updates from 2023 rules draft

Rule titleSpecificationsInterpretation / Comment
F.5.14Steering protection - Steering system racks or mounting components that are external to the Primary Structure must be protected from frontal impact. The protective structure must:
a. Meet F.3.2.1.n or Equivalent
b. Go to the vertical limit of the steering component(s)
c. Go to the local width of the chassis 		Steering rack needs a protective structure to protect from frontal impact.
Rule titleSpecification V.3.2 - SteeringInterpretation / Comment
V.3.2.1The Steering Wheel must be mechanically connected to the front wheels. Steering wheel must be physically connected to steering assembly. ie. Only physical actuation methods
V.3.2.2Electrically actuated steering of the front wheels is prohibited.Specifies Rule V.3.2.1 above
V.3.2.3Steering systems must use a rigid mechanical linkage capable of tension and compression loads for operation.Must handle significant frontal and rearward forces from driver and road forces
V.3.2.4The steering system must have positive steering stops that prevent the steering linkages from locking up (the inversion of a four-bar linkage at one of the pivots). The stops may be placed on the uprights or on the rack and must prevent the wheels and tires from contacting suspension, bodywork, or Chassis during the track events.Motion of steering must be restricted via stops to prevent tire interference with car and steering from locking up (insufficient for end of rack to be all)
V.3.2.5Allowable steering system free play is limited to seven degrees (7°) total measured at the steering wheel.Limit play from wheel to tires, ensure doesn't exceed 7°
V.3.2.6The steering rack must be mechanically attached to the Chassis.Mount steering securely - explore mounting options where entire rack can be installed / removed without welding or cutting
V.3.2.7Joints between all components attaching the Steering Wheel to the steering rack must be
mechanical and be visible at Technical Inspection. Bonded joints without a mechanical backup
are not permitted.		All connections should be secure and have sufficient safety factor. (bonding / secondary retention mostly needed for carbon fiber or non-welded joints) Explore carbon fiber, will most likely just weld for simplicity
V.3.2.8Fasteners in the steering system are Critical Fasteners, see T.8.2.Refer to T.8.2. Will most likely use NAS bolts.
V.3.2.9Spherical rod ends and spherical bearings in the steering must meet V.3.1.5 above.Must be mounted in double shear 
and / or captured by having a screw/bolt head or washer with an outside diameter that is larger 
than spherical bearing housing inside diameter
V.3.2.10Rear wheel steering may be used.
a. Rear wheel steering must incorporate mechanical stops to limit the range of angular
movement of the rear wheels to a maximum of six degrees (6°).
b. The team must provide the ability for the steering angle range to be verified at Technical
Inspection with a driver in the vehicle.
c. Rear wheel steering may be electrically actuated.		Rear wheel steering is an option, must have stops and limited to 6° but may be electrically actuated. Most likely too complex to justify time gains, could look into anyway.
Rule titleSpecification V.3.3 - Steering WheelInterpretation
V.3.3.1In any angular position, the Steering Wheel must meet T.1.4.2.Steering wheel must not exceed any point outside Front cockpit Hoop.
V.3.3.2The Steering Wheel must be attached to the column with a quick disconnect.Use similar connection to last years
V.3.3.3The driver must be able to operate the quick disconnect while in the normal driving position
with gloves on. 		ensure enough clearance for use by driver (also consider pneumatic shifting possibly)
V.3.3.4The Steering Wheel must have a continuous perimeter that is near circular or near oval.
The outer perimeter profile may have some straight sections, but no concave sections. “H”,
“Figure 8”, or cutout wheels are not allowed.		Could use similar steering wheel to last year. **If pneumatic shifting is used, consider new steering wheel, and possible mounting to accommodate**
Other NotesSteering assembly should have minimal play
Steering assembly should have reasonable required input force
