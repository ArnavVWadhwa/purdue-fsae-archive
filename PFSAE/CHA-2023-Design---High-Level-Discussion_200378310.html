Comments:

I don't entirely buy the idea of a bulkhead raise being super critical for aero, but it would be nice! We tried something along these lines earlier and the results weren't spectacular, granted the analysis behind that was with some incredibly blunt pencils. More recently I think we've found things with front wing design that can still get enough mass flow underneath the car even with the current bulkhead height.


The bigger constraint is towards the center and rear of the chassis (image below is a cross section at the symmetry plane). Right now we have very little control over undertray profile underneath chassis, which is the region of the car where you have the best possible flow conditions you can get for underbody downforce. 


Diffuser geometry is somewhat constrained by where that last chassis tube is. There's a limit to diffuser angle before you get too much flow separation, so if that tube is going to be close to the ground....so is the end of the diffuser (limits your downforce), raising that rear tube would open up options with what we can do with diffuser geometry.


Ideally we want the lowest point of the undertray to be somewhat forward biased, and well forward of where the lowest point of the chassis currently is. Can't really do that now with how low that point of the chassis is, so you either need to have a section of undertray that's essentially flat with constant ground clearance and very little curvature (there's zero aerodynamic reason to have an undertray if it's flat), or line up the lowest point of your undertray with where the lowest point of the chassis currently is (very bad for tyre wake ingestion, and diffuser stall characteristics). Moving that up a little bit would also be very useful, especially combined with a raise of that rear chassis tube. Would help with pressure recovery, dealing with rear tyre wakes, and aero sensitivities to crosswinds and ride height.
