Aerodynamics in FSAE

  • Readings
    • Fundamentals of Aerodynamics, John Anderson - Chapters 1-6, 15-16.3, 16.5-18.2, 18.6-20.5 (i.e. all the incompressible stuff)
      • More or less the standard for fluid mechanics. If you have a strong interest or want to get ahead for AAE 333/334, read this
      • If interested, do practice problems (as boring as it might be) to make sure that you understand the concepts fully
      • This goes into more of the theoretical basis of understanding fluid dynamics in general. It's definitely a great read to get an advanced grasp on Aerodynamics in general
      • https://drive.google.com/file/d/1PGmsoeyt1AKRLfFOx1NLyiVLIbVnyfd7/view?usp=sharing
    • Aerodynamics powerpoint from OptimumG
      • More racecar specific aerodynamics
      • Sums up pretty much all the racecar aerodynamics book you will find out there
      • https://drive.google.com/file/d/0B0-nr_DqNK0fQmlBMmNnUkhIRm

    • Doug McLean's Understanding Aerodynamics

      • Best aerodynamics book out there and my favorite book. Period.
      • Goes more in depth than any class you are likely to take unless you are planning on doing PhD
      • Covers all the fundamentals
      • Very hard to read. Need strong understanding of aerodynamics.
      • https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1D1cft6hZINrV7TKrEdWTicOx0dXCzxmh
    • High Lift Aerodynamics
      • Bible of high lift aerodynamics
      • Must read according to many F1 aerodynamists
      • Goes in details about Stratford recovery and multielement wings
      • https://charles-oneill.com/aem614/ReferenceMaterial/A+M+O+Smith+HIGH+LIFT+AERODYNAMICS.pdf

Computational Fluid Dynamics

  • Wall Y+ paper

    • This is probably a little too much a little too fast, but I can’t stress it enough how important Wall Y+ is for what we do. 

    • https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Szv-VLPxsEjGfV-M8iMg5glbDK1tW1DA/view?usp=sharing

  • Fluid Mechanics 101
    • Fantastic YouTube channel covering CFD from a user's perspective (which doesn't really exist in any of Purdue class, at least in AAE)
    • Watch everything, like everything, it's that good
    • https://www.youtube.com/@fluidmechanics101 

  • CFD Online 

    • Basically a website tailored fully towards people running CFD simulations, with good resources, books, forums to ask questions. If someone can’t answer a specific question then it has very likely been asked on the forums page

    • https://www.cfd-online.com/

Composites

  • Mechanics of Composite Materials
    • This book is a must read for anyone who wants to dive into more into the world of composites and structural analysis of composites 
    • https://drive.google.com/file/d/1eiBc6lggZNcQukaztbVO7rhqW_g1mRq3/view?usp=sharing

  • U Washington CLT Examples

    • Practice for CLT. Use this as further test cases for your CLT script if you make one.
    • https://drive.google.com/file/d/1yDz-0vCxFT13OgVjLUSViLQfIw5Koqk7/view?usp=sharing
