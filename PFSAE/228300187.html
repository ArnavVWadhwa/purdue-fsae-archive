Purdue Formula SAE : Cooling Pg. 3: DCR

Created by Horton, Tyler Jason, last modified on Jul 22, 2022

LINK TO DCR PRESENTATION


Project Scope:

  • Provide enough cooling capacity so the coolant temperature stays below the safe maximum. This should be done without needing to reduce the power the engine is producing. 

Stakeholders:

    • Powertrain
      • The cooling system will work to cool the engine
    • Chassis
      • Radiators and other cooling system components will be mounted to the chassis through tabs and other methods
    • Aero
      • The aero team will be providing us an air mass-flow number from CFD for the cooling sim so we can size the radiators
      • They will also handle the ducts/sidepods
    • SIQ
      • SIQ will be involved with selecting and implementing a coolant temperature sensor(s)
      • Also involved with the selection and implementation of an electric water pump

Design Requirements:

  • Rules
  • Vehicle Performance
    • Want to keep the coolant temp under 100C
    • Do not want to rely on fans 
      • Keep electrical consumption down
      • Still keep car cool if fans fail for some reason
    • Want to keep system as light as possible
    • Keep the radiators as small as possible to reduce drag on the car
    • Keep everything well-packaged to make assembly and maintenance easy
  • Unknown requirements
    • How much heat we will have to get rid of
    • Air mass flow
    • Water mass flow

Design Overview:

Rough Concept:

The rough plan is to have two radiators in parallel to cool the coolant from the engine. We will need to use an electric water pump since the stock pump will be removed to make room for the dry sump oil system. We will have a swirl pot to remove air from the system, as well as a catch can to collect water that overflows from the system as the coolant heats up. 

Components:

    • Two radiators
      • One mounted on each side of the car, likely in parallel
      • Look into custom radiators from C&R
    • Swirl Pot
      • Custom because a lot of the ones available will be too large
    • Catch Can
      • Custom
      • Liked the carbon wrapped beer cans from PF22, look into this again
        • Lightweight, strong, passes rules
    • Electric Water Pump
      • Needed because we remove stock water pump for the dry-sump system
      • Purchased
      • Chosen based on water mass flow, electric consumption, pump size and weight
    • Coolant Temperature Sensor(s)
      • Mounted at inlet and outlet of engine
      • If only one, mounted at outlet of engine
    • Aluminum tubing
    • Silicone hoses
  • Water path:
    • Leave Engine Outlet > Swirl Pot > Split into both radiators > Combine again > Water Pump > Engine Inlet
