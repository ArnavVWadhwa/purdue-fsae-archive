Purdue Formula SAE : Hub Bearings: 1 Bearing vs. 2 Bearings

Created by Liang, David C, last modified on Jul 31, 2023

https://www.hubs.com/blog/high-precision-cnc-machining-formula-sae-race-car/


The bearings allow the hub and components beyond the hub to freely rotate within the uprights of the car, as shown by the picture above. Thus, they must be able to withstand the different loads generated by the car with minimal fatigue/loss of performance. 

On this page, we will be discussing whether it would be more beneficial to have one or two bearings.


Benefits of a two bearing design:

  • With the lateral forces on the bearings, we would likely have to source a thicker, longer, and more robust bearing to withstand those forces as opposed to using two spaced bearings. Increasing the number of bearings improves the load distribution and allows for a more stiff setup and reduce friction without having to use the larger and longer bearings (confirm with hand calcs)
  • The two bearing design may be lighter than the one bearing design (confirm with hand calcs)
  • There is reduced heat and friction to the bearings in a dual bearing setup, which can reduce wear as well as improve performance
  • Having two bearings allows for redundancy if one fails


Benefits of a one bearing design:

  • More simple and saves space in the hub/uprights
  • Two bearings may not be needed (confirm with hand calcs)
