Purdue Formula SAE : 2020 Chassis Design

Created by Unknown User (ehoerl), last modified on Nov 01, 2019

Goals

Overall Season Goals: 

  1. Design Finals
  2. Top 10 in Endurance
  3. Top 20 in Autocross, Skid Pad, Acceleration, Cost, Business Presentation, and Efficiency

3 Primary Sub-team Goals: 

  1. Achieve dynamic event performance goals (2 & 3) by reducing weight to under 60 pounds
  2. Achieve autocross, skid pad and endurance performance goals (2 & 3) by meeting or exceeding torsional rigidity / deflection target from suspension  
  3. Achieve performance goals (2 & 3) in dynamic events through improved ergonomics (driver surveys) (comfortable 20 min stint)

Trade-offs

Bulkhead Height: 

bulkhead height left perfectly flat rather than lifted due to increased drag penalty hurting performance more than they lift and mass flow benefits

Engine Height:

engine height increased slightly to leave room for the under-tray trading cg height increase for increased down force. 

Reduced Rear Box Complexity: 

rear box complexity reduced improving manufacturability and reducing the overall mass of the chassis. cons include the increased stress on the rear diff tube as well as increased complexity of rocker and rear wing mounting. 


Square Bulkhead Tubes: 

bulkhead tubes square to increase stiffness (increased second moment of area of the tube, and increased contact area of the anti-intrusion plate on the tubes). 


Non-Structural Top Cockpit Members: 

done to reduce the mass of the chassis. 


Other Non-Structural Members: 

non critical structural tubes or over built areas of the car reduced to smaller non structural size as compared to previous years


Steering Wheel Size and Height: 

Steering wheel size was increased from 8.25 to 10.25 in to reduce the amount of effort required to turn the car. This required an increase in height of the FRH which added mass to the car. 


Single Piece Carbon Seat: 

A single piece carbon seat was opted for this year to further reduce driver fatigue by increasing the support around the drivers hips and back. This replaces a multi-piece design with an expanding foam seat insert. 


Non-adjustable Headrest: 

This was done to reduce complexity of the headrest assembly as well as increase the stiffness of the system and increase safety. 


Performance Metrics

Ergonomics: 

This was a major focus of this years design. Modifications to the seat, steering wheel position, steering wheel geometry, clutch position, headrest, and pedal box (suspension) were made to improve the overall drive-ability of the car without negatively impacting other aspects of vehicle performance. This started with the development of the cockpit mock up. This was used to place the steering wheel and also test different back angles and pedal box positions.

Front Suspension Point Compliance:

For the front of the car it was determined that camber compliance is the most critical design point. The base chassis is the chassis as of 10/18. This test demonstrated that adjustments to tube sizes while keeping geometry constant had no major impact on camber compliance and the increased mass was more of a points penalty than the increased camber compliance. Changes to overall chassis geometry may have a larger impact on this factor though this was not explored further do to time constraints (also rules and suspension node locations largely dictate the front chassis geometry).
