Purdue Formula SAE : PF21-22 Chassis Pedal Box Goals/Targets

Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory, last modified on Jan 03, 2021
  • Case 1: 40mph @1.5g deceleration
    • Target: Same Pedal Force as PF20 at 40mph @1.5g
      • Use Adam's Car to predict Tractate forces at case; Back calculate to applied force based on PF20 Geometry
      • Excel Calculator for PF22 hits Adam's Tractate Force at case using that PF20 Applied Force
      • Target: Make sure works for our drivers (5th percentile male) ***(Design Judge: Meet 5th percentile female we would go to smaller master cylinders)***
  • Case 2: 70mph @max deceleration (Hits error in Adam's Car)
    • Target: Pedal Force 5th percentile male can apply will lock the wheels
    • We Hit Targets CAD (~131deg Knee; ~30deg thigh) ~220lb Pedal = 980N 
    • Target: Brake Pressure in MC, lines, and Calipers are below operating pressure rating
  • Case 3: 2000N Force Applied
    • Target:  Brake Pressure in MC, lines, and Calipers are below peak pressure rating
    • Target: Structural Limits so pedal box does not fail FEA
  • Case 4: Maximum Accelerator Pedal Force (Value in loads page ~150lbs)
    • Target: Structural Limits so pedal box does not fail FEA
  • Case 5: Pedal Box
    • Target: Full Range of Adjustability
    • Target: Accelerator Functioning Stop FEA
    • Target: Accelerator meets PF20 Force Required by factor of 4 (Increasing spring force)


Points of Failure:

  • Brake Pedal Fulcrum
  • Bolted Master Cylinders
  • Gas Pedal Stop
