Purdue Formula SAE : 7. Palmers Pursuit Boulder Air 90deg Regulator Manual

Created by Unknown User (hammerly), last modified on May 03, 2023

The purpose of this page is to provide information on the assembly and function of the Palmers Pursuit Regulator used for the 2022/23 season, because there is no operations manual available online. This regulator was initially spec'd out by Alex Krueger for initial pneumatics design throughout the 2021/22 season for its output pressure range of 0-250 psi. Because of the rules limiting the orientation of the tank (T.6.1.5), the axis of the tank may not point at the driver. A 90 degree bend makes the packaging for this system much easier.

The adjustment screw changes the position of the plunger, which in turn varies the output pressure. Backing out the adjustment screw all the way will drop the output pressure to zero. It is recommended to back out this screw, pressurize the tank, then monitor the working pressure while turning the screw in up to the desired working pressure.

This regulator also has a pressure relief feature. This feature has caused several headaches before I understood how it works. At a certain input pressure, the pin may back out of the seat, then redirect airflow out of a hole in the cap. To disable this feature, disassemble the seat from the 90 degree asa. Thread the silver bolt all the way into the 90deg asa, so that the head of the bolt is flush. Then, assemble the seat, pin, and valve spring - as well as the rest of the regulator. Because the silver bolt is threaded in all the way, it compresses the valve spring to the point where the pressure relief feature is disabled.

Additional recommendations and advice for using this regulator:

  • Order the regulator far in advance. Palmers Pursuit is just one guy doing it alone, so the lead time is ~3-4 weeks. Order directly from their website here https://palmerspursuit.com/products/90-boulder
  • The tube's threads are extremely fragile and prone to cross-threading. If you cross-thread the tube, RTV will be your best friend. Carefully use a heat gun to decrease the curing time.
  • Use gas-resistant - yellow - teflon tape with pneumatics fittings. Fuel will degrade typical white teflon tape and result in leaks.


If you have any additional issues with this regulator, you may reach out to Palmers Pursuit directly via call or text - their phone number is +1 (916) 477-0762.
