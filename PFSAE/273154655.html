Purdue Formula SAE : Part 6 | Vehicle Dynamics

Created by Kurisko, Keith Gregory, last modified on Sep 23, 2023

Most of the important information needed to complete this onboarding is found in RCVD and Tune To Win. The Goal is to introduce you to basic vehicle dynamic principles and think through how vehicle architecture influences performance.


Project:

  1. Using Excel create a calculator that evaluates load transfer for both longitudinal and lateral cases. (IGNORE AERO LOADING)
    1. Inputs:
      1. Lateral Acceleration
      2. Longitudinal Acceleration
      3. Track Width
      4. Wheelbase
      5. Cg Height
      6. Vehicle Weight
      7. Weight Distribution
      8. FLLTD
    2. Outputs:
      1. Fz all four corners
        1. Longitudinal Calculations
        2. Lateral Calculations
    3. Sweep Parameters (3 different values for each):
      1. Track Width
      2. Wheelbase
      3. CG Height
      4. FLLTD
  2. Take ResultsÂ 
    1. Input FZ into calculator ->MATLAB Tire Model and Usage - Purdue Formula SAE - Confluence
    2. Output of tire forces from MATLAB and place into excel. Study the difference in tire loads between each parameter sweep.
  3. Document and put together short PowerPoint slides explaining results and what is hypothesized.




Equations to Know (*Not all of the needed equations are listed):

Lateral:

dFZ = m*g*Ay*cg/track

dFZ*FLLTD = Front Load Transfer

dFZ*(1-FLLTD) = Rear Load Transfer

Longitudinal:

dFZ = m*g*Ax*cg/wheelbase
