Background

Historically, 4130 steel has been our preferred choice of material for the chassis frame, instead of alternatives such as 1018 or 4340 steel. This can be traced back ... pretty far (2018 is the furthest I looked and yep it was 4130). The same goes for our heat treatment process. Justifications for 4130 steel aside, the main reason we heat treat is to relieve post-weld stress and temper martensite formed in the chassis from intense exposure to heat (reminder for myself to make a page about heat treatment). However, in the past, we never really tried to quantify or verify this (at least not that I know of), and simply trusted that it was worth it to heat treat. Basically, this test serves as a way to quantify the improvement in toughness of 4130 steel and our welds post heat treatment.
