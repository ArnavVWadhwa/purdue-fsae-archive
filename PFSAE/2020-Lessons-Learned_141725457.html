Purdue Formula SAE : 2020 Lessons Learned

ProblemCausesSolutions
Scrapped hubTolerances on hole positions were out when drilling up old holesDo not attempt to drill through holes for tight tolerance (<.005) applications
Upright clearances to CAslack of thorough packaging steady

Do a better packaging study

Use built in NX tools for clearance checks - make this a tool the team is comfortable with

Stud to hub press fitsNot totally sureHoles nominally drilled to .328. Studs at .331 seem to be press fit but one sheared material. Need to assure good chamfer diameter step up to avoid sharp edges that can shear materail. .003 press fit is also probably too much. suspect .002 is good. Also, measurement with telescope gauges for diameters this small is sure to fail. Just trust that the drill bit made the size it claims.
Rocker initial positionsDamper must start out fully extended (~7.75 inches), if not spring is free to fall off. We had been assuming that it starts in middle position ~7.25 in design and got away with not actually putting it there for a few years. But this year it hit the rocker...Get static length of damper correct in design phase (~7.75 inches)
Wheel studs being pushed outnot enough torque in wheel nut. So wheel hits studs hard and can push them loose..this design is not great because high friction dependance. Redesign at some point. In the mean time make torque spec for wheel nut...
Pedal Box BindingTabs were not completely planar

Manufacture PB assembly and use it to jib tab welding
