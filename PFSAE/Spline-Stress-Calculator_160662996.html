Purdue Formula SAE : Spline Stress Calculator

Created by Unknown User (dewey2), last modified on Nov 29, 2020

This calculator is primarily for analyzing the internal spline on the sprocket and sizing the relevant geometry. The calculations are based on those found in Machinery's Handbook. The outputs are tensile yield, compressive yield, and shear ultimate safety factors.


Comments on using the tool:

  • Spline Type - Flexible vs Fixed
    • A flexible spline allows for minimal misalignment and play, while a fixed spline provides no misalignment and no play (essentially a press fit). For the sprocket, a flexible spline is selected, since it slips onto the differential splines and is not pressed on.
  • Max RPM
    • This is the RPM of the rear axle at the maximum speed of the vehicle
  • Pressure Angle
    • For the Drexler differential, the pressure angle is 60 degrees
  • Lewis Form Factor
    • This is inputted as 1.5 as a suggestion from Machinery's Handbook
