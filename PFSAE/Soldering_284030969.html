Purdue Formula SAE : Soldering

Created by Min, Jihong, last modified on Jan 19, 2024

A quick overview of surface-mount soldering. More details will be added in the future

1. Inspect PCB for defects, check with multimeter for any shorts

2. Clean PCB with alcohol and paper towel/cotton buds

3. Secure board and stencil in place.

3.a. Find a solid flat surface to work on. Surface shouldn't bend or deform under pressure.

3.b. Use scrap PCBs to hold PCB in place. It is crucial there is ZERO wiggle room. Also, scrap PCBS should be same thickness as work PCB!

← Scrap PCBs secured in place with tape, with work PCB snugly fit in. No wiggle room! Notice stencil secured on one side with tape.

3.c. Secure stencil in place. Again, VERY important the stencil lines up PERFECTLY with the solder pads underneath.

← Notice how only silver shows through the stencil and no red? You want the stencil holes to line up perfectly with the solder pads underneath!!!! Soldermask should not be visible!

4. Squeegee paste!

4.a. Use something like a credit card to squeegee solder paste across all holes on the stencil. It's OK to take multiple squeegees to cover all pads. However, THE STENCIL SHOULD NEVER MOVE WHEN SQUEEGEEING!

← I used a small rectangular PCB as a scoop to transfer solder paste onto a credit card, then used the card to squeegee paste across the stencil. Cuz it was hard to scoop from the paste jar with the card.

4.c. First squeegee, you want your squeegee tool (credit card, etc.) to be almost perpendicular to the stencil. Apply a lot of pressure on it to make sure no paste gets left behind.

I will have a video here soon.

4.d. Following squeegees should be done at approx. 45 degree angle. Get all the paste into the stencil holes. Again, THE STENCIL SHOULD NEVER MOVE!!! Stencil movement will ruin solder paste application. You will have to wash everything off and try again.

← near-perfect solder paste application

5. Place components. PAY ATTENTION TO PROPER VALUE AND ORIENTATION!

5.a. Do not place thru-hole components! That goes last.

← components placed on pasted pads. It's like an insane game of Operation (do kids even play that nowadays?)

6. CAREFULLY transfer PCB onto hot plate.

7. Reflow! Solder should start melting around 250 degrees C, maybe a bit higher.

← On hot plate, warming up

7.a. Check for tombstoning (when components stand up instead of staying on their pads). If that happens, that means solder paste application was imperfect and they will have to be manually fixed later. Not a big deal but annoying. Check for other errors too. You will fix them all later.

← WE DON'T WANT THIS!!!

7.b. Do not move the components during reflow! You might knock parts off their pads.


7.c. Visually confirm all parts are soldered properly, or at least stuck in place.

7.d. Turn off the hot plate, let the PCB cool on it for like 30 seconds.

7.e. VERY CAREFULLY transfer the PCB onto a cool surface, like a glass tabletop, and let it cool for a couple minutes. BE VERY CAREFUL!!!! The components are only held in place via surface tension of molten solder. The PCB will still be very hot and solder might not have cooled yet so even a cough can blow everything off the PCB and that's GG.


8. Solder on all through-hole components! Solder the shortest parts first, then work your way up to the tallest parts. This makes it much easier

9. Inspect PCB for shorts, tombstoning, and other mistakes and fix them!

8.a. Stuff to look out for:

    • cold solder joints

    • tombstoning

    • incorrect orientation/value components

    • shorts and solder bridges

8.b. Check with multimeter for any shorts


10.  Clean PCB with fine brush and alcohol. Final inspection with multimeter!

11. Cross your fingers and APPLY POWER!!!!

11.a. If something starts smoking or smells like cancer, disconnect power! Components will get very hot if you have a short between power lines. If you want to be stupid like me, touch every component to make sure they're not getting hot. Be careful not to burn yourself tho

← YAY! IT WORKS!


There's a very good chance your board does not work after first power-up. This is very normal. Make sure you don't have any dust, flux, or other residue on the PCB that might create a short or bridge between two signals. Probe the board with a multimeter to make sure voltage values are correct at the right places. You will eventually narrow down the issue to a suspicious component or two.
