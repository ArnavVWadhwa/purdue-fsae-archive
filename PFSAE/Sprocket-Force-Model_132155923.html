Purdue Formula SAE : Sprocket Force Model

Created by Unknown User (dewey2), last modified by Unknown User (sinha54) on Mar 17, 2022

The force model on the sprocket is a function of the chain tension, pressure angle of the chain links, and articulation angle of the chain around each tooth. This equation comes from R.C Binder's book The Mechanics of the Roller Chain Drive based on mathematical studies by R.C. Binder. This formula can likely be found in other chain drive books as well. This model does not account for any chain snapping due to elongation of the chain, friction angle, or deformation of the teeth due to fatigue. The chain tension is calculated by dividing the peak torque at the front sprocket by the pitch radius of the front sprocket. Splitting magnitude into 2 components for ANSYS FEA

This may be a bit tricky to visualize. To start out with some definitions, the pressure angle (phi) is the angle in which the force acts on the tooth (relative to the tooth, not the overall coordinate system). The articulation angle (gamma) is how much the force vector will rotate for each tooth.

So, as the forces get wrapped around the sprocket, the forces will be multiplied by an angle containing the pressure angle + the articulation angle times the number of teeth minus 1 (n -1) that have been wrapped. Additionally, the angle should start off negative, since the initial tooth forces at the top of the sprocket will be directed downwards (see picture below).Â This brings the angle to -1*phi + gamma * (n-1), where n is the number of teeth.

This angle can be used to split the magnitude of the force by utilizing the sin and cos functions. I've drawn a coordinate system that you can reference from or you can use the coordinate systems that aligns with what plane you drew the sprocket on.
