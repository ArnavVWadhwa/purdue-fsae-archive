The Suspension confluence page is organized in this way:

  • 2020: anything that is only of interest for the 2020 car. This includes specific info about 2020 hardware, reminders for the 2020 season, etc.
  • 2021: anything that is only of interest for the 2021 car. This includes specific info about 2021 hardware, reminders for the 2021 season, etc.
  • Design resources: documentation on how to design various subsytems. This also includes info about how to use industry tools properly.
  • Design tools: any custom design tool that we want to keep using and do a good job tracking changes to goes here
  • Lessons learned: things we'd rather forget, but should remember
  • Manufacturing: general manufacturing practices that are suspension specific. If the manufacturing process being discussed is specific to an individual component, it should be listed under Component Histories and Specific Information
  • Testing and Validation Results: results of any test that is validates a tool or process that is done every year. This should not include test specific to a vehicle. These should go under that year.


Minimum Viable Product Suspension

  • General:
    • Pass rules
    • Be manufactured and assembled on time
  • Brakes:
    • Lock all four wheels
    • Have adjustable balance (required to lock front first regardless of weather conditions)
    • Require pedal force that is acceptable for drivers
    • Have adequate analysis / testing to prove safety
  • Steering:
    • Navigate smallest competition corner (currently autoX hairpin)
    • Have steering force that is manageable for drivers 
    • Have adequate analysis / testing to prove safety
    • Avoid tierod inversion (going over center for 4-bar linkage)
  • Actuation:
    • Have travel required for rules (no one actually has this, but needs to look like it wants to be a suspension)
    • Avoid rocker inversion (going over center for 4-bar linkage)
