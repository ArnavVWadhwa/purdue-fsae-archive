Purdue Formula SAE : Coolant Temperature | Sensors

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Jul 31, 2023

Notes:

  • Engines have a narrow temperature band in which they operate most effectively
    • Too cold and fuel has trouble atomizing before the combustion process
    • Too hot and preignition might occur
  • A slightly cooler engine will allow for a few extra degrees of spark advance
  • Idle speed and enrichment increase at colder temperatures
  • Thermistor (typically NTC)
    • 5V Input
    • Increasing temperatures are read as higher return voltages (resistance drop)
  • Must not read in an air pocket as this will cause incorrect compenstations
