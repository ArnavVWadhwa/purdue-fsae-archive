Purdue Formula SAE : Brakes Burndown (Post-IDR, Pre-MRR)

Created by Briles, Grayson Cade, last modified on Oct 25, 2023

Lot of good feedback from Frank from IDR! Now to implement it The actual SUS IDR feedback, in all its glory, can be found here. To summarize the IDR feedback into stuff that we can/should/must do in the upcoming week:

  • Stress Modeling
    • Try using a shell mesh w/ very high element counts on the rotors
    • Ignore/devalue external corner stresses
    • Use bearing stress hand calcs (from MMPDS) to either validate or replace the more complex FEA
    •  Ignore deformation. Not really important here and shouldn't be high anyways
    • Remove displacement in Z from ENTIRE face, try applying just to where it's sandwiched by the hub
      • Using frictional contacts (even as low as 0.01 mu) should remove the need for z displacement constraint on the whole face
  • Thermal Modeling
    • Increasing the drill holes on the rotor may save weight, but ultimately means the brake rotors will heat up to higher temps/quicker.
      • More mass = more energy "storage" in the rotors, so maybe reevaluate the whole hole (lol) resizing operation 
  • Overall Design
    • If we have time, remove some material where we can. NOT A PRIORITY!!!
    • Tolerancing between rotor and retainer to account for thermal expansion (prevent binding up)
    • Make sure part files are up-to-date, no clearance issues, etc.
    • Do FEA documentation!
    • Spec out material stock, get ready for MRR
