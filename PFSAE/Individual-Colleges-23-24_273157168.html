Good Morning Dr. Raman,


My name is Helen Rumsey. I'm a senior in Mechanical Engineering and the President of Purdue Formula SAE. I'm reaching out to you and the College of Engineering about sponsoring the Formula SAE team. Our team is comprised of 70 students, 80% of whom are students in the College of Engineering. Each year we design, build, and manufacture a new car to compete in the SAE Competition against other colleges. 

Our team’s mission is to represent the pinnacle of project-based and student-led undergraduate engineering at Purdue University by developing the abilities of team members to their maximum potential in engineering and team building. In the past three years, we’ve consistently placed top ten overall, including top 3 in the design event. One of our goals for 2024 is to win overall to best represent Purdue Engineering. Our performance is a reflection of our knowledge transfer from year to year and understanding of our design and validation each year. Being a part of our team teaches students invaluable technical experience and work ethic, which they use to establish successful engineering careers. 


I’ve been involved with Formula SAE since my freshman year. I joined with limited knowledge of automotive engineering, design, analysis, and manufacturing but quickly had the opportunity to learn. I’ve designed components in the steering system, frame, and pneumatic shifter, learned manual machining techniques and how to weld, gained leadership experience, and learned valuable time management skills. I’ve learned immense amounts which has prepared me for industry and allowed me to choose whatever career path and company interests me.


Building a new car each year is very expensive. The team spends close to $50,000 on components for the car and each additional improvement, development, or test adds to our yearly cost. We want to continue to improve and provide our students with opportunities to learn but need monetary support to do this. The team reaches out to companies and applies for grants, but we’re still short $20,000 of our yearly goal. We’d greatly appreciate any support you and the College of Engineering can give. If you’d like to set up a time to meet for more information or have any questions, please reach out. Also, please forward this along to whom the information may be relevant.


I’ve attached our team’s sponsor guide and included a link to our PF23 vehicle launch video and a track day with all three SAE teams.


Thank you,
