Approximate Time to Completion:

8 Hours for stock prep of all 4

3 Hours of CAM

12-15 Hours of Setup and Running for all CNC Ops

~26 Hours Total

High Level Steps:

Stock Prep, Manual Mill

Bandsaw the stock to just over thickness of centers.

Prepare plates matching thickness of centers, use mill and fly cutter to make both sides to be flat and parallel

For stock size, I went roughly 0.010" large on thickness as CNC lathe would take it down to thickness and leave a better finish while doing it. Length and width is less important, but keeping it consistent across all of them is generally a good rule to avoid headaches. You'll want to make it small enough so that the endmill can exit the edge of stick without much travel. This way the lead in/out is minimalized, and the corners break off in 4 distinct chunks on CNC, rather than a large ring which is dangerous. Usually a safe bet is going 0.100-0.250 larger than the overall part dimensions

Drill 4 holes (hub bolt holes), this will be used for bolting to the fixture block on the CNC mill

Cut outer round, CNC Mill

Bolt to sacrificial block for CNC mill fixturing via 4 drilled holes

Cut outer diam, leaving some room to actual part (OC ~7.400 in)

Also cut inner bore, this will allow for holding in the CNC lathe

Cut Circular Profile, CNC Lathe

This operation can be done on the mill (was also designed with that intent, hence 0.500in radius fillet), but JohnW insists Lathe for this, rightfully so for the better surface finish)

Cut Pockets, Outer profile, drill 12 holes

Once again, bolt to sacrificial block for CNC mill fixturing via 4 drilled holes

Run CNC to mill cutouts as well as drill 12 holes for wheel shell
