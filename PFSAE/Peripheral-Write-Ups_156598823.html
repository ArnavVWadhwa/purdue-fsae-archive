Peripheral Write ups: (only worry about this if I assigned you a Peripheral over the summer of 2020)

GOAL of these: to eliminate the use of the phrase "because we did it last year"

Peripheral Assignments: 

  • INTAKE: Jak K.
  • EXHAUST: Jack P.
  • COOLING: Miles L.
  • Oil: Carson W. 
  • Fuel: Jayden A. 
  1. Watch Design Presentation Video from 2020, as well as read through the 2020 design powerpoint (located in the drive)
    1. get a feel for what "design" is
    2. keep in mind that the online event was a lot less "brutal" than the in person event
  2. Due November 6th, I want a full report on each of your assigned peripherals.
    1. We will have a meeting / get together after November 6th, and I would like each of you to present your peripheral to the rest of the sub-team.
      1. Prepare to ask and be asked questions by other subteam members (and maybe Ben / JP / Alex / Me). 
      2. Think of every single possible question anyone could ask, and be prepared to answer it. (Real Design Finals will be like this)
  3. Components of "writeup"
    1. Theory (biggest part of the whole writeup. needs to be very detailed)
      1. talk about what the peripheral does for the powertrain
      2. goals for peripheral and how they relate the overall goals of the powertrain subteam
        1. why / how goals were chosen quantitatively / qualitatively
        2. examples: more power? better driver feel? etc. (keep in mind you need to backup every claim with quantitative data or driver feedback)
      3. other peripherals that may interact with it (if applicable)
        1. how does the interaction affect the design of the peripheral
      4. Talk about each component / why we use certain types of fasteners / connectors / materials etc.
        1. example: why do we use AN-4 on the fuel lines? 
          1. Our answer now: because we did it last year
          2. Our answer after the paper is written: because it is the smallest fitting / line that meets the requirements X, Y, Z according to ____. 
    2. Math Models
      1. any custom models used in excel or matlab that helped make design decisions for the peripheral
    3. Validation of Models
      1. procedure to test on dyno (if applicable)
      2. procedure to test on car (if applicable)
    4. CAD
      1. general overview on what steps were needed to model the peripheral in cad
    5. Fabrication
      1.  detailed fabrication of the peripheral (don't explain the "why" in this section. This section is purely fabrication. The why should have been addressed in "theory." )
        1. materials
        2. processes
        3. tools
        4. time / manpower
        5. etc.
    6. Troubleshooting
      1. things to check during testing
      2. what is considered a check later / what is considered stop the car now and shut it off.
    7. References: screenshots / links / figures
      1. include all figures here, also include figure in applicable location if needed as well. If not required leave in this section to reference it in text.
