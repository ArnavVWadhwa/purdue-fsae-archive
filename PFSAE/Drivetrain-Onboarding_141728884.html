Software to Familiarize with:

NX 12:

  • About:
    • This is the computer-aided design (CAD) package the team uses for all design
    • It is one of the go to CAD packages for the automotive and aerospace industries. Daimler, FCA, General Motors, Boeing, and SpaceX all use it.
  • To Access
    • It can be found on the ME computers, as well as any ITAP computer. The team also has some licenses for it to be installed on personal computers. Ask your team lead about this if required.
  • To learn NX basics: 
    • Follow the team CAD training videos linked here
    • Make sketches, then solids bodies, then assemblies. Drawings for these are provided in the CAD practice 2 pdf below
      • CAD practice 2.pdf
    • Tutorials are readily found on youtube.
    • To assess your readiness to design parts for the team, make the components and assembly in the vblock2012_upload pdf below
      • vblock2012_upload.pdf
    • Consider trying to utilize expressions in NX for parametric modeling (Tools → Expressions)
      • Plenty of tutorials on youtube about this
    • If you want something a bit more challenging, go to the sprocket design confluence page and try designing a parametric sprocket
      • Link here: Sprocket Design – See CAD Section

ANSYS Workbench:

  • About:
    • This is the finite element analysis (FEA) software uses in most cases.
    • It is used by almost any company that does FEA. Most companies would also have other FEA packages. ANSYS is great for analyzing small individual components or assemblies where problem size is not an issue. For large systems, most companies would turn to a different package.
    • Any FEA setup includes a pre processor, solver, and post processor. ANSYS Workbench includes all three. Other workflows / programs may have these separated out.
  • To access:
    • The student version is free on ANSYS' website: https://www.ansys.com/academic/free-student-products
      • The student license has a small size limit for what you can mesh and solve, but is still good for learning how to do basic things
    • The ME computers also have it installed for use by ME students
  • To learn:
    • This series of tutorials is useful: https://studentcommunity.ansys.com/thread/fea-webinars-for-fsae-teams-hosted-by-leap-australia/
    • The brake pedal video here is also good: https://studentcommunity.ansys.com/thread/fsae-baja-video-tutorials/
