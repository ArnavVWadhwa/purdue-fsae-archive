Purdue Formula SAE : 2/13/2022 - Kinematics Check (PF22)

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified on Mar 23, 2022


Relationships Measured

  • Wheel rate against spring rate, (Front and Rear, Left and Right)
  • Camber gain against bump travel, (Front and Rear, Left and Right)
  • Steering gain in bump (bump toe), (Front and Rear, Left and Right)
  • Camber gain through steering, (Front, Left and Right)


Process used:

  • Put chassis on blocks. Shim with combination of block so that it's level in pitch and roll. We used some rachet straps and C-clamps to keep chassis from moving on the table
  • Remove shocks but reinstall shock pots:
    • Be sure to check shock pot calibrations by measuring movement and comparing to the shock pot displacement
  • Add shims to get chassis level. Use digital protractor to measure the angle of a tube that was jigged carefully to measure chassis flatness.
  • Put floor jack beneath suspension corner and adjust corner height so that the lower control arm (LCA) matches its angle in CAD
    • Ended up using a scissor jack that comes with most cars for emergency use, way easier to make fine adjustments than floor jack
  • Use TIG wire and tape measure to indicate vertical travel
  • At each height, record camber with digital protractor and record shock pot from MoTeC
  • Repeat for all heights and all corners
  • For measuring bump toe, we put a angle plate against cardboard to see rotation, then disconnected springs but raised and lowered the chassis with a jack to stroke the suspension through bump travel
