Purdue Formula SAE : Stellantis Wind Tunnel Smoke Wand Correlation

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Mar 18, 2023
CFDWind TunnelComments

Most likely due to geometry error, CFD shows a higher initial upwash and flattens out quicker.

Under predicting tire separation. Tire wake is significantly lower in CFD

Front wing tip vortex flow is good

Vorticity is much higher in WT. CFD is too diffusive. (02_14s)

Diveplane wake looks good. But there is excessive inwash  too early in CFD. (02_46s)

The vortex itself looks good. But the wake gets inwashed way too fast. With radiator there, inwash never really happens in the wind tunnel. (03_7s)

Inwash is much more representative on this side of the car. (04_3s)

Tire squirt looks relatively close. (05_14s)

At this point, the squirt is traveling too much along the y axis

The flow is much more willing to travel through the radiator than what CFD says. The flow also loses far less speed when traveling through. Flow is also much more turbulent  (06_11s)

Flow is far less inwashed on the exhaust side. Also flow is much more turbulent. (07)

A lot more flow coming out of the giga diffusers than what the CFD is predicting. Even though according to the tufts there are not the best flow, at least at 20 mph. (13_13s)
