Purdue Formula SAE : 2021 Cooling Sim Redesign Notes

Created by Skrzyszowski, Mateusz, last modified by Unknown User (dnocon) on Jul 14, 2021


  • time step will solely be based off of wheel speed frequency
  • rpm will not affect time step of wheel speed in calculation
  • to ensure logging frequencies are the same, take slowest frequency and interpolate it so it is the same speed as the faster frequency, note that frequency and convert to time step (1/Hz)

Air Mass Flow Function - Done

  • Input: Wheel Speed
  • Output: Air Mass Flow

Water Mass Flow Function - Done (Tentatively based on an equation from Cal Poly)

  • Input: rpm
  • Output: Water Mass Flow

Radiator Heat Rejection Function - Done

Inputs: air mass flow function, water mass flow function, size

Outputs: Heat Rejected


Engine Heat Generation Function - Done (Tentatively based on an equation from Cal Poly)

Inputs: rpm

Output: heat generation

Notes: just a few measurements that gets interpolated

Main Function to Calculate Delta T across Rad

Inputs:

  • Initial Temp (gets iterated after final temp is calculated)
  • Radiator Heat Rejection
  • Engine Heat Rejection


Have a loop that increments by the logging frequency to get this to be transient. 

Start with initial temp going into engine), do calculation, calculate final temp leaving radiator and going into the engine again), increment, final temp becomes initial temp, recalculate. 

Repeat loop until you run out of data.
