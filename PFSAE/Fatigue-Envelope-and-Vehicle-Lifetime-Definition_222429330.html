Purdue Formula SAE : Fatigue Envelope and Vehicle Lifetime Definition

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified by MacNab, Zachary Robert on Sep 30, 2023

In recent history, the team has only seen one major fatigue failure - PF18 Wheel center failure on PF20 testing. Despite that, this consideration should be made, even if only for design event points and for sake of good engineering practice.

There needs to be an important distinction made here though about cycle counting. Some components accumulate cycles with each throttle application, each corner taken, or each brake application. For example, a control arm is loaded upon corner entry and relieved at corner exit, that corner is one cycle. In rotating components though such as hubs and wheels, a cycle is each rotation the wheel makes while the car corners. So this difference could be 1 vs 30 cycles.


GP Track One Lap, Wheel Cycle Counts:

If you add those distances up, you get 1210 ft = 0.23 miles


Therefore for every 100 miles of testing, assuming our testing is at GP track or any autocross setup we do is similar to GP track:
