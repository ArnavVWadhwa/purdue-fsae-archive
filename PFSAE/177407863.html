Analysis
9) I like the difference of on vs. off throttle load cases
10) Load cases page (43) – what are your outcomes/ goals?
11) Effects of engine on stiffness?
12) Based on your description of the LLTD model: The chassis can be thought of as a series of
torsional springs between masses which apply moments when the vehicle corners. To provide
intuition before completing more complex FEA models hand calculations were performed on the
model in Figure 3.7.2 which are summarized in Figure 3.7.4.” it seems based on vehicle masses
to use inertia to impart loads on the chassis. What about applied suspension loads? I think that
is where I get the most confused about using this model to design your structural stiffness.
13) Because using LLTD were you tuning front vs. rear stiffness? If so, how?
14) Torsion FEA shows deflection of suspension links and test beam. Is this deflection realistic? Does
it change your results?
15) Margins of safety seem very high (higher than team goals that Ben lays out in the beginning and
also in a different format) and you also missed your mass target by a small amount. Seems like
you should have been able to lower mass and also still meet margin of safety targets.
16) Suspension link/ component design? Did their deflections impact your thinking?
17) Did you analyze for deflection at suspension mounting points? (ex. Under a 70 mph turn, by how
much does your outside tie-rod mounting point on the chassis deflect?) Is this enough to not
affect vehicle handling?
18) Swan neck mounting and compliance? Did you analyze aero loads to make sure your chassis
wasn’t deflecting and potentially going to fatigue due to aero load cycling.
19) If your chassis was going to fail at any location, where would it be? Where is your lowest factor
of safety and under what load case does it happen? – This helps show that you know your
chassis well.
20) Any full vehicle test learnings? I know this is hard for chassis, but one example would be do
sweep through your tuning range and show you can take the car from bad to great to bad. Don’t
just focus on ARB changes because I know that is how suspension likes to make adjustments but
spring and other components that impart load on the chassis and will challenge its stiffness. You
could even make graphs of lap time or driver feedback on the y-axis and tuning range (soft to

stiff on the x-axis and show that somewhere in the middle of your tuning range is the best
performance for each driver.
