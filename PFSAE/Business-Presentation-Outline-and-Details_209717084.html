Purdue Formula SAE : Business Presentation Outline and Details

Created by Panchal, Aarnav Nilesh, last modified on Feb 24, 2022

Outline:

  • Marketing Strategy - How we target the maximum profit from consumers while maintaining cost efficiency
    • Operational Efficiencies
      • Upstream Cost Increases
        • Research and development costs have increased as we have reached a bounding limit for the advancements of the IC system, switching gears to a brand new area within Electric Powertrains is complicated because this implies breaking through the barrier of battery development and looking into energy density // relearning design standards and practices
      • Labor Hours Per Vehicle Produced
        • Currently we have found that we have a staggeringly large amount of labor hours per vehicle produced within assembly, this is driving up costs and will be solved through part standardization and assembly automation, reducing the time and people needed to create the same amount of cars.
      • Supply Chain Deficiencies
        • COVID showed us the weakness of our supply chain, as such we've decided to standardize many parts to streamline what we utilize in our vehicles, this will allow us to also reduce assembly complexity while also reducing the range of automation we will need
          • ie if we standardize our engines we can make it so that we have multiple of the same assembly line doing the same thing for multiple products
          • Basically what Toyota does tbh
        • https://hbr.org/2020/09/global-supply-chains-in-a-post-pandemic-world
    • Push to Electric Powertrain
      • Obviously a lot of hype around Electric Vehicles however the statistics show that there is still a gigantic market for IC vehicles as EV's haven't become as established or as reliable as IC cars.
        • EV is the future, as such we are attempting to break into that market as well to stay competitive with the rest of the market, to do this we are committing funds and resources to the research and development of electronic systems before an eventual rebranding from IC to EV.
          • Implement market trends alongside figures of how the market changes
        • https://www.iea.org/reports/global-ev-outlook-2021/trends-and-developments-in-electric-vehicle-markets
        • https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1468087419877990
    • Labor Force Updates
      • Turnover Rate
        • The turnover rate has increased, primarily due to the stresses Covid has caused and we have decided to alleviate this with a WFH option, embracing non-traditional workspaces, this will allow us to gain more productivity out of our employees and reduce facility costs
          • Everything is already run online/ through the cloud this will be easy to transition towards
          • Research on the effect of WFH on productivity
        • https://www.natlawreview.com/article/key-employment-issues-facing-employers-automotive-industry
      • Nontraditional Workspaces
        • See above Point
        • https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8247534/
      • Competitive Employment Strategy
        • Offer the chance to work on the cutting edge within R&D, the opportunity to work in a changing workplace where new things like WFH are embraced alongside competitive pay with savings made from operational efficiencies.
