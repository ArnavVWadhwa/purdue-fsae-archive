Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Jun 11, 2023

Simulation: CZ-021

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Reduced angle of attack of the second element and increased slot gap size

Increased slot gap size to have more energy going under the element in the absence of the endplate cut. Increased slot gap also necessitates reduced angle of attack to maintain stagnation point. The reduced angle of attack in turn helps with stagnation point optimization on the third element.Â 
Added tire wake diffuser

Aims to reduce tire squirt by upwashing high energy flow away from the ground. Of course some energy will make its way back to the ground with shear force of the rotating tires, but hopefully this is enough to make a difference.

Also should produce quite a bit of local load. If this works, multielement is 100% worth considering.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

Much more loading the flaps. There is probably more performance by continuing moving in this direction, but I'm not really thinking about optimization right now. There are more important things to do.

Flow field down at the tire squirt diffuser is simply a mess. Anything for this location will probably end up being sensitive. The blob of separation on the suction side gets ingested into the undertray, and depowered the vortex structure, reducing load quite significantly.
