Purdue Formula SAE : Shifting Performance Requirements

based on previous logged data I was not able to find an accurate baseline shift time for 2018 and 2019 cars. The shift time was for sure under 200ms, if we want to be able to measure decreased shift times, we will need to log gear position and log rpm faster and thottle position as well for good flat foot justification.


Flat foot justification, might have to be a driver confidence and reliability decision.

Because the data logged by the throttle position is currently only at 10hz the resolution of seeing how quickly a driver can come off the throttle is limited to about 200ms, this data is not logging fast enough for making a decision on flat foot shifting.

Based on some super jank Assetto Corsa sim testing, to perform a lift for a shift takes about 50-100ms. This lift time could probably be negated in the shift time with proper driver practice, but flat foot could be justified from a repeatability perspective. I would argue it could be worth at least 50ms for acceleration shifting times. Que "put it in the lap sim"
