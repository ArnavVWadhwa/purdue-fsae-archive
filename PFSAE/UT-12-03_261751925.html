Purdue Formula SAE : UT-12-03

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 27, 2023

Simulation: CZ-005

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Sidepod inlet flow alignment

Changed surfacing method from swept to through curve mesh to get a more consistent initial upwash geometry. Now, the entire top surface has the same, steepened upwash angle.

Outwash angle was reduced from 10 degrees to 5 to improve lateral flow attachment.

Outer side tunnel strake

Strake will produce pressurization on the outboard face due to inwash induced by vortex in VC. The pressurization will act to reduce de-power VC vortex itself, allowing it to survive longer, which reduces loss in both VC and outer side tunnel.

The strake will also discretize vortex in the other side tunnel, reducing loss and increasing downforce.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

VC vortex breakdown is delayed as expected, but not by a lot.

The wake is still feeding the outer side tunnel, causing loss of energy. This really needs to be figured out.

The inner part of the outer side tunnel has two discrete vortices. The first one formed by inwash interacting with the strake. The second one formed due to additional inwash brought in by the outer vortex.

The second vortex did not appear out of nowhere, the strength of the inner side tunnel vortex was reduced to make that happen, since that outer vortex induced inwash is a strong source of power for the inner side tunnel vortex.Â
