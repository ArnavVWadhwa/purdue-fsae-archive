Current Flat Modeling Process:

This is an old method of constructing an electronics harness, utilizing a 2D model of the CAD layout and over laying wires on top of the page to simulate the approximate routes. This allows you have a physical reference, with the correct dimensions of the actual car, without being on the actual car.    


                                                     

                                                          Figure 1: PF22 Harness Version 1


Pros: 

  • The correct scale to car  
  • Easy to visualize where splits in the harness pathing are located
  • Easily compactable if constructed out of paper (as shown in Figure 1), allowing multiday construction if necessary   
  • Fairly inexpensive with regular printing 

Cons: 

  • Not very good for visualizing 3 dimensional wire routes 
  • can't indicate minor dimensions errors (due to lack of the actual car reference)
  • requires a test fitting process
