Purdue Formula SAE : Halfshafts Design (CAD Purposes)

Created by Unknown User (dewey2), last modified on Feb 15, 2020

The halfshafts are put into CAD to determine:

  • Halfshaft angles
  • Clearances between the chassis and other components
  • Halfshaft lengths to order from RCV/FSAEparts

The halfshafts are not cut to length based on the CAD model, but rather from actual fitment on the car. This is due to overall manufacturing tolerances, so an adjustable jig is used to find the ideal halfshaft length. The halfshafts should have an angle less than 12 degrees, as specified by RCV, the manufacturer of the tripods. This angle is measured through the movement of the wheel, 1 inch above and below the stationary position.


Resources:

  • RCV FSAE Brocure detailing dimensions, CV specifications
    • https://www.rcvperformance.com/media/fsae/FSAE_Brochure.pdf
  • FSAE Parts to purchase halfshafts


Halfshafts extend from the inboard CV housings to the outboard CV inserts inside the wheel hubs. The left halfshaft is angled 6.08 degrees and the right halfshaft is angled 5.19 degrees, far less than the maximum 12 degrees.
