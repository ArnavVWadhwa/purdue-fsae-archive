Purdue Formula SAE : Lessons Learned PF23 | AER

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified by Zhang, Chenkai on Jun 15, 2023

Design

  • Do not simply run yaw 15 and yaw 0, build a simplistic aero map from time to time to make sure your package is insensitive
  • Designing for yaw 15 limits overall performance, try to design for yaw 0 and de-sensitize to yaw 15
  • Car with higher weight pitches more, take that into consideration when setting ride height minima
  • 5% aero adjustment in each direction is not enough, have more
  • If you want to do electrical DRS, motors for RC crawlers with titanium gears are a really good choice. NC state was able to achieve 10 ms actuation time with those
  • With aero maps, you can calculated weighted CL and CD based on the amount of time car spends in the regime and how important the regime is. A good weighted CL and CD can be a good one value representation of the performance of aero package
  • Different design deadline for aero, maybe per part deadline

Manufacturing

  • Groups working on different parts to make manufacturing go faster
  • Manufacturing lead in addition to team lead to increase speed and reduce load for team lead
  • More mold machining sponsors for better scheduling
  • Keep good relationship with BIDC

Design Event

  • Have CFD plots ready at hand. I got asked something as simple as showing a CFD picture of pressure distribution around the wing
  • Try very very very hard for wind tunnels. Half of the design event was about CFD validation
  • Always prepare for skidpad radius aero vs. non aero question

Validation

  • Wind tunnel
    • Ride height setting
      • Triple check the ride height you run in CFD is reachable in real life
      • Using ride height datums isn't the best ideas since there is a lot of going back and forth. Do practice runs and figure out the rod end length
    • Tufts vs. Flow Viz
      • Separation shows up much better with flow viz than tufts
      • Flow viz recipe to try:
        • 1 liter of paraffin or diesel oil
        • Add TiO2. Enough to make a thin slurry that's in between paraffin and lubricating oil in consistency
        • Add half a teaspoonful of fluorescein color indicator 
        • Add a few drops of oleic acid or linseed oil as wetting agent to allow flow viz to flow better
    • Figure out if the car can be lifted up when mounted and adjust test plans accordingly
    • Practice practice practice
      • Practice every change
      • When making the test plan, consult what actually can be done quickly on the car 
  • On track
    • Rolling resistance is nonlinear with speed. Unless you can figure out the nonlinearity, coast down is useless in practice (maybe good for design)
    • Don't do 70 mph for high speed steady state since the car can't stay at that speed fast enough for the downforce to reach steady state
