Tips and Tricks 



Why it matters what size you design your fillets 

Tool with a radius larger than the fillet radius 

  • Large increase in tool engagement and tool load 
  • Some material is left unmachined
  • Terrible Noise and possible tool damage 
  • Bad surface finish 

Tool with the same radius as the fillet 

  • Still has a large increase in the engagement angle of the tool as it gets into the corner 
  • Bad surface finish 
  • High pitch squeaking noise when tool makes full engagement with the fillet

Tool with a smaller radius than the fillet radius 

  • still an increase in tool engagement and tool load
    • But this can be accommodated for by lowering the feed rate in the corner
  • The Rule of thumb is the tool radius should be no more than roughly 80% of the fillet radius
