Purdue Formula SAE : PF23 Differential Ramp Angle Test Plan

Created by Unknown User (amattu), last modified on Apr 07, 2023

Goal: Understand differential tuning settings and feed results into simulation tool.

Tunable variables:

  • Preload
  • Ramp Angles
  • Number of active clutch interfaces

This test will focus purely on ramp angles. Preload analysis and deceleration angles will be run at a later date to streamline the choice of acceleration ramp angles.

Procedure:

Test types:

Launch (lockup analysis): Perform three launches using launch control. End each launch once at 30mph.


Understeer gradient test (objective data): Drive in a constant radius and slowly increase speed at a constant rate. Once understeer overpowers and the car veers off the constant radius, end the test. Perform test twice.

Perform UG testing after tire temps and pressures are at racing spec.


Custom Track Needs

2 low speed corners (1 LH, 1 RH), constant radius

2 low speed corners (1 LH, 1 RH), reducing radius

2 high speed corners (1 LH, 1 RH)

2 chicanes

2 slaloms


connect the last exit of low speed corner to high speed corner


Required signals:

  • Inputs
    • Steering Angle
  • Outputs
    • Wheel speeds
    • Yaw rate
    • Lateral acceleration

Post Processing:

Launch

  • Average V_0 as the front wheel linear velocities.
  • SR = (V / V_0) - 1
  • Isolate slip ratio for each wheel 
  • Calculate lockup from slip ratio differences

UG

  • Plot steering angle vs. lateral acceleration and linearize.
  • Slope of the linear approximation is understeer gradient.
