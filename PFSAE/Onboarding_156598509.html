The website you are currently on is called Confluence. It is a wiki site that the team uses for knowledge transfer and design documentation. Feel free to browse around to see the work we have done in the past few years. Although the Formula Team has been around since 1992, Confluence is has only been around since 2019. 

I know you are all excited to start contributing to the vehicle, however not all of you are familiar with various tools the team uses, and even more specific tools each subteam uses. 

The onboarding also process consist of 2 projects. The New Member Onboarding Project, and the Subteam Specific Onboarding Project.

  1. The New Member Onboarding Project is a simple Siemens NX task that will walk you through the basics of NX and the functions we utilize to design parts on the vehicle. 
  2. The Subteam Specific Onboarding Project will be assigned based on the subteam you join: Suspension, Chassis, Powertrain, Electronics, Aerodynamics, or Business. 

In addition:

  1. Read 2024 Goals

  3. Read Tune to Win by Carroll Smith
  4. Attend Weekly Subteam Presentations + Quizzes (Recordings will be made and posted to GroupMe if you can not make it)

  6. Reading through Design | Global and Manufacturing | Global on confluence

WHY NX?

We use NX for CAD (computer-aided-design) because of its capability to handle large assemblies (the whole car in our case), top down modeling functions (designing a part using geometry of other parts), and wide-spread industry use by aerospace and automotive companies.

In general, the team uses a top-down modeling method in which components are designed in context and critical dimensions are controlled using the larger skeleton or reference models. Tools that are commonly used by the team include: Sketching and Sketch Constraints, Extruding / Cutting, Wave Geometry Linker, Assembly Constraints, etc.

Tune To Win

Tune to Win has long been considered the most fundamental and accessible book about racecar design and development. If you're confident about what subteam you'd like to join, you can go ahead and look at the subteam specific onboarding page in the chart at the bottom of this page and read the portions of Tune to Win recommended there. If you're not sure which subteam sounds most interesting (which is totally fine!), reading all the sections could help inform your decision. A PDF can be found in the google drive folder below.
