Purdue Formula SAE : Shifter Design V1

Created by Cole Henry Kniesly, last modified on Nov 26, 2021

Criteria and Constraints:

Criteria:

    • Up and down shift without driver having to pull in clutch
    • Up shift by pulling, down shift by pushing
    • Design a new handle system that would only require the driver to use the shifter handle
    • Clutch should be actuated only on the downshift of the shifter handle
    • Make a system that is shifts more smoothly, more ergonomic, reliable, and durable than PF20

Constraints:

    • Room in cockpit, Chassis Templates
    • Allow for adjustability for driver preference and to find the best force required setting
    • Driver Ergonomics

      • FORCE TARGETS:

        MotionPeak Force40% Peak Force (Typical)
        Finger Grip56lbf (Sustained 33lbf **Launch Control)23lbf (Sustained 14lbf **Launch Control)
        Pull42lbf17lbf
        Push30lbf12lbf
    • Auto shift engages clutch only on downshift and there must be an option for driver to manual hold in clutch to either shift or activate launch
      • On auto downshift clutch must be engaged before full angular displacement of shifter at WR450 end
    • Measured Forces for Clutch WR450: 157N
    • Shifter Actuation Angle Estimated 20 degrees using images and PowerPoint protractor
    • MUST MUST Interface with Current tabs and setup with Welded Frame and hardware

Used idea:

Key:

  • Left handle is the Shifter Handle
  • Right handle is the Clutch Handle
  • Red line is the chain connecting the handles
  • Blue lines are the respective cables for the handles

How it works:

  • When the shifter handle is pulled back to upshift, the chain goes slack. Therefore, only the shifter cable is actuated
  • When the shifter handle is pushed forward to downshift, the chain goes taunt and pulls the shifter. Therefore, the shifter and the clutch are both actuated.
  • When both handles are squeezed together for launch, both cables are engaged.

Design:


Key:

The handle on the left is the shifter handle with a 3D printed ball handle on top of it.

The handle of the right is the clutch handle.

The black line represents the #25 chain connecting the shifter handle to the clutch handle.

The red line represents the shifter cable.

The blue line represents the clutch cable.


Use Cases:

During up shifts the shifter handle is pulled backwards allowing the chain to go slack.

During down shifts, the the shifter handle is pushed forwards causing the chain to go taunt and likewise pull the clutch towards the shifter, and henceforth disengaging the clutch.

During takeoff, the shifter and clutch handles are pulled together, which shifts the car and releases the clutch

Adjustability options:

  • Switch which holes the chain is connected to
  • Change type and size of 3D printed portion of handle
  • Change which set of holes are used to connect the handles to the car.
  • Change which holes the cables connect to. This would require major changes in the tab.


Design Considerations:

  • The clutch cable guide was based off of PF20's design. The clutch cable should be pulled in straight so the guide is rounded so the cable is always tangential to the path of motion.
  • The shifter is connected using a push pull cable. The cable requires a tab 6-9in away to hold the cable steady and to allow shifting.
  • The clutch is known to be very hard to pull so a form of "gearing" is required.
