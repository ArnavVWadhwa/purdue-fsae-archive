Purdue Formula SAE : Aero vs Suspension Terms for Cross Winds and Pitch Center

Created by Unknown User (reyno105) on Feb 03, 2020

Aero will use the term "yaw angle" to discuss accounting for cross winds on the car. To suspension people, the word yaw generally refers to the car actually yawing as in being in a corner. This is only modeled by having the car actually going though a curved CFD wind tunnel (but is also reasonably well approximated by straight line). So when discussing accounting cross winds and / or cornering sims, just be sure to define terms clearly at the start. This caused a bit of confusing between in 2020.


Another past confusion point was an aero request for pitch center. To a suspension person, this sounds like the kinematic pitch center created as a result of anti squat / anti dive geometry. What aero wanted was just the height at which center of drag is creating a moment on the car changing the balance. This height is always the center of drag to the ground. Suspension kinematics has nothing to do with it.
