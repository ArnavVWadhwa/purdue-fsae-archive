Purdue Formula SAE : Initial Research/Design | Steering Rack PF24

Created by Jiang, Zhao, last modified by Singhal, Ria on Oct 31, 2023

The custom steering rack idea was brought up at DCR1 over the summer. To figure out feasibility of a custom rack and determine a good starting point, we talked to Owen Mamrol (omamrol@purdue.edu) who is ergo lead at Baja, since Baja has been designing and manufacturing a custom steering rack for years. Talking to him was insanely helpful and he shared a bunch of resources including their confluence page on rack design which is way better than ours (3 Steering Rack & Pinion Design). He also told us that shigley's is absolutely goated and that we should avoid undercutting our gear teeth (see gear math page for more on this).

Formulas:

Gear Pitch = (# of teeth +2)/OD

Optimal pinion teeth # = 20

https://www.apexdyna.nl/en/news/why-does-the-ideal-pinion-have-20-teeth#:~:text=Summary,and%20service%20possibilities%20are%20optimal.

  • The number of teeth (z). This value is: z = d/m
  • Module (m). Ratio between the pitch circle in millimeters and the number of teeth. Anglo-Saxon countries use the “Diametral Pitch” instead, which is inversely proportional to the module. The value of the module is determined by calculating the material resistance in relation to the force to be transmitted and the gear ratio. Two mating gears must have the same module: m = d/z
  • Pitch Diameter (d) is the diameter of the pitch circle; its value is: d = m x z
  • Outside Diameter (de) is the diameter of the outside circle; its value is: de = m (z + 2);    de = d + 2m
  • Root Diameter (df) is the diameter of the root circle; its value is: df = m (z – 2.5)  or df = de – 2h
  • Center Distance (dc) is the distance between the shafts of the gear and the pinion; its value is:  dc = (D + d) / 2, where “D” corresponds to the pitch diameter of the gear and “d” to the pitch diameter of the pinion
