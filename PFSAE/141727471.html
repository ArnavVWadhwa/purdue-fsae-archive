Purdue Formula SAE : RC Shifting R6 Mount | PF20

Created by Unknown User (jpeerbol), last modified by Paradise, Hunter on Jul 16, 2023

Designed to fit "standard" size servo. Uses Servo block as base so different models and manufactures could be used with the mount.

Designed specifically with Savox SB2290SG and SB2270SG in mind because easy dimensions and step files could be sourced from ServoCity. Should be same form factor as Savox's waterproof servo line with is IP67 rated.

This was put together in a few hours and was designed to be as easy as possible to make quickly, this should be redesigned if used for competition or serious testing.


The servo that was initially selected https://www.savoxusa.com/collections/monster-performance/products/savsb2292sg-monster-performance-black#technical-details for it's torque and speed

Servo block was selected because it is easy to mount, takes loading off of the servo and any servo can be swapped in, could swap out for waterproof rated servo or something with more torque or speed.

Servo block is fastened together with 92360A130, 6-32 button heads, 1/4" long with nylon patch. Nylon patch should be better than loctite for vibration because it is mounted directly to the engine.

Shift linkage is made from 60645K11 left and right 10-32 rod ends (shortened by .375" to make the small linkage distance), they are cheap, quick to get, use standard threading and aero already uses them. NAS6203 hardware is used because it is standard across the car and high quality. The coupler is made from 91780A290, drilled out and tapped with left and right 10-32's. A short enough 10-32 coupler with left and right threads could not be found, this was the easiest way to make a coupler. The left and right threads allows the linkage distance to be quickly and easily adjusted.

The Yamaha shift arm is threaded for something metric but the minor diameter of the threads fits the NAS6203 bolt perfectly, maybe keep an eye on the bolt to make sure there isn't play and that it doesn't wear bad.

The bearing in the servo block looks to be sealed, but keep an eye on it after the car gets run in the rain.

Add checking bolt tightness to check list before running, the shifter is a critical component

Things to improve:

Fix the offset of the mount, a double arm was used to make to offset work correctly with the stock shift arm, could easily be improved.
