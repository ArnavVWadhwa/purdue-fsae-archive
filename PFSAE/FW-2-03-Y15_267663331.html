Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-03 Y15

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 11, 2023

Simulation ML-045

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Increased chord of 3rd and 4th elements in the inner section.

This is mostly to fix the odd look of the wing, but also should result in superior front DF generation at the expense of RW DF. 

In Y15, this change may result in more wake than before, so RW downforce may continue to drop.

Reprofiled upwashing elements.

This orientation should result in the most upwash of any of the iterations due to the large radius of the LE of the first element and the high exit angle of the final element. This should ultimately massively power up the outwashing vortex which should result in superior UT performance, and potentially better RW performance. 

Unfortunately, this comes at the cost of FW performance which will likely drop, and drag will inevitably increase. 

Interested to see if this change is worth it.

Angled strakes.

The more the strake is outboard the more the strake is angled outboard. This is because this should result in higher vorticity in the first two channels and attempts to alleviate some of the inwash going to the innermost strake that is blowing up in Y15.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

Innermost strake vortex still bursting. 

FW gain likely came from upper flap/increased chord of final two elements. 

Extremely low mass flow rate due to the vortex bursting.

If the vortices don't burst there's potentially 25 points to gain from UT performance. Getting this right is hyper important.
