Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-05

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 12, 2023

Simulation ML-047

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Changed strake orientation to a decreasing cascade. Also eliminated the swept portion of strakes, since it didn't make a difference in performance.

As the vortices get close to bursting, they are separated from the strake 1 by 1. 

This should move the bursted vortex more outboard, leaving more clean air inboard for UT performance and for the tire squirt suppressor to work better. 

Made the endplate on the upwashing flaps tighter to the airfoils.


This should result in a lesser vortex on the bottom side, which should strengthen the main downwashing outwashing vortex generated.

Increased slot gap of 2nd element by .1 in.


Should reduce separation in region seen on downwind side.

Removed diveplane.

I'm not convinced the diveplane is actually doing much of anything.

In FW-2-00, I accidentally didn't include the diveplane in the export, and the performance seemingly did not change.

CFD Analysis

Vortex breakdown didn't occur; however, since the 2nd strake didn't extend far enough, the inwash generated was too extreme. With that being stated, I think the problem lies mostly within the fact that the 1st channel does not house a vortex. 

To ensure a vortex forms here I think I am going to increase the space the channel has by moving the strake more inboard. 


Diveplane definitely helps to reduce outboard tire wake, so its certainly worth keeping, plus, the diveplane is likely why the FW lost df in this iteration.
