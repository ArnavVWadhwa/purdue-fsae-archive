Purdue Formula SAE : Throttle Pedal Return Spring Sizing Documentation

Created by Aaltonen, Emil Johan, last modified on Jan 04, 2023

The throttle pedal return spring sizing depends on a few factors.

  1. The length of the pedal (Moment arm of the drivers foot)
  2. Desired resistance (Reaction force at the point where the driver is pressing, both in the neutral and fully pressed states)
  3. Angular throttle pedal travel 
  4. Diameter of the throttle pedal insert (The spring must fit around it, and it contracts when in use.


Formula drive → 2023 → Chassis→Throttle Pedal→Throttle Pedal Calcs (Excel File)


The excel table shown above calculates a max torque rating for a spring for various combinations of force and pedal travel values. In the calculation, the pedal length is assumed to be known.

This year we used two springs from the spring store that both had a max torque of around 30 in-lbs. This satisfies the driver request of a reaction force of 5-10 lbf. 

The math is based off a simple moment balance with the drivers foot on one side and the spring on the other.
