Purdue Formula SAE : FW-13-07

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on May 31, 2023

Simulation: CZ-012

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Strake cut

Added a step to the strake to discretize the vortex. Given a vortex and two vortices with the same combined strength, two vortices with the same combined strength will usually be less lossy. 

The worry is how well the vortex will be discretized, since the physical size of the vortex that form on the first edge will be big, big enough that the second vortex will form straight into it. Since they are co-rotating, they might just merge and still be a single vortex.

Another worry is the cut increases the total inwash produced by the outer vortex, which will be bad for undertray performance.

Front wing outwash vane

The idea is shed a vortex and place the vortex core right above the inner edge of the tire. The downwash and outwashing sides will suppress tire wake and front wing wake, the upwashing side will depower whatever is happening behind the tire (although the physical presence of the flow field is questionable), and the inwashing side will inwash high CpT flow from the side of the car to improve rear wing performance.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

Hard to see but two discrete vortices are formed

The raised ride height also resulted in outer vortex wake being inwashed a lot quicker

The net result is no meaningful change in wake geometry.

Front wing lost quite a bit of downforce with this element

Outwashing winglet vortex is higher than I liked. And combined with its relatively weak strength, no meaning impact was made on tire wake.

FC vortex and OW vortex merge almost immediately downstream of the tires despite their physical distance. As it travels downstream, the merged vortex gets outwash by the tire wake vortex, but still produce meaningful downwash on the rear wing.
