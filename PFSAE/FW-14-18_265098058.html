Purdue Formula SAE : FW-14-18

Created by Zhang, Chenkai, last modified on Jul 11, 2023

Simulation: CZ-047

Changes/Justifications

Description of the Change

Picture

Justification

Flow conditioner repositioning

Moved flow conditioner downstream and up while reducing its angle of attack. This should reduce the the adverse pressure gradient experienced by the front wing, leading to front wing load increase. Reduced its angle of attack for better local flow alignment. Worry is with lowered angle of attack, this will create more lift, and offset the performance gain from front wing. Offsetting it up is not ideal for rear wing either, since it will have a less direct effect of flow turning for front wing upwash.

CFD Results Analysis

CFD Picture

Description

Interestingly, but reducing its angle of attack, increasing its height and putting it in cleaner flow, flow conditioner produced additional pressurization despite being physically further away from the front wing.

Issue with this placement is that front wing inner vortex smacks straight into the top surface of flow conditioner, which produced a lot of unnecessary suction peak that I would like to get rid of. 

By moving it back, the pressurization from flow conditioner now work in tandem with tire squirt suppressor, resulting in UT performance gain. 


As far as rear wing goes, it did lose some performance as expected.
