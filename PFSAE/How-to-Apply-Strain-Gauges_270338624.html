Purdue Formula SAE : How to Apply Strain Gauges

Created by Paradise, Hunter, last modified on Aug 29, 2023

Micro-Measurements Documentation:


Part 1: Prep Surface

Step 1: Strip power coat or covering using a low-grit sandpaper


Step 2: Work your way to higher grit sandpaper ensuring that the area is large enough to apply the strain gauge but not unnecessarily so


Step 3: Use silicon-carbide sandpaper to get a fine surface finish


Step 4: Clean surface using isopropyl alcohol

Continue to wipe the surface until the pad comes up clean


Part 2: Strain Gauge Application

Step 5: Put on gloves

This adhesive is very strong and comes with many warnings it is advised that you cover your hands

=


Step 6: Apply phosphoric acid surface cleaner to pad and rub on surface


Step 7: Repeat process for ammonia surface cleaner


Step 8: Transfer strain gauge to installation tape

Make sure that the strain gauge is oriented with solder terminals into the tape as the side touching the tape will be the side that faces up on the test object


Step 9: Align mounting tape with test object and place lightly


Step 10: Lift up tape such that strain gauge is not touching the test object but the tape is still attached


Step 11: Apply adhesive catalyst to back of strain gauge


Step 12: Apply adhesive liberally to test object underneath the strain gauge


Step 13: Lay down strain gauge and press firmly and evenly for approximately 1 minute


Step 14: Let adhesive cure for 5 minutes to 24 hours

The tape should be left covering the gauge until wires are ready to be soldered on
