  • 3/22 - Shop/ Rebuild and Inspection
    • SUMMARY: Differential rebuilt to manufacturer spec, figured out accurate bolt lengths, flushed contaminants
    • Differential:
      • Upon disassembly multiple observations were made, mainly that there is a noticeable amount of wear in the differential
      • Scoring on short tulip side flange housing, need to keep this in check for next rebuild after 4 Grissom weekends
      • Sprocket side issues:
        • Leak cause was determined, the sprocket side tulip bolt was long by at least 2 millimeters, this results in it wearing up against the sprocket side pinion, causing it to be torqued out of the threads, this also results in the bolt head sitting offset from the crush washer, causing the crush washer to be loose allowing for leakage into the tulip/boot
      • Once rebuilt 40 mL of oil was flushed through the differential to clear contaminants, possible dust from the long sprocket side bolt was flushed through
      • NOTE: the tulips are now properly fitted with M8-1.25-12mm bolts torqued to 220 in-lbf on both the sprocket and short sides. The outer M6 bolts are also torqued to 132 in-lbf as per manufacturer spec
      • Differential was left in a lateral position to observe possible leakage, will update sometime from 3/23-3/25
    • Sprocket:
      • No noticeable wear, the sprocket is holding up adequately
    • Towbar:
      • Need to source stock, will be done for a laser run on Thursday
        • Carson Williams (PT Lead for 2021-22 Season) brought up possible low cycle fatigue on the cross braces, this needs to be observed on quick jack usage.


  • 3/23 - Shop/Diff check
    • SUMMARY: Diff is not leaking whatsoever, the towbar was checked as the cross brace failed
    • Differential:
      • No leaks
    • Towbar:
      • Failed due to side loading (working theory)
      • observations:
        • The brace in and of itself is not seated inside the side plates on the inner face, this means that the welds failed on the inside of the brace cutouts
        • The shear failure occured in the center of the X-brace, the right side of the X is higher than the left, the brace definitely failed in shear due to side loading and improper welding, it does not seem that the part is entirely flawed
      • Action Plan is to increase the cross brace thickness, X brace plate width, and increase the cutout holes to accommodate for weld material within the side plates.

  • 3/26 - Grissom/ Shop teardown of failed turnbuckle and rod end assembly
    • SUMMARY: Upleft Rod end connecting to upleft failed under compression during a launch control acceleration run
    • Differential / Uprights:
      • Intact (Thank God)
      • Visually uprights are intact, will be used until spares are made (~2 week process)
    • Halfshafts, Boots, & CV's:
      • CV's need to be analyzed
    • Chain & Turnbuckles:
      • Upleft Turnbuckle
        • Failed under compression, seems to have buckled
          • ~0.4 in of thread engagement in buckled rod end
          • Shear pattern on pinned end
        • DISCOVERY: Rod end is rated to 2800 lbf on McMaster Carr, this gives rod end 0.277 MS (Drivetrain Structures calculations on Drive), this means that in the event of any crazier loading we WILL fail.
          • Rod end used was a suspension rod end
        • Ultimate conclusion is that the rod end failed because it was out of spec, XAM 3 will be used from now on.
    • Sprocket:
      • Sprocket teeth are marred from chain pulling off after rod end failure, will be using spare and manufacturing another when time permits
    • Towbar:
      • Towbar is especially ****ed as the undertray chewed it up
      • 1/8 in cross brace towbar is going to be made with McMaster Carr order inbound
