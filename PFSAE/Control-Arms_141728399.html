Purdue Formula SAE : Control Arms

Created by Unknown User (reyno105), last modified by Unknown User (xia107) on Dec 05, 2021

To make these, we make a jig plate that has holes for each control arm. This plate is usually 1/4" steel and gets waterjet. We also make a little aluminum arm thing that place the pushrod tabs.


CAD

  • Bring in the actuation assembly, constrain in place, pull linked bodies.
  • Try to place CAs such that it would be hard to mix up which hole is for which CA (make sure lengths are different in a given region since that's easiest to measure)
  • Account for CAs that have different size rod ends that would thus take different sized bolts. Not all CAs will be able to fixate their spherical bearings to the same hole. Be sure to adjust the inboard points as well if necessary

Jig Plate

  • Can usually waterjet holes at exact size. Bolts will either fit precisely or you will need to drill them to right size to account for taper.

Bearing Cups:

  • Turn OD
  • Drill thru at smallest ID
  • Drill thru at diameter for reamer (look up reamer chart)
  • Boring bar / drill largest diameter
  • Use reamer to hit diameter of middle section. Don't push past end into next wall w/ reamer. It will cause diameter increase losing press fit

CA Inserts

  • Turn OD
  • Drill through using tap chart
  • Mill flat spots for wrenches using collet block

Getting Tubes

  • In the past we ground the ends to shape by hand via guess and check. Last year we had cartesian  VR3 do them. (VR3 does all the chassis tubes, so reach out to chassis for this)

Welding

  • To make symmetric control arms, the left side control arms are made, the table gets flipped, then the right side control arms are made.
  • Leave the control arm on the table while it cools from welding. This reduces the amount the tubes get sucked in by the weld, causing the CA to need to be preloaded when installed on the car.
  • Make sure to get orientation of bearing cups right. Get this wrong and bearings come out likely failing bolt and at least making play for days.
  • Use bolts + a sacrifice bearing to position during welding.
  • If using a sacrificial spherical bearing for welding (positioning the bearing carrier at the top of the a-arm), use a bearing from an Aurora Rod end, you'll have to cut it out with an angle grinder.
    • These are 10-15 thou smaller, giving pretty decent positional control but not requiring a press (save the press in for final assembly and not yield anything during the jigging process)

  


Bearing cut out of a rod end:

(use a cutting disc and go most of the way through the rod end lug, bearing should come out with a hammer and punch)

Assembly:

  • Make sure to get bearing cup orientation right for upper control arms
  • You will need to yank the CAs apart a bit because of weld cooling sucking them in
