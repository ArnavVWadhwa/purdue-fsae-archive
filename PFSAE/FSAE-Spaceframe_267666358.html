In the context of the Formula SAE competition, a spaceframe must at least consist of certain frame members with dimensions and characteristics defined by the rules. The required members are listed as follows:

  1. Front Bulkhead | FBH
  2. Front Bulkhead Support | FBHS
    1. Upper Front Bulkhead Support 
    2. Lower Front Bulkhead Support
  3. Front (Roll) Hoop | FRH
  4. Front Hoop Bracing | FHB
    1. Upper Front Hoop Bracing 
    2. Lower Front Hoop Bracing
  5. Side Impact Structure | SIS
    1. Lower Side Impact Structure
    2. Middle Side Impact Structure
    3. Upper Side Impact Structure
  6. Main (Roll) Hoop | MRH
  7. Main Hoop Bracing | MHB
  8. Main Hoop Bracing Supports | MHBS
  9. Shoulder Harness Mounting Bar | SHMB

** Listed above are usually the main tubes required by rules. Additional requirements may exist depending on the designs of other components and frame load paths.

Listed according to 2023 Rules V.2

Front Bulkhead

Description: A close-loop, close-sectioned tubular member usually rectangular or near rectangular in shape. 

Why is it needed: Crash-protection structure that protects driver's legs during front impact situations

Front Bulkhead Support

Description: Frame members that connect the front bulkhead and front hoop

Why is it needed: Transfer forces from front bulkhead to front hoop and vice versa

Front (Roll) Hoop

Description: A close-sectioned tube that extends from one side of the chassis, up and over the driver's legs and extends to the other side of the chassis

Why is it needed: Rollover protection, (should be the one of the biggest load-bearing member of the frame)

Front Hoop Bracing 

Description: Frame members connecting the front bulkhead to the front hoop

Why is it needed: Transfer force (loads) from front bulkhead to front hoop during crash case

Side Impact Structure

Description: Members connecting the front and main hoop

Why is it needed: Side-impact protection

Main (Roll) Hoop

Description: Main protection structure. 

Why is it needed: Makes sure driver doesn't break their skull during a roll-over

Main Hoop Bracing

Description: Connects "top" of main hoop to rear-most support structure

Why is it needed: Transfer loads from the main hoop to the rear structure and vice versa

Main Hoop Bracing Support

Description: Members of the frame connecting the main hoop brace back to the main hoop

Why is it needed: Transfer loads from rear-impact crash to the main hoop

Shoulder Harness Mounting Bar

Description: Frame members on which the driver shoulder harness is mounted 

Why is it needed: Protects driver during crash case
