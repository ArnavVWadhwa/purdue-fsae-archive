To start:

Read Tune to Win by Carroll Smith.  An overall understanding of race car design is very important. The PDF for this is linked in the suspension onboarding page. This book is a great place to get a basic understanding of what our team is trying to accomplish with design, as well as some fundamentals on how we do it. In addition to this, skim the FSAE rulebook.

What we do:

Powertrain is in charge of multiple subsystems, all of which contribute in some way to the engine of the car. It is our job to make our engine perform as optimally and reliably as possible within the competition rules. To do this, we must design, manufacture, and test the following systems:

  • Intake (How the engine gets its air)

  • Exhaust (How the engine expels its air)

  • Cooling (How the engine keeps from overheating)

  • Fuel (How the car provides fuel for combustion)

  • Oil (How the engine lubricates itself)

  • Engine Tune (How the engine is told to run)

Videos to watch:

  • “How a Car Engine Works” https://youtu.be/DKF5dKo_r_Y

    • Notes: bare bones basics 

  • “HOW IT WORKS: Internal Combustion Engine” https://youtu.be/vIJ50aUiBgM

    • Notes: Good to understand the roots of where newer technology came from but these are the main differences between the video and what we use.

    • Carburetors are a mechanical device that mixes air and fuel. Our team uses separate fuel injectors and throttle body. Some cars have direct fuel injection and some have port injection. We use port injection. 

    • We use an electronic fuel pump. The one shown in the video is for a (v8) and many v8’s use a mechanically driven fuel pump. 

    • The video mentions distributors / points / coil. This is how many older cars controlled spark and timing. We control all spark through MoTeC.

    • The most obvious difference is that the video shows a v8 we use an inline 4.

  • “How a Motorcycle Transmission Works! (Animation)” https://youtu.be/E2CybLSrN5Q

    • Notes: We have not altered anything in the R6 transmission; however, an understanding of how a bike transmission works is helpful. 

    • Sequential Transmissions are different from H pattern transmissions that are in your manual cars. Upshifting without using the clutch is the main difference.

  • "Manual Mill Overview": Manual Mill Overview
    • What is the point of designing a part if it is unable to be manufactured? 
    • This is a short and quick confluence page of how a mill works and should give you a decent idea of what kind of operations can be done on a mill.
  • "Manual Lathe Overview": Manual Lathe Overview 
    • Again, what is the point of designing a part if it is unable to be manufacture? This is is super important, and I will stress this point multiple times!
    • This page should give you a general idea of what operations can be done on a lathe.
  • "Press Brake Metal Bending Crash Course" https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=73QtcoXrHb4
    • This is a good video about how metals can be bent. This is especially useful if you are working with sheet metal.
    • Generally, it is easier to bend a piece of metal into a specific shape then it is to mill that same shape out of a block, so remember that when designing your component. Ease of manufacturing is almost as important as manufacturability itself!
    • Think about making sheet metal components like folding paper, but remember that you need to be able to use a break to bend those pieces. Think about that when designing your components and how you can create designs that have impossible bends.
