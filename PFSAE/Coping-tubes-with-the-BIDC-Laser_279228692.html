Purdue Formula SAE : Coping tubes with the BIDC Laser

Created by Hardan, Jonathan Charbel on Nov 16, 2023

With the Power of the BIDC laser cutter, coping tubes has never been easier. 

Step 1: Import Tubes from NX into Solidworks

i) NX doesn't have a specific function we need to use so we are switching softwares


Step 2: Create a sketch and extrude cut a portion of the tube. 

i) In the sketch make a small triangle with one vertex at the center of the tube and the other two just outside the OD of the tube.

ii) Once the sketch has been complete, use the Cut Extrude tool to cut out this sliver along the whole length of the tube


Step 3: Surface-Flatten Tool

i) use the surface flatten tool to create a "rolled out" version of the tube. In the dialogue box, select the outer face of your tube as the Face/Surface to Flatten (blue box), and select the edge of the cut you made that is on the outside of the tube to be the edge you flatten about (pink box). Put your accuracy on high and show both mesh and flat preview. It should turn out like this. 

ii) make sure to remove all shadows and fancy view settings from Solidworks or you WILL crash Solidworks


Step 4: Exporting as DXF/DWG: 

i) once you have your flat tube, export the Solidworks file as a DXF or DWG. In the DXF/DWG dialogue box, select the edges you want to cut.


Step 5: Prepping DXF/DWG:

i) open up Autocad and open your drawing. In Autocad, make sure your dimensions are correct and then align the tube with the origin such that it looks like this: 

Hint: Measure out the angle between an edge and the x-axis and use the rotation tool to align it with the x-axis. Then just reassign the origin to the bottom left corner.


Conclusion: 

Once your drawing is like this, you are ready to take you tubes to the BIDC so that you can get them cut.
