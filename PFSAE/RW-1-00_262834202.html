Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Reduce lower endplate area

In order to reduce the impact of rollover due to crosswinds, the endplate is to be reduced in area. The following design reduces the lateral size of the endplate from 25 inches to 22 inches (this is a bit sketchy but doing some limit testing here) and utilizes a spline to cut out the endplate on the lower surface side of the RW endplate. 

The curvature attempts to utilize the geometry similar to a dive plane, which should generate additional DF. Additionally, the endplate follows the RW curvature in order to reduce the adverse effects of inwashing flow. 

I am fully expecting separation on the dive plane-like portion. This may result in additional inwashing flow resulting in worse performance due to the low pressure generated from flow sep.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

Flow separates WAY later than I expected, this resulted in a 6-point RW DF jump. Additionally, the inwashing flow mentioned previously just ends up generating an upwashing vortex. This vortex ends up merging with the main vortex generated by the RW. Potentially could be a substantial area of drag increase since drag increased by roughly 2 points. 


How does this RW affect the undertray? The additional upwash generated by the side gurneys results in marginally worse stall characteristics at the outlet. Slightly reducing undertray DF. 


Overall, good change, improved DF by 3 points overall, and shifted the balance backward 3%.
