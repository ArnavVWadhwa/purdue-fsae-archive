Suspension


Chassis

Similarly to previous years, the preliminary design event was held in a small venue toghether with 10-ish other teams, hence it was very hard to hear what the judge was saying.

Questions:

  • What was your target torsional rigidity value for the chassis?
  • Why did you set that target and how did you reach that conclusion?

→ To which Christian explained that we didn't have a "numerical" target for torsional rigidity and that our aim was a rules-minimum chassis becasue based on PF20 destructive testing, it was concluded that a rules-minimum chassis allowed for sufficient tunability of suspension (i.e., suspension wasn't too heavily affected by a rules-minimum-stiff chassis)

  • Explain what "suspension tunability" means
    • What happens if the chassis is overly stiff or overly flexible?

Okay so the judge repeated this same question 3 times even after different explanations given by Christian and Arpit, which include (LLTD control, oversteer to understeer tuning, control and response capabilities, starting from assumption of infinitely stiff chassis, etc.)

  • What were your target margins of safety for the chassis (under your load cases)?

→ 20% for yield and 40% for ultimate 

  • What happens if there is a bump in loading and the stress experienced by the chassis exceeds that?
    • How did you guys decide on these 20% and 40% numbers?
  • Why did you guys choose not to triangulate this area (the area around FBHS and FRH)? (refer to this page for more details) 
  • During a cornering case, which tube is the weakest link in the chassis? 
  • Which area of the chassis was the most challenging to design?
