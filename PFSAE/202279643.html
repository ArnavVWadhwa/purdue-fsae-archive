Syncing the SharePoint to your computer

OneDrive is super cool and integrates with your Purdue Office 365 stuff (you'll probably use this stuff at a real job and it makes life easier so practice using it now)

Pre-req

Have onedrive downloaded to you computer to save local files, it must be logged into your purdue account, this may be a second onedrive program (onedrive for business) if you already use a personal onedrive. https://www.itap.purdue.edu/services/microsoft-office-365.htmlÂ (i usually just download all office 365 products from this link for purdue access)

      1. Navigate to the files tab on the teams channel you want to locally download files, (probably MoTeC I2)
      2. Select Open in SharePoint
      3. It will open a link in your web browser
      4. Select add folder to OneDrive, you may need to click the 3 dots to expand the options
      5. You will now have a folder locally on your OneDrive that will sync

Enabling the database feature in i2

The easiest and best way to access data in i2 is through the database feature that i2 has. This will enable you to see all the data stored in the drive on one page, with a lot of information about each piece of data.

i. Open i2 Pro and find the top toolbar

ii. Click on tools, and then click on "Options". A window should pop up.

iii. Navigate to the "Database" page at the top of the window's toolbar.

iv. Click add where it says Search Folders and add the synced sharepoint folder containing i2 data.

v. Remove any other folders from the "Search Folders" window, and then click "OK" at the bottom.

vi. Now click on "File" at the top toolbar of i2, then click on "Open Log File".

vii. At the top left corner of this window, you should see a silver symbol next to the folder icon. Reference the picture below for this icon. Click on this icon.

viii. Now you are in the "Database". Here you can sort through files and open them very quickly.
