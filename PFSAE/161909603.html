Purdue Formula SAE : Tuning List/Walkthrough

Created by Williams, Carson Kenneth, last modified by Unknown User (reyno105) on Apr 03, 2021

Safety/Pre-tune Checklist

  • Oil should not feel like water. CHECK IT
  • Coolant tank filled.
  • No water leaks/oil leaks. 
  • Fuel in tank and fuel pressure.
  • Make sure the sensors work. Most importantly-lambda, inlet air pressure and temperature, oil pressure, fuel pressure in injectors, throttle position
  • Engine runs and generates load
  • FIRE EXTINGUISHER Unknown User (reyno105) 
  • Make sure battery is connected and everything is secure on the table. (No rattling objects)


Things to do/watch before tuning

Record the absolute throttle position because it will affect volumetric efficiency if you change the calibration of the throttle sensor.

Watch lambda and make sure it does not go completely out of whack

Watch oil pressure to make sure you aren't going to explode another engine.

Watch coolant temperature during long runs to make sure it doesn't exceed spooky temperatures


VOLUMETRIC EFFICIENCY TUNING!

Engine Charge Cooling Gain Calibration

  • Choose a fixed operating point at a reasonable load that can easily be maintained
  • Set fuel mixture aim (FMA) to 1.0 lambda (maybe lower if you want)
  • Adjust engine efficiency so that the exhaust lambda is equal to the new FMA
  • Set FMA to .9 lambda and check exhaust lambda is to .9 FMA

                  ***If the Exhaust lambda > FMA reduce the charge cooling gain primary***

                  ***If the Exhaust lambda < FMA increase the charge cooling gain primary***

  • When you switch between the two values and Exhaust lambda is tracking the fuel mixture aim perfectly you can move on


Start Tuning

  1. Turn off closed loop fuel
  2. Switch to the secondary map
  3. Hold the engine at each 1000 rpm and you can quickly press Q for a quick change in the efficiency table
  4. Boom you have an engine efficiency map. Crazy
