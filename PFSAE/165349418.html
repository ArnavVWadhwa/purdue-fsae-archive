Purdue Formula SAE : Flanged Bushings / Traffic Cones

Created by Unknown User (reyno105) on Dec 10, 2020

Traffic cones can all be found under TC in global hardware. The video on this page (Standard Team Bolts, Washers, and Nuts - Purdue Formula SAE - Confluence) covers accessing that hardware.


TC-CS-0X-XX

TC-SS-0X-XX


TC = it's a traffic cone

CS / SS = Carbon steel and stainless steel. Carbon steel traffic cones are 4130 normalized and stainless steal  are ETD 150. (Admittedly ETD 150 is not stainless steel but stainless steel was the original plan)

-0X = diameter of bolt in 16ths of an inch. 03 = #10, 04 = 1/4", 05 = 5/16" etc

-XX = length of traffic cone shank in in 1/16 of an inch


example: TC-SS-06-10 is an ETD 150 traffic cone for a 3/8 bolt and has a shank that 10/16" (5/8") long  



They are sized so that they can hold the compressive load of torqueing the bolt (preload). Below spreadsheet includes those calcs.
