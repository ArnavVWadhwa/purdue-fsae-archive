Changes/Justifications:

Changes PictureJustification 
  • Cut made across the upper endplate as such.
  • Endplate was shortened 7 inches vertically and cut was made 18.3 inches deep.
  • Angle of 120 degrees at the vertex


  • It will hopefully create an out-washing vortex due to the pressure difference between the outside and rear wing, as per Chenkai and Matthew, to improve the outwash.

Baseline


Iteration


Addition of a very low-pressure region on the vertex of the cut. Instead of being on the endplate, it seems to impose itself on the airfoils as well.

Very low-pressure region generated on the trailing end of the endplate as well: not too sure if this is wanted.

Also, a gaping region through the curved lower trailing edge. I don't know where that's coming from

Baseline


Iteration

Verified by aftfore view. The cut actually induced inwash (I think) on the airfoils. This possibly explains the loss of downforce. A possible solution would be somehow angling the endplate extrusion so the low-pressure region occurs outside the endplate. 

Also noticed what I think is a larger vortex outside the endplate due to the cut. Again, maybe an angled extrusion might help.
