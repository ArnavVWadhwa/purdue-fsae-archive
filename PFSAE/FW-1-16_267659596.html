Purdue Formula SAE : FW-1-16

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Jul 31, 2023

Simulation ML-035

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification
Maintained strake orientation from 1-15, and made a more defined separation point.

The defined separation point should act to reduce separation losses.
Down washing flaps changed to include a dihedral section.

This should result in a downwashing outwashing vortex that improves tire wake behind the tire and improves the flow field entering the RW region. 

The flap was also moved more downstream to capitalize more on the downwashing affect the vortex has on the tire wake.

The sweep was altered because I don't think aligning the sweep with the flow matters in the inner section because of the wake produced on the down wind side. This should reduce flow separation seen on this side.

CFD Analysis

CFD PictureDescription

This Iteration:


FW-1-14:

This iteration displays vortex bursting on the inner most strake. This is likely due to the strake separating from the 2nd element too early. This causes a low-pressure region at the separation which results in the higher energy flow being drawn to it, reducing vortex strength, resulting in premature vortex breakdown. 

This Iteration:

FW-1-14:


Despite premature vortex breakdown, this iteration performed better regarding the flow field to the UT as seen by the performance improvement. Inwash was considerably reduced, likely due to fewer low-pressure regions inboard. 


Due to the flow sep on the downwind side, the vortex is upwashing and inwashing; however, since this is the side that isn't critical, the performance of downstream components was not hurt. 

The vortex generated on the windward side controls the downstream RW flow field considerably, leading to exceptional RW performance.
