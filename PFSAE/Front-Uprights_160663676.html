Purdue Formula SAE : Front Uprights

Created by Unknown User (xia107), last modified by Unknown User (roger305) on Nov 29, 2021

Design Criteria/Constraints:

  • Must work alongside other designs
  • Lighter than PF20
  • all necessary mounts
  • handles load
  • reasonable packaging

Requirements:

  • Packaging
    • Adjustable camber +1.0 to -1.5 degrees
    • no interferences at full toe out and toe in
    • hardware must be accessible with /some/ ease for maintenance and installations
    • Connects to control arms and tierod and sustains loads
    • brake caliper mounts
    • hall sensor mount
  • Loads
    • Withstands temperature 250 F
    • hits appropriate safety factors
      • 1.4 - ultimate strength, 1.2 - dynamic uncertainty factor, etc.
    • meets load cases

Components:

  • Camber Piece
  • Front/Rear Upright
  • Shims

Modeling Process:

  • 4 main components
    • bearing hole
    • upper control arm mount
    • lower control arm mount
    • tierod mount
  • models utilize layout part file with suspension points from chassis folder
  • Points of high stress: FEA forever
    • Tierod lug mounts 
    • Control Arm Mounts
  • Giant lipped hole
    • lip for reduction of lateral motion of hub 
  • hall mount and caliper mount points from sus point layout - predetermined
