Purdue Formula SAE : RC Servo Shifter | PF20

Created by Unknown User (jpeerbol), last modified by Paradise, Hunter on Jul 16, 2023

Tested with Savox SB2292SG.

Provided voltage makes a noticeable difference is speed and torque.

Was able to shift out of neutral to first or second. Not enough torque to shift out of second gear. Shifting in to higher gears seems to require more torque.

Would like to measure stall current. Was poorly measured, but seemed to be peaking at 3 amps, not ~10 amps as spec'd by savox 


Possible issues:

shift arm ratio, was tested with slightly shorter arm on servo, should have provided more torque?


Maybe try a more powerful servo or cut losses on this project.

If continuing figure out speed specification for shifting, I believe servos are rated at no load for speed so a shifting load of 500+ oz-in could dramatically slow the shift.

Could look at Savox SB2290SG, roughly twice the torque and half the speed.

Or https://www.pololu.com/product/1391/specs this thing is massive, the largest PWM controlled rc servo I could find. Can run on 12V, rated at 1600 oz-in, if you can't shift with this, give up and make it a push-pull cable. This looks like it will need a different mount.


Notes: shifter mount was flexing, if continuing with this project the base plate will probably need more support
