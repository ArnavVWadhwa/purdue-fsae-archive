Purdue Formula SAE : FW-2-12

Created by Matthew James Lamear, last modified on Aug 21, 2023

Simulation ML-054

Changes/Justifications

Description of the ChangePictureJustification

Increased length of 2nd and 3rd strakes.

NOTE: Reverted back to FW-2-08 since the previous designs saw no considerable improvements.

This should result in a more powerful vortex on the third strake, with a lesser vortex on the 2nd strake. The hope is that the 4th strake vortex merges with the 3rd which allows it to survive longer and power up to reduce inwash and potentially having that vortex survive all the way to next to the tire would have a massive impact on UT performance.

CFD Analysis

Vortex almost live the whole way, strake ends too early and ultimately leads to vortex bursting just prior to vortex merging.

This is actually an interesting concept, one that I now understand relating to F1 front wing design. The outwashing strakes, including shorter strakes outboard and longer strakes inboard allows for vortex merging to generate a substantial vortex in the outboard section and then the inboard strakes provide a barrier of outwashing vortices which allows the outer vortex to remain healthy. 

Not sure if this concept would work for FSAE because I think the inner strakes would burst much too early. But if that is controllable it could lead to a considerable amount of FW/UT performance.
