Purdue Formula SAE : Jim Shaw Questions 10/1/2022

Created by Unknown User (dewey2), last modified by Unknown User (xia107) on Oct 01, 2022

General Analysis Setup Questions

  • Frictional Contacts
    • Formulation: Best practices
      • Leave as program controlled? Some materials have suggested some formulations are better for certain tasks (i.e. Normal Lagrange for interference)
      • Augmented Lagrange is generally the default
    • Allowable penetration and Normal Stiffness
      • What is a way to determine an appropriate allowable penetration? Less penetration would probably lead to more difficult convergence
      • Is this tied to the normal stiffness factor? Higher factor = less penetration, more difficult convergence, more accurate results?
      • Is a higher normal stiffness always better for solution accuracy at the cost of convergence time?
    • Pinball region
      • As long as the two contact surfaces remain in the pinball region throughout solving/substeps is it sufficient?
        • Frictional contact → Does pinball look for the nodes attached to the target/contact surfaces?
        • Bonded/no separation → Does pinball take all nodes in the pinball?
      • Is there a downside to have too large of a pinball region? Does it assume all elements within pinball are relevant to the contact?
  • Mesh
    • For contacts, is quad - tri / hex-tet contacts ok or should contacts generally have same element type and order?


More Specific Analysis Questions

  • Brake Rotor
    • Stabilization damping factor 
      • Initial model has a 0.003" gap between two contact surfaces that are set to a frictional contact
      • A stabilization damping factor of 0.1 was used to obtain a solution to compensate for rigid body motion
      • Reducing this value to 0.05 didn't seem to impact the results
      • Does this factor affect the results, or is it ignored once rigid body motion is gone? Would you need a damping factor convergence?
    • Normal Stiffness Factor
      • Changed from 1 to 3 in several increments
      • Solution changed slightly, maybe appeared to have less penetration (visually inspected, not measured)
      • Solve time did increase as expected, when do you say it's fine or should we just leave it to program controlled?
  • Diff Uprights
    • Trying to model a bearing press fit
    • Frictional contact
    • Interference of 0.0006" via contact status tool
    • Pinball set to 0.0007"
    • I get a solution and it shows the area is stressed due to the interference, but doesn't quite seem correct in terms of stress and displacement contours
