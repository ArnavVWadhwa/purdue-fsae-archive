Purdue Formula SAE : Electronics Presentation Notes

Created by Unknown User (jpeerbol), last modified on Jun 15, 2020


Our Judge: Anthony Musci

Team Lead: Michael Tam

Also There: Jessie Krawitz

For systems integration/electronics integration

 

Questions/Answers:

What did you do that is unique?

  • We talked about the shifter

 

Tell me about the algorithm used for the shifter.

  • Shifter is in parallel with the shift arm which is unique from other team
  • Refer to the slide for shifting in the presentation with flow diagram

 

Does the shifter have error messages? What are the failsafes?

  • The shifter does not have error messages. There is the 50 msecond limit on shifting.

 

Give me an idea of the big picture of the team.

  • The wiring harness
  • Various other projects to make car run better and to acquire data

 

Decisions made/thought process when designing the shifter?

  • Each component is off the shelf and had a variety of parameter used to find the best option including cost, power consumption, and others specific to the component(such as torque and the motor)

 

Explain the Dash/steering wheel and expand on traction and launch control.

  • Shows driver data
  • Make sure to talk about time stamp(for determining discrepancies in the run) and next page buttons.
  • Traction and launch control are motec scripts and we have on/off as well as intensity controls for them.
  • Dash has basic controls and steering wheel has some shifting capabilities as well as page button and time stamp.
  • We have 3 main pages with varying depths of information
  • Timestamp marks time to look at data at x time to see when it’s a problem

 

Walk me through the no lift shifting

  • Our no lift shifting(flat foot) checks the engine rpm and throttle position
  • If throttle is mostly open and rpm is high then it upshifts

 

Does the system know what gear it is in?

  • Yes it does. Output/input speed gets gear ratio as backup to sensor

Post Design Thoughts

To be completely honest, it was pretty bad. it was less about getting shafted by the judge and more lacking the answers on my part when probed by the judge. i believe that this is the result of a couple of factors: by setting the expectation that we're aiming for design finals, he was looking for proof of much more higher level design than i provided. secondly, there was the subject of system integration that i couldn't address because i hadn't prepared for it at all. you could argue that it was out of my scope, but that doens't really fly when you're in front of the judge and telling him that you're the systems guy.

What should we do?

At least for electronics, it probably makes sense to still prepare the proof for the basics but expect it to be glossed over. figure out what's meant by higher level- maybe i should have focused on things like the behavior of certain modules, why i included them, and then how they were utilized in the context of the overall functionality. assuming that you've done the work, make sure you can answer why it's the way it is.

Alongside this, we should either explore the systems integration part from the perspective of the whole car, getting the chief engineer to do that, or make it its own role. given the historic limit of humans on the team, it would be best to combine it with another role. there's pros and cons for packaging it with electronics TL or chief; on one hand, the chief would be in the best position to do so as he has to take every subsystem into acount already. on the other hand, we're constantly finding that electronics honestly needs to play a bigger role in following other subsystems and figuring out how we can best serve the car. i'm not sure how it would be best to embed our subsystem into the others, but maybe explore assigning individual ELE members to join other subteams or interview other members (definitely not setting up a spy network for a hostile takeover of the team). the alternative is probably interrogating other TLs, but this is limited to you as another TL and the time everyone has for anything. actually, maybe try a mixture of both- there are certain things that only come up in convos involving two dedicated members, but it might help to let our liason ponder about the possibilities. plus, it would help solve the problem of limited inter-team relation building between ELE and the other subteams.

Other ideas include more data tracking and tracing (through I2 pro- examine and process historic data to see trends over time? better data analysis?), as well as maybe showing more team data usage cases. might be worth a shot to also cover team structure and organization, data base and knowledge, but i feel that this starts to step away from car design and toward more organization building, which is not really the focus of the design event
