Purdue Formula SAE : Cylinder Head Flow Bench

Created by Williams, Carson Kenneth, last modified by Horton, Tyler Jason on Nov 08, 2023

One of the most crucial components towards developing a high flow engine that correlates well with simulations is understanding the flow through the engine. Its easy to calculate flow through parts like runners and exhaust tubes but very difficult to come up with flow coefficients and mass flow for components like intake and exhaust ports coupled with valves that are changing in height.

A standard tool in the industry used to measure this is called a flow bench.Â 

These are super expensive to purchase and not viable to repeatedly use someone else's, so its pretty standard for people to make DIY versions:


In principle they are super basic to set up with not many parts:

Hardware:

  • Vacuum source (typically a shop vac)
  • Moveable bench
  • Sealable ring to the cylinder head (sometimes 3d printed to match cylinder head geometry) (would be cool if it was clear so we could blow smoke into the ports and see swirl patterns)
  • Pressure relieve valve to balance vacuum pressure behind cylinder head
  • Sometimes use a water height indicator to make sure at "industry standard vacuum pressure"

  • Dial indicator for measuring valve lift
  • Lightweight spring(s) so the valves are easy to push down
  • Some form of threaded pushrod to incrementally open the valve(s)

Electronics:

  • Mass Airflow sensor (typically a cheap OEM MAF that also has temperature and a data sheet)
  • Pressure sensors if not using a water height indicator (this is probably the way to go)
  • Arduino or some DAQ device
  • Laptop for recording things
