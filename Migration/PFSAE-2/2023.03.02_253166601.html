      • SIQ:
      • Formatting: 
        • Text sizing - do not reduce standard line spacing as this hurts readibility and looks inconsistent. We can use as small as 14pt font.
        • Figure labels: They should be size 14. The Figure X.X.X part should be bolded. The whole thing should be italicized. example:
          • Figure 1.2.3: Comparison of hours slept before and after becoming responsible for a section of the design report
          • this figure would be in section 1, on the 2nd slide (counting starts with 1 as the dark title slide), and the 3rd figure on that page
          • Also, figure labels can be on any side of the image, but they should be centered wherever they are.
          • The first word of the figure label should capitalized. The remaining words are only capitalized if they need to be bc they're proper nouns
        • First slides: should be "Overview and Goals"
        • Section slides: the individual slide titles should be "Subsection - slide content" The first slide that includes the outline then has the name of each subsection.
        • For citations in text, use [1]. Two forms of in text citations are:
          • If you just state a number of theory, you can put "[1]" at the end of the sentence.
          • If you are directly referencing the ciation, you should give it's name in italics and add the [1] after. example
            • Airframe Stress Analysis and Sizing [1] was used to create warm feelings in design judges regarding the design capability of the team...
        • Citations at the bottom of the slide use: 1. Citation text
