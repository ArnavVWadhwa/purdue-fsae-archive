Miscellaneous

  1. Throughout the design period (PDR - CDR - IDR - MRR) I also gave design feedback on all subsystems of the car via a handful of CAD Review sessions. I primarily focused on feedback oriented towards mass optimization, design for manufacturing, install/disassembly, and interactions with testing logistics.
  2. Supporting with manufacturing work, mainly focused on stock prep for uprights and hubs with the goal of being productive without detracting from the educational experience of other subteam members. In other words, I tried to focus on the less interesting tasks such as squaring up stock. As of 11/21/2022 I have spent ~80 hours in the machine shop.
  3. Development of NX front and rear suspension Kinematics files for the purpose of checking component collisions, clearances, and travel throughout steering and heave actuation. How-to of that process here - NX Kinematics.
  4. In an attempt to make our fatigue considerations more substantiated, I did a brief study of the load cycle counts that rotating components might experience in one full season. This is primarily focused on brakes, hubs, and wheels. The cycle counts derived from PF22 vehicle data is here - Fatigue Envelope and Vehicle Lifetime Definition.
  5. Another notable contribution is just general support to Zach during his first semester as team lead. This included keeping suspension CAD organized throughout design and giving design guidance to suspension project owners.
