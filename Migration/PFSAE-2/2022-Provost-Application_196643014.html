Connor's draft from PF20: 

Dear Office of the Provost,

I am writing this in regards to a potential sponsorship for the Purdue student engineering organization, Formula SAE (Society of Automotive Engineers). Our goal as an organization is to prepare students for careers in engineering, manufacturing, business, technology, and science. Due to the support of our current sponsors and dedicated eighty team members, our team was able to place 33rd out of 108 at the Formula SAE Michigan event this year. We have been racing for over 25 years with Purdue University and have a 4th place finish at Michigan in 2009, 13th in 2014, and 14th in 2018. We’re striving for much greater success in the future, and through your support we will. We want to expand our competition season to include the Formula West competition in California. This will allow our members to participate in another round of meticulous technical judging of design and performance, and career opportunities through SAE sponsors. We are also developing and testing a carbon fiber monocoque which is a massive design challenge that our undergraduate students have undertaken. We are looking for your support to propel our students hard work into successful careers as engineers.


New draft for PF22:

Hi Kristi,

Our team would like to apply for sponsorship from the Office of the Provost. Our program aims to prepare students for careers in engineering related fields by competing in the Formula SAE competition each year, which involves designing, manufacturing, and competing with an open wheel racecar. This competition requires detailed engineering analysis, precision fabrication of parts, thorough project management, and a solid business foundation to succeed. Our team has members from the colleges of engineering, technology, and management to create a vehicle that can compete at a national level. Each competition is attended by upwards of 100 teams from universities around the country. Our organization has finished in the top 15 teams at our competitions 8 times in our 30 year history, with our best finishes coming this past year during the 2021 season, including a 2nd place finish out of 132 teams worldwide in the design event, and 6th and 9th place finishes at competitions in Michigan and Nevada. The process of building the car allows for our members to apply material learned in class to a real world project, providing tangible skills that can be used in industry during internships and after graduation. Our team has no requirement for major, class standing, or prior experience, and there are no dues associated with our club, meaning any student can join and start learning.

Our team receives approximately $50,000 in sponsorship each year, with ~$20,000 coming from within Purdue and the remaining $30,000 coming from corporate sponsors. This year, our team is making a major architecture change by switching to a new engine to further improve our competition results, incurring greater costs compared to previous years. As shown in our attached budget, we have a deficit of approximately $20,000. We would like to request $12,000 from the office of the Provost this year if funds are available. We have several leads on additional funding to eliminate our deficit.

Let me know if you need any other information from us 

Tyler
