Taken from reddit post here.

"We did a lot of development on ETC back in the early 2000s when it was first allowed. We had to do a bit of development and adapt it to the new engine when they lifted the ban a few years ago. We have an engine dyno in house and worked a lot on calibration and control/reliability.

Did you mean rev matching for downshifts? Can easily use it to blip throttle if needed for down shifts. For upshifting allows you to use different shifting techniques, such as spark or throttle cut. Makes it easier on the driver not having to lift/blip manually. Or cuts out reliability problems with using a mechanical blipper. We can see more control on throttle curves and resolution. Can use different maps for different drivers or events. Like skid pad has a very different map from autocross etc. Can also easily limit throttle travel on the fly for fuel consumption or new driver training. Easier to modulate throttle for instances like cold start or hot start. Adjust idle remotely on the fly, etc. Just allows you more control in every specific instance. Can easily optimize all steps of the process without any need for manual input."

-Someone from Wisconsin
