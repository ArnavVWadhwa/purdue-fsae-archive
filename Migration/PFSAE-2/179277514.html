Leadership

  • 1:1s were good keep those. They were better for keeping track of schedule than all other stuff
  • Everyone says this but i still messed it up. More intermediate deadlines, even for good people. If someone thinks it will work first time, they'll put it off. ALWAYS. And people are optimistic about things working the first time.
  • Do blockers in TL meetings. People often try to get held up on something out of their control so they can not worry about doing something. These constraints are often not real
    • Try is a strong word, but it has seemed like people are a little too happy to get held up on issues owned by other folks on the team
  • When someone shows you their work is careless, believe them the first time. Communicate this to team leads often. We waste too much time trying to give projects to people who won't ever be able to good independent work because they treat team projects like class projects - if the analysis runs it's good. It's very different if someone makes honest mistakes, but stuff like not constraining CAD dimensions has pretty consistently been a sign that good work isn't going to happen.
