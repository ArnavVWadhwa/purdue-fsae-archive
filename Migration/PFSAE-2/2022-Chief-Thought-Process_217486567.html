Final Engine Selection Decision (May 2021):

  • The end of the 2020 season and the start of the 2021 season created an overlap in which I was faced with the decision to switch from the 4-Cylinder R6 architecture to the 1-Cylinder WR450F Architecture.
  • Ben Reynold's architecture study suggested that the 1-Cylinder would yield 40 more points, more significant than any realistic R6 architecture modifications.
    • This was with an assumed torque curve taken from Akron
  • 2020 was the first time the waterbrake dyno had been fully operational since 2012, and we were able to successfully tune the PF20, R6 powertrain. I was confident we could do similar work on a single cylinder. 
    • Before summer we attempted to implement to implement the WR450F onto the dyno but we ran out of time between synchronization, ignition, and other harness issues. 
    • I really wanted to have a baseline torque curve before making this engine change decision
  • 2020 was also the last year of the R6, meaning that any new engine we could get our hands on would be a used engine. (not taking advantage of our yamaha sponsorship to get complete long blocks)
  • For the past several years before 2020/2021, powertrain development on this team was very poor
    • "we did it last year" was said a scary amount when dealing with R6 architecture design / manufacturing
    • Throwing the team and myself in the "deep end" would ensure we fully understood and knew the limits of our design. 
  • With all this information, it seemed that the best step moving forward was to choose the WR450F
    • Full sending this without the preliminary torque curve 
    • Trusted myself to figure out everything we needed to get the engine fully operational, on time
      • I knew that the worst case scenario, we would push first run back from early november to mid february
      • After all it takes professional companies at least 3 work weeks to calibrate a simple engine fully
